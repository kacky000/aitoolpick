# Doctolib Review 2026: Is It Worth It?

**Short answer:** Doctolib is the dominant online booking and telehealth platform for healthcare in France, Germany, and Italy. It's free and excellent for patients. For practitioners, it's a polished, reliable system — but it's a meaningful monthly subscription and is genuinely useful only if you're based in one of its European markets. Outside Europe, choose a local alternative.

This review covers what Doctolib actually does, what it costs, and who should (and shouldn't) use it.

> **Information last verified: June 2, 2026.** Practitioner pricing is quoted on request and varies by country and specialty — always confirm current rates directly with Doctolib before committing.

## What is Doctolib?

Doctolib is a European healthcare technology company (founded in France in 2013) that lets patients find practitioners, book appointments online, and attend video consultations. For doctors, dentists, and clinics, it provides scheduling, patient records, reminders, and telehealth in one system. It is the market leader in France and has expanded into Germany and Italy.

## Key Features

- **Online booking** — Patients book, reschedule, or cancel 24/7 without phoning the practice, cutting front-desk load.
- **Telehealth** — Built-in video consultations with integrated booking and (in supported regions) payment.
- **Patient management** — Centralized records, history, and notes accessible across a practice's practitioners.
- **Calendar sync** — Two-way sync that prevents double-booking across a practitioner's existing calendar.
- **Automated reminders** — SMS and email reminders that measurably reduce no-shows.
- **Secure messaging** — GDPR-compliant messaging between patients and practices.
- **Multi-practitioner support** — Shared scheduling for clinics and group practices.

## Pricing

Doctolib is **free for patients** — searching and booking never costs anything. Practitioners pay a monthly subscription, billed in euros and quoted on request; published figures have historically been in the region of **€129/month** for a solo practice, with team and enterprise pricing arranged directly.

## Pros

- Market leader in European healthcare booking with deep practitioner coverage
- Completely free and fast for patients, with instant confirmation
- Strong, well-integrated telehealth
- Automated reminders that cut no-show rates
- Reliable calendar sync for busy practices

## Cons

- **Europe-only** — effectively useful only in France, Germany, and Italy
- **Cost adds up for solo practices** — the monthly fee is significant for a single practitioner
- **French-first** — the interface and support are most mature for the French market
- Opaque, quote-based pricing makes upfront budgeting harder

## Who should use Doctolib?

**A good fit for:**
- Patients in France, Germany, or Italy who want fast online booking
- Medical and dental practices in those markets wanting to reduce admin and no-shows
- Clinics and hospitals needing multi-practitioner scheduling and telehealth

**Look elsewhere if you:**
- Practice outside Doctolib's European markets
- Are a solo practitioner who books few enough appointments that the fee is hard to justify

With a strong 4.4/5 reputation, Doctolib is the clear leader in European healthcare booking. For patients it's a free, fast way to find care. For practices in France, Germany, or Italy, it's a capable all-in-one system worth its fee if it meaningfully cuts admin time and no-shows. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Doctolib free?

Yes — Doctolib is completely free for patients to search for practitioners and book appointments. Practitioners and practices pay a monthly subscription to list and manage their bookings.

### How much does Doctolib cost for doctors?

Practitioner pricing is quoted on request and varies by country and specialty. Published figures have historically been around €129/month for a solo professional, with team and hospital pricing arranged directly with Doctolib. Confirm the current rate before subscribing.

### What countries does Doctolib work in?

Doctolib operates in France, Germany, and Italy. It is the market leader in France and is not currently a practical option for patients or practices outside these European markets.

### Is Doctolib safe and GDPR-compliant?

Doctolib operates under EU data-protection rules (GDPR) and uses secure, encrypted messaging for patient communication. As with any health platform, review its current privacy terms for the specifics that apply to you.

### What are the best alternatives to Doctolib?

In Europe, [Jane App](/tools/jane-app/) and [SimplePractice](/tools/simplepractice/) are common alternatives for smaller practices. In the US, [Zocdoc](/tools/zocdoc/) is the leading patient-facing booking platform.
