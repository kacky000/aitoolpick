**Google Meet** is a popular software tool used by individuals and teams for productivity and collaboration. In this review, we evaluate its features, pricing, pros, cons, and alternatives for 2026.

Google Meet has spent years being "the video tool you use if you already have Google Workspace." For a long time that was a polite way of saying "it's not as good as [Zoom](/tools/zoom/), but it's free." In 2026, that framing no longer holds up. Gemini integration has transformed Meet into a serious competitor on AI-driven meeting intelligence, and the core video experience has closed most of the quality gap.

Here is an honest review of Google Meet in 2026 — what it does well, where it still trails, and whether Google Workspace companies should cancel [Zoom](/tools/zoom/).

## The Short Answer

**Choose Google Meet if:** Your company is on Google Workspace, your meetings are mostly internal, or you want strong AI meeting features (Gemini) bundled into the suite you already pay for.

**Skip Google Meet if:** You run webinars or large external events, you need the absolute best video quality in poor network conditions, or you need deep AI meeting intelligence on the free tier.

## Pricing at a Glance

Google Meet is included in Google Workspace plans:

- **Free (personal Google account):** 60-minute limit, 100 participants, basic features
- **Business Starter ($7.20/user/month):** 60-minute limit, 100 participants, meeting recording not included
- **Business Standard ($14.40/user/month):** 24-hour meetings, 150 participants, recording, noise cancellation, Gemini basic
- **Business Plus ($21.60/user/month):** 500 participants, attendance tracking, advanced Gemini features
- **Enterprise (custom):** 1,000 participants, live streaming, Gemini Advanced

Note: Gemini Advanced features like meeting summaries and "take notes for me" are included in Business Standard and above. This is a significant pricing advantage over Microsoft 365 (Copilot is $30/user/month extra).

## What Google Meet Does Well in 2026

**Gemini-powered meeting intelligence.** "Take notes for me" produces usable meeting summaries automatically. Gemini can answer questions about what was discussed, generate action items, and translate transcripts across 100+ languages. The feature set now matches or exceeds [Zoom](/tools/zoom/) AI Companion and costs nothing extra for Workspace customers.

**Browser-native experience.** Meet runs entirely in Chrome. No app install, no sign-in dance for guests with Google accounts, no update prompts during a call. For companies with managed devices, this is a significant IT win.

**Calendar integration.** Every [Google Calendar](/tools/google-calendar/) event gets a Meet link by default. Scheduling is one click, and Gemini can now suggest optimal meeting times across attendees' calendars.

**Noise cancellation and low-light adjustment.** Both work well out of the [box](/tools/box/). The AI-powered background noise removal is now on par with Krisp.

**Adaptive layouts.** The 2025 UI refresh finally delivered good gallery, spotlight, and tile-resize behavior. It no longer feels clunky compared to [Zoom](/tools/zoom/).

## Where Google Meet Still Trails

**Video quality under poor network conditions.** [Zoom](/tools/zoom-meetings/) still wins here. If you are on hotel Wi-Fi or a spotty mobile connection, Zoom degrades more gracefully.

**Webinar and events capabilities.** Google Meet lacks a true competitor to Zoom Events or Zoom Webinars. For external-facing events, registration workflows, and monetization, Meet is not the answer.

**Breakout rooms and in-meeting engagement.** Polls, Q&A, and breakout rooms exist but feel less polished than Zoom's implementations.

**Virtual backgrounds and studio effects.** Google has steadily improved these, but Zoom still has the edge on visual effects.

**Third-party integrations.** Zoom has a larger ecosystem of partner integrations (recording services, transcription, analytics).

## Who Should Use Google Meet as Their Main Tool

1. **Google Workspace companies running mostly internal meetings** — You are already paying for it, and Gemini closes the AI gap.
2. **Remote-first teams under 100 people** — Meet handles this size well without any add-ons.
3. **Companies that value browser-native meetings** — No app install, no friction for guests.
4. **Teams that value AI meeting notes but don't want to pay Zoom or Copilot extra** — Gemini delivers this in-plan.

## Who Should Keep Zoom Alongside Meet

1. **Sales, customer success, and external-facing teams** — Zoom's reliability advantage matters when a dropped call is a lost deal.
2. **Companies running webinars or virtual events** — Zoom Events is still in a different league.
3. **Teams with customers on poor connections** — Zoom degrades better.

## Verdict

Google Meet in 2026 is finally a legitimate Zoom alternative for Google Workspace customers. Gemini integration is the feature that closed the gap — AI meeting notes bundled at no extra cost neutralize Zoom's AI Companion advantage for most internal use cases.

If your meetings are mostly internal and you are on Google Workspace, cancel Zoom. If your meetings drive revenue — sales calls, webinars, customer events — keep both.
