**[Descript](/tools/descript/)** prices on a per-user monthly fee, with the real constraints hidden in transcription hours and AI feature limits rather than the headline price. Here's exactly what each plan includes and which one fits your editing volume.

## Descript Plans and Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Price (annual) | Transcription | Right for |
|------|-------|-----------|----------|
| Free | $0 | 1 hr/month | Trying it out |
| Hobbyist | ~$16/user/mo | 10 hrs/month | Casual creators |
| Creator | ~$24/user/mo | 30 hrs/month | Regular podcasters/YouTubers |
| Business | ~$50/user/mo | 40 hrs/month | Teams and agencies |

Monthly billing runs noticeably higher than annual. The number that determines your plan isn't the price — it's how many hours of audio/video you transcribe and edit each month.

## Free Plan: A Real Trial, Not a Product

Descript's free tier lets you experience the core "edit video by editing text" workflow, but the limits make it evaluation-only:

- 1 hour of transcription per month
- Watermarked exports on some features
- Limited AI Speech (Overdub) and filler-word removal
- 720p export

It's enough to understand whether Descript's text-based editing fits your workflow. It is not enough to publish regularly.

## Hobbyist Plan (~$16/user/month)

Hobbyist removes watermarks and raises limits to a usable level for occasional creators:

- 10 hours transcription/month
- 1080p export
- Filler-word and silence removal
- Basic AI features

Good for someone publishing a few short videos or one podcast episode a month. Heavy weeks will hit the 10-hour cap fast.

## Creator Plan (~$24/user/month)

This is the plan most serious solo creators land on:

- 30 hours transcription/month
- 4K export
- Full **AI Speech (Overdub)** — clone your voice to fix mistakes by typing
- **Studio Sound** — AI audio cleanup that rivals dedicated tools
- Eye contact correction, green screen, and AI effects
- Unlimited AI actions

For a weekly podcaster or YouTuber, Creator's 30 hours and full AI suite is the sweet spot. The Studio Sound and Overdub features alone replace separate audio tools.

## Business Plan (~$50/user/month)

Business adds team and security features rather than dramatically more capability:

- 40 hours transcription/month
- SSO and advanced security
- Team collaboration and shared drives
- Priority support
- Higher AI usage limits

Choose Business for security/SSO requirements or multi-editor teams — not just for the extra 10 transcription hours.

## The Hidden Cost: Transcription Hours Are Per-User

The transcription cap resets monthly and does not roll over, and on team plans it's allocated per seat with some pooling. Long-form creators (multi-hour podcasts, raw footage editing) burn through hours faster than the plan tier suggests. Estimate your real monthly source footage before picking a tier — overage forces an upgrade.

## Is Descript Worth the Price?

**Free → Hobbyist** is worth it only if you publish occasionally and want watermark-free 1080p exports.

**Creator (~$24/mo) is the plan to beat.** For anyone publishing weekly, the 30 hours plus full AI suite (Overdub, Studio Sound) consolidates editing, audio cleanup, and voice fixes into one subscription. This is where Descript justifies its cost.

**Business** is for teams and SSO requirements, not for more editing power per se.

If your work is short-form social video, [CapCut](/tools/capcut/) is cheaper and more timeline-native. If it's spoken-word content — podcasts, talking-head video, courses — Descript's text-based workflow is hard to beat at this price.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Descript free?

Yes, Descript has a free plan with 1 hour of transcription per month and limited AI features. It's suitable for trying the tool but not for regular publishing.

### Which Descript plan is best for podcasters?

The Creator plan (~$24/user/month) is the sweet spot for regular podcasters, offering 30 transcription hours, full AI voice (Overdub), and Studio Sound audio cleanup.

### What is the difference between Descript and CapCut?

Descript edits video and audio by editing a transcript and excels at spoken-word content. CapCut is a timeline-based video editor focused on short-form social clips and is generally cheaper.
