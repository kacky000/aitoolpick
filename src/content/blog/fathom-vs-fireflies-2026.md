# Fathom vs Fireflies.ai 2026: AI Note-Taker Showdown

Fathom and Fireflies.ai both promise the same thing: join your meetings, record everything, and hand you useful notes when the call ends. In practice, they solve different problems. Fathom is built for individuals who want clean, instant summaries with no setup. Fireflies is built for teams that need every call logged, searchable, and synced to a CRM.

Here's how they compare in 2026 across features, pricing, and real-world fit.

## Fathom Overview

Fathom earned its reputation by being the meeting assistant that just works. Install it, join a Zoom call, and get a clean summary seconds after the meeting ends. No configuration, no learning curve, no pressure to upgrade.

The free tier is what sets Fathom apart. You get unlimited recordings, transcription, and AI summaries at no cost — a rarity in this space. Most competitors gate their best AI features behind paid plans; Fathom gives them away. The summaries are structured around highlights, action items, and key decisions, and they're ready almost instantly.

Fathom started as Zoom-only and has since added support for Google Meet and Microsoft Teams. The interface stays minimal and fast. If you open the app after a call, you see your summary, your transcript, and nothing else. For individuals and founders who take 5-15 calls a week, this simplicity is a feature, not a limitation.

Where Fathom falls short is depth. Search across past meetings is basic. Analytics are limited. CRM integrations exist on paid plans but lack the granularity that sales teams expect. If you need to mine 200 past calls for a specific objection a prospect raised, Fathom wasn't designed for that.

## Fireflies.ai Overview

Fireflies treats every meeting as structured data. Calls are transcribed, tagged by topic, and stored in a searchable archive. The conversation intelligence layer tracks talk-time ratios, sentiment, and keyword frequency — metrics that matter when you're managing a sales pipeline or coaching reps.

The standout feature is **AskFred**, Fireflies' conversational AI. You can ask questions like "What did the client say about pricing?" across any meeting or set of meetings, and get direct answers pulled from transcripts. It turns your meeting history into a queryable knowledge base.

Fireflies works natively with Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, and several dialers. Its CRM integrations go deep — auto-logging call notes to Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zoho with field-level mapping, not just a text dump. For revenue teams, this alone can justify the cost.

The tradeoff is complexity. Fireflies has more settings, more dashboards, and more features to configure. If you just want meeting notes, you'll ignore most of what Fireflies offers. Read the full [Fireflies.ai review](/blog/fireflies-ai-review-2026) for a deeper look.

## Feature Comparison

### Transcription Quality

Both tools deliver accurate transcription with speaker identification. Fireflies supports more languages natively and handles multi-accent calls slightly better in our testing. Fathom's transcripts are clean and fast but primarily optimized for English. For English-only teams, the difference is negligible.

### AI Summaries

Fathom generates summaries within seconds of a call ending. They're structured, concise, and consistently useful. Fireflies' summaries are more detailed — broken into topics, action items, and follow-ups — but take slightly longer to process. If speed matters, Fathom wins. If depth matters, Fireflies wins.

### Integrations

Fireflies has the edge here. Its CRM integrations map specific fields (deal stage, next steps, competitor mentions) directly into Salesforce and HubSpot records. Fathom's CRM sync is functional but more surface-level — it pushes notes, not structured data. Both integrate with Slack, Notion, and Zapier.

### Search and Analytics

This is where the gap widens. Fireflies lets you search across your entire meeting archive by keyword, speaker, topic, or date. AskFred adds natural-language querying on top. Fathom's search is basic — you can find meetings and scan transcripts, but you can't ask complex questions across multiple calls. For teams that accumulate hundreds of meetings, Fireflies' search is a genuine productivity tool.

### CRM Sync

Fireflies wins. Auto-logging with field mapping, activity tracking, and the ability to push specific call segments to CRM records. Fathom's CRM features are available on paid plans and work well for basic note syncing, but they don't match Fireflies' depth for sales workflows.

## Who Should Choose Which

### Choose Fathom if:

- You work solo or in a small team and want meeting notes without paying
- You primarily use Zoom and value a minimal, fast interface
- You don't need to search across hundreds of past meetings
- Budget is a primary concern — the free tier covers most individual needs

### Choose Fireflies.ai if:

- Your team runs 20+ calls per week and needs them logged to a CRM
- You want to search past meetings like a database
- Conversation analytics (talk time, topics, sentiment) inform your coaching
- You need multi-platform support beyond Zoom

## Verdict

Fathom is the better free meeting assistant. Its no-cost plan is genuinely usable long-term, and the experience is fast and frictionless. If your needs stop at "record my calls and give me good notes," Fathom wins.

Fireflies is the better team meeting platform. Its search, analytics, and CRM integrations turn meetings into actionable data. If you manage a revenue team or need to extract insight from a growing archive of calls, Fireflies justifies its cost.

Most individuals should start with Fathom. Most teams with 5+ people and a CRM should evaluate Fireflies.
