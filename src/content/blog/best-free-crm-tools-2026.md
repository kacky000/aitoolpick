A CRM (Customer Relationship Management) tool is essential for tracking leads, managing customer relationships, and growing revenue. But not every business can afford $50/user/month enterprise software. The good news: several excellent CRM tools offer genuinely useful free plans in 2026.

Here are the best free CRM options, ranked by how much you can actually accomplish without paying.

## 1. HubSpot CRM — Best Overall Free CRM

HubSpot's free CRM is the gold standard for small businesses getting started with customer management. It's not a limited trial—it's a permanently free product.

**What you get for free:**
- Up to 1,000,000 contacts (no limit on users)
- Contact management with company and deal records
- Email tracking and notifications
- Meeting scheduling
- Live chat and chatbot builder
- Basic reporting dashboards

**Limitations:** No workflow automation, limited email templates, HubSpot branding on forms and chat.

**Upgrade path:** Starter plans begin at $15/month.

**Best for:** Small businesses, startups, and freelancers who want a robust free CRM they won't outgrow quickly.

## 2. Monday.com CRM — Best Free CRM for Visual Teams

[Monday.com](/tools/monday) offers a CRM product built on their visual work OS. The free plan (up to 2 users) includes basic CRM functionality.

**What you get for free:**
- Up to 2 seats
- Unlimited boards (up to 1,000 items)
- Kanban and list views for pipeline management
- 200+ templates including CRM-specific layouts
- Mobile app access

**Limitations:** 2-user cap, no automations, no integrations, limited storage.

**Upgrade path:** Basic CRM at $12/seat/month with automations and integrations.

**Best for:** Solo entrepreneurs or 2-person teams who want a visual, drag-and-drop CRM experience.

## 3. ClickUp CRM — Best Free CRM for Project-Heavy Teams

[ClickUp](/tools/clickup) isn't a dedicated CRM, but its free plan can be configured into a powerful customer management system with custom fields, views, and automations.

**What you get for free:**
- Unlimited tasks and users
- Custom fields for lead scoring, deal value, and status
- Kanban, List, and Table views
- Basic automations (100/month)
- Docs for proposals and contracts

**Limitations:** 100 MB storage, limited dashboards, no advanced reporting.

**Upgrade path:** Unlimited at $7/user/month unlocks full CRM capabilities.

**Best for:** Teams that manage projects AND customer relationships in one place. Agencies and consultancies benefit most.

## 4. Notion CRM — Best Free DIY CRM

[Notion](/tools/notion) lets you build a custom CRM from scratch using databases, relations, and views. The free plan is generous for personal use.

**What you get for free:**
- Unlimited pages and blocks
- Databases with filters, sorts, and views
- Relations and rollups for connecting contacts to deals
- Templates from the Notion community
- API access for integrations

**Limitations:** No built-in email tracking, no automation, no pipeline analytics. Requires manual setup.

**Upgrade path:** Plus at $10/user/month for team features and larger uploads.

**Best for:** Users who want total customization and already use Notion for other tasks. Freelancers and solopreneurs who prefer one tool for everything.

## 5. Airtable CRM — Best Free Spreadsheet-Style CRM

[Airtable](/tools/airtable) combines the familiarity of spreadsheets with database power, making it excellent for lightweight CRM use.

**What you get for free:**
- Up to 1,000 records per base
- Grid, Kanban, Calendar, and Gallery views
- Form view for lead capture
- Attachment support
- Basic automations (100/month)

**Limitations:** 1,000 record cap per base, 1 GB attachment storage, limited automation runs.

**Upgrade path:** Team at $20/seat/month for more records and features.

**Best for:** Small teams comfortable with spreadsheets who need more structure than Google Sheets but less complexity than HubSpot.

## How to Choose Your Free CRM

**Ask yourself these questions:**

1. **How many people need access?** If more than 2, HubSpot or ClickUp (both offer unlimited free users).
2. **Do you need email tracking?** Only HubSpot offers this on the free plan.
3. **Do you want a pre-built CRM or custom?** HubSpot and Monday are ready to go; Notion and Airtable require setup.
4. **Will you outgrow the free plan?** Check the upgrade pricing—some tools get expensive fast.

## When to Upgrade to Paid

Free CRM tools work well until you hit these thresholds:
- **Team size exceeds 5-10 people** and you need permissions/roles
- **You need workflow automation** to handle repetitive tasks
- **Reporting requirements** go beyond basic dashboards
- **Email sequences and marketing** become part of your sales process

At that point, HubSpot Starter ($15/month) or [Monday.com](/tools/monday) Basic ($12/seat/month) offer the smoothest upgrade from their free tiers.

## The Bottom Line

For most small businesses starting out, **HubSpot CRM** is the best free option—no user limits, generous contact capacity, and a clear upgrade path. If you're already using [ClickUp](/tools/clickup) or [Notion](/tools/notion) for project management, building a CRM inside your existing tool saves context switching.
