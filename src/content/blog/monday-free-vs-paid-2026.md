**Monday Free** and **Paid** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Monday.com's pricing can be confusing — especially the minimum seat requirements and per-seat billing. Here's a clear breakdown of what each plan actually includes and whether the free plan is enough.

## Free Plan: What's Included

Monday.com's free plan is one of the most limited in the [project management](/category/project-management/) space:

- **Up to 2 users only**
- Up to 3 boards
- Unlimited docs
- 200+ templates
- Kanban view
- Up to 1,000 items
- iOS and Android apps

That's it. No timeline, no Gantt, no automations, no integrations. It's essentially a demo.

## Basic Plan ($9/seat/month)

The Basic plan adds:

- **Minimum 3 seats** ($27/month minimum)
- Unlimited boards and items
- 5 GB storage
- Prioritized customer support
- Dashboard (1 board)
- iOS and Android apps

Still no automations or integrations. The 3-seat minimum means you're paying at least $27/month even if you're a team of 2.

## Standard Plan ($12/seat/month)

This is where Monday.com becomes genuinely useful:

- **250 automations/month**
- **250 integrations/month**
- Timeline and Gantt views
- Calendar view
- Guest access
- Dashboard (5 boards)
- 20 GB storage

Most teams need Standard at minimum for the automations and timeline views.

## Pro Plan ($19/seat/month)

Pro adds advanced features for larger teams:

- **25,000 automations/month**
- **25,000 integrations/month**
- Private boards
- [Time tracking](/category/time-tracking/)
- Formula column
- Chart view
- Dashboard (20 boards)
- 100 GB storage

## Enterprise Plan (Custom Pricing)

Enterprise adds:

- Advanced security and governance
- HIPAA compliance
- Unlimited automations
- Premium integrations
- Multi-level permissions
- Tailored onboarding
- 1,000 GB storage

## The Real Cost: Seat Minimums

Monday.com's biggest pricing catch is the **3-seat minimum** on all paid plans. Here's what that means:

| Plan | Per Seat | Minimum Seats | Minimum Monthly Cost |
|------|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Free | $0 | — | $0 (2 users max) |
| Basic | $9 | 3 | $27/month |
| Standard | $12 | 3 | $36/month |
| Pro | $19 | 3 | $57/month |

If you're a solo user or a team of 2, you're overpaying by 33–50%.

## Is Upgrading Worth It?

**The free plan is NOT enough** for any real work. It's a trial, not a working tool. You can't even invite a third team member.

**Basic is only worth it if** you just need a shared task board without automations. Most teams skip Basic entirely.

**Standard is the sweet spot** for most teams. Automations, timeline views, and integrations [make](/tools/make/) Monday.com actually powerful.

**Pro makes sense if** you need time tracking, private boards, or heavy automation usage.

## How It Compares to Competitors

Monday.com's pricing is mid-range but the seat minimums [make](/tools/make/) it expensive for small teams:

- **[ClickUp](/tools/clickup/)**: Free plan supports unlimited users, paid starts at $7/user with no minimums
- **[Asana](/tools/asana/)**: Free plan supports up to 10 users
- **[Notion](/tools/notion/)**: Free plan is generous for individuals, $10/user for teams

For teams of 3+, Monday.com is competitive. For smaller teams, consider [ClickUp](/blog/clickup-vs-monday-2026) or [Notion](/blog/notion-vs-monday-2026) instead.

## The Verdict

Monday.com's free plan is basically a trial. If you're evaluating Monday.com, go straight to the Standard plan trial — that's where you'll see its real value. The automations and timeline views are what make Monday.com worth using, and those start at Standard ($12/seat/month, 3-seat minimum).
