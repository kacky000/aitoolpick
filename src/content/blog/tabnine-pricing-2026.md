Tabnine's pricing looks nothing like it did a year ago. The company killed its free, Basic, and legacy Pro plans in April 2025 and pivoted hard toward enterprise buyers. If you're searching for a cheap individual AI coding assistant, Tabnine isn't that anymore.

Here's exactly what the plans cost, what they include, and who they're actually built for now.

No monthly billing option exists. No individual licenses. Every purchase requires team-level commitment with annual billing.

## What Got Killed (and Why It Matters)

Before April 2025, Tabnine offered a free Basic plan with limited completions, a $9/month Dev plan, and a $12/month Pro plan. All three are gone.

The old free tier was popular with individual developers who wanted privacy-focused code completion without paying. Tabnine decided that market wasn't profitable enough to serve and shifted entirely to selling to engineering teams.

If you're currently on a legacy plan, it's been sunset. There's no grandfathering.

## Code Assistant — $39/user/month

This is Tabnine's entry-level paid plan. Annual billing only, team purchase required.

**What you get:**

- **AI code completions** — inline suggestions as you type, trained on permissively licensed code
- **AI chat** — ask questions about your codebase, get explanations, generate code snippets
- **Codebase understanding** — Tabnine indexes your repository to give context-aware suggestions
- **IDE support** — VS Code, JetBrains IDEs, Neovim, Eclipse
- **Privacy controls** — code never used for model training, option for on-premise deployment
- **Admin dashboard** — usage tracking, seat management, team controls
- **SOC 2 compliance** — enterprise-grade security

**What you don't get:**

- Agentic workflows (multi-step autonomous tasks)
- Context Engine (advanced cross-repo context)
- Custom model fine-tuning

**Annual cost:** $468 per user per year. For a team of 5, that's $2,340/year before any negotiation.

## Agentic Platform — $59/user/month

Everything in Code Assistant, plus autonomous agent capabilities. Annual billing only.

**What you get (on top of Code Assistant):**

- **Agent workflows** — Tabnine can autonomously complete multi-step tasks like refactoring across files, writing tests, or implementing features from descriptions
- **Context Engine** — launched March 2026, this indexes your entire codebase, documentation, and organizational knowledge to ground AI responses in your specific engineering context
- **Cross-repository understanding** — the agent can reference patterns and conventions from other repos in your organization
- **Custom workflow definitions** — define repeatable agent tasks specific to your team's processes

**Annual cost:** $708 per user per year. For a team of 10, that's $7,080/year.

## Enterprise — Custom Pricing

For organizations that need deployment flexibility and deeper integration.

**What you get (on top of Agentic Platform):**

- On-premise or VPC deployment options
- Custom model fine-tuning on your codebase
- SAML SSO
- Advanced audit logging
- Dedicated customer success manager
- Custom SLAs
- Priority support

Enterprise pricing isn't published. Expect negotiations based on seat count and deployment requirements.

## No Free Tier, No Monthly Billing

This is the biggest change from old Tabnine. Two hard constraints:

1. **No free option.** Not even a trial tier with limited completions. Tabnine offers a time-limited trial, but there's no permanent free plan.
2. **Annual billing only.** You can't pay month-to-month to test it out. You're committing to a full year upfront.

Both of these push Tabnine squarely into the "procurement decision" category rather than "developer downloads it and expenses it later."

## Who Should Consider Tabnine in 2026?

**Good fit:**

- Engineering teams at companies where code privacy is non-negotiable (finance, healthcare, defense)
- Organizations that need on-premise AI deployment
- Teams already using Tabnine Enterprise and evaluating the Agentic upgrade
- Companies where procurement prefers annual contracts with SOC 2 vendors

**Not a good fit:**

- Individual developers (no individual plans exist)
- Freelancers or small teams on a budget
- Anyone who wants to try before committing to annual billing
- Developers who just want smart autocomplete at the lowest price

## The Strategic Shift

Tabnine's pricing tells a clear story: they've abandoned the individual developer market to focus on enterprise deals. The March 2026 Context Engine launch doubled down on this — it's a feature that only makes sense for large codebases with multiple repos and established conventions.

Whether this strategy works depends on whether Tabnine can differentiate enough from [GitHub Copilot](/tools/github-copilot/), which also offers enterprise features but at roughly half the per-seat cost with a more flexible pricing model. [Cursor](/tools/cursor/) takes a different approach entirely, charging per-user with credit pools rather than flat per-seat fees.

If you're an individual developer looking for AI coding assistance, the market has plenty of options that won't require an annual team contract. If you're an engineering leader evaluating AI tools for a 50-person team with strict compliance requirements, Tabnine's pitch starts to make more sense.
