# Best Whiteboard Tools for Teams in 2026

Online whiteboards have become essential for remote and hybrid teams. Whether you're running design sprints, retrospectives, or strategic planning sessions, having the right visual collaboration tool matters.

Here are the best whiteboard tools for teams in 2026, ranked by features, pricing, and use case.

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Best For | Free Plan | Paid From | Rating |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Miro | Workshops & facilitation | 3 boards | $8/user/mo | 4.5/5 |
| FigJam | Design teams | 3 files | Free w/Figma | 4.4/5 |
| Lucidspark | Visual brainstorming | 3 boards | $7.95/user/mo | 4.3/5 |

## 1. Miro — Best Overall Whiteboard

**Price:** Free (3 boards) | $8/user/mo (Starter) | $16/user/mo (Business)

Miro is the industry standard for online whiteboarding. With 2,500+ templates, facilitation tools (voting, timers, emoji reactions), and deep integrations, it's the most complete whiteboard platform available.

**Why it's #1:**
- Most extensive template library (2,500+)
- Best facilitation tools for running workshops
- Strong integrations with Jira, Asana, Slack
- AI-powered clustering and summarization
- Excellent for large, distributed teams

**Drawbacks:** Per-user pricing adds up fast. Performance can lag on very large boards with hundreds of objects.

## 2. FigJam — Best for Design Teams

**Price:** Free with Figma | Included in Figma Professional ($15/editor/mo)

FigJam is Figma's collaborative whiteboard tool. If your team already uses Figma for design, FigJam is a no-brainer — it's included in your subscription and lives right alongside your design files.

**Strengths:**
- Seamless Figma integration
- Clean, fun interface with stamps and stickers
- Great for design critiques and brainstorming
- No additional cost for Figma users
- Simple enough that non-designers love it too

**Drawbacks:** Fewer templates than Miro. Limited facilitation features. Not ideal if you don't use Figma.

[Compare Miro vs FigJam →](/blog/miro-vs-figma-2026)

## 3. Lucidspark — Best for Visual Brainstorming

**Price:** Free (3 boards) | $7.95/user/mo (Individual) | $9/user/mo (Team)

Lucidspark is the whiteboard component of the Lucid suite (alongside Lucidchart for diagramming). It excels at structured brainstorming with features like idea voting, breakout boards, and automatic grouping.

**Strengths:**
- Excellent brainstorming features
- Pairs perfectly with Lucidchart
- Collaborative sorting and voting
- Breakout boards for group exercises
- Good integration ecosystem

**Drawbacks:** Less polished than Miro. Template library is smaller. Better as a Lucid add-on than a standalone tool.

## 4. Microsoft Whiteboard — Best Free Option for M365 Users

**Price:** Free with Microsoft 365

If your organization uses Microsoft 365, you already have Microsoft Whiteboard — no extra cost. It's built into Teams and integrates with the entire Microsoft ecosystem.

**Strengths:**
- Free with Microsoft 365
- Integrated directly in Teams meetings
- Ink-to-shape and ink-to-text
- Loop components support
- Good for quick meeting sketches

**Drawbacks:** Limited templates (40+ vs Miro's 2,500+). Basic facilitation features. Not designed for intensive workshop use.

## 5. Whimsical — Best for Wireframes + Whiteboards

**Price:** Free (limited) | $10/user/mo (Pro)

Whimsical combines whiteboards with wireframing, flowcharts, mind maps, and docs — all in a beautifully designed package.

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class wireframing
- Beautiful, clean design
- Combines multiple visual tools in one
- Fast and responsive
- Great for early-stage product thinking

**Drawbacks:** Smaller template library. Less suited for large workshop facilitation. Smaller community than Miro.

## 6. Canva Whiteboard — Best for Non-Designers

**Price:** Free (with Canva) | $15/user/mo (Canva Pro)

[Canva's](/tools/canva) whiteboard feature brings its drag-and-drop simplicity to visual collaboration. It's especially good for teams that already use Canva for marketing materials.

**Strengths:**
- Familiar Canva interface
- Access to Canva's massive asset library
- Easy to create polished presentations from boards
- Free plan is generous
- Great for marketing and content teams

**Drawbacks:** Not designed for technical diagramming. Limited facilitation tools. Better for presentation-quality visuals than workshop facilitation.

## How to Choose the Right Whiteboard Tool

**Choose Miro if:** You run regular workshops, need advanced facilitation features, and want the most templates and integrations.

**Choose FigJam if:** Your team uses Figma and you want a whiteboard that lives alongside your design files.

**Choose Lucidspark if:** You already use Lucidchart and want brainstorming tools that integrate with your diagramming workflow.

**Choose Microsoft Whiteboard if:** You use Microsoft 365 and want a free, no-friction whiteboard in Teams.

**Choose Whimsical if:** You need wireframing + whiteboarding in a single, beautifully designed tool.

**Choose Canva Whiteboard if:** You want visual collaboration with access to Canva's design assets.

## Final Recommendation

For most teams, **Miro** remains the best all-around choice in 2026. Its depth of features, template library, and facilitation tools justify the per-user cost — especially if you run regular collaborative sessions.

If budget is a concern, **FigJam** (for Figma users) or **Microsoft Whiteboard** (for M365 users) offer excellent value at no additional cost.
