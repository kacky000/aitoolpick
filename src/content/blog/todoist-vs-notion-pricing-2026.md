**[Todoist](/tools/todoist/)** and **[Notion](/tools/notion/)** attract the same productivity-minded users but solve different problems. Todoist is a fast, focused task manager. Notion is an all-in-one workspace — docs, databases, wikis, and tasks rolled together. On price, Todoist is cheaper at the top tier; the decision comes down to whether you want one sharp tool or a flexible everything-app.

## Pricing Overview

### Todoist Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price (Annual) | Price (Monthly) |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 |
| Pro | $5/user/mo | $7/user/mo |
| Business | $8/user/mo | $10/user/mo |

### Notion Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price (Annual) | Price (Monthly) |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 |
| Plus | $10/user/mo | $12/user/mo |
| Business | $15/user/mo | $18/user/mo |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

The headline: **Todoist is meaningfully cheaper.** Todoist Pro ($5) is half the price of Notion Plus ($10), and Todoist Business ($8) undercuts even Notion's entry paid tier. Note Notion AI features are bundled into paid plans now, which is part of why Notion's pricing sits higher.

## The Real Difference: Focused App vs All-in-One Workspace

**Todoist** does one thing extremely well: capturing and organizing tasks. Quick entry, natural-language due dates, projects, labels, filters. It's fast and stays out of your way — but it's a task manager, full stop.

**Notion** is a flexible workspace where tasks are just one block among docs, databases, wikis, and project trackers. That power is also its tax: you build your own system, which takes setup time, and you pay more per seat for the breadth.

The honest framing: Todoist is the better *task* tool; Notion is the better *workspace*. If all you need is a great to-do list, paying for Notion is overkill. If you want notes, docs, and databases unified, Todoist can't do that.

## Cost Scenario: Solo vs 10-Person Team

**Solo user:**
- **Todoist Pro:** $5/month
- **Notion Plus:** $10/month (or Free, which is generous solo)

For an individual, Todoist Pro is the cheaper paid upgrade, while Notion's free plan is famously capable for one person.

**Team of 10:**
- **Todoist Business:** 10 × $8 = **$80/month**
- **Notion Plus:** 10 × $10 = **$100/month**
- **Notion Business:** 10 × $15 = **$150/month**

Todoist Business is about **20% cheaper** than Notion Plus — but Notion does far more at that price. The question isn't cost alone; it's whether your team needs shared docs and databases (Notion) or just shared task lists (Todoist).

## Free Plan Comparison

- **Todoist Free:** 5 personal projects, recurring dates, 3 filters — solid for solo use.
- **Notion Free:** Unlimited pages and blocks for individuals, with collaboration limits — one of the most generous free tiers in productivity software.

Notion's free plan is the stronger of the two for a single user who wants room to build; Todoist Free is the cleaner choice if you only want a task list.

## Verdict

**Choose Todoist** if you want an affordable, focused task manager — Pro at $5 and Business at $8 are excellent value for individuals and small teams.

**Choose Notion** if you want a single workspace for docs, databases, and tasks together, and you'll use that breadth to justify the higher per-seat price.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Todoist cheaper than Notion?**
Yes. Todoist Pro ($5/user) is half of Notion Plus ($10/user), and Todoist Business ($8) is cheaper than Notion's entry paid tier.

**What's the difference between Todoist and Notion?**
Todoist is a focused task manager; Notion is an all-in-one workspace for docs, databases, wikis, and tasks. They serve different needs.

**Which has the better free plan?**
For building a personal system, Notion Free is more capable. For a simple task list, Todoist Free is cleaner.

**Can Notion replace Todoist?**
Notion can manage tasks, but it requires setup and lacks Todoist's speed and natural-language quick capture. Many users run Todoist for tasks and Notion for docs.
