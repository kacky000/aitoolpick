Jira's pricing is more complex than most project management tools. Beyond the core cloud plans, Atlassian layers on additional products — Confluence, Jira Product Discovery, Jira Service Management — that make the total cost significantly higher than the headline numbers suggest.

Here is every number you need to know before committing to Jira in 2026.

## Jira Cloud Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Users | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Up to 10 | Basic boards, 2 GB storage, community support |
| Standard | $7.53–$8.60/user/month* | Up to 35,000 | Audit logs, 250 GB storage, role management |
| Premium | $17/user/month | Up to 35,000 | Advanced roadmaps, Atlassian Intelligence, archiving |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited | Multiple instances, 99.95% SLA, unlimited storage |

All paid plans are billed annually. Monthly billing is available at a ~20% premium.

*Jira Standard uses tiered pricing — the per-user rate decreases as team size grows. Smaller teams (1-10 users) pay approximately $8.60/user; larger teams (100+ users) pay approximately $7.53/user. Verify current rates at atlassian.com/software/jira/pricing.

## What Each Plan Actually Gets You

### Free Plan
The free tier is usable but limited. Up to 10 users can manage projects, use Scrum and Kanban boards, and run basic automations (100 runs/month). You get community support only — no SLAs, no phone support.

**Best for:** Solo developers, tiny teams testing Jira before committing.

### Standard ($8.60/user/month)
Standard adds the features that matter for small-to-mid teams: project-level roles, audit logs, data residency, and increased automation (1,750 runs/month per user). Storage jumps to 250 GB.

**Best for:** Engineering teams of 10-50 that have moved past the free tier's limits.

### Premium ($17/user/month)
Premium is where Jira gets genuinely powerful. Advanced Roadmaps lets you plan across teams and see dependencies. Atlassian Intelligence (AI features) is enabled. Automation runs jump to unlimited. Archiving prevents old projects from cluttering your workspace.

**Best for:** Engineering orgs with multiple teams, or anyone who needs cross-team planning.

### Enterprise (Custom)
Enterprise adds multiple Atlassian cloud instances (useful for companies with compliance requirements), enterprise-grade SLAs, centralized user management, and Atlassian Access for SSO/SCIM. Pricing is negotiated per contract.

**Best for:** Companies with 500+ users, strict compliance requirements, or M&A scenarios with separate Atlassian instances.

## Add-On Costs That Inflate Your Bill

Jira's headline price is only part of the story. Most organizations end up paying for additional Atlassian products:

| Add-On | Price |
|--------|-------|
| Confluence (wiki/docs) | $5.16/user/month (Standard) |
| Jira Product Discovery | $10/creator/month |
| Jira Service Management | $22.05/agent/month (Standard) |
| Atlassian Access (SSO) | $4/user/month |
| Atlas (team goals) | Included in Premium+ |

A typical 50-person engineering org on Jira Premium + Confluence Standard + Atlassian Access pays roughly **$29.76/user/month** — nearly $18,000/year.

## Is Jira Worth the Price?

**Yes, if:**
- You have 50+ engineers with complex workflows
- You are already using Confluence and Bitbucket (the integration value is real)
- You need Atlassian Intelligence or Advanced Roadmaps
- Compliance and audit logs are required

**No, if:**
- Your team is under 30 people (Linear is faster and cheaper all-in)
- You don't need the Atlassian ecosystem
- Your engineers complain about Jira daily (a signal the ROI is negative)

## How to Reduce Your Jira Bill

1. **Audit your users:** Remove inactive accounts. Standard charges per seat, including infrequent users.
2. **Use the free plan for contractors:** External contractors don't need paid seats if they only need read access.
3. **Delay Jira Product Discovery:** JPD is useful but non-essential. Add it when your PM team explicitly needs roadmap tooling.
4. **Negotiate Enterprise pricing:** Atlassian sales teams have flexibility on Enterprise contracts, especially for multi-year deals.
