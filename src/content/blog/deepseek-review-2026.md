DeepSeek burst onto the AI scene in late 2024 and hasn't slowed down since. With its V4 models now live, free web chat, and API pricing that undercuts every major competitor by 10–50x, it's become impossible to ignore. But is cheap the same as good? Here's what you need to know in 2026.

## What Is DeepSeek?

DeepSeek is an AI research lab based in China that builds large language models. Unlike OpenAI or Anthropic, DeepSeek releases open-weight versions of its models, meaning developers can download and run them on their own hardware.

The company offers two ways to use its AI:

- **DeepSeek Chat** — A free web interface at chat.deepseek.com (no subscription required)
- **DeepSeek API** — Pay-per-token access for developers building applications

## Pricing: Absurdly Cheap

This is where DeepSeek stands out most. Here's how V4 pricing compares:

V4 Flash is roughly **18x cheaper** than GPT-4o on input tokens. V4 Pro, the premium reasoning model, still costs less than half of what OpenAI charges. And right now there's a promotional 75% discount on V4 Pro running through May 31, 2026.

The free web chat has no daily limits for standard conversations, which makes it one of the most accessible AI tools available.

## What DeepSeek Does Well

### Coding and Technical Tasks

DeepSeek V4 consistently performs at or near GPT-4 level on coding benchmarks. It handles Python, JavaScript, SQL, and most mainstream languages competently. The 1M token context window means you can feed it entire codebases.

### Reasoning

The R1 reasoning model (now integrated into V4 Pro) shows its work step-by-step, similar to OpenAI's o1. For math, logic puzzles, and multi-step analysis, it's genuinely competitive with the best models on the market.

### Cost-Sensitive Applications

If you're building an AI feature into a product and processing millions of tokens daily, DeepSeek can reduce your AI costs by 90%+ compared to OpenAI or Anthropic. Startups and indie developers have been flocking to it for this reason.

## Where DeepSeek Falls Short

### Privacy Concerns

DeepSeek is a Chinese company subject to Chinese data laws. If you're handling sensitive business data, healthcare information, or anything regulated, this matters. The open-weight models can be self-hosted to avoid this issue, but the hosted API routes through DeepSeek's servers.

### Content Moderation

The model has noticeable guardrails around politically sensitive topics, particularly those related to Chinese politics. For most business and technical use cases this won't matter, but it's worth knowing.

### Ecosystem

There's no plugin marketplace, no native integrations with Slack or Google Workspace, and no equivalent of ChatGPT's GPTs or Claude's Projects. The API is OpenAI-compatible, which helps, but the user-facing product is bare-bones compared to ChatGPT or [Claude](/reviews/claude).

### Writing Quality

For long-form content, creative writing, and nuanced communication, [Claude](/reviews/claude) and [ChatGPT](/reviews/chatgpt) still produce noticeably better output. DeepSeek tends toward a more mechanical tone.

## Who Should Use DeepSeek?

- **Developers on a budget** — The API pricing is unbeatable for prototyping and production
- **Researchers** — Open weights mean full transparency and customization
- **Startups** — Reduce AI costs without sacrificing much quality
- **Curious users** — The free chat is a zero-risk way to try a powerful model

## Who Should Look Elsewhere?

- **Enterprise teams** needing SOC 2 compliance and data residency guarantees → [Claude](/reviews/claude) or [ChatGPT](/reviews/chatgpt)
- **Writers and marketers** wanting the best prose quality → [Claude](/reviews/claude)
- **Non-technical users** wanting an all-in-one AI assistant → [ChatGPT](/reviews/chatgpt) or [Gemini](/reviews/gemini)

## The Verdict

DeepSeek V4 is a legitimate top-tier AI model available at a fraction of the cost of its competitors — or completely free. The trade-offs are real (privacy jurisdiction, smaller ecosystem, weaker creative writing), but for coding, reasoning, and cost-sensitive API usage, it's one of the best options available in 2026.
