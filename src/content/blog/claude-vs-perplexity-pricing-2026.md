**[Claude](/tools/claude/)** and **[Perplexity](/tools/perplexity/)** both price their core plans at $20/month, but they are built for different jobs. Claude is a deep-reasoning assistant prized for writing and analysis quality; Perplexity is a search engine that answers with live, cited sources. At the same price, the better value comes down to whether you need *thinking* or *sourcing*.

This guide compares every tier and which $20 subscription delivers more for your workflow.

## Pricing Overview

### Claude Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Right For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Trying Claude, light use |
| Pro | $20/mo | Professionals, daily users |
| Max | $100 or $200/mo | Power users, heavy workloads |

### Perplexity Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Right For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Casual search, ~5 Pro searches/day |
| Pro | $20/mo ($200/yr) | Researchers, daily search users |
| Max | ~$200/mo | Power users wanting top models |

## The Key Difference: Reasoning vs Cited Search

At $20, you are choosing between two strengths.

**Claude Pro** gives you generous access to Anthropic's strongest models, Projects for organizing work, and the writing and reasoning quality Claude is known for. It is the better tool for *producing* — drafting, analyzing documents, coding, and long-form thinking.

**Perplexity Pro** gives you unlimited cited searches across live web sources, model choice (including Claude), and file uploads. It is the better tool for *finding* — current information, research with sources, and fact-checking.

A useful frame: **Claude helps you write and reason; Perplexity helps you research and cite.**

## Cost Scenario: One Year of Daily Use

- **Claude Pro**: $20 × 12 = **$240/year**
- **Perplexity Pro**: $20 × 12 = **$240/year**, or **$200** on the annual plan (~$16.67/mo)

Identical at the monthly rate. Perplexity's annual discount makes it the marginally cheaper of the two if you commit for a year.

## The Premium Tier: Very Different Buyers

- **Claude Max ($100 or $200/mo)**: Dramatically higher usage limits for people who hit Pro's caps — heavy coders, researchers, and anyone running Claude all day. Two tiers let you scale spending to usage.
- **Perplexity Max (~$200/mo)**: Priority access to top models and higher search limits for heavy researchers.

Claude's two-step Max ladder ($100 then $200) gives heavy users a middle option that Perplexity does not match.

## Free Tier Comparison

- **Claude Free**: Real access to a capable model with tighter usage caps — enough to evaluate quality.
- **Perplexity Free**: Full search interface with ~5 Pro searches/day and unlimited standard searches.

If you want to *test writing/reasoning quality*, Claude's free tier shows it off. If you want *free cited search*, Perplexity's free tier is the stronger pick.

## Verdict

**Choose Claude** if your work is writing, analysis, coding, or anything where output quality and deep reasoning matter. The Max tiers also scale better for heavy daily use.

**Choose Perplexity** if your work is research, fact-finding, and current-information tasks where citations matter — and the annual plan makes it slightly cheaper.

Many users pair them: Perplexity to research with sources, Claude to write and reason over the findings. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Claude or Perplexity cheaper?

Both Pro plans cost $20/month. Perplexity is slightly cheaper if you pay annually ($200/year ≈ $16.67/mo); Claude has no annual discount but offers two higher Max tiers.

### What is each one best at?

Claude excels at writing, reasoning, and coding. Perplexity excels at cited, search-grounded research. They complement each other rather than directly compete.

### Should I get Claude Max or Perplexity Max?

Get Claude Max ($100/$200) if you hit Pro's usage caps on writing/coding workloads. Get Perplexity Max (~$200) if you need priority models and higher limits for heavy research.

### Can I use both together?

Yes. A common workflow is researching with Perplexity for sourced facts, then using Claude to write and analyze based on those results.
