# BambooHR vs Gusto 2026: HR Management or Payroll-First?

BambooHR and Gusto both handle HR tasks, but they start from opposite ends of the problem. BambooHR is an HR management platform that treats payroll as an add-on. Gusto is a payroll platform that expanded into HR. That difference shapes everything — features, pricing, and who each tool is really built for.

If you already run payroll somewhere else and need a proper system for managing employees, BambooHR is the stronger pick. If you need to pay people accurately and on time and want HR basics bundled in, Gusto is hard to beat.

## Quick Comparison

| | BambooHR | Gusto |
|---|----------|-------|
| Starting Price | Custom (~$6-8/employee/mo + base fee) | $40/mo + $6/person |

| Performance Management | Yes (reviews, goals) | Limited |

| Best For | Mid-size companies (50-500) | Small businesses (1-100) |

## Pricing

### BambooHR

BambooHR doesn't publish pricing — you have to request a quote. Based on publicly available data and user reports, expect roughly $6-8 per employee per month plus a base platform fee. Payroll is a separate add-on with its own per-employee charge. The total cost scales up quickly once you add payroll, time tracking, and performance management modules.

This isn't unusual for HR platforms targeting mid-market companies, but it does make it harder to budget upfront compared to Gusto's transparent pricing.

### Gusto

| Plan | Base Fee | Per Person |
|------|----------|------------|
| Simple | $40/mo | $6/person/mo |
| Plus | $80/mo | $12/person/mo |
| Premium | Custom | Custom |

Every Gusto plan includes full payroll and automatic tax filing. The Plus plan adds PTO management, time tracking, and next-day direct deposit. Premium adds dedicated support, compliance alerts, and HR resource center access.

For a 20-person company, Gusto Simple runs $160/month. That's payroll, tax filing, and basic HR included — no add-on fees.

**Pricing verdict:** Gusto is more transparent and generally cheaper for small teams, especially when you factor in payroll. BambooHR's total cost can exceed Gusto's once you add payroll and other modules, but it may deliver more value for larger companies that need deep HR features.

## Payroll

This is the clearest difference between the two platforms.

Gusto was built as a payroll tool from day one. It handles automatic federal, state, and local tax calculations, files your tax forms, and sends W-2s and 1099s at year-end. It supports contractor payments in all plans, which matters if you work with freelancers. Pay runs are straightforward — most users finish in under five minutes. If you enable AutoPilot, Gusto runs payroll automatically on schedule.

BambooHR added payroll later, and it shows. The payroll module is a paid add-on that covers the basics — direct deposit, tax filing, pay stubs — but it's not as polished or feature-rich as Gusto's. If payroll accuracy and automation are your top priority, Gusto has a clear lead here.

**Payroll verdict:** Gusto wins. It's included in every plan, handles tax filing automatically, and supports contractor payments. BambooHR's payroll is functional but costs extra and doesn't match Gusto's depth.

## HR Management and Employee Database

BambooHR's core strength is managing people, not paying them. The employee database is well-organized, with custom fields, org charts, document storage, and a clean directory that employees actually enjoy using. You can track everything from job history to training certifications in one place.

PTO tracking is flexible — you can set up multiple accrual policies, handle different leave types, and let employees request time off through the self-service portal. Managers approve requests with a click, and balances update automatically.

Gusto has an employee directory and basic document management, but it's simpler. PTO tracking is available on the Plus plan and above, and it works well enough for straightforward policies. For companies with complex leave rules or multiple office locations, BambooHR offers more configurability.

**HR verdict:** BambooHR is the better HR platform. Its employee database, PTO system, and document management are more mature and flexible than Gusto's.

## Onboarding

BambooHR offers a full onboarding workflow builder. You can create task lists, assign them to different team members, collect electronic signatures on documents, and set up new hire packets that go out automatically before someone's first day. The experience feels polished for both the new employee and the HR team managing the process.

Gusto's onboarding is more streamlined. New hires fill out their own tax forms, direct deposit details, and personal information through a self-service flow. It handles the compliance paperwork well, but there's less room to customize the onboarding experience with company-specific tasks and welcome materials.

For companies that care about first-day impressions and structured 30-60-90 day plans, BambooHR is the stronger choice. For companies that mostly need new hires to fill out paperwork correctly, Gusto gets the job done.

## Performance Management

BambooHR includes performance management tools — goal tracking, peer feedback, manager assessments, and scheduled review cycles. You can run annual reviews, quarterly check-ins, or any cadence that works for your team. This is a genuine differentiator; most payroll-first platforms don't touch performance management at all.

Gusto doesn't offer performance reviews or goal tracking. If you need these features alongside Gusto, you'll need a separate tool like Lattice or 15Five.

## Applicant Tracking

BambooHR has a built-in applicant tracking system (ATS). You can post jobs, manage candidates through a hiring pipeline, schedule interviews, and collaborate with your hiring team — all without leaving the platform. It's not as powerful as a dedicated ATS like Greenhouse, but it covers the basics well and keeps your hiring data connected to your employee records.

Gusto does not include applicant tracking. Job posting and candidate management require a third-party integration.

## Benefits Administration

Gusto shines here. It supports health insurance, 401(k) retirement plans, HSA and FSA accounts, commuter benefits, and workers' compensation — all managed within the platform. Gusto acts as a licensed broker in most states, so you can shop for and enroll in health plans directly through the platform.

BambooHR offers benefits tracking (recording who's enrolled in what) but doesn't function as a benefits broker. You'll still need a separate broker or benefits platform to actually administer health insurance and retirement plans. BambooHR connects with various benefits providers through integrations, but the experience isn't as seamless as Gusto's built-in approach.

**Benefits verdict:** Gusto is the better choice if you want to manage benefits enrollment, health insurance shopping, and retirement plans in one place.

## Reporting and Analytics

BambooHR provides a solid reporting suite — headcount, turnover, time-off usage, and custom reports with filters and visualizations. The data covers the full employee lifecycle since BambooHR is capturing more HR information to begin with.

Gusto's reporting focuses on payroll — tax liability, payment history, contractor spend, and benefits costs. It's useful for finance and accounting teams but doesn't offer the same breadth of HR analytics that BambooHR does.

## Who Should Pick Which

**Choose BambooHR if:**
- You already have a payroll provider and need stronger HR management
- You want built-in performance reviews and applicant tracking
- Your company has 50+ employees and needs structured HR workflows
- Onboarding experience and employee lifecycle tracking are priorities
- You need detailed HR analytics beyond payroll data

**Choose Gusto if:**
- Payroll and tax compliance are your primary concern
- You need benefits administration (health, 401(k), HSA) in one platform
- You're a small business under 100 employees wanting simplicity
- You pay contractors regularly and need 1099 support
- Transparent, predictable pricing matters to you

## The Bottom Line

BambooHR and Gusto solve different problems. BambooHR is the better HR platform — it handles the full employee lifecycle from hiring to performance reviews. Gusto is the better payroll platform — it nails tax filing, benefits administration, and paying people on time. The right choice depends on which problem you're actually trying to solve first.

For many small businesses, Gusto's all-in-one payroll and benefits package is the simpler, more cost-effective starting point. For growing companies that need structured HR processes and already handle payroll elsewhere, BambooHR is worth the investment.
