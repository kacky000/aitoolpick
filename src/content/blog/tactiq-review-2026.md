# Tactiq Review 2026: Free Meeting Transcription That Actually Works

Most meeting transcription tools want you to install a desktop app, connect a bot to your calendar, and commit to a subscription before you've even tested the product. Tactiq takes the opposite approach: install a Chrome extension, join a meeting, and start getting transcripts. It's one of the simplest ways to capture what was said in a Google Meet, Zoom, or Teams call — and the free plan is actually usable, not just a trial wrapped in a "free" label.

After testing Tactiq across dozens of meetings in 2026, here's what works, what doesn't, and who should use it.

## What is Tactiq?

Tactiq is a **Chrome browser extension** that provides real-time transcription for video meetings. It works with Google Meet, Zoom (web client), and Microsoft Teams (web client). During a meeting, it displays a live transcript in a sidebar. After the meeting, it uses AI to generate summaries, action items, and follow-up suggestions.

It launched as a Google Meet transcription tool and has since expanded to cover the three major meeting platforms. The core pitch is simplicity: no bots joining your call, no desktop software, no complex setup.

## Pricing

| Plan | Price | Transcriptions | AI Credits |
|------|-------|---------------|------------|
| **Free** | $0 | 10/month | 5 AI credits/mo |
| **Pro** | $12/mo ($8/mo annual) | Unlimited | Unlimited AI |
| **Team** | $20/user/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited AI + admin |

The free plan gives you 10 transcriptions per month, which breaks down to roughly 2-3 per week. Each transcription can be any length — there's no per-meeting minute cap, which is generous. The 5 AI credits cover things like generating summaries and action items.

Pro removes all limits for $12/month (or $8/month if you pay annually). For anyone averaging more than 3 meetings a week that need transcription, Pro pays for itself in time saved.

The Team plan adds admin controls, shared transcript libraries, and user management at $20/user/month.

## Key Features

### Real-Time Transcription

Tactiq transcribes as the meeting happens. The transcript appears in a sidebar panel overlaid on your meeting window. Each speaker's words are tagged with their name and a timestamp. You can scroll back through the transcript during the meeting itself — handy when someone says a number or a URL and you need to double-check it.

Accuracy is solid for clear audio in English. In testing, it hovered around 90-95% accuracy with decent microphones and minimal crosstalk. Heavy accents, poor connections, or multiple people talking at once degrade accuracy, as with any transcription tool.

### AI Summaries and Action Items

After a meeting, Tactiq's AI (GPT-4 under the hood) generates a structured summary with key discussion points, action items attributed to speakers, decisions made, and follow-up suggestions. It identifies lines like "John, can you send the report by Friday?" and surfaces them as discrete tasks you can export to Notion, Google Docs, or your CRM.

The summaries save 10-15 minutes of manual note-taking per meeting. They're not perfect — the AI sometimes interprets a hypothetical as a decision — but they're useful enough to change your post-meeting workflow.

### Integrations

Tactiq connects to Google Docs, Notion, Slack, HubSpot, Salesforce (Team plan), and Zapier. The CRM integrations are particularly valuable for sales teams — after a discovery call, transcript and action items flow directly into your pipeline without copying and pasting.

## What Tactiq Does Well

- **Zero-friction setup** — Install the extension, join a meeting. No calendar connection, no bot participant, no desktop app.
- **Genuinely useful free plan** — 10 transcriptions/month with no per-meeting minute cap. Competitors often limit sessions to 30-40 minutes; Tactiq doesn't.
- **Three platforms, one extension** — Google Meet, Zoom, and Teams covered consistently.
- **Privacy-friendly** — Runs as a browser extension, not a meeting bot. No OAuth access to your Google Workspace or M365 tenant needed, which makes IT approval easier.

## Where Tactiq Falls Short

- **Chrome only** — No Firefox, Safari, or desktop app support. You must run meetings through Chrome's web client, which isn't ideal for Zoom power users.
- **No offline transcription** — You can't upload recordings. Live meetings only.
- **10 meetings/month is tight** — Daily standups burn half your free allocation in a week.
- **Limited non-English support** — Accuracy and AI summary quality drop outside English.
- **AI credits capped on Free** — Only 5 AI summaries out of 10 transcriptions. The raw transcript without AI analysis is less useful.

## Who Should Use Tactiq?

- **Freelancers and solopreneurs** who need affordable meeting notes (Pro is $12/mo or $8/mo annual)
- **Small teams** running meetings in Chrome without IT overhead
- **Sales reps** who want call notes flowing into HubSpot or Salesforce automatically
- **Students and researchers** with fewer than 10 meetings per month

## The Verdict

Tactiq does one thing well: it makes meeting transcription simple and affordable. The Chrome extension approach trades flexibility for ease of use, and for many people that's the right trade. If you run meetings in your browser, want AI summaries without a heavy setup process, and don't need to transcribe recordings, Tactiq is a smart pick — especially at $8-12/month.

Just know its limits. Chrome-only access, no offline transcription, and a 10-meeting free cap mean it won't work for everyone. But for the right use case, it's one of the most practical meeting AI tools available in 2026.
