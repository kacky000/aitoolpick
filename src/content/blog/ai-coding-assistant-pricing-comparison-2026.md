# AI Coding Assistant Pricing Comparison 2026: Every Tool's Real Cost

The AI coding market in 2026 is crowded, and pricing is all over the map — flat subscriptions, credit pools, daily quotas, token credits, and pay-per-compute. This guide puts the major tools side by side so you can see what each actually costs and which is cheapest for how you work.

## The Master Cost Table

| Tool | Free tier | Entry plan | Heavy/Power | Billing model |
|------|-----------|------------|-------------|---------------|
| **GitHub Copilot** | Yes (2,000 completions/mo) | Pro — **$10/mo** | Pro+ $39/mo | Usage-based AI credits (completions free) |
| **Windsurf** | Yes (light daily quota) | Pro — **$15/mo** | Teams $30/user | Daily quotas (reset every 24h) |
| **Cursor** | Yes (Hobby) | Pro — **$20/mo** | Pro+ $60 / Ultra $200 | Monthly credit pool |
| **OpenAI Codex** | Yes (limited) | Plus — **$20/mo** | Pro $200/mo | Token-based credits (via ChatGPT) |
| **Claude Code** | No | Claude Pro — **$20/mo** | Max 5x $100 / Max 20x $200 | Usage-capped (via Anthropic plans) |
| **Devin** | No | Core — **$20/mo** + $2.25/ACU | Team $500/mo | Pay-as-you-go (Agent Compute Units) |

The entry prices cluster between $10 and $20 — but the billing *model* is what determines your real monthly bill.

## Three Billing Philosophies

**1. Flat subscription (most predictable).** Windsurf ($15 quota) and Cursor ($20 pool) give you a known monthly number. Completions are free; only premium agent work is metered. Best for developers who want budget certainty.

**2. Usage-scaled credits (cost rises with use).** GitHub Copilot (AI credits, 1 credit = $0.01), OpenAI Codex (token credits), and Claude Code (usage caps) all start cheap but scale with how hard you push premium models. Copilot's completions stay free; Codex and Claude Code bill the heavier work against an allowance you can exhaust.

**3. Pay-per-compute (scales fastest).** Devin bills by ACU — a single full feature can cost $22–45 on top of the $20 platform fee. Cost tracks autonomous work delivered, not seat-time.

## The Cheapest Pick for Each Use Case

- **Lowest sticker price, light use** → **GitHub Copilot Pro ($10/mo)**. Unlimited free completions and a $10 credit allowance cover casual users who may never pay more.
- **Cheapest predictable daily driver** → **Windsurf Pro ($15/mo)**. Flat quota, no surprises, genuinely usable free tier.
- **Best balance of price and AI-first power** → **Cursor Pro ($20/mo)**. Bigger premium pool, multi-model, free completions — workable even for full-time coders.
- **Already on ChatGPT** → **OpenAI Codex** (bundled in Plus, $20). Low friction, but watch credit burn if you run many parallel agents.
- **Want Anthropic's models for big refactors** → **Claude Code**, realistically $100/mo (Max 5x) for full-time use since the $20 Pro tier caps out fast.
- **Delegating autonomous tasks** → **Devin**, but only if the hands-off PRs justify $2.25/ACU. Expensive for active coding, reasonable for occasional delegation.

## Where the Hidden Costs Hide

- **Cursor / Windsurf**: heavy agent days can push you to a higher tier (Pro+, Ultra) — budget for the jump if you're a power user.
- **Copilot**: the new June 2026 AI-credit system means premium chat/agent work can add overage beyond your $10 allowance.
- **Codex**: parallelism multiplies token-credit burn; heavy users land around $100–150/mo effective.
- **Claude Code**: the realistic daily-driver price is $100/mo, not the $20 entry tier.
- **Devin**: per-ACU billing means a busy month can run into the hundreds.

## How to Choose Without Overpaying

1. **Start free.** Copilot, Windsurf, Cursor, and Codex all have free tiers — test before you pay.
2. **Match the model to your pattern.** Steady daily coder? Flat plans (Windsurf/Cursor). Bursty or parallel? Watch usage-scaled tools closely.
3. **Separate editor from agent.** Tools like Devin and Codex are delegation engines, not editors — budget them as a separate line item alongside a daily editor.
