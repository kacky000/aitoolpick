Asana is one of the few enterprise-grade project management tools that quietly offers **50% off paid plans** for verified nonprofits — on top of an already generous free tier. For nonprofit teams juggling grant deliverables, campaign timelines, and volunteer coordination, that's a real cost lever. This is how to qualify, what you actually get, and where Asana fits next to alternatives.

## Asana's Nonprofit Discount in 2026

| Plan | Standard price | Nonprofit price |
|------|---------------|-----------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 |
| Starter | $10.99/user/mo | **$5.50/user/mo (50% off)** |
| Advanced | $24.99/user/mo | **$12.50/user/mo (50% off)** |
| Enterprise | Custom | Discounted via sales |

The 50% discount applies for the life of the account once approved — not a 12-month trial. That makes Asana one of the cheapest "real" PM tools at scale for nonprofits.

## Who Qualifies?

Asana verifies eligibility through partners (Goodstack/Percent). General criteria:

- Registered 501(c)(3) in the US or equivalent recognized nonprofit status outside the US
- Active charitable / educational / religious mission
- Not a school, hospital, or government agency (separate programs may apply)
- Verified within ~5 business days

If your org appears in TechSoup, you're almost certainly eligible.

## How to Apply (15 Minutes)

1. Sign up for an Asana account with your nonprofit's email domain
2. Visit asana.com/nonprofits (or contact Sales)
3. Fill out the discount form with org name, EIN/registration number, mission description
4. Asana routes verification through Goodstack — upload your tax-exempt letter
5. Wait 1–5 business days for approval
6. Discount applies automatically to your subscription

Tip: apply **before** you commit to a paid plan. Retroactive discounts are messier than starting at the discounted rate.

- **Grant deliverable tracking**: timeline views map cleanly to grant milestones

## Free vs Discounted Starter

The Free plan is genuinely useful for nonprofits under 10 people:

| Feature | Free | Starter (50% off = $5.50) |

| Users | 10 | Unlimited |
| Projects | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Tasks | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Timeline / Gantt | ❌ | ✅ |
| Custom fields | ❌ | ✅ |
| Forms | Basic | Branded |

If you need **Timeline view** or **reporting dashboards**, the $5.50 Starter is worth it the moment you have 3+ active programs.

## Common Nonprofit Workflows in Asana

### 1. Grant Lifecycle

A project per grant with sections for: Application → Awarded → Reporting → Closeout. Tasks for each deliverable with due dates. Portfolio view rolls up all grants.

### 2. Campaign Calendar

A campaign project with Timeline view. Tasks for content, email, social, paid ads. Custom field "Channel" lets you filter views per team.

### 3. Volunteer Onboarding

A template project copied per new volunteer cohort. Tasks for orientation, training, first shift. Forms collect signups directly into the intake section.

### 4. Board Meeting Prep

A recurring project that resets monthly. Tasks for agenda, financials, minutes. Automatic due-date shift each cycle.

## FAQ

**Is Asana totally free for nonprofits?** No — the Free plan is free (10 users). Paid plans get 50% off after verification.

**How long does verification take?** Typically 1–5 business days through Goodstack/Percent.

**Does the discount apply to add-ons?** No — only Starter and Advanced subscriptions. Asana AI add-ons are full price.

**Asana or Monday for nonprofits?** Asana has stronger free tier and lifelong discount. Monday has prettier dashboards. Most nonprofits land on Asana for budget reasons.
