Superhuman is the premium **AI email client**, and its pricing reflects that. There's no free tier and the plans cost far more than typical email apps. So what do you actually pay, and is it worth it? Here's the complete **Superhuman pricing** breakdown for 2026.

## Superhuman Pricing at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per mo) | Key features |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $30 | $25 | Superhuman AI, calendar, productivity coaching, team collaboration |
| Business | $40 | $33 | Everything in Starter + Auto Drafts, Ask AI, custom auto labels, HubSpot/Salesforce |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Advanced admin, security, dedicated support |

There is **no free plan** and no freemium tier. The cheapest way in is the Starter plan billed annually at **$25/month**.

## What's Included

### Starter ($30/mo)

The Starter plan covers the core Superhuman experience: blazing-fast keyboard-driven inbox, Superhuman AI, calendar and scheduling, group productivity coaching, team collaboration, and unified admin and billing. For most individuals, Starter is all you need.

### Business ($40/mo)

The Business plan adds the heavier AI and CRM features: **Auto Drafts** (AI pre-writes replies in your voice), **Ask AI** (query your whole inbox in natural language), custom auto labels, HubSpot and Salesforce integration, and the Recent Opens feed. This tier targets sales teams and power users who lean on automation.

### Enterprise

Enterprise pricing is custom and aimed at larger orgs needing advanced security and admin controls. You contact Superhuman's team for a quote.

## Annual vs Monthly

Annual billing saves you money on both paid tiers: Starter drops from $30 to **$25/month** and Business effectively lands around **$33/month**. If you're confident you'll stick with it, annual billing is the better deal.

## Is Superhuman Worth It?

That depends entirely on email volume. The math is simple: if Superhuman saves you even 20–30 minutes a day and your time is valuable, $30/month pays for itself. If you only get a few dozen emails a day, the premium is hard to justify when free and low-cost alternatives exist.

**Worth it if:** you live in your inbox, process hundreds of emails daily, and value speed above all.

**Skip it if:** your volume is modest or you're price-sensitive — a tool like Shortwave delivers most of the value far cheaper.

## Cheaper Alternatives

If $30/month feels steep, you have strong options:

- **Shortwave** — free plan, paid from $8.50/mo, Gmail-native
- **Spark** — free plan, team features, ~$8/mo
- **Gmail + Gemini** — already included with Google Workspace

## Bottom Line

Superhuman costs **$30/month** (Starter) to **$40/month** (Business), with annual billing knocking off about 15–17%. There's no free tier, so it's a deliberate premium product. It's worth it for high-volume email power users who value speed, but most people will get better value from Shortwave or a built-in Gmail/Outlook AI assistant.
