**[Kit](/tools/kit/)** — the email platform formerly known as ConvertKit — prices the way most email tools do: a free tier to start, then a bill that climbs with your subscriber count. The plan names are simple, but the real cost depends entirely on how big your list gets. Here's the full breakdown for 2026.

## Kit Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Starting price | Subscriber cap | Right for |
|------|----------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Newsletter (Free)** | $0 | Up to 10,000 | New creators validating an audience |
| **Creator** | $25/mo | Scales with list size | Growing creators who need automation |
| **Creator Pro** | $50/mo | Scales with list size | Established creators and small businesses |

> Starting prices reflect the lowest subscriber bracket (around 1,000 subscribers). All paid prices rise as your list grows.

### Newsletter (Free) — Up to 10,000 subscribers

Kit's free plan is unusually generous:

- Unlimited landing pages and signup forms
- Email broadcasts (one-off sends)
- Sell digital products and subscriptions
- One basic automation and one email sequence

The catch: you can't use Kit's full visual automation builder, and you're limited to a single sequence. For a creator just starting out, it's enough to grow to several thousand subscribers before paying anything.

### Creator — From $25/month

Everything in Free, plus:

- Unlimited automations and email sequences
- Audience segmentation and tagging
- Free migration from another tool
- Live chat and email support

This is the tier most serious newsletters land on. The price scales by subscriber count — expect roughly $25/mo at 1,000 subscribers, ~$66/mo at 10,000, and more beyond that.

### Creator Pro — From $50/month

Everything in Creator, plus:

- Newsletter referral system to grow your list
- Advanced reporting and subscriber scoring
- Facebook custom audiences
- Priority support

Creator Pro is for creators monetizing seriously, where the referral engine and deeper analytics pay for themselves.

## How Subscriber Tiers Affect Your Bill

Email pricing is deceptive because the headline number is just the entry point. Here's roughly how Kit's Creator plan scales:

| Subscribers | Creator (monthly, approx.) |
|-------------|----------------------------|
| 1,000 | $25 |
| 5,000 | $50 |
| 10,000 | $66 |
| 25,000 | $115 |
| 50,000 | $200+ |

Always model your **projected** list size, not your current one — that's where the real cost lives.

## How Kit Compares on Price

| Tool | Free plan | Paid starts | Built-in monetization |
|------|-----------|-------------|------------------------|
| Kit | ✅ (10,000 subs) | $25/mo | ✅ Products + subscriptions |
| [Beehiiv](/blog/beehiiv-pricing-2026) | ✅ (2,500 subs) | $39/mo | ✅ Ads + subscriptions |
| [Substack](/blog/substack-pricing-2026) | ✅ (unlimited) | 10% of revenue | ✅ Paid subscriptions |
| [Mailchimp](/blog/mailchimp-pricing-2026) | ✅ (500 contacts) | $13/mo | ⚠️ Limited |

Kit's free subscriber cap of 10,000 is the most generous flat allowance here. Where [Substack](/blog/substack-pricing-2026) takes 10% of your paid revenue, Kit charges a predictable flat fee regardless of how much you earn — better for creators making real money, worse for those still at zero revenue.

## Is Kit Worth the Cost?

### It's worth it if:

- You're a **creator-first** sender who wants automation without complexity
- You plan to **sell digital products** directly from your email tool
- You want **predictable flat pricing** instead of a revenue cut
- You value the referral and recommendation network for organic growth

### Look elsewhere if:

- You're pre-revenue and want **zero fixed cost** — [Substack](/blog/substack-pricing-2026)'s revenue share fits better
- You need **heavy ecommerce email** flows — [Mailchimp](/blog/mailchimp-pricing-2026) or a dedicated platform may suit
- Your list is huge and mostly free — the per-subscriber pricing gets expensive fast

## Tips to Save on Kit

1. **Stay on Free as long as you can** — 10,000 subscribers is a lot of runway
2. **Clean your list** — Kit bills by subscriber, so prune cold contacts regularly
3. **Pay annually** — save roughly two months versus monthly billing
4. **Start on Creator, not Creator Pro** — only upgrade once the referral system would pay for itself

## The Bottom Line

Kit's pricing rewards creators who are actually making money: a flat fee beats a revenue percentage once your newsletter earns real income. The free plan's 10,000-subscriber ceiling gives you genuine room to grow before paying a cent. Just remember the headline $25 is a floor, not a ceiling — your bill rises with every subscriber, so model your growth before you commit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Kit the same as ConvertKit?

Yes. ConvertKit rebranded to Kit in 2024. The features, plans, and pricing structure carried over — only the name changed.

### Is Kit really free?

Yes, up to 10,000 subscribers, with limited automation. Once you need full automation, segmentation, or pass the subscriber cap, you move to the Creator plan starting at $25/month.

### How does Kit pricing scale?

Both paid plans scale by subscriber count. The starting prices apply to small lists; expect the bill to roughly double every time your list grows by an order of magnitude.
