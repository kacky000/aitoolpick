Canva is the go-to design tool for non-designers — drag-and-drop simplicity, thousands of templates, and a generous free plan make it accessible to anyone. But at $15/month for Pro, some users want more creative control, better typography, or tools built for specific use cases like UI design or video editing.

Whether Canva feels too limiting, too expensive, or too template-dependent, these seven alternatives offer different strengths in 2026.

---

## Why People Look for Canva Alternatives

Canva excels at accessibility but has limitations:

- Professional designers often find it too restrictive for custom work
- Template dependency can make designs look generic
- No vector editing or advanced illustration tools
- Brand kit and premium features locked behind the Pro plan
- File export options are limited compared to professional tools

For full context, see our [Canva review for 2026](/blog/canva-review-2026).

## 1. Adobe Express — Best for Adobe Ecosystem Users

**Price:** Free / $9.99/month (Premium)
**Best for:** Users who want Canva simplicity with Adobe's asset library

Adobe Express is Adobe's direct answer to Canva — a simplified design tool with drag-and-drop editing, templates, and AI-powered features. The key advantage is access to Adobe's massive stock library and seamless integration with Photoshop, Illustrator, and other Creative Cloud apps.

**Why it beats Canva for some users:**
- Access to Adobe Stock assets and Adobe Fonts
- Remove background and generative AI features included
- Better integration with professional Adobe tools
- Brand kit available on the free plan

**Where Canva wins:** Canva has more templates, a more intuitive interface, and better collaboration features. Adobe Express can feel like it's pushing you toward the broader Creative Cloud.

Compare them: [Canva vs Adobe Express](/blog/canva-vs-adobe-express-2026).

## 2. Figma — Best for UI/UX Design and Prototyping

**Price:** Free / $15/month (Professional) / $45/month (Organization)
**Best for:** Product teams, UI designers, and developers

If you're using Canva for UI mockups or app designs, Figma is the proper tool for that job. It's the industry standard for UI/UX design with real-time collaboration, prototyping, and developer handoff features that Canva simply doesn't have.

**Why it stands out:**
- Industry-standard UI/UX design tool
- Real-time collaboration like Google Docs for design
- Prototyping and interactive components built in
- Auto Layout and Variants for scalable design systems
- Dev Mode for developer handoff

**Limitations:** Steep learning curve for non-designers. Overkill for simple social media graphics. Requires design knowledge to use effectively.

Read our [Canva vs Figma comparison](/blog/canva-vs-figma-which-design-tool).

## 3. Visme — Best for Presentations and Data Visualization

**Price:** Free / $12.25/month (Starter) / $24.75/month (Pro)
**Best for:** Marketers creating infographics, presentations, and data-driven visuals

Visme is a Canva alternative that specializes in data visualization, infographics, and interactive presentations. If your design needs lean toward charts, reports, and business presentations, Visme's data widgets and animation features make it a stronger choice.

**Why it stands out:**
- Superior data visualization widgets and chart types
- Interactive and animated presentations (not just static slides)
- Better infographic templates and tools
- Brand kit with more control than Canva's
- Embeddable content for websites

**Where Canva wins:** Canva is simpler, has more social media templates, and is better for quick, everyday design tasks. Visme is more specialized.

Compare them: [Canva vs Visme](/blog/canva-vs-visme-2026).

## 4. Google Slides — Best Free Presentation Tool

**Price:** Free
**Best for:** Students, educators, and teams needing free collaborative presentations

If you're using Canva primarily for presentations, Google Slides offers a completely free alternative with real-time collaboration, commenting, and seamless Google Workspace integration. It won't match Canva's template variety, but it's reliable and requires zero learning curve.

**Why it stands out:**
- Completely free with a Google account
- Real-time collaboration with commenting and suggesting
- Works offline with Chrome extension
- Deep Google Workspace integration

**Limitations:** Template selection is basic. No social media design capabilities. Design features are minimal compared to Canva.

See: [Canva vs Google Slides](/blog/canva-vs-google-slides-2026).

## 5. Piktochart — Best for Infographics

**Price:** Free / $14/month (Pro) / $29/month (Business)
**Best for:** Content marketers, educators, and HR teams creating infographics and reports

Piktochart focuses on infographics, reports, and visual storytelling. If your primary design need is turning data and content into visual formats, Piktochart's templates and data visualization tools are more purpose-built than Canva's general-purpose approach.

**Why it stands out:**
- Purpose-built infographic templates and tools
- Visual reports and poster creation
- Interactive charts with data import from spreadsheets
- Simpler interface focused on information design

**Where Canva wins:** Canva is more versatile. Piktochart is narrowly focused on infographics and reports — if you need social media graphics, video, or presentations, Canva covers more ground.

---

## 6. Pixlr — Best Free Photo Editor

**Price:** Free / $4.90/month (Plus) / $14.99/month (Premium)
**Best for:** Users who need photo editing capabilities beyond Canva's basic tools

Pixlr is a browser-based photo editor that offers Photoshop-like features — layers, masks, blend modes, and advanced retouching tools — at a fraction of the price. If Canva's photo editing feels too basic, Pixlr gives you professional-grade editing in the browser.

**Why it stands out:**
- Advanced photo editing (layers, masks, blend modes)
- AI-powered tools for background removal, object removal
- Browser-based — no download required
- Starting at $4.90/month — much cheaper than Photoshop

**Limitations:** Not a design tool — no templates, no social media presets, no brand kits. Pixlr is a photo editor, not a Canva replacement for graphic design.

---

## 7. Crello (VistaCreate) — Best Canva Clone

**Price:** Free / $13/month (Pro)
**Best for:** Users who want a Canva-like experience with different templates

VistaCreate (formerly Crello) is the closest Canva clone on the market. Same drag-and-drop interface, same template categories, same use case — but with a different template library that may have options you won't find on Canva. It's worth checking if you feel Canva's templates have gotten stale.

**Why it stands out:**
- Similar interface to Canva — zero learning curve
- Different template library with unique designs
- Animation and video editing included
- Competitive pricing at $13/month

**Where Canva wins:** Canva has a larger template library, more integrations, and a more polished experience. VistaCreate is a solid alternative but doesn't surpass Canva in any major area.
