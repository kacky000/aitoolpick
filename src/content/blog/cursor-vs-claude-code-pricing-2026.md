# Cursor vs Claude Code Pricing 2026: Which Costs Less to Run?

Cursor and Claude Code attract the same developers — people who want a serious, agentic AI coding tool rather than just autocomplete. But they're priced on different logic. Cursor is a flat-subscription editor with a credit pool. Claude Code isn't a standalone product at all; you reach it through an Anthropic plan, and there's no free tier. Here's how the real monthly cost compares.

## The Plans

| Tier | Cursor | Claude Code (via Anthropic) |
|------|--------|------------------------------|
| Free | Hobby (limited) | **None** — no free tier |
| Entry | Pro — **$20/mo** | Claude Pro — **$20/mo** |
| Power | Pro+ — $60/mo | Claude Max 5x — $100/mo |
| Heavy | Ultra — $200/mo | Claude Max 20x — $200/mo |
| Team | Teams — $40/user/mo | Claude Team — $30/seat/mo |

Both start at **$20/month**, but what that $20 buys is structured very differently.

## How Each One Limits You

**Cursor** gives you a $20 credit pool for premium models on the Pro plan, with unlimited Tab completions. When the pool depletes, you slow down, pay overage, or upgrade. The cap is a *budget* — a dollar pool you draw against.

**Claude Code** gives you usage caps, not a dollar pool. On Claude Pro ($20/mo), you get standard limits — and Claude Code consumes them quickly because the tool reads multiple files, plans, and iterates. A single complex refactor can eat a big chunk of your daily Pro allowance. That's why most serious Claude Code users land on **Max 5x ($100/mo)** for 5× the usage, or **Max 20x ($200/mo)** for effectively unconstrained daily use.

This is the crucial difference: **Cursor's $20 plan is genuinely workable for a full-time developer** because completions are free and only premium agent work draws the pool. **Claude Code's $20 Pro tier often isn't enough** for full-time use — the realistic daily-driver price is $100/mo (Max 5x).

## Real-World Cost Comparison

- **Full-time solo developer:**
  - Cursor: ~$20/mo on Pro, maybe $60 on Pro+ for heavy agent days.
  - Claude Code: realistically **$100/mo (Max 5x)** to avoid hitting limits mid-session.
  - **Cursor is meaningfully cheaper** for the typical full-time coder.

- **Occasional/part-time use:**
  - Cursor: free Hobby tier or $20 Pro.
  - Claude Code: $20 Pro can work if your sessions are short and infrequent — but there's no free tier to test with.
  - **Cursor wins** on the free-tier on-ramp alone.

- **Heavy, all-day agentic work:**
  - Both hit $200 (Cursor Ultra / Claude Max 20x).
  - At this tier it's a wash on price — choose on model quality and workflow.

## What You Get for the Money

Claude Code's premium tiers buy direct, deep access to Anthropic's latest models with a terminal-native agent that excels at large refactors, codebase-wide changes, and migrations. Cursor buys a polished AI-first editor with multi-model choice (including Claude models) and free unlimited completions. If you specifically want Claude's reasoning for big autonomous tasks, Claude Code's higher effective cost may be worth it. If you want flexible daily coding at the lowest price, Cursor wins.

## Team Pricing

Claude Team at $30/seat/mo undercuts Cursor Teams at $40/user/mo — but remember each seat is still usage-capped, so heavy teams may feel the ceiling. Cursor's per-seat credit pool behaves more predictably for mixed-intensity teams.

## The Bottom Line

For most full-time developers, **Cursor is the cheaper daily driver** — its $20 plan is actually sufficient, while Claude Code's practical entry point is $100/mo. Choose **Claude Code** when you specifically want Anthropic's models for heavy autonomous work and are willing to pay Max-tier pricing for it.
