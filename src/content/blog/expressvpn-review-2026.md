# ExpressVPN Review 2026: Is It Still Worth the Price?

ExpressVPN has been one of the most recognized names in the VPN space for years. It consistently ranks near the top in speed tests, unblocks streaming services reliably, and has a clean, simple interface. But it's also the most expensive mainstream VPN on the market.

In 2026, with NordVPN, Surfshark, and others offering similar features at lower prices, the question isn't whether ExpressVPN is good — it's whether the premium is justified.

## What You Get

ExpressVPN operates 3,000+ servers across 105 countries. That's a solid network, though NordVPN's 6,400+ servers in 111 countries is noticeably larger. In practice, both cover the regions most people care about.

Key specs:

- **Simultaneous connections:** 8 devices
- **Protocols:** Lightway (proprietary), OpenVPN, IKEv2
- **Kill switch:** Yes, on all platforms
- **Split tunneling:** Yes
- **No-logs policy:** Independently audited by PwC and KPMG
- **Jurisdiction:** British Virgin Islands

The Lightway protocol is worth highlighting. It's ExpressVPN's proprietary protocol, built for speed and quick reconnection. Switching between Wi-Fi and mobile data, or waking your laptop from sleep, results in near-instant reconnection. It's genuinely smoother than OpenVPN in daily use.

## Pricing

This is where ExpressVPN asks you to commit.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Monthly | $12.95/mo |
| 6-Month | $9.99/mo |
| 12-Month | $6.67/mo |
| Free Plan | None |

There's no free tier. You get a 30-day money-back guarantee, which is effectively a free trial, but you do need to pay upfront.

For comparison, NordVPN's Basic plan starts at $3.59/mo on a 2-year commitment. That's nearly half the cost of ExpressVPN's best annual rate. Over a year, you're paying about $80 for ExpressVPN vs. roughly $43 for NordVPN Basic. The gap adds up.

## Speed and Performance

ExpressVPN is fast. In our testing, Lightway consistently delivered speeds within 5-10% of the base connection on nearby servers. Long-distance connections (US to Asia, Europe to Australia) showed more variability, but ExpressVPN generally landed in the top tier.

What matters more than raw speed for most users is consistency. ExpressVPN rarely drops below usable speeds, even on congested servers. You're unlikely to notice you're connected to a VPN during normal browsing, streaming, or video calls.

Downloads and torrenting are well-supported across the full server network, with no restrictions on P2P traffic.

## Streaming

This is one of ExpressVPN's strongest selling points. It reliably unblocks:

- Netflix (US, UK, Japan, and most other regional libraries)
- Disney+
- Hulu
- BBC iPlayer
- Amazon Prime Video
- HBO Max

Streaming services actively block VPNs, so no provider guarantees 100% access at all times. But ExpressVPN's track record is among the best in the industry. When a server gets blocked, they typically rotate IPs within a day or two.

## Privacy and Security

ExpressVPN's no-logs policy has been independently audited multiple times. The company is based in the British Virgin Islands, which has no data retention laws and sits outside the Five Eyes/Fourteen Eyes intelligence-sharing alliances.

In 2017, Turkish authorities seized an ExpressVPN server as part of an investigation. No user data was found — which is about the strongest real-world validation a VPN's no-logs claim can get.

ExpressVPN also runs RAM-only servers (TrustedServer technology), meaning data is wiped on every reboot. This eliminates the risk of persistent data storage on physical disks.

## Apps and Usability

ExpressVPN's apps are clean and straightforward. Connect in one click, pick a server location, and you're done. Advanced settings exist but stay out of the way. The experience is consistent across Windows, macOS, Linux, iOS, and Android.

Browser extensions for Chrome, Firefox, and Edge let you control the VPN from your browser without opening the full app.

The 8-device simultaneous connection limit is adequate for most individuals. A family of four with phones and laptops could run into the limit, but most solo users won't.

## Pros

- Fast, consistent speeds with the Lightway protocol
- Reliable streaming unblocking across major platforms
- Independently audited no-logs policy with real-world validation
- Clean, easy-to-use apps on all platforms
- RAM-only server infrastructure

## Cons

- Most expensive major VPN — nearly double NordVPN's price
- Only 8 simultaneous connections (NordVPN offers 10)
- No built-in ad blocker or threat protection
- Smaller server network than NordVPN (3,000+ vs. 6,400+)
- No free plan or trial — just a money-back guarantee

## Who Should Use ExpressVPN?

ExpressVPN makes sense if you prioritize simplicity, speed consistency, and reliable streaming access, and you're willing to pay a premium for those things. It's a set-and-forget VPN that works well without tinkering.

It's harder to recommend if you're budget-conscious or want extras like ad blocking and malware protection bundled in. [NordVPN](/blog/nordvpn-review-2026/) covers those needs at a lower price point.

## The Bottom Line

ExpressVPN is still a top-tier VPN in 2026. The speeds are excellent, the privacy credentials are strong, and the streaming access is best-in-class. But the price gap between ExpressVPN and its competitors has widened rather than narrowed. You're paying a premium for polish and reliability — not for features the competition lacks.

If budget matters, NordVPN gives you more for less. If you just want a VPN that works perfectly every time you connect and don't mind paying for that peace of mind, ExpressVPN delivers.
