Descript and Riverside both target podcasters and video creators, but they solve different problems. **Descript** is an AI-powered editing suite that lets you edit audio and video by editing text. **Riverside** is a recording platform built to capture studio-quality audio and video from remote guests, regardless of internet conditions.

If you mostly edit solo content, Descript is likely the better fit. If you run an interview-based podcast or record remote guests regularly, Riverside has clear advantages. This guide breaks down where each tool wins so you can pick the right one. ## Descript Overview

Descript treats your audio and video like a document. Import or record media, and Descript generates a transcript. Delete a sentence from the transcript and the corresponding audio/video disappears. It sounds like a gimmick until you use it — for cutting filler words, rearranging sections, or tightening a rough recording, text-based editing is genuinely faster than scrubbing a timeline.

**Key features in 2026:**

- **Text-based editing** — Cut, rearrange, and trim by editing the transcript directly. No timeline scrubbing required.
- **AI voice cloning (Overdub)** — Train a model on your voice and generate new words you never recorded. Useful for fixing mispronunciations or inserting corrections without re-recording.
- **Filler word removal** — One click removes "um," "uh," "like," and "you know" across an entire recording.
- **Eye contact correction** — AI adjusts your gaze to appear like you're looking at the camera, even when reading notes.
- **Screen recording** — Built-in screen and webcam capture, making it a one-stop tool for tutorials, walkthroughs, and video essays.
- **Publishing** — Export to MP4, MP3, or publish clips directly to social platforms.

Descript works well as an all-in-one tool for solo creators who record, edit, and publish from one place. It's less ideal for multi-track remote interviews because it wasn't designed primarily as a recording platform. ## Riverside Overview

Riverside focuses on one thing and does it exceptionally well: recording high-quality audio and video with remote participants. Each participant's audio and video is recorded locally on their device and uploaded afterward, so a guest's shaky Wi-Fi connection won't degrade your final output.

**Key features in 2026:**

- **Local recording** — Audio and video are captured at full quality on each participant's machine, not streamed through a server. This is the core advantage over Zoom, Google Meet, or any cloud-based recording.
- **Separate tracks** — Each participant gets their own audio and video track, giving you full control in post-production.
- **AI transcription** — Automatic transcription with speaker labels. Supports 100+ languages.
- **Text-based editing** — Riverside added transcript-based editing in 2025. It's functional but not as mature as Descript's implementation.
- **Magic Clips** — AI identifies highlight moments and generates short clips for social media.
- **Browser-based** — Guests join via a link in their browser. No app download required.

Riverside is purpose-built for podcasters who interview guests. If your workflow is "record a conversation, then edit it," Riverside gives you better raw material to work with than almost any alternative. For a full breakdown, check our [Riverside review](/blog/riverside-review-2026).

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Recording

Descript includes a built-in recorder for screen, webcam, and microphone capture. It works fine for solo recording but doesn't offer local recording for remote participants.

Riverside's local recording is the industry standard for remote podcast and video interviews. Audio quality stays consistent regardless of network conditions. Each participant records natively on their device.

**Winner:** Riverside, especially for remote interviews.

### Editing

Descript's text-based editing is the most refined in the market. Edit words in the transcript and the media follows. It handles multi-track audio, screen recordings, and video compositing in a single interface.

Riverside added text-based editing, but it's more limited. For heavy editing — rearranging segments, layering B-roll, adding transitions — most Riverside users still export to a dedicated editor like Adobe Premiere or DaVinci Resolve.

**Winner:** Descript by a wide margin.

### Transcription

Both tools include AI transcription. Descript's is slightly more accurate for English content and integrates tightly with the editor. Riverside supports more languages (100+) and includes speaker detection out of the box.

**Winner:** Tie. Both are accurate enough for professional use.

### AI Features

Descript leads here with voice cloning, filler word removal, eye contact correction, and AI-generated captions. These features save real time in post-production.

Riverside's AI features are more focused: auto-transcription, speaker detection, and Magic Clips for social content. Useful, but narrower in scope.

**Winner:** Descript for AI editing tools.

### Export and Publishing

Descript exports to MP4, MP3, WAV, and can publish clips to YouTube, TikTok, and LinkedIn directly. Riverside exports separate audio and video tracks per participant in WAV, MP3, MP4, or combined formats. Riverside's separate-track exports give you more flexibility in professional post-production workflows.

**Winner:** Depends on your workflow. Descript for quick publishing, Riverside for pro post-production.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Descript if you:

- Record solo content (tutorials, video essays, screen recordings)
- Want to edit audio and video without learning a traditional editor
- Need AI tools like voice cloning and filler word removal
- Prefer an all-in-one workflow from recording to publishing
- Repurpose long-form content into social clips frequently

### Choose Riverside if you:

- Run an interview-based podcast with remote guests
- Need studio-quality audio regardless of guest internet quality
- Want separate audio/video tracks for professional post-production
- Record in multiple languages
- Prioritize recording quality over editing features

## Verdict

These tools complement each other more than they compete. Riverside is a recording tool. Descript is an editing tool. Some podcasters use both — Riverside to capture interviews, Descript to edit them.

If you have to pick one: **solo creators and content repurposers should go with Descript.** The text-based editing and AI features save hours per week. **Interview-heavy podcasters should go with Riverside.** No other tool matches its recording quality for remote guests, and the $15/month Standard plan is hard to beat.

Neither tool is a bad choice. The right answer depends on whether your biggest pain point is recording or editing.
