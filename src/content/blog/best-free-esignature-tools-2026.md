You don't always need a paid subscription to collect a legally binding signature. If you sign documents only occasionally, a **free e-signature tool** can cover you completely. Here are the best free options in 2026 — and the catches to watch for.

## Are Free E-Signatures Legally Binding?

Yes. In the US (ESIGN Act, UETA) and EU (eIDAS), electronic signatures collected through these tools are legally binding for the vast majority of business documents. The free tiers use the same underlying signing technology as paid plans — they just limit volume or features.

## 1. Dropbox Sign — Best Free Experience

Dropbox Sign's free plan gives you **3 signature requests per month** with a clean, simple interface and basic templates. It's the most polished free signing experience, ideal for freelancers and light users.

**Free limit:** 3 requests/month, 1 sender.

## 2. PandaDoc — Best Free for Proposals

PandaDoc's free e-sign plan covers **unlimited documents for signing** plus basic upload-and-sign features. If you occasionally send simple proposals or contracts, it's generous and the upgrade path is strong.

**Free limit:** core e-sign features; advanced templates and analytics are paid. See [DocuSign vs PandaDoc](/blog/docusign-vs-pandadoc-2026/).

## 3. SignWell — Best Free for Small Teams

SignWell's free plan allows a limited number of documents per month with reminders and templates included. It's the best free pick if you want room to grow into cheap paid tiers later.

**Free limit:** a few documents/month.

## 4. Signaturely — Best for Occasional Use

Signaturely keeps a minimal free tier aimed at people who sign a couple of documents a month. No frills, but quick and genuinely free.

**Free limit:** very low volume.

## 5. Your PDF Reader's Built-In Signing

Don't overlook the free signing already built into tools you own — Adobe Acrobat Reader, Preview on Mac, and many PDF apps let you self-sign and fill forms at no cost. For documents only **you** need to sign (not collect from others), this is the simplest free route.

**Free limit:** self-signing only, no request tracking or audit trail.

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Free monthly limit | Best for |
|------|--------------------|----------|
| Dropbox Sign | 3 requests | Polished free experience |
| PandaDoc | Core e-sign | Proposals |
| SignWell | A few docs | Small teams |
| Signaturely | Very low | Occasional use |

## When to Upgrade to Paid

Move to a paid plan when you:

- Send **more than a handful** of documents a month
- Need **multiple senders** or team templates
- Want **document analytics** and reminders
- Require **advanced authentication** or compliance features

At that point, compare the [best e-signature software in 2026](/blog/best-esignature-software-2026/) or the cheaper [DocuSign alternatives](/blog/best-docusign-alternatives-2026/).

## Bottom Line

For most occasional signers, **Dropbox Sign** offers the best free experience, while **PandaDoc** is the best free option if you send proposals. Start free, and only pay once your volume actually demands it.
