[Kagi](/tools/kagi/) asks a provocative question: would you pay $10/month for a search engine? In a world where Google is free, that's a tough sell. But after months of daily use, we can say definitively — for certain people, Kagi is worth every penny. Here's why.

## What Is Kagi?

Kagi is a paid, ad-free search engine with AI-powered summaries and extensive customization. Founded in 2022, it positions itself as the anti-Google: no ads, no tracking, no SEO spam, and results you can customize to prioritize sources you trust.

The business model is simple: you pay with money instead of attention and data. This alignment means Kagi's incentive is better results (to keep you subscribed) rather than more ad clicks.

## Kagi Pricing 2026

| Plan | Price | Searches | Key Features |
|------|-------|----------|--------------|
| Starter | $5/mo | 300/month | Basic search, limited AI |
| Professional | $10/mo | Unlimited | Full AI summaries, all features |
| Ultimate | $25/mo | Unlimited | Maximum AI usage, early features |

There's no free plan. Kagi's philosophy is that free means ad-supported, which is the problem they're solving. The Starter plan at 300 searches/month sounds limited, but most people search less than they think (check your browser history).

## Key Features

### Ad-Free Search Results

The most immediately noticeable difference from Google: no ads. Ever. No sponsored results at the top, no shopping ads, no "people also ask" boxes pushing organic results down. Just search results ranked by relevance.

This sounds minor until you realize how much visual clutter Google adds. Kagi results feel calm and focused by comparison.

### AI Summaries (Quick Answer)

For factual queries, Kagi generates an AI summary at the top of results drawing from multiple sources. It cites its sources (linked), so you can verify. This is similar to Google's AI Overviews but without the advertising conflicts — Kagi's summaries aim to answer your question, not to keep you searching.

### Domain Customization

This is Kagi's killer feature for power users. You can:

- **Boost** domains: Results from boosted sites rank higher (e.g., boost StackOverflow, MDN, Hacker News)
- **Block** domains: Never see results from blocked sites (e.g., block content farms, SEO spam)
- **Lower** domains: Reduce ranking without full blocking

Over time, your Kagi results become increasingly personalized to your preferences — not through surveillance but through explicit choices you control.

### Lenses

Pre-configured search filters for specific contexts:
- **Programming**: Only developer-focused sites
- **Academic**: Scholarly papers and university sites
- **News**: Recent news from quality sources
- **Forums**: Reddit, HN, Stack Exchange, etc.

Custom lenses let you create your own filtered search experiences.

### Privacy

- No tracking whatsoever
- No advertising profile built on you
- Search history stored only on your account (deletable)
- No data sold to third parties
- Queries not used to train AI models

### Universal Summarizer

Paste any URL and Kagi summarizes it. Works on articles, papers, YouTube videos (from transcripts), podcasts, and more. Useful for quickly evaluating whether a long piece is worth reading in full.

## Pros

- **No ads**: Clean, focused results without commercial interference
- **Better result quality**: For technical queries especially, results feel more relevant
- **Domain customization**: Powerful personalization you control
- **AI summaries**: Concise answers with cited sources
- **Privacy**: No tracking, no profiling, no data sales
- **Lenses**: Context-specific search modes
- **Fast**: Results load quickly, no JavaScript bloat
- **Universal Summarizer**: Summarize any URL [instantly](/tools/instantly/)

## Cons

- **No free plan**: Requires $5-25/month subscription
- **Smaller index than Google**: Some obscure queries return fewer results
- **Starter plan limit**: 300 searches/month may not be enough for heavy searchers
- **Requires habit change**: Setting Kagi as default means trusting a new engine
- **No local business features**: Google Maps integration for local search is still unmatched
- **Image/video search less mature**: Google and Bing still better for media search
- **Niche product**: Small team means slower feature development than Google

## Who Is Kagi Best For?

- **Developers** tired of SEO-gamed tutorial sites outranking real documentation
- **Researchers** wanting clean results without commercial interference
- **Privacy advocates** who refuse to be tracked
- **Power users** who want to customize their search experience
- **Anyone** frustrated with Google's declining result quality and ad overload

## Who Should Stick With Google?

- People who search rarely (hard to justify monthly cost)
- Heavy local/maps search users (Google Maps is irreplaceable)
- Users satisfied with Google's current quality
- Budget-conscious individuals (search is free elsewhere)
- Image/video-heavy searchers

## The Verdict

Kagi earns a **4.6/5** in 2026. It delivers on its promise of better search through a better business model. The results are genuinely higher quality for technical and research queries, the customization is powerful, and the privacy is real.

The question isn't whether Kagi is good — it's whether paying $10/month for search makes sense for you personally. If you search frequently (50+ times/day), value privacy, and are frustrated with Google's ad-heavy, SEO-spammed results, Kagi pays for itself in saved time and reduced frustration.

If you search casually and don't mind ads, Google remains perfectly functional and free.

**Recommendation**: Try the Starter plan ($5/month, 300 searches) for one month. Set Kagi as your default search engine. If you find yourself relieved by the clean results and actually enjoy searching again, upgrade to Professional. If not, no harm done.
