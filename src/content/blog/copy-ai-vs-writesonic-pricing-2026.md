# Copy.ai vs Writesonic Pricing 2026: GTM Platform vs Volume Writer

Copy.ai and Writesonic both hand you a free tier and a low-cost entry, which makes them look like the same kind of budget AI writer. They aren't anymore. Writesonic has stayed a content-volume tool with prices that top out at $79. Copy.ai has pivoted into go-to-market (GTM) workflow automation, and its serious tier costs $249. Same starting line, completely different finish.

## The Plans Side by Side

| Plan | Copy.ai | Writesonic |
|------|---------|------------|
| **Free** | Yes — 2,000 words/mo | Yes — free tier |
| **Entry paid** | Starter $49/mo | Individual $16/mo |
| **Mid** | — | Standard $79/mo |
| **Top** | Advanced $249/mo | Standard $79/mo |

Writesonic is cheaper at every named tier. Copy.ai's $249 Advanced has no Writesonic equivalent — because it isn't selling the same thing up there.

## How Copy.ai Pricing Works

Copy.ai now markets itself as a GTM platform — sales outreach, lead enrichment, and marketing workflow automation. Its [Copy.ai review](/blog/copy-ai-review-2026/) lists Free (2,000 words/month), Starter at $49/month, and Advanced at $249/month for workflow automation and higher limits.

The $249 jump is deliberate. Copy.ai's growth bet is automated revenue workflows, not blog drafts. Starter at $49 is fine for writing, but the platform's real (expensive) value is the automation layer above it.

## How Writesonic Pricing Works

Writesonic stays focused on content output and cost-per-word. Its [Writesonic review](/blog/writesonic-review-2026/) shows a free tier, Individual at $16/month, and Standard at $79/month, with a noted tradeoff: output quality is inconsistent on lower plans and word limits apply on cheaper tiers.

Writesonic's whole pitch is volume for a low price. There's no $249 automation tier because automating GTM isn't what it's trying to be.

## The Real Cost Comparison

**Solo writer / freelancer:** Writesonic Individual ($16) crushes Copy.ai Starter ($49) on price for plain content generation. Both have free tiers to test, but for paid writing volume, Writesonic is far cheaper. **Writesonic wins.**

**Small marketing team writing content:** Still Writesonic — Standard ($79) handles team volume well below Copy.ai's pricing logic for anything beyond Starter. Unless you need workflows, Copy.ai's value doesn't justify the climb. **Writesonic wins.**

**Sales / GTM team automating outreach:** Now it flips entirely. Copy.ai Advanced ($249) automates lead enrichment and outbound sequences that Writesonic doesn't attempt. Comparing it to Writesonic's $79 is apples-to-tractors. **Copy.ai wins — only it does this job.**

## They've Diverged

A year or two ago these were near-twins. Today the decision is simple: if you want to *write*, Writesonic is the cheaper, more direct choice across every tier. If you want to *automate go-to-market motions*, Copy.ai's $249 Advanced is the product — and Writesonic isn't a competitor for that work at all. Don't pay Copy.ai's premium for writing you could do on Writesonic for $16–$79.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Plain content volume, low budget?** Writesonic Individual $16.
- **Team content production?** Writesonic Standard $79.
- **Automating sales/GTM workflows?** Copy.ai Advanced $249.
- **Just testing AI writing?** Either free tier — Copy.ai's 2,000 words or Writesonic's free plan.

## The Bottom Line

Writesonic is the cheaper writer at every tier ($16–$79). Copy.ai is the more expensive GTM automation platform ($249 at the top) that happens to also write. Pick on the job: writing favors Writesonic, workflow automation favors Copy.ai. Their shared free tiers make them look alike, but their ceilings tell the real story.
