Looking for the best grammarly alternatives? We tested and compared the top options available in 2026, evaluating features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance.

Grammarly is the most popular writing assistant, but it's not the only option. Whether you want something cheaper, more privacy-focused, or with different features, several strong alternatives exist in 2026.

Here are 7 Grammarly alternatives worth trying, from free tools to premium AI writing assistants.

For context, see our [Grammarly review](/blog/grammarly-review-2026) and [free vs paid comparison](/blog/grammarly-free-vs-paid).

## 1. ProWritingAid — Best for Serious Writers

**Pricing**: Free (limited), $10/month (Premium)

ProWritingAid goes deeper than grammar checking. It analyzes readability, sentence structure, overused words, pacing, and writing style. The detailed reports help you actually improve your writing over time, not just fix individual errors.

Integrates with Word, Google Docs, Scrivener, and browsers.

**Best for**: Authors, bloggers, and anyone who wants to improve their writing [craft](/tools/craft/).

## 2. Hemingway Editor — Best for Clarity

**Pricing**: Free (web), $19.99 (desktop app, one-time)

Hemingway focuses on readability. It highlights complex sentences, passive voice, adverbs, and hard-to-read passages with color coding. It won't catch every grammar error, but it'll [make](/tools/make/) your writing clearer and more direct.

The one-time pricing for the desktop app is refreshing in a subscription-heavy market.

**Best for**: Writers who want concise, readable prose.

## 3. QuillBot — Best for Paraphrasing

**Pricing**: Free (limited), $9.95/month (Premium)

QuillBot's paraphrasing tool is its standout feature. Paste text and get it rewritten in multiple styles — fluent, formal, creative, simple. It also includes a grammar checker, summarizer, and citation generator.

**Best for**: Students and professionals who need to rephrase and refine text.

## 4. LanguageTool — Best for Privacy and Multilingual Support

**Pricing**: Free (limited), €4.99/month (Premium)

LanguageTool is open-source and supports 30+ languages, making it the best option for non-English writers. The privacy focus is a key differentiator — you can self-host it for complete data control.

Grammar and style checking is solid, though not as deep as Grammarly or ProWritingAid for English.

**Best for**: Multilingual writers and privacy-conscious users.

## 5. Wordtune — Best for Rewriting Sentences

**Pricing**: Free (10 rewrites/day), $9.99/month (Plus)

Wordtune rewrites your sentences to make them clearer, more formal, or more casual. Unlike grammar checkers that fix errors, Wordtune improves already-correct text. The AI suggestions are often surprisingly good.

**Best for**: Non-native English speakers and anyone who wants better phrasing.

## 6. Ginger — Best Budget Alternative

**Pricing**: Free (basic), $7.49/month (Premium)

Ginger offers grammar checking, sentence rephrasing, translation (40+ languages), and a text reader. It's cheaper than Grammarly Premium and covers the basics well, though the suggestion quality doesn't quite match.

**Best for**: Budget-conscious users who need solid grammar checking.

## 7. ChatGPT — Best AI-Powered Writing Assistant

**Pricing**: Free, $20/month (Plus)

ChatGPT isn't a traditional grammar checker, but it's become a powerful writing tool. Paste your text and ask it to proofread, improve clarity, adjust tone, or rewrite sections. The flexibility exceeds any dedicated grammar tool.

The downside is that it requires more active prompting than Grammarly's passive corrections.

**Best for**: Users who want flexible AI writing help beyond grammar checking.
