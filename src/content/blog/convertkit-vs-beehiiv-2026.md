# ConvertKit vs Beehiiv 2026: Email Marketing Meets Modern Newsletter

ConvertKit (now officially "Kit") has been the go-to email marketing platform for creators since 2013. Beehiiv, built by the ex-Morning Brew team, launched in 2021 with a different thesis: newsletters are the product, not just a marketing channel.

Both platforms serve creators. But they approach the job from opposite directions — ConvertKit from email marketing, Beehiiv from newsletter publishing. That difference shapes everything from pricing to feature priorities.

Here's how they actually compare in 2026.

## ConvertKit Overview

ConvertKit — rebranded to Kit in 2024 but still universally known by its original name — is an email marketing platform designed for creators who sell things. Courses, ebooks, memberships, paid newsletters, coaching — ConvertKit's feature set is built around turning subscribers into customers.

**What ConvertKit does well:**

- **Visual automations**: Build complex email sequences triggered by subscriber behavior, purchases, tag changes, and more. This is ConvertKit's standout feature — the automation builder is genuinely powerful and still ahead of most competitors.
- **Commerce built in**: Sell digital products and subscriptions directly through ConvertKit. No Shopify or Gumroad needed. You get checkout pages, delivery, and sales tracking.
- **Landing pages and forms**: Create opt-in pages without a separate website. Dozens of templates, decent customization.
- **Creator Network**: Cross-promote with other newsletter creators to grow your list organically.
- **Tagging system**: Segment subscribers with tags rather than separate lists. Flexible and clean for managing one audience with varied interests.

ConvertKit's weakness is the newsletter reading experience itself. Emails look fine in inboxes, but there's no public-facing web version with SEO, no built-in referral program, and limited analytics compared to newsletter-native platforms.

For a deeper look, see our full [ConvertKit review](/blog/convertkit-review-2026).

## Beehiiv Overview

Beehiiv treats your newsletter as a media property, not just an email list. Every newsletter gets a website. Every post is a web page that can rank in Google. The whole platform is engineered for growth and monetization at scale.

**What Beehiiv does well:**

- **Growth tools**: Built-in referral program (readers earn rewards for sharing), recommendation network, magic links for frictionless sign-up, and subscriber acquisition analytics.
- **Ad network**: Beehiiv places relevant ads in your newsletter and pays you. No need to find sponsors yourself — though you can run direct sponsorships too.
- **SEO-ready web hosting**: Every newsletter gets a website with customizable pages, blog posts, and proper SEO controls. This is something ConvertKit simply doesn't offer.
- **Analytics**: Detailed engagement data — open rates, click maps, subscriber growth curves, 3D analytics, and attribution tracking.
- **Paid subscriptions**: Charge readers directly. Beehiiv takes 0% (you only pay Stripe's processing fee).

Beehiiv's weakness is automation. If you need complex conditional sequences — "if subscriber bought product X, wait 3 days, then send sequence Y unless they clicked Z" — Beehiiv's automation tools are basic compared to ConvertKit's visual builder.

## Pricing Comparison

### ConvertKit Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Subscribers | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Newsletter (Free) | $0 | Up to 10,000 | Email broadcasts, landing pages, forms, Creator Network |
| Creator | $25/mo | Up to 1,000 | Automations, sequences, third-party integrations |
| Creator Pro | $50/mo | Up to 1,000 | Subscriber scoring, advanced reporting, priority support |

Prices scale with subscriber count. At 10,000 subscribers, Creator jumps to ~$100/mo and Creator Pro to ~$140/mo. The free plan is generous on subscriber count but locks you out of automations entirely.

### Beehiiv Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Subscribers | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Launch (Free) | $0 | Up to 2,500 | Unlimited sends, web hosting, basic analytics |
| Scale | $42/mo | Up to 10,000 | Ad network, paid subs, automations, custom domain |
| Max | $85/mo | Up to 100,000 | Advanced analytics, priority support, API access |
| Enterprise | Custom | 100,000+ | Dedicated support, custom integrations |

Beehiiv's free tier caps at 2,500 subscribers — far less than ConvertKit's 10,000. But Scale unlocks the full monetization stack, and at 10,000 subscribers Beehiiv ($42/mo) costs significantly less than ConvertKit Creator (~$100/mo).

**Pricing verdict**: ConvertKit is cheaper if you're under 10K subscribers and don't need paid features. Once you're monetizing and growing past a few thousand subscribers, Beehiiv's flat pricing tiers are more cost-effective.

## Feature Comparison

### Automation

**Winner: ConvertKit — decisively.**

ConvertKit's visual automation builder lets you create branching sequences based on subscriber actions, purchases, tags, page visits, and custom events. It's the core of the product and it shows.

Beehiiv added automations on Scale and above, but they're limited to basic drip sequences and simple triggers. If your business relies on sophisticated email funnels, ConvertKit is the clear choice.

### Design and Editor

**Winner: Beehiiv — for newsletters. ConvertKit — for traditional emails.**

Beehiiv's editor is purpose-built for newsletter content: rich text, embedded media, clean web rendering. Every email doubles as a blog post on your site.

ConvertKit's editor prioritizes deliverability — plain-text-first design that lands in primary inboxes. It looks simpler but performs well for sales-focused emails.

### Analytics

**Winner: Beehiiv.**

Beehiiv provides click maps, subscriber growth attribution, referral tracking, and detailed engagement metrics out of the box. ConvertKit's analytics cover the basics (opens, clicks, revenue) but lack the depth that data-driven newsletter operators want.

### Monetization

**Winner: Depends on your model.**

- **Selling products/courses**: ConvertKit. Built-in commerce, checkout pages, and automated product delivery.
- **Newsletter-native monetization**: Beehiiv. Ad network revenue, paid subscriptions, and a referral program that turns readers into growth engines.

Both take 0% of your revenue (Stripe fees still apply for paid subscriptions).

### Integrations

**Winner: ConvertKit.**

ConvertKit integrates with hundreds of tools — Zapier, WordPress, Shopify, Teachable, Webflow, and practically every course/membership platform. Beehiiv's integration library is growing but still smaller, relying more on Zapier and its API for connections ConvertKit handles natively.

## Who Should Choose Which

**Choose ConvertKit if:**
- You sell digital products, courses, or memberships and need automated sales funnels
- Complex email automations are central to your business
- You already have a website and need email marketing, not a publishing platform
- You want the largest integration ecosystem for creators

**Choose Beehiiv if:**
- Your newsletter IS the product (not just a marketing channel)
- You want built-in growth tools — referral programs, recommendations, SEO
- You plan to monetize through ads and paid subscriptions
- You want a website included without building one separately

## The Verdict

ConvertKit and Beehiiv aren't really competing for the same job. ConvertKit is an email marketing platform that happens to support newsletters. Beehiiv is a newsletter platform that happens to send emails.

If you're a creator who sells things and needs sophisticated automations to nurture leads into buyers, ConvertKit is the stronger tool. If you're building a newsletter as a standalone media business — growing an audience, selling ads, running paid subscriptions — Beehiiv is purpose-built for exactly that.

The simplest test: where does your revenue come from? If it's products you sell to your list, go with ConvertKit. If it's the newsletter itself, go with Beehiiv.
