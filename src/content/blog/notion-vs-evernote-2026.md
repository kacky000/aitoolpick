**Notion** and **Evernote** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Notion and Evernote represent two very different philosophies of note-taking. Evernote pioneered the digital note-taking category. Notion reimagined it as part of an all-in-one workspace. In 2026, the gap between them has only grown wider.

Here's an honest comparison to help you decide which one deserves your time.

## Quick Overview

**Notion** is an all-in-one workspace combining notes, databases, wikis, and project management. It's free for personal use and $10/user/month for teams.

**Evernote** is a dedicated note-taking app focused on capturing, organizing, and searching notes. After its Bending Spoons acquisition, pricing starts at $10.83/month for the Starter plan.

## Feature Comparison

### Note-Taking

Evernote's core note-taking experience is straightforward — create a note, type or paste content, organize into notebooks. The web clipper remains excellent for saving articles and web pages.

Notion's note-taking is block-based. Every paragraph, heading, image, and embed is a block you can rearrange. This gives you far more flexibility but requires a slight learning curve.

**Winner: Evernote** for simple note capture, **Notion** for structured and rich documents.

### Organization

Evernote uses notebooks and tags. It's a simple system that works well for people who think in categories. The search is powerful, including OCR for text within images.

Notion uses a hierarchy of pages and databases. You can create infinite nesting, linked databases, and relational structures. It's vastly more powerful but can feel overwhelming for simple use cases.

**Winner: Notion** — far more organizational flexibility.

### Databases and Structured Data

This isn't even a contest. Notion's database feature — with multiple views (table, board, calendar, gallery, timeline), filters, formulas, and relations — is its killer feature. You can build CRMs, project trackers, content calendars, and more.

Evernote has no database functionality whatsoever.

**Winner: Notion** overwhelmingly.

### Offline Access

Evernote offers full offline access on all platforms, including mobile. Your notes sync automatically when you're back online.

Notion has improved its offline support but it's still not as reliable. The mobile offline experience can be inconsistent, especially with large workspaces.

**Winner: Evernote** — offline is still a real strength.

### Web Clipper

Evernote's web clipper is legendary. It can save full pages, simplified articles, bookmarks, or screenshots. The clipping experience is polished and reliable.

Notion's web clipper exists but is more basic. It captures pages as Notion pages, which is useful but not as refined as Evernote's clipper.

**Winner: Evernote** — still the gold standard for web clipping.

## Who Should Choose Notion?

- Users who want **one tool** for notes, tasks, wikis, and databases
- **Teams** that need a shared workspace
- Anyone who values **customization and flexibility**
- People comfortable with a **slight learning curve**
- Users looking for a **free option** that doesn't feel crippled

## Who Should Choose Evernote?

- Users who need **rock-solid offline access**
- People who rely heavily on **web clipping**
- Users who want **simple, no-frills note-taking**
- Anyone with years of notes already in Evernote (migration takes effort)
- Users who value **OCR search** within images and PDFs

## The Verdict

In 2026, Notion is the stronger choice for most users. Its free plan alone beats Evernote's paid plans in functionality. The database features, team collaboration, and customization options make it a complete workspace, not just a note-taking app.

Evernote still has its strengths — offline access, web clipping, and OCR search are genuinely best-in-class. But its aggressive pricing and limited free tier make it hard to recommend unless you specifically need those features.
