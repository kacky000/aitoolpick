**Evernote** and **Onenote** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Evernote and Microsoft OneNote have been competing in the [note-taking](/category/note-taking/) market for over a decade. But 2025-2026 has been a significant period of change for both: Evernote went through ownership changes and pricing revamps, while OneNote deepened its integration with Microsoft Copilot AI.

If you are choosing between them in 2026, here is what actually matters.

## The Fundamental Question

Before comparing features, ask yourself one question: **Do you already pay for Microsoft 365?**

If yes, you already have OneNote included at no extra cost, and it has improved significantly in recent years. Evernote is hard to justify on top of an existing Microsoft 365 subscription unless you have specific requirements OneNote cannot meet.

If no, both tools compete more equally.

## Evernote: What Changed in 2024-2026

Evernote had a turbulent few years. The Bending Spoons acquisition in 2023 led to significant layoffs and product changes. In 2024-2026, the changes include:

- **Free plan heavily restricted** — The free plan now limits you to 1 notebook and 50 notes (down from unlimited). This is a significant downgrade from what longtime users remember.
- **Price increases** — Evernote Personal is now ~$14.99/month or ~$129.99/year. Evernote Professional is ~$17.99/month.
- **Web Clipper and search still strong** — These remain Evernote's best features.
- **AI features added** — Evernote AI assistant for search and note creation.

The price-to-value ratio for Evernote has declined. Users who remember the old Evernote sometimes find the current product a hard sell at current prices.

## OneNote: What Changed in 2024-2026

Microsoft has invested significantly in OneNote:

- **[Microsoft Copilot](/tools/copilot/) integration** — OneNote with Copilot (requires Microsoft 365 Copilot license) can summarize notebooks, draft content, and extract insights from notes
- **Improved mobile apps** — OneNote mobile improved substantially in 2024
- **Better formatting and structure** — More template support and improved canvas
- **Teams integration** — Notes created in Teams meetings sync to OneNote

OneNote's primary advantage remains its inclusion in Microsoft 365 at no additional cost.

## Where Evernote Is Still Better

**Web Clipper** — Evernote's Web Clipper is still the gold standard for saving web content. It handles article formatting, full-page captures, and selective clipping better than any competitor, including OneNote's clipper.

**Search quality** — Evernote's search, particularly for OCR within images and scanned documents, is slightly ahead of OneNote. If you heavily use scanned documents, Evernote's search is genuinely superior.

**Independent ecosystem** — If you use multiple operating systems (including Linux, where OneNote has limited native support), Evernote works more consistently across platforms.

## Where OneNote Is Better

**Price** — OneNote is free with a Microsoft account and unlimited. At no extra cost for Microsoft 365 subscribers, it is essentially impossible to beat on value.

**Infinite canvas** — OneNote's freeform layout lets you position notes, images, and drawings anywhere on a page. This is excellent for visual thinkers and stylus/tablet users.

**Handwriting and stylus** — On Windows tablets and Surface devices, OneNote's handwriting support is the best in the category.

**Collaboration** — OneNote notebooks can be shared via OneDrive with full co-editing. Integration with [Microsoft Teams](/tools/microsoft-teams/) means notes from meetings appear automatically in the right notebook.

**Copilot AI** — For organizations with Microsoft 365 Copilot, OneNote gets some of the most powerful AI note features available anywhere.

If you are paying $14.99/month for Evernote and not getting Microsoft 365, you could pay $6.99/month for Microsoft 365 and get OneNote plus Word, Excel, PowerPoint, OneDrive (1TB), and more.

## Who Should Use Evernote

- Users who depend heavily on the **Web Clipper** for research workflows
- People who have years of notes in Evernote and the migration cost is not worth it
- Those who need consistent cross-platform support including Linux
- Heavy users of **scanned document search** who rely on Evernote's OCR

## Who Should Use OneNote

- Anyone already paying for **Microsoft 365**
- **Tablet and stylus users** who want a freeform canvas
- Teams using **Microsoft 365 collaboration** (SharePoint, Teams)
- Anyone who wants unlimited note storage at **no extra cost**
- Students and educators with **Microsoft 365 Education** licenses

## Should You Consider Alternatives?

Both Evernote and OneNote are showing their age in some ways. Newer tools have emerged:

- **Notion** — More powerful for structured notes and project management. See our [Notion vs Evernote comparison](/blog/notion-vs-evernote-2026).
- **Obsidian** — Better for knowledge management and linking ideas. See our [Obsidian vs Evernote comparison](/blog/obsidian-vs-evernote-2026).

## The Bottom Line

In 2026, the calculus is straightforward for most users:

If you have Microsoft 365, use OneNote. It is free, improved, and well-integrated.

If you do not have Microsoft 365 and heavily use web clipping for research, Evernote Personal might be worth it — but evaluate whether Notion or [Obsidian](/tools/obsidian/) might serve you better at a lower cost.

If you are starting fresh with no existing notes in either system, OneNote or [Notion](/tools/notion/) are better starting points than Evernote given its recent pricing changes.
