Most practice management tools are built for therapists or medical clinics. Practice Better is different — it's designed from the ground up for **nutritionists, dietitians, functional-medicine practitioners, and health coaches**. This **Practice Better review** covers what it does well, where it falls short, and whether it's worth it in 2026.

## What is Practice Better?

Practice Better is an all-in-one platform for health and wellness practitioners. Beyond the usual scheduling and billing, it adds client programs, food and mood journaling, protocols, and supplement recommendations — the workflows that nutrition and coaching practices actually run on.

## Key features

- **Client management** — detailed client profiles, documents, and history
- **Scheduling & booking** — online booking with reminders
- **Telehealth** — built-in secure video sessions
- **Programs & protocols** — build multi-week client programs and reusable protocols
- **Food & lifestyle journaling** — clients log meals, mood, and habits between sessions
- **Billing & packages** — invoicing, packages, and recurring payments
- **Forms & questionnaires** — intake and assessment forms

That **$25 Starter** is one of the most affordable entry points in the category — cheaper than [SimplePractice](/pricing/simplepractice/) at $29 and well under [Jane App](/blog/jane-app-pricing-2026/) at $54. See the full [Practice Better pricing](/pricing/practice-better/) details.

## Pros

- **Built for nutrition & coaching** — journaling, protocols, and programs that generalist tools lack
- **Affordable entry tier** — $25 Starter undercuts most competitors
- **Built-in telehealth** — no separate video subscription
- **Client engagement tools** — journaling keeps clients accountable between sessions
- **Recurring billing & packages** — fits coaching business models

## Cons

- **Not for behavioral health** — therapists need psychotherapy-specific notes; choose [TherapyNotes](/blog/therapynotes-pricing-2026/) instead
- **Lighter on medical billing** — less suited to insurance-heavy clinics than Jane App or Tebra
- **Feature depth on higher tiers** — the most useful program features sit on Professional and Plus

## Who should use Practice Better?

Practice Better is the best pick for **nutritionists, dietitians, functional-medicine practitioners, and health coaches** who run programs and want clients engaged between sessions. If that's you, the journaling and protocol tools are genuinely differentiated.

If you're a **therapist**, look at TherapyNotes or [SimplePractice alternatives](/blog/best-simplepractice-alternatives-2026/). If you run a **multidisciplinary clinic**, compare [Practice Better vs SimplePractice](/compare/practice-better-vs-simplepractice/) and [Practice Better vs TherapyNotes](/compare/practice-better-vs-therapynotes/).

## Verdict

For its target audience, Practice Better is excellent — and at $25 to start, it's one of the best values in wellness practice management for 2026. The reasons to look elsewhere are clear: behavioral-health specialization (TherapyNotes) or heavy medical billing (Jane App, Tebra). For nutrition and coaching, it's a top recommendation.
