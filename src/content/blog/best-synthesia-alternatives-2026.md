Synthesia pioneered AI avatar videos for corporate training and marketing. But at $22/month for the Starter plan (with limited minutes), you might want alternatives that better fit your budget or use case. Here are the best Synthesia alternatives in 2026.

## 1. HeyGen

**Best for:** Marketing videos and personalized outreach

HeyGen matches Synthesia's avatar quality while adding video translation and personalized video features. Its API makes it easy to generate personalized sales videos at scale.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Creator $24/mo, Business $72/mo
- **Standout feature:** Video translation with lip-sync in 40+ languages
- **Limitation:** Higher starting price than Synthesia's Starter plan

## 2. D-ID

**Best for:** Quick talking-head videos from photos

D-ID turns any photo into a talking avatar video. It's simpler than Synthesia but faster for basic use cases like social media content and short explainer clips.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Lite $5.90/mo, Pro $29.99/mo, Advanced $99.99/mo
- **Standout feature:** Turn any face photo into a talking video in seconds
- **Limitation:** Less natural avatar movements than Synthesia

## 3. Colossyan

**Best for:** Corporate training and L&D teams

Colossyan is purpose-built for workplace learning. It includes built-in translation, SCORM export for LMS integration, and a scenario builder for interactive training content.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Starter $28/mo, Pro $96/mo
- **Standout feature:** SCORM export and interactive scenario builder
- **Limitation:** Less versatile for marketing use cases

## 4. Elai

**Best for:** Turning blog posts into videos

Elai converts text articles and URLs into AI avatar videos automatically. Paste a blog post URL, and it generates a scripted video with slides and narration — useful for content repurposing at scale.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Basic $23/mo, Advanced $100/mo
- **Standout feature:** URL-to-video conversion
- **Limitation:** Avatar quality slightly behind Synthesia

## 5. Pictory

**Best for:** Text-to-video with stock footage

Pictory takes a different approach: instead of AI avatars, it creates videos from text using stock footage, captions, and AI voiceover. Great for social media clips and blog-to-video repurposing.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Starter $19/mo, Professional $39/mo, Teams $99/mo
- **Standout feature:** Auto-summarization of long-form content into video
- **Limitation:** No AI avatars — stock footage only

## 6. Fliki

**Best for:** Budget-conscious creators

Fliki combines text-to-video, AI voiceover (in 75+ languages), and stock media in a clean interface. At $28/month for the Standard plan, it offers solid value for solo creators.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Standard $28/mo, Premium $88/mo
- **Standout feature:** 2,000+ lifelike AI voices in 75+ languages
- **Limitation:** Avatar options are more limited than Synthesia

## 7. Descript

**Best for:** Podcast and video editors who want AI features

Descript isn't a pure AI avatar tool — it's a full audio/video editor with AI capabilities including voice cloning, filler word removal, and transcript-based editing. If you need to edit video alongside AI generation, Descript is uniquely powerful.

- **Pricing:** Free tier; Hobbyist $24/mo, Creator $33/mo, Business $40/mo
- **Standout feature:** Edit video by editing text; AI voice cloning
- **Limitation:** Not designed for avatar-based corporate training
