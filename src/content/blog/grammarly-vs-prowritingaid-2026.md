**Grammarly** and **Prowritingaid** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Grammarly and ProWritingAid are two of the most popular AI writing assistants, but they serve slightly different needs. Grammarly is polished, fast, and works everywhere. ProWritingAid goes deeper on style analysis and offers more value for serious writers on a budget. Here's how they compare.

## Quick Verdict

**Choose Grammarly** if you want seamless integration across every app you use, fast real-time corrections, and a polished experience. **Choose ProWritingAid** if you're a serious writer who wants deep style analysis and can get more value from a lower-cost annual plan.

## Pricing

| Plan | Grammarly | ProWritingAid |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Free | Basic grammar | Basic grammar + style reports |
| Monthly | $30/month | $30/month |
| Annual | $12/month (billed yearly) | $10/month (billed yearly) |
| Lifetime | N/A | $399 one-time |

ProWritingAid's lifetime plan is its biggest differentiator. For writers who will use a writing assistant for years, $399 once versus $144/year (Grammarly) represents real savings over time.

## Grammar and Spelling Accuracy

Both tools catch basic grammar and spelling errors reliably. Grammarly edges ahead for everyday professional writing — its suggestions are faster and more contextually aware.

ProWritingAid is more thorough for long-form fiction and creative writing, where it catches subtle issues that Grammarly sometimes misses.

For business writing, emails, and professional documents, Grammarly is the more reliable tool. For novels and long creative pieces, ProWritingAid's deeper analysis adds value.

## Style Analysis

ProWritingAid's main strength is its 25+ writing reports. These analyze:
- Sentence length variation
- Overused words and phrases
- Passive voice frequency
- Readability scores
- Dialogue tags (for fiction writers)
- Sticky sentences (too many common words)
- Pacing analysis

Grammarly offers style suggestions in its Premium plan but focuses on clarity, conciseness, and tone. It doesn't provide the same depth of analytical reports.

For writers actively trying to improve their craft, ProWritingAid's reports are genuinely educational. Grammarly helps you write correctly; ProWritingAid helps you write better.

## Integrations

Grammarly integrates with essentially everything:
- Gmail, Outlook
- Google Docs
- Microsoft Word
- Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari (browser extension)
- Slack, Notion, LinkedIn
- Desktop app for macOS and Windows

ProWritingAid integrates well but with a narrower range:
- Microsoft Word and Outlook (native add-in)
- Google Docs
- Scrivener (popular with fiction writers)
- Chrome and Firefox extensions

If you need AI writing assistance everywhere — including Slack messages, browser forms, and social media — Grammarly is more comprehensive. ProWritingAid is better when your writing happens in a dedicated tool like Word or Scrivener.

## AI Writing Features

Both tools have added AI writing generation features. Grammarly's AI can rephrase sentences, adjust tone, and generate full paragraphs. ProWritingAid has added similar features.

Grammarly's AI feels more polished and integrated. ProWritingAid's AI writing features are improving but feel like a secondary feature rather than a core capability.

For AI-assisted writing, Grammarly is more capable.

## Who Should Choose What

**Choose Grammarly if you:**
- Write across many different apps daily
- Need fast, seamless real-time corrections
- Want the most comprehensive browser and app integration
- Write business content, emails, and social media

**Choose ProWritingAid if you:**
- Are a serious writer working on novels, screenplays, or long-form content
- Want deep style analysis and writing improvement reports
- Are on a tight budget and can use the annual or lifetime plan
- Write primarily in Microsoft Word or Scrivener

## Free Tier Comparison

Both free tiers are usable but limited. Grammarly Free catches grammar and spelling but locks style suggestions behind Premium. ProWritingAid Free includes access to its style reports but limits document length and report runs per month.

For serious writing, both tools require paid plans to show their full value.
