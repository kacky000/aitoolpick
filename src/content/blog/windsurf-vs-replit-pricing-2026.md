# Windsurf vs Replit Pricing 2026: $15 AI Editor vs $20 Full-Stack Builder

Windsurf and Replit both put AI at the center of coding, and their entry plans land $5 apart — Windsurf Pro at $15/month, Replit Core at $20/month. But they're built for different builders. Windsurf is an AI IDE: a code editor with the Cascade agent, priced on daily quotas, for developers working in a real codebase on their own machine. Replit is a full platform: write, run, and deploy a whole app in the browser, with Replit Agent handling multi-step builds. The $5 gap is small next to that split.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Windsurf | Replit |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Free** | $0, light daily quota | Starter — $0, tight daily limits |
| **Entry paid** | Pro — $15/mo, 50 premium/day | Core — $20/mo, $20 credits |
| **Team tier** | Teams — $30/user/mo | Pro — $100/mo flat (up to 15) |
| **Pricing model** | Daily quotas (reset every 24h) | Monthly usage credits |
| **What it is** | AI code editor (IDE) | Full-stack build + host in-browser |

## The Free Tiers

**Windsurf Free ($0)** gives a light daily quota of AI interactions plus unlimited Tab completions — designed to test the Cascade agent before paying. Usable for light sessions, but you'll hit the quota ceiling fast if you lean on Cascade.

**Replit Starter ($0)** lets you write code, run projects, and try Replit Agent, but with tight daily limits — enough to learn or test fit, not to ship.

Verdict on free: Windsurf to feel AI-native editing in your own codebase; Replit to run a whole app end-to-end in the browser.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($15) vs Core ($20)

**Windsurf Pro — $15/month.** Windsurf scrapped its old credit system for daily quotas — Pro gives 50 premium AI interactions per day that reset every 24 hours, plus unlimited Tab completion. At $15, Pro undercuts [Cursor's $20 Pro plan](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/) while offering the same tier of frontier models. Note: older docs may still reference the previous credit tiers ($20/$40/$200) — the quota system is what's in effect now. Windsurf assumes you're a developer working in a real project who wants AI alongside your editing.

**Replit Core — $20/month.** Includes $20/month in usage credits covering Agent steps, generation, and debugging. Replit's value is the complete loop: prompt, build front and back end, and deploy — all in the browser, no terminal. Burn through credits and you can still code manually, but Agent and premium AI pause until the next cycle or you buy more.

The real difference: Windsurf makes *you* faster in your own codebase; Replit *builds and hosts* the whole app for you. The pricing models reflect it — Windsurf's daily quota suits steady everyday coding; Replit's monthly credits suit bursts of building. Developer in an editor? Windsurf's $15. Want a hosted product without infrastructure? Replit's $20.

## Where They Scale

**Windsurf Teams ($30/user/month)** keeps per-seat pricing for development teams and is competitive with GitHub Copilot Business. **Replit Pro ($100/month flat)** covers up to 15 builders under one subscription — scaling by *headcount* with a single fee rather than per seat. For a 3-person team, three Windsurf seats (~$90) roughly match Replit Pro's flat $100; at 10+ builders, Replit's flat rate clearly wins on cost.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Developer who wants a cheap AI editor in your own codebase?** Windsurf Pro ($15/mo).
- **Want a full-stack app built and hosted without a terminal?** Replit Core ($20/mo).
- **Development team working per-seat?** Windsurf Teams ($30/user/mo).
- **Team of up to 15 builders on one platform?** Replit Pro ($100/mo flat).
- **Daily, steady coding?** Windsurf's quota model fits better than burning credits.
- **Honest take?** Windsurf to write code faster; Replit to build and ship an app whole.

## The Bottom Line

The $5 gap is noise; the workflow is everything. Windsurf Pro ($15) is an AI IDE with a generous daily quota — ideal for developers coding steadily in their own project who want frontier models at a lower price than Cursor. Replit Core ($20) builds, runs, and hosts a full-stack app from a prompt — ideal when you want a shipped product without managing infrastructure. Pick Windsurf if you live in an editor; pick Replit if you want the whole app handed to you, running.
