## The Best Free Tools

### Google Analytics 4 — Best Overall Free Tool

GA4 is the foundation. Every website should have it running regardless of what else you use.

**Strengths:**
- Free with no data limits
- Deep integration with Google Ads
- Event-based tracking catches everything
- BigQuery export on free tier

**Weaknesses:**
- 14-month data retention default
- Complex setup compared to UA
- No session recordings

**Best for:** Any website as the primary analytics layer.

### Microsoft Clarity — Best Free Session Recorder

Clarity is the hidden gem of 2026. Completely free, no traffic limits, and includes heatmaps plus session recordings that GA4 can't provide.

**Strengths:**
- 100% free, no session limits
- Heatmaps + session recordings
- Rage click and dead click detection
- GA4 integration built-in

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited funnel analysis
- No A/B testing
- Data retention: 90 days

**Best for:** Any site wanting session replay without paying for Hotjar. [Full Clarity review →](/blog/microsoft-clarity-review-2026/)

## Best for Product Teams

### Mixpanel — Best for Event-Based Product Analytics

Mixpanel tracks user behavior through custom events, making it ideal for SaaS products and apps.

**Pricing:**
- Free: 20M events/month, 90-day history
- Growth: $20/month (unlimited events, 12-month history)
- Enterprise: Custom

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class funnel and retention analysis
- User-level tracking with segmentation
- Real-time dashboards
- Cohort analysis

**Weaknesses:**
- Requires developer setup for proper event tracking
- Can get expensive at scale

**Best for:** SaaS products and mobile apps needing funnel analysis. [Mixpanel vs. Heap →](/blog/heap-vs-mixpanel-2026/)

### Heap — Best for Auto-Capture Analytics

Heap captures every user interaction automatically, then lets you define events retroactively.

**Pricing:**
- Free tier: Limited
- Growth: ~$3,600/year
- Pro/Premier: Custom

**Strengths:**
- No pre-planning required — every click is captured
- Retroactive event definition
- Strong integrations (Salesforce, Marketo)

**Weaknesses:**
- Expensive for small teams
- Auto-capture can generate data noise
- Steeper learning curve

**Best for:** Product teams who want to analyze past behavior without re-instrumenting.

## Best for UX Research

### FullStory — Best Enterprise Session Replay

FullStory captures full session replays with DX Data for enterprise-grade UX analysis.

**Pricing:**
- Business: $300/month (estimated)
- Advanced/Enterprise: Custom

**Strengths:**
- Pixel-perfect session replays
- DX (Digital Experience) Data layer
- Strong privacy controls (data masking)
- Integrates with Amplitude, Segment

**Weaknesses:**
- Premium pricing
- Overkill for small sites

**Best for:** Enterprise teams running CRO programs at scale. [Full FullStory review →](/blog/fullstory-review-2026/)

### Hotjar — Best for SMBs Needing Heatmaps + Surveys

Hotjar combines heatmaps, session recordings, and user surveys in one tool.

**Pricing:**
- Basic: Free (35 daily sessions)
- Plus: $32/month
- Business: $80/month

**Best for:** Marketing teams wanting to combine quantitative and qualitative data without enterprise pricing.

## Best Privacy-First Options

### Plausible — Best Simple Privacy-Compliant Analytics

Plausible is a lightweight, GDPR-compliant alternative to GA4 with a clean single-page dashboard.

**Pricing:** Starts at $9/month (10k pageviews)

**Strengths:**
- No cookie banners required
- Open-source option (self-host free)
- Extremely simple UI

**Weaknesses:**
- No session recordings
- Limited segmentation

### PostHog — Best Open-Source Option

PostHog combines product analytics, session replay, feature flags, and A/B testing in one open-source platform.

**Pricing:** Free (self-host), or cloud plans starting at $0 with usage-based pricing

**Best for:** Technical teams who want full control over their data.

## How to Choose

| Situation | Recommended Stack |
|-----------|------------------|
| Just starting out | GA4 + Microsoft Clarity (both free) |
| SaaS/app product team | Mixpanel or PostHog |
| E-commerce CRO | Hotjar + GA4 |
| Enterprise UX research | FullStory or Heap |
| Privacy-first / EU audience | Plausible + Clarity |

## The Free Stack That Covers Everything

If budget is zero: **GA4 + Microsoft Clarity** covers 90% of analytics needs for most websites — traffic data, session replays, heatmaps, funnels, and conversion tracking. Start here before paying for anything else.
