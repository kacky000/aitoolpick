Reclaim.ai is one of the few AI scheduling tools that gives you a genuinely functional free plan. No 14-day trial that vanishes — an actual free tier you can use indefinitely. But the paid plans unlock features that matter, and the pricing has shifted since the early days.

Here's exactly what each plan costs and what you get in 2026.

## Reclaim.ai Pricing at a Glance

| Plan | Annual (per month) | Monthly | Best For |
|------|--------------------|---------|----------|
| Lite | Free | Free | Solo users, basic time blocking |
| Starter | $10/mo | ~$14/mo | Power users, full integrations |
| Business | $15/mo | ~$21/mo | Teams up to 100 |
| Enterprise | $22/mo | Custom | Large organizations, SSO/SCIM |

Annual billing saves roughly 29% compared to monthly. All paid plans include a free trial period so you can test before committing.

## Plan-by-Plan Breakdown

### Lite (Free) — $0/month

The free Lite plan covers more ground than most competitors' entry-level paid tiers. That's not marketing spin — it's genuinely useful for a single user who wants basic AI scheduling.

**What you get:**
- Smart time blocking for tasks and habits
- Calendar sync (Google Calendar)
- Up to 3 integrations
- Basic scheduling links
- Buffer time between meetings
- Travel time holds

**What you don't get:**
- Unlimited task and habit slots (capped on Lite)
- Full integration access (Todoist, Asana, Jira, ClickUp, Linear)
- Team scheduling features
- Priority support
- Advanced analytics

The Lite plan works well if you're a solo user who mainly needs time blocking and basic calendar defense. You can block focus time, create habits like lunch breaks or exercise, and let Reclaim automatically reschedule them when meetings conflict.

The biggest limitation is the integration cap. If your tasks live in Todoist or your projects are in Asana, you'll hit the wall fast on Lite.

### Starter — $10/month (annual)

This is where Reclaim gets serious. Starter removes the integration cap and unlocks the full power of task syncing.

**Everything in Lite, plus:**
- Unlimited tasks and habits
- Full integrations: Todoist, Asana, Jira, ClickUp, Linear, Slack
- Unlimited calendar syncs
- Smart 1:1 meeting scheduling
- Team management features
- Up to 10 seats

For most individual users, Starter is the sweet spot. The ability to pull tasks from your project management tool and have Reclaim automatically schedule them into available calendar slots is the core value proposition — and it's locked behind this tier.

At $10/month on annual billing, it's priced competitively. [Motion](/tools/motion/) starts at $12.73/month (annual) for its Pro AI plan, and [Sunsama](/tools/sunsama/) charges $20/month annual with no free tier at all. For a comparison of how Sunsama and Reclaim stack up on value, check our [Sunsama Pricing 2026](/blog/sunsama-pricing-2026/) breakdown.

### Business — $15/month (annual)

Business is built for teams that want organization-wide calendar optimization.

**Everything in Starter, plus:**
- Unlimited Smart Meetings
- 12-week advance scheduling
- CRM event routing
- Onboarding workshops
- Up to 100 seats
- Team analytics and reporting

The jump from $10 to $15 per seat makes sense if you're managing a team where meeting coordination is a constant headache. The 12-week advance scheduling and Smart Meetings features help teams automatically find optimal times across multiple calendars.

If you're a solo user or small team under 10, you probably don't need Business. Starter covers the essentials.

### Enterprise — $22/month (annual)

Enterprise adds the compliance and admin features larger organizations require.

**Everything in Business, plus:**
- SCIM provisioning
- SSO (Single Sign-On)
- Domain capture
- Organization-wide analytics
- Dedicated support
- 100+ seats

Standard enterprise fare. If your IT department requires SSO and SCIM, this is where you land. Otherwise, Business covers it.

## Is the Free Plan Actually Enough?

For a surprising number of users, yes. The Lite plan handles the core use case — blocking time for tasks, protecting focus hours, and auto-rescheduling when your calendar shifts. If you work solo and don't rely heavily on external task managers, you can run on Lite indefinitely.

You'll want to upgrade when:
- **You use Todoist, Asana, Jira, or ClickUp** — the integration sync is a game-changer, and it's Starter-only
- **You need Smart 1:1s** — automated scheduling for recurring one-on-ones with direct reports
- **You hit the habit/task cap** — Lite limits how many concurrent time blocks you can maintain

## Annual vs Monthly: Which Billing Makes Sense?

The 29% savings on annual billing is significant — roughly $3-4/month per seat depending on the plan. If you've used the free tier for a month and know Reclaim fits your workflow, annual is the obvious choice.

If you're unsure, start with Lite. Use it for 2-3 weeks. If you find yourself wanting the integrations or hitting limits, switch to Starter monthly for a month, then convert to annual once you're committed.

## The Verdict

Reclaim.ai's pricing is straightforward and competitive. The free Lite plan is genuinely useful — not a crippled demo designed to frustrate you into paying. Starter at $10/month unlocks the features most individuals need. Business at $15/month makes sense for teams.

If you're choosing between Reclaim and pricier alternatives like Motion or Sunsama, the question comes down to how much AI control you want. Reclaim assists your scheduling. Motion owns it. [Sunsama](/tools/sunsama/) replaces it with a daily planning ritual. Pick the philosophy that matches how you work, not just the price tag.
