**Clickup** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

ClickUp is known for offering one of the most feature-packed free plans in the productivity space. But when you need more, which paid plan makes sense? In this guide, we break down every ClickUp pricing tier so you can make an informed decision.

## ClickUp Pricing Overview (2026)

| Plan | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free Forever | $0 | Individuals and small teams getting started |
| Unlimited | $7/user/mo | Small to mid-size teams that need full features |
| Business | $12/user/mo | Mid-size teams with advanced needs |
| Enterprise | Custom | Large organizations with security requirements |

All paid plans are billed annually. Monthly billing is available but costs more (Unlimited jumps to $10/user/mo).

## Free Forever Plan

ClickUp's free plan is genuinely impressive and includes:

- **Unlimited tasks** and members
- **100MB storage** per file
- **Docs, whiteboards, and dashboards** (limited)
- **In-app video recording**
- **24/7 support**

The catch? You're limited to 100 automations per month, 5 spaces, and a single dashboard. For a solo freelancer or a very small team exploring the platform, it's more than enough to get started.

## Unlimited Plan ($7/user/month)

This is where ClickUp becomes a serious contender. The Unlimited plan adds:

- **Unlimited storage**
- **Unlimited integrations** and dashboards
- **Gantt charts and timeline views**
- **Resource management**
- **Agile reporting**
- **1,000 automations per month**

For most small to mid-size teams, this plan hits the sweet spot. At $7/user/month, it undercuts both Asana ($10.99) and Monday.com ($9) while offering comparable or superior features.

## Business Plan ($12/user/month)

The Business plan is designed for teams that need advanced project management:

- **10,000 automations per month**
- **Advanced time tracking**
- **Timelines and workload management**
- **Custom exporting**
- **Advanced public sharing**
- **Google SSO**

If you're running multiple teams or departments and need granular control over permissions and workflows, the Business plan provides the tools you need.

## Enterprise Plan (Custom Pricing)

For large organizations, the Enterprise plan includes:

- **Unlimited automations**
- **Advanced permissions**
- **HIPAA compliance**
- **Custom role creation**
- **Dedicated success manager**
- **API access with higher rate limits**
- **MSA and SAML SSO**

You'll need to contact ClickUp's sales team for pricing, which typically starts around $20-25/user/month based on team size and requirements.

## ClickUp AI Add-On

ClickUp Brain (their AI feature) is available as an add-on for $5/user/month on any paid plan. It includes:

- AI writing assistance in Docs
- Task summarization
- Project updates generated automatically
- Natural language search across your workspace

Whether the AI add-on is worth it depends on how heavily you use ClickUp Docs. If your team writes a lot of documentation, the time savings can be significant.

## Which Plan Should You Choose?

Here's our recommendation based on team size:

- **Solo/1-3 people**: Start with the Free plan. Upgrade to Unlimited only when you hit storage or automation limits.
- **4-15 people**: Go with Unlimited ($7/user/mo). It offers the best value for growing teams.
- **15-50 people**: Consider Business ($12/user/mo) for better permissions, advanced time tracking, and more automations.
- **50+ people**: Talk to Enterprise sales for custom pricing and dedicated support.

## Is ClickUp Worth the Price?

Yes — ClickUp offers arguably the best value in the project management space in 2026. The free plan alone beats many competitors' paid plans, and the Unlimited tier at $7/user/month is hard to beat for what you get.

The only reason to look elsewhere is if you find ClickUp's feature density overwhelming, in which case simpler tools like [Trello](/blog/trello-vs-asana-for-small-teams/) or [Todoist](/blog/todoist-review-2026/) might be a better fit.
