[Calendly](/tools/calendly/) and [Cal.com](/tools/cal-com/) solve the same problem — let people book time with you — but they come from opposite directions. Calendly is the polished market leader with deep enterprise integrations. Cal.com is the open-source challenger that gives away what Calendly charges for.

The right choice depends on whether you value a refined, managed experience or flexibility and cost control.

The pricing story is straightforward. Cal.com's free plan includes unlimited event types and unlimited bookings — features that require Calendly's $10/month Standard plan. If you're a freelancer or solo consultant who needs more than one booking link, Cal.com saves you $120/year before you think about anything else.

For teams, the gap narrows. Calendly Teams at $16/user/month vs Cal.com Teams at $15/user/month is negligible. The real question at team scale is whether Calendly's CRM integrations and routing forms justify the slight premium.

## Where Calendly Wins

### Polished UI and Onboarding

Calendly's booking pages, admin dashboard, and setup flow are noticeably more refined. The booking experience feels premium — clean animations, logical steps, and minimal friction. For client-facing use (sales demos, consulting bookings), the polish matters because your booking page is often the first touchpoint.

Cal.com's interface is functional and improving, but it still has the feel of a developer-oriented tool. Some configuration screens require more clicks than necessary, and the booking page design options are less sophisticated out of the box.

### CRM Integrations

Calendly's Teams plan includes native Salesforce and HubSpot integrations that auto-log meetings, sync contact properties, and trigger CRM workflows. When a prospect books a demo, the meeting appears in Salesforce with the prospect's routing form answers attached. For revenue teams running pipeline operations through a CRM, this is table-stakes infrastructure.

Cal.com connects to CRMs through Zapier or custom webhooks, which works but requires setup and maintenance. If your sales team lives in Salesforce, Calendly's native integration eliminates a meaningful operational burden.

### Ecosystem Breadth

Calendly integrates natively with Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, GoTo Meeting, Marketo, Intercom, and hundreds of other tools. The integration library is broader and more battle-tested than Cal.com's, which covers the essentials but has gaps in niche verticals.

If you've already read our [Acuity Scheduling vs Calendly](/blog/acuity-scheduling-vs-calendly-2026) comparison, you know Calendly's ecosystem advantage extends across the entire scheduling category.

## Where Cal.com Wins

### The Free Plan

This is Cal.com's strongest card. Unlimited event types, unlimited bookings, calendar connections, custom branding, and workflow automations — all free. Calendly gates most of these behind its $10/month Standard plan.

For individuals and small businesses, Cal.com's free plan eliminates the scheduling tool line item entirely.

### Self-Hosting

If data privacy, compliance, or vendor independence matters to your organization, Cal.com is the only serious option. You can run the full platform on your own infrastructure — AWS, DigitalOcean, a bare-metal server, wherever. Your scheduling data never touches a third-party server.

This matters for healthcare organizations (HIPAA), European companies (GDPR with full data residency), and any team that needs to guarantee data sovereignty. Calendly's Enterprise plan offers SOC 2 compliance and SSO, but you can't self-host.

### Full Customization

Cal.com is MIT-licensed open source. You can modify the booking flow, add custom fields, build integrations against internal systems, change the UI, or embed scheduling directly into your product. Calendly offers configuration; Cal.com offers modification.

For SaaS companies that want to embed scheduling as a feature in their own product (like a marketplace connecting service providers with customers), Cal.com's codebase is a foundation. Calendly's embed is a widget.

### Cal.ai Phone Scheduling

Cal.com's AI agent (Cal.ai) handles scheduling over the phone at $0.29 per minute. A caller speaks naturally — "I need a 30-minute consultation next Tuesday afternoon" — and the AI checks availability, books the meeting, and sends confirmations. No human involvement.

Calendly has no equivalent. This is a genuinely novel capability that makes sense for businesses where clients prefer calling over clicking a link: medical offices, law firms, real estate agencies.

## Decision Framework

**Choose Calendly if:**
- You're on a sales team integrated with Salesforce or HubSpot
- You need routing forms to qualify and distribute inbound leads
- You value a polished, enterprise-grade booking experience
- Your organization needs SSO, SCIM, and dedicated account management
- You want everything managed — no infrastructure to maintain

**Choose Cal.com if:**
- You need more than one event type and don't want to pay for it
- Data privacy or compliance requires self-hosting
- You're a developer or technical team that wants to customize the scheduling flow
- You want to embed scheduling as a native feature in your own product
- Budget matters — Cal.com's free tier covers what Calendly charges $10-16/month for

## The Bottom Line

Calendly is the safer, more polished choice for teams embedded in enterprise workflows. Its CRM integrations, routing forms, and admin controls are purpose-built for revenue operations.

Cal.com is the smarter financial choice for individuals, small teams, and technically capable organizations. Its free plan outperforms Calendly's paid Standard tier, and self-hosting gives you control that no hosted solution can match.

If you're a solo consultant deciding between the two, start with Cal.com's free plan. You get unlimited event types, and you can always migrate to Calendly later if you outgrow it. If you're a sales team of 10+ running Salesforce, start with Calendly Teams — the CRM integration alone saves enough operational overhead to justify the cost.
