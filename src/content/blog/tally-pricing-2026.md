[Tally](/tools/tally/) has the most generous free plan in the form builder space. Unlimited forms. Unlimited submissions. No response caps. No sneaky limits that force you to upgrade after a week of real use.

The real pricing question with Tally isn't "can I afford it?" — it's "do I even need to pay at all?" Here's how each tier breaks down so you can figure out where you actually land.

## Tally Pricing Overview

| Plan | Price | Forms | Submissions | Best For |
|------|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited | Unlimited | Most users, honestly |
| Pro | $29/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited | Branding control, teams, advanced features |
| Business | $89/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited | Data retention policies, email verification, compliance |

All plans include unlimited forms and unlimited submissions. That's not a typo.

## Free Plan — What You Actually Get

Tally's free plan covers about 99% of what the average user needs. That claim comes directly from Tally themselves, and after testing it, they're not exaggerating by much.

**Included in the free plan:**
- Unlimited forms
- Unlimited submissions
- Conditional logic (show/hide fields based on answers)
- Calculator fields
- Payment collection (Stripe integration)
- File uploads (up to 10MB per file)
- Integrations: Zapier, Airtable, Google Sheets, Notion, Slack, webhooks
- Custom thank-you pages
- Email notifications
- Form analytics
- Embeddable forms
- Hidden fields and URL parameters

That feature list would be a paid plan at most competitors. [Typeform](/tools/typeform/) charges $25/month just to remove branding and get 100 responses. [Fillout](/tools/fillout/) is more generous than Typeform but still gates features behind paid tiers. Tally gives you all of this for nothing.

**Free plan limitations:**
- Tally branding on forms ("Made with Tally")
- File uploads capped at 10MB per file
- No custom domains
- No custom CSS
- No partial submission tracking
- No team workspaces
- Standard support (not priority)

The branding is small and unobtrusive — a "Made with Tally" badge at the bottom of your form. For internal forms, surveys, or early-stage projects, most people won't care.

## Pro Plan — $29/month

Pro is where you go when the free plan's limitations actually matter to your business.

**Everything in Free, plus:**
- Remove Tally branding
- Custom domains (forms on yourdomain.com)
- Custom CSS for full design control
- Partial submissions (see incomplete responses)
- Increased file upload limits
- Team collaboration (shared workspaces)
- Google Analytics integration
- Custom email notifications (personalized sender, templates)
- Workspace organization features
- Priority support

The two features that typically drive upgrades are **branding removal** and **custom domains**. If you're embedding forms on a client-facing website or running a business where professionalism matters, the "Made with Tally" badge becomes a problem.

Partial submissions are underrated — seeing where people drop off your form helps you fix friction points. If you're running lead generation or application forms, this data is valuable.

### Is Pro Worth $29/Month?

That depends entirely on your context.

**Pro makes sense if:**
- You're client-facing and need clean, branded forms
- You want forms on your own domain
- You need partial submission data for conversion optimization
- You collaborate with a team on form creation
- You need custom CSS to match your brand exactly

**Pro is overkill if:**
- You're using forms internally (team surveys, feedback, requests)
- You're a solo creator with simple form needs
- You don't care about the Tally branding badge
- You're early-stage and every dollar matters

At $29/month, Pro competes with Typeform's Basic plan ($25/month for 100 responses) and Fillout's paid tier. The difference is that Tally's paid plan still has unlimited submissions — you're paying for branding and collaboration features, not response volume.

## Business Plan — $89/month

Business adds compliance and enterprise-oriented features.

**Everything in Pro, plus:**
- Data retention controls
- Email verification on form submissions
- Enhanced security features
- Advanced team management
- Priority onboarding

This tier exists for companies with data handling requirements. If you need to auto-delete submissions after a set period (GDPR compliance), or you need to verify email addresses before accepting form responses, Business is where those features live.

Most small businesses and creators will never need Business. It's for organizations where form data intersects with compliance requirements.

## How Tally Compares on Price

| Tool | Free Tier | Free Submissions | Paid Starting Price | Paid Submissions |
|------|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Tally | Unlimited forms | Unlimited | $29/mo (Pro) | Unlimited |
| [Typeform](/tools/typeform/) | 10 responses/mo | 10 | $25/mo (Basic) | 100/mo |
| [Fillout](/tools/fillout/) | 1,000/mo | 1,000 | $19/mo (Starter) | 5,000/mo |

The comparison is stark. Typeform gives you 10 free submissions per month. Tally gives you unlimited. Jotform caps you at 100 free submissions and charges $34/month for 1,000. Tally's free plan outperforms most competitors' mid-tier paid plans on volume alone.

The only tool that matches Tally on free submissions is Google Forms — but Google Forms lacks conditional logic, payment integration, custom design, and the overall UX polish that makes Tally pleasant to build with.

## Tally's Business Model: How Do They Make Money?

A fair question when the free plan is this generous. Tally is bootstrapped (founded in Belgium, no VC funding) and monetizes through Pro and Business subscriptions. Their bet is that a percentage of free users — particularly agencies, SaaS companies, and growing teams — will eventually need branding removal, custom domains, or team features.

This bootstrapped approach means they're not burning through venture capital to subsidize free users. The model is sustainable, which matters if you're building your workflow around a tool. For a full look at Tally's features beyond pricing, see our [Tally Review 2026](/blog/tally-review-2026/).

## Who Should Pay for Tally?

**Stay on Free if:** You're an individual, small team, or early-stage company using forms for surveys, feedback, signups, or internal processes. The free plan genuinely covers this.

**Upgrade to Pro if:** You're running client-facing forms where branding matters, you need custom domains, or you want partial submission tracking to optimize conversion rates. The $29/month is reasonable for the value at this stage.

**Upgrade to Business if:** Your organization has compliance requirements around data retention or needs email verification on submissions. You probably already know if you need this.

## The Verdict

Tally's pricing strategy is refreshingly simple: give away almost everything for free, and charge for polish and professionalism. The free plan isn't a lead-in to aggressive upselling — it's a genuinely complete product.

For most users, the free plan is the plan. Start there. If you grow into needing branded forms or team collaboration, Pro at $29/month is fair. But don't pay upfront "just in case." Tally doesn't punish you for starting free.
