**[ClickUp](/tools/clickup/)** and **[Notion](/tools/notion/)** both pitch themselves as the "one app to replace them all," but they lean opposite directions. ClickUp is a project-management platform with tasks, docs, and dashboards built around getting work done. Notion is a flexible workspace built around docs and databases. On price they're close — the decision is about which model fits your team.

The headline: **ClickUp is cheaper at the entry paid tier** — Unlimited at $7/user undercuts Notion Plus at $10/user, and ClickUp Business ($12) sits below Notion Business ($15).

## The Real Difference: PM Platform vs Flexible Workspace

**ClickUp** is built for managing projects and teams: tasks with statuses and dependencies, multiple views (list, board, Gantt, calendar), goals, time tracking, and dashboards. It's opinionated toward *execution* — tracking who's doing what by when.

**Notion** is built around documents and databases. You can absolutely manage projects in Notion, but you assemble the system yourself from flexible building blocks. Its strength is knowledge — wikis, docs, notes — with project tracking layered on top.

The honest framing: ClickUp is the stronger dedicated *project manager*; Notion is the stronger *knowledge workspace*. Teams that live in task boards and timelines lean ClickUp; teams that live in docs and wikis lean Notion.

## Cost Scenario: 10-Person Team

- **ClickUp Unlimited:** 10 × $7 = **$70/month**
- **Notion Plus:** 10 × $10 = **$100/month**
- **ClickUp Business:** 10 × $12 = **$120/month**
- **Notion Business:** 10 × $15 = **$150/month**

At the entry paid tier, ClickUp is **30% cheaper** ($70 vs $100) and includes deeper project-management features (Gantt, time tracking, automations) out of the box. Notion's higher price buys flexibility and a superior docs/wiki experience, not more PM muscle.

## Free Plan Comparison

- **ClickUp Free Forever:** Unlimited members and tasks with usage caps on advanced features (limited storage, limited uses of certain views/automations). Surprisingly full-featured for small teams.
- **Notion Free:** Unlimited pages/blocks for individuals with collaboration limits — best for personal use or very small teams.

ClickUp's free plan is the stronger choice for a *team* that wants real project management at $0; Notion's free plan shines for *individual* knowledge work.

## Verdict

**Choose ClickUp** if you want a dedicated project-management platform — it's cheaper at the entry tier ($7 vs $10) and packs more PM features (timelines, time tracking, dashboards) by default.

**Choose Notion** if your team's center of gravity is docs, wikis, and databases, and you'll use that flexibility to justify the higher per-seat price.

Yes at the entry tier — ClickUp Unlimited is $7/user vs Notion Plus at $10/user, and ClickUp Business ($12) is cheaper than Notion Business ($15).

**What's the difference between ClickUp and Notion?**
ClickUp is a project-management platform focused on task execution; Notion is a flexible workspace focused on docs and databases. They optimize for different work.
