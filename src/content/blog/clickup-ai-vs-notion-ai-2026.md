**Clickup Ai** and **Notion Ai** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Both [ClickUp](/tools/clickup) and [Notion](/tools/notion) have gone all-in on built-in AI. ClickUp calls theirs **ClickUp Brain**; Notion calls theirs **Notion AI**. Both cost extra on top of your base subscription, and both promise to help you work faster.

Which one actually delivers? This comparison breaks it down.

## Quick Summary

| Feature | ClickUp Brain | Notion AI |
|---------|--------------|----------|
| Price | $9/user/month (add-on) | $10/user/month (add-on) |
| Writing assistance | Yes | Yes |
| Summarization | Yes | Yes |
| Task management AI | Strong | Limited |
| Q&A over your workspace | Yes (project context) | Yes (Notion pages) |
| AI automations | Yes | Limited |
| Image generation | No | No |
| Best for | PM-heavy teams | Knowledge and writing teams |

## What ClickUp Brain Does

ClickUp Brain is deeply integrated with ClickUp's task and project management features. That's its distinguishing characteristic.

You can ask Brain questions like:
- "What tasks are overdue in my Marketing project?"
- "Summarize all comments on this task from the past week"
- "Generate a status update for this project based on what's been completed"

It also handles standard AI writing tasks: drafting documents, writing task descriptions, summarizing long comment threads, and generating templates.

**The standout capability** is how Brain connects across your workspace. It can pull in context from tasks, docs, goals, and comments to answer questions about your actual work — not just generic prompts.

**The limitation** is price. At $9/user/month, a 10-person team pays $90/month in AI fees on top of the base plan. That adds up fast.

## What Notion AI Does

Notion AI is a writing-first AI assistant. It lives inside Notion's document and database structure, which means it excels at:

- Drafting, editing, and summarizing documents
- Auto-filling database properties (generating summaries, tags, or status labels)
- Answering questions about your Notion workspace content
- Translating and reformatting content

The Q&A feature (ask questions about your Notion pages) is legitimately useful for teams with large wikis. You can ask "What is our refund policy?" and it pulls the answer from your documentation.

**The standout capability** is the combination of writing assistance and database auto-fill. Marketing teams building content calendars and product teams maintaining specs get real leverage here.

**The limitation** is that it's weak on project management AI. You can't ask "what tasks are overdue" the way you can in ClickUp.

## Head-to-Head: Task Management AI

**ClickUp Brain wins here.** If your primary use of AI is to get a handle on your project work — status updates, overdue tasks, next actions — ClickUp Brain is substantially better than Notion AI. It has the project context built in.

Notion AI doesn't have native task management in the same way. You can build task databases in Notion, but the AI assistance is less targeted.

## Head-to-Head: Writing and Document AI

**This is a draw, leaning toward Notion AI.** Both tools write, summarize, and edit competently. Notion AI feels slightly more natural for long-form writing because Notion's editor is designed [around](/tools/around/) documents. ClickUp Brain writes well too, but the interface is more task-centric.

For teams who write a lot (content teams, legal, policy, product specs), Notion AI feels more at home.

## Head-to-Head: Knowledge Base Q&A

**Notion AI has an advantage** for teams with large documentation libraries. If you've spent years building a Notion wiki, the ability to query it in natural language is genuinely useful. ClickUp Brain can answer questions about project context, but it's less useful as a knowledge base search tool.

## Pricing Reality

Both AI tiers cost roughly $9-10/user/month as an add-on. To get the full value:

- **ClickUp**: Free plan + Brain add-on ($9) = $9/user/month total
- **Notion**: Plus plan ($10) + AI add-on ($10) = $20/user/month total

ClickUp's AI is actually the better deal if you're starting from scratch. Notion AI requires a paid Notion plan to be meaningful.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose ClickUp Brain if:**
- You manage projects and want AI that understands task context
- You need automated status updates and project summaries
- You want AI integrated into your workflow tools, not just your documents

**Choose Notion AI if:**
- Your team relies on Notion for documentation and knowledge management
- Writing, editing, and summarizing documents is your primary use case
- You want AI auto-fill for databases and templates

**Consider standalone AI tools if:**
- You want the most capable AI for general use — [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt) or [Claude](/tools/claude) are more powerful for complex reasoning and writing

## The Bottom Line

ClickUp Brain is the better AI for project-focused teams. Notion AI is the better AI for knowledge-focused teams. Neither replaces a standalone AI like [ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/) for complex tasks — but both add meaningful automation within their respective platforms.

The most important question: do you already use ClickUp or Notion? If yes, the AI add-on for your current tool is probably worth trying before switching platforms.
