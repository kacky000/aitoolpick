**HubSpot alternatives** are worth considering when the free CRM doesn't cover your needs and Professional pricing ($800-3,600/month) exceeds your budget. Whether you want a cheaper marketing platform, a simpler CRM, or a more powerful enterprise tool, there are strong alternatives for every situation.

## Why Look for HubSpot Alternatives?

HubSpot's free CRM is excellent. The problem is the cliff between Starter and Professional plans — $15/month to $800/month is a 53× jump. Most teams need Professional features (automation, A/B testing, advanced reporting) but can't justify the price.

Common reasons to switch:
- Marketing automation at a lower price point
- Sales-only CRM without paying for marketing features
- Simpler tool that doesn't require a dedicated admin
- More customization than HubSpot allows

## 1. Salesforce — Best for Enterprise CRM

**Best for:** Large enterprises with complex sales processes

Salesforce is the market leader in enterprise CRM. It's more powerful and more customizable than HubSpot, but it requires significant implementation investment and technical expertise.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Essentials | $25/user/month |
| Professional | $75/user/month |
| Enterprise | $150/user/month |
| Unlimited | $300/user/month |

**Why choose Salesforce over HubSpot:** If you have a complex sales process, need deep customization, or already have Salesforce integrations across your company, Salesforce is the better long-term platform.

**Why stick with HubSpot:** HubSpot is easier to implement, better for inbound marketing, and more integrated across marketing/sales/service without heavy customization.

## 2. Pipedrive — Best for Sales Teams on a Budget

**Best for:** Sales-focused teams wanting a clean, simple CRM

Pipedrive is purpose-built for sales pipeline management. It's easier to use than HubSpot, significantly cheaper, and doesn't bundle unnecessary marketing features.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Essential | $14/user/month |
| Advanced | $34/user/month |
| Professional | $49/user/month |
| Enterprise | $99/user/month |

**Why choose Pipedrive:** If your primary need is sales pipeline management — not marketing automation — Pipedrive is cleaner, cheaper, and faster to adopt. The visual pipeline is one of the best in the industry.

**Missing vs HubSpot:** No native landing pages, limited [email marketing](/category/email-marketing/), no built-in SEO or social tools.

## 3. ActiveCampaign — Best for Email Marketing + CRM

**Best for:** Teams prioritizing advanced email marketing with CRM integration

ActiveCampaign combines powerful email marketing automation with a capable CRM. The automation builder is arguably more flexible than HubSpot's and starts at a much lower price.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Starter | $15/month (1K contacts) |
| Plus | $49/month (1K contacts) |
| Professional | $79/month (1K contacts) |
| Enterprise | Custom |

**Why choose ActiveCampaign:** If email marketing automation is your primary need, ActiveCampaign delivers comparable or better functionality at a fraction of HubSpot Professional's price. The CRM is solid for sales teams who don't need HubSpot's full suite.

**Missing vs HubSpot:** Weaker built-in landing pages, no native ads management, less polished reporting.

## 4. Zoho CRM — Best Value All-in-One

**Best for:** Budget-conscious teams wanting comprehensive CRM features

Zoho CRM is part of the broader Zoho suite, which includes email marketing, helpdesk, [project management](/category/project-management/), and more — similar to HubSpot's hub model but significantly cheaper.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | Up to 3 users |
| Standard | $14/user/month |
| Professional | $23/user/month |
| Enterprise | $40/user/month |
| Ultimate | $52/user/month |

**Why choose Zoho:** Zoho CRM Plus bundles CRM, email marketing, social, helpdesk, and analytics at ~$57/user/month — significantly cheaper than equivalent HubSpot Professional plans. If value per dollar matters, Zoho is hard to beat.

**Missing vs HubSpot:** Less polished UX, steeper learning curve, smaller partner ecosystem, weaker content and education resources.

## 5. Monday CRM — Best for Visual Pipeline Management

**Best for:** Teams already using [Monday.com](/tools/monday/) for project management

Monday CRM builds CRM functionality into [Monday.com](/tools/monday/)'s familiar board-based interface. If your team already loves Monday.com's visual style, this is the most natural CRM upgrade.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Basic | $12/user/month |
| Standard | $17/user/month |
| Pro | $28/user/month |
| Enterprise | Custom |

**Why choose Monday CRM:** The visual, customizable boards [make](/tools/make/) it easy to see your pipeline at a glance. If you use Monday.com for projects, having CRM in the same tool reduces context switching.

**Missing vs HubSpot:** Limited email marketing, no landing pages, weaker automation than HubSpot Professional.

## 6. Mailchimp — Best for Email-Only Marketing

**Best for:** Small businesses that primarily need email marketing

If HubSpot feels like overkill and you mainly need email marketing with basic CRM capabilities, Mailchimp is the most well-known option with a solid free tier.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | Up to 500 contacts, 1K sends/month |
| Essentials | $13/month (500 contacts) |
| Standard | $20/month (500 contacts) |
| Premium | $350/month (10K contacts) |

**Why choose Mailchimp:** If you need email marketing and a basic contact database, Mailchimp is simpler and cheaper. The free tier allows real campaigns without HubSpot's branding restrictions.

**Missing vs HubSpot:** No advanced CRM, limited automation depth, no sales pipeline, no built-in SEO or social tools.

## 7. Notion — Best for Teams Avoiding Traditional CRM

**Best for:** Small teams that prefer flexible, document-based workflows

Notion isn't a CRM, but many small teams use it as one by building custom databases for contacts, deals, and pipeline tracking. Combined with [Notion's free tier](/compare/notion-vs-hubspot), it costs nothing and adapts to exactly how your team works.

**Why choose Notion:** Zero cost, ultimate flexibility, works well for teams with non-standard sales processes. Combine with a tool like [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) for automation.

**Missing vs HubSpot:** No email tracking, no automation without integrations, no reporting, not built for sales workflows.

## Bottom Line: Which HubSpot Alternative Should You Choose?

- **Sales team, no marketing needs →** Pipedrive
- **Email marketing is the priority →** ActiveCampaign
- **Want everything HubSpot offers, at lower cost →** Zoho CRM
- **Enterprise sales with complex requirements →** Salesforce
- **Already use Monday.com →** Monday CRM
- **Small business, just need email →** Mailchimp
