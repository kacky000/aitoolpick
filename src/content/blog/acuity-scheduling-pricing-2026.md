**Acuity Scheduling pricing** in 2026 runs from $16 to $49 per month, with no free plan — only a 7-day free trial. All three tiers include core scheduling features; the differences are in the number of staff calendars and advanced customization options.

## Acuity Scheduling Plans at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly Price | Annual Price | Staff Calendars | Client Limit |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Emerging | $20/mo | $16/mo | 1 | Unlimited |
| Growing | $34/mo | $27/mo | 6 | Unlimited |
| Powerhouse | $61/mo | $49/mo | 36 | Unlimited |

All prices above: annual billing price first, monthly billing in parentheses. Annual billing saves approximately 20%.

## What Every Plan Includes

All Acuity plans include:
- Unlimited appointment types and booking pages
- Client self-scheduling with availability rules
- Automated email and SMS reminders
- Payment collection via Stripe, Square, and PayPal
- Package and gift certificate selling
- Intake forms (with conditional logic)
- Automatic time zone detection and conversion
- iCal sync and Google Calendar integration
- Basic reporting

## Emerging Plan: $16/month (annual)

**Best for:** Solo practitioners — coaches, therapists, trainers, photographers.

Emerging gives you one staff calendar: your own. You can create unlimited appointment types, accept payments, and collect intake forms. This is a complete scheduling solution for a solo service business.

What Emerging cannot do:
- No subscription billing (recurring memberships)
- No multiple staff calendars
- No advanced customization (CSS, custom confirmation pages)
- No API access
- No removal of Acuity branding from confirmation emails (partially)

At $16/month (annual), Emerging is the right tier for most solo service providers testing Acuity. The intake forms and package selling alone differentiate it from Calendly free or even Calendly Standard.

## Growing Plan: $27/month (annual)

**Best for:** Small practices with multiple practitioners (health clinics, coaching teams, studio instructors).

Growing adds:
- **6 staff calendars**: Each practitioner gets their own calendar, availability settings, and booking page
- **Subscription/membership selling**: Recurring billing for monthly memberships (e.g., "Unlimited yoga classes — $89/month")
- **Resource scheduling**: Book physical resources (rooms, equipment) alongside appointments
- **Advanced customization**: Custom confirmation pages and intake form logic

The 6-staff-calendar limit at $27/month is notable value for small practices. Compare: Calendly Teams at $16/user means 6 users = $96/month. Acuity Growing at $27/month for 6 staff is 72% cheaper.

## Powerhouse Plan: $49/month (annual)

**Best for:** Larger practices, studios, or multi-location service businesses.

Powerhouse adds:
- **36 staff calendars**: Suitable for mid-size organizations
- **API access**: Build custom integrations or internal tools
- **Advanced permissions**: Staff can manage their own schedules without seeing others'
- **Custom CSS**: Fully brand your booking page

For most users, Growing is sufficient. Powerhouse makes sense for businesses needing 7+ staff calendars or requiring API access for custom integrations.

## No Free Plan — The Biggest Drawback

Acuity's lack of a free plan is its most significant competitive weakness. Every alternative offers some free tier:

| Tool | Free Plan |
|------|----------|
| **Calendly** | ✅ 1 event type, unlimited bookings |
| **Cal.com** | ✅ Unlimited event types |
| **Google Calendar** | ✅ Appointment slots (basic) |
| **Acuity Scheduling** | ❌ 7-day trial only |

If budget is a constraint, Cal.com or Calendly free let you start without commitment. Acuity requires paying from day 8 of your relationship with the tool.

## Annual vs Monthly Billing

| Plan | Annual | Monthly | You Save (Annual) |
|------|--------|---------|------------------|
| Emerging | $16/mo | $20/mo | $48/year |
| Growing | $27/mo | $34/mo | $84/year |
| Powerhouse | $49/mo | $61/mo | $144/year |

Annual billing is the default recommendation if you're committed to the platform. The 20% savings is meaningful across all three tiers.

## Is Acuity Scheduling Worth It?

**Worth it if:**
- You run a coaching, therapy, wellness, or similar service business
- Client intake at booking time is important to your workflow
- You sell session packages or monthly memberships
- You have 2-6 practitioners who need shared scheduling (Growing tier's value is strong)

**Not worth it if:**
- You just need to share meeting booking links (use Calendly or Cal.com)
- Budget is tight and you can't justify $16/month without a free trial period
- You need deep CRM integration for a sales team (use Calendly Teams)
- You need a scheduling tool primarily for team meetings (use Calendly)

## Bottom Line

Acuity Scheduling's pricing is competitive for its target audience: service businesses where intake, packages, and client management matter. The Growing tier at $27/month for 6 staff calendars is exceptional value for small practices compared to per-user alternatives.

The absence of a free plan is a real barrier for new businesses testing the market. If you can commit to the 7-day trial and your use case matches Acuity's strengths, the pricing is fair.
