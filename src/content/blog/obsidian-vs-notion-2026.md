# Obsidian vs Notion 2026: Which Note App Should You Use?

Obsidian and Notion are the two most-debated note apps of 2026, but they are built on opposite philosophies. Obsidian is a local-first, file-based knowledge tool. Notion is a cloud-based all-in-one workspace. Choosing between them is less about features and more about how you think and what you value.

## The Core Difference

**Obsidian** stores your notes as plain Markdown files on your own device. You own the files, you control sync, and a graph view links your ideas together.

**Notion** stores everything in the cloud as flexible "blocks," combining notes, databases, wikis, and project boards in one workspace your whole team can share.

This single distinction — local files vs cloud blocks — drives almost every other tradeoff.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Obsidian | Notion |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Storage** | Local Markdown files | Cloud blocks |
| **Offline** | Full, native | Limited |
| **Linking** | Backlinks + graph view | Mentions + relations |
| **Databases** | Via plugins | Native, powerful |
| **Collaboration** | Limited | Excellent |
| **Customization** | Plugins + themes | Templates + blocks |
| **Privacy** | Data stays local | Data in cloud |

## Where Obsidian Wins

- **Ownership and privacy.** Your notes are plain files on your machine. No vendor can lock you out.
- **Knowledge graphs.** Backlinks and the graph view make it ideal for connected thinking, research, and personal knowledge management.
- **Speed and offline.** Local files mean instant, fully offline access.
- **Extensibility.** A huge plugin ecosystem lets you mold it to almost any workflow.

## Where Notion Wins

- **All-in-one workspace.** Notes, databases, project boards, and wikis live together.
- **Collaboration.** Real-time editing and sharing make it a team favorite.
- **Databases out of the box.** Powerful, no-plugin relational databases.
- **Polish.** Templates and a clean interface get teams productive fast.

## Pricing

Obsidian is free for personal use, with paid add-ons for official sync and publishing. Notion has a free personal tier and paid plans that scale with team features. For solo users, both can be free; for teams, Notion's per-seat pricing is the bigger consideration.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Obsidian if** you value privacy and data ownership, think in connected notes, work mostly solo, and want everything offline. It is the better tool for researchers, writers, and personal knowledge management.

**Choose Notion if** you need a shared team workspace, want databases and project management alongside notes, and prefer cloud convenience over local control.

Many people even use both: Obsidian for private thinking, Notion for shared work.

## Final Verdict

There is no universal winner — only the right fit. **Obsidian** is the choice for private, connected, offline thinking. **Notion** is the choice for collaborative, all-in-one workspaces. Decide which value matters more — ownership or collaboration — and the answer becomes clear.
