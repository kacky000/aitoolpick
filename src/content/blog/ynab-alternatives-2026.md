YNAB (You Need A Budget) is one of the most effective personal finance tools ever made. The zero-based budgeting methodology has helped countless people pay off debt and build savings. But at $99 per year (or $14.99/month), it's not cheap — and it's not for everyone.

If you want the budgeting benefits without the YNAB price tag, these five alternatives cover every budget style and price point. Some are free. Some are cheaper. All of them are worth considering.

---

## Why People Look for YNAB Alternatives

YNAB's subscription price has increased over the years and is now $99/year or $14.99/month. For some users, that's a worthwhile investment — the app genuinely changes financial behavior. But common reasons people look elsewhere include:

- The price feels high for "just a budgeting app"
- YNAB's learning curve is steeper than expected
- You want automatic bank syncing without extra fees
- You prefer a different budgeting methodology (e.g., envelope vs. zero-based)
- You want a one-time purchase instead of a subscription

For full context on what you'd be giving up, read our [complete YNAB review for 2026](/blog/ynab-review-2026).

## 1. Monarch Money — Best Overall YNAB Alternative

**Price:** $14.99/month or $99.99/year
**Free trial:** 7 days
**Best for:** Couples and households, comprehensive financial overview

Monarch Money has emerged as the top YNAB alternative for people who want a full-featured personal finance platform. It tracks spending, investments, net worth, and budgets in a single clean interface. The collaboration features make it especially good for couples managing shared finances.

**Why it beats YNAB for some users:**
- Simpler onboarding — less philosophy, more practical tracking
- Investment and net worth tracking built in
- Better bank sync reliability and coverage
- Shared accounts work seamlessly for couples

**Where YNAB wins:** YNAB's zero-based budgeting methodology is more prescriptive, which some users need to actually change their financial behavior. Monarch is more of a tracker with budgeting features — YNAB is a budgeting tool with tracking features.

**Verdict:** If you want a YNAB replacement that doesn't require re-learning how to budget, Monarch Money is the first app to try.

---

## 2. Goodbudget — Best Free Envelope Budgeting App

**Price:** Free (10 envelopes) / Plus at $8/month or $70/year
**Best for:** Envelope budgeting fans, couples, debt payoff

Goodbudget is a modern take on the classic envelope budgeting system — the same method that inspired YNAB's approach. You allocate money to digital envelopes before spending, and the app tracks whether you're staying within each budget category.

**Why it stands out:**
- Free tier is genuinely usable (10 envelopes covers most people's categories)
- Cross-device sync lets couples track spending together
- No bank account connection required — manual entry keeps you engaged with your money
- Available on iOS, Android, and web

**Limitations:** No investment tracking. Manual transaction entry is a feature for some, a dealbreaker for others. The free tier caps you at 10 envelopes, which can feel restrictive.

**Verdict:** If you love envelope budgeting but can't justify YNAB's price, Goodbudget is the natural alternative — and the free tier is a real option, not just a trial.

## 3. EveryDollar — Best for Dave Ramsey Followers

**Price:** Free (manual entry) / Premium at $17.99/month or $79.99/year
**Best for:** Dave Ramsey fans, Baby Steps method, debt payoff focus

EveryDollar was created by Ramsey Solutions and uses the same zero-based budgeting approach as YNAB. If you're familiar with Dave Ramsey's Baby Steps program, EveryDollar integrates directly with that framework.

**Why it stands out:**
- Zero-based budgeting similar to YNAB
- Free tier available (manual entry only)
- Motivational integration with Ramsey's financial philosophy
- Clean, simple interface that's easier to learn than YNAB

**Limitations:** The free version requires manual transaction entry — bank sync costs extra. The Premium plan at $17.99/month is actually more expensive than YNAB. The philosophy is heavily tied to the Ramsey ecosystem, which isn't for everyone.

**Verdict:** A solid choice if you're following Dave Ramsey's program. For everyone else, the free tier is worth exploring, but the paid plan is hard to justify over YNAB.

## 4. Copilot — Best for iPhone and Mac Users

**Price:** $13.99/month or $95.88/year
**Free trial:** 2 months
**Best for:** Apple ecosystem users, data visualization lovers

Copilot is an Apple-exclusive budgeting app (iOS and Mac only) known for its beautiful design and smart automation. It uses machine learning to categorize transactions and spot patterns in your spending without manual effort.

**Why it stands out:**
- Best-in-class UI among budgeting apps
- Smart transaction categorization that actually learns your habits
- Subscription tracking and bill management built in
- Excellent charts and spending insights

**Limitations:** Apple-only — no Android or web app. Slightly cheaper than YNAB annually but not dramatically so. Less emphasis on proactive budgeting methodology compared to YNAB.

**Verdict:** If you're fully in the Apple ecosystem and want a beautiful, low-effort financial tracker, Copilot is hard to beat. It's not a full YNAB replacement for zero-based budgeting purists, but it's arguably better for passive spending awareness.

---

## 5. Actual Budget — Best Free and Open-Source Option

**Price:** Completely free (self-hosted) / $4/month for hosted cloud sync
**Best for:** Privacy-focused users, developers, YNAB power users

Actual Budget started as a paid app and went fully open-source in 2022. It's now one of the most powerful free budgeting tools available — with a methodology very similar to YNAB (zero-based, envelope-style, forward-looking). You can self-host it for complete privacy and zero cost.

**Why it stands out:**
- Completely free if you self-host
- YNAB-like methodology without the subscription cost
- Full data ownership — your financial data stays on your device
- Growing community of plugins and integrations
- The cloud-hosted version is only $4/month

**Limitations:** Self-hosting requires some technical comfort (Docker or local server setup). The UI is functional but not as polished as commercial alternatives. Smaller feature set than YNAB's current version.

**Verdict:** For technically comfortable users who are frustrated paying $99/year for YNAB, Actual Budget is the most direct replacement available. The methodology is nearly identical and the price is right.
