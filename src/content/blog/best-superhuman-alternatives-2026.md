Superhuman is fast and polished, but **$30/month with no free tier** sends a lot of people looking for alternatives. The good news: in 2026 you can get most of Superhuman's AI-inbox magic — drafting, summaries, keyboard speed — for a fraction of the price, and sometimes free. Here are the best **Superhuman alternatives**.

## Why Look for an Alternative?

Superhuman's main drawbacks are simple:

- **Price** — $30/mo (Starter) to $40/mo (Business), no freemium
- **No free plan** to test long-term
- **Overkill** if your email volume is modest

If any of those apply, one of these alternatives will serve you better.

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Free plan | Starting price | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shortwave | Yes | $8.50/mo | Gmail value pick |
| Spark | Yes | ~$8/mo | Teams & multi-account |
| Canary Mail | Yes | ~$3/mo | Privacy-first AI |
| Gmail + Gemini | Yes | Bundled | Existing Google users |
| Outlook + Copilot | No | Add-on | Microsoft 365 teams |
| Notion Mail | Yes | Bundled | Notion-centric workflows |

## 1. Shortwave — Best Overall Alternative

Shortwave is the closest like-for-like swap. It's Gmail-native, keyboard-driven, and built around an AI executive assistant that summarizes, drafts, and searches your inbox. With a **free plan** and paid tiers from **$8.50/month**, it delivers most of Superhuman's value at a quarter of the cost.

See the direct matchup in [Superhuman vs Shortwave](/blog/superhuman-vs-shortwave-2026/).

## 2. Spark — Best for Teams

If your reason for wanting Superhuman is productivity but you also collaborate on email, Spark is a better fit. Shared inboxes, delegation, and AI compose make it strong for teams, and it supports Gmail, Outlook, iCloud, and IMAP. Free plan plus paid tiers around **$8/month**.

## 3. Canary Mail — Best for Privacy

Canary Mail brings AI writing and summaries with a privacy-first stance and encryption options. It works across Gmail, Outlook, and IMAP, and is one of the cheapest options here. Ideal if you want AI help without routing everything through big-tech models.

## 4. Gmail + Gemini — Best Free Option

You may already have a capable AI inbox. Gmail's built-in Gemini handles "Help me write," thread summaries, and smart search — no new app, no extra cost for Workspace users. It's not as fast as Superhuman, but it's free.

## 5. Outlook + Copilot — Best for Microsoft Users

If you're on Microsoft 365, Copilot in Outlook drafts replies, summarizes threads, and coaches tone inside the client you already use. Pricing is a per-user add-on, so weigh it against your existing Microsoft bill.

## 6. Notion Mail — Best for Notion Users

For teams whose work lives in Notion, Notion Mail keeps email in the same ecosystem with AI labeling and drafting. The integration is the draw rather than raw speed.

## How to Choose

- **Want the closest swap?** → Shortwave
- **Work on a team?** → Spark
- **Privacy matters most?** → Canary Mail
- **Already pay for Google or Microsoft?** → Gemini or Copilot

## Bottom Line

You don't need to pay $30/month for a great AI inbox. **Shortwave** is the best overall Superhuman alternative for Gmail users, **Spark** wins for teams, and **Gmail + Gemini** is the best free option. Try the free tiers before committing.
