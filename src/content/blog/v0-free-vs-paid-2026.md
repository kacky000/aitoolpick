Vercel's v0 turns a prompt into a working React/Next.js UI in seconds, and its free plan is genuinely useful for trying that out. But the included credits run dry quickly once you build for real. This guide breaks down exactly what you get on v0 free vs paid in 2026, so you know whether the $20/month Premium plan is worth it.

## Free vs Paid at a Glance

| Feature | Free | Premium ($20/mo) |
|---------|------|------------------|
| Price | $0 | $20/month |
| Monthly credits | $5 included | $20 included |
| Projects | Up to 200 | Up to 200 |
| Credit rollover | 65 days | 65 days |
| Best for | Trying v0, small UI | Regular UI building |

## What the Free Plan Gives You

The free tier exists to let you experience v0's prompt-to-UI workflow without paying:

- **$5/month in included credits**
- Up to **200 projects**
- Generate React/Next.js components from text or images
- Push straight to Vercel deployment

The catch is the **$5 credit ceiling**. Each generation and iteration consumes credits, and a few rounds of refining a complex component can exhaust the monthly allowance fast. Free is perfect for kicking the tires; it's not enough to build a product UI.

## What You Unlock With Premium ($20/month)

Premium quadruples your credit budget and is the plan most regular users actually need:

- **$20/month in included credits** — 4× the free allowance
- Same 200-project ceiling and 65-day credit rollover
- Headroom to iterate on real components without constantly hitting limits

The standout is simply not running out mid-build. On free you ration prompts; on Premium you iterate freely. For local-IDE workflows instead of v0's hosted generation, compare [v0 vs Cursor](/blog/v0-vs-cursor-2026/).

## The Credit Reality

v0 bills on usage, so the sticker price is really a credit floor:

| Usage profile | Likely monthly cost |
|---------------|--------------------|
| Trying v0, occasional UI | $0 (Free, $5 credits) |
| Regular component building | $20 (Premium) |
| Heavy daily UI generation | $30+ (Team or extra credits) |
| Multi-seat team | $30/user (Team) |

Credits roll over for 65 days, which softens the cliff, but heavy users move to **Team ($30/user)** or **Business ($100/user)**. This usage-based pattern mirrors the wider category — see how it plays out in [Replit free vs paid](/blog/replit-free-vs-paid-2026/).

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Just exploring prompt-to-UI?** Free with $5 credits is a real test drive.
- **Building UI regularly?** Premium at $20 removes the constant credit anxiety.
- **Generating UI daily or in a team?** Team at $30/user.
- **Comparing against a full IDE?** See [Replit vs v0](/blog/replit-vs-v0-2026/).

## FAQ

**Is v0 free?** Yes — the free plan includes $5/month in credits and up to 200 projects, with no credit card required to start.

**Do v0 credits roll over?** Yes, for 65 days on both free and paid plans.

**Why do I run out of credits so fast on free?** Each generation and refinement consumes credits; $5 covers only a handful of complex iterations.

**Is Premium worth it over free?** If you build UI more than occasionally, yes — $20 gives 4× the credits and lets you iterate without rationing.
