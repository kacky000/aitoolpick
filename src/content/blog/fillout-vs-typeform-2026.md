# Fillout vs Typeform 2026: The Budget-Friendly Challenger Takes On the King

Typeform has been the go-to for beautiful, conversational forms for nearly a decade. But [Fillout](/tools/fillout/) has been quietly gaining ground by offering most of the same features at a fraction of the price — with a free tier that actually lets you do real work.

If you're choosing between these two in 2026, here's what you need to know.

## Quick Comparison

| | Fillout | Typeform |
|---|---------|----------|
| Free Plan | Yes — 1,000 responses/mo | Yes — limited |
| Starter Price | $15/mo | $25/mo (Basic) |
| Forms Limit (Free) | Unlimited | Limited |
| Signature Feature | Generous free tier + integrations | Conversational one-at-a-time UI |
| Best For | Budget-conscious teams, startups | Brand-focused companies wanting polished UX |
| Templates | Growing library | Large, established library |

## Pricing: Fillout Wins on Value

This is where Fillout makes its strongest argument.

### Fillout Pricing

| Plan | Price | Responses/mo | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | 1,000 | Unlimited forms, unlimited seats |
| Starter | $15/mo | 2,000 | All question types, custom themes |
| Pro | $40/mo | 5,000 | Remove branding, priority support |
| Business | $75/mo | Higher limits | Advanced features, compliance |

### Typeform Pricing

| Plan | Price | Responses/mo | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | 10 | 10 questions per form |
| Basic | $25/mo | 100 | Unlimited questions |
| Plus | $50/mo | 1,000 | Remove branding, custom subdomain |
| Business | $83/mo | 10,000 | Priority support, advanced analytics |

The gap is stark. Fillout's free plan gives you 1,000 responses per month. Typeform's free plan gives you 10. That's not a typo — ten responses.

To get 1,000 responses on Typeform, you need the $50/month Plus plan. On Fillout, that's free. Even if you compare paid tiers, Fillout's $15/month Starter plan gets you more responses than Typeform's $25/month Basic plan.

For small teams and startups, this price difference adds up fast.

## The Typeform Experience: What You're Paying For

Typeform isn't overpriced without reason. Its conversational form experience — showing one question at a time with smooth transitions — genuinely converts better for certain use cases. Completion rates for well-designed Typeform surveys are often 10-20% higher than traditional multi-question forms.

The aesthetic quality is hard to match. Typeform forms feel polished, branded, and professional out of the box. If you're using forms for customer-facing surveys, lead capture, or anything where presentation matters for brand perception, Typeform still holds an edge.

Typeform also has a mature ecosystem: a large template library, robust analytics, and deep integrations with marketing tools like HubSpot, Salesforce, and Mailchimp.

## Where Fillout Catches Up (and Pulls Ahead)

Fillout isn't just a cheaper Typeform clone. It has genuine advantages:

### Unlimited Forms on Every Plan

Even the free plan lets you create as many forms as you want. Typeform restricts form count on lower tiers.

### Unlimited Seats

Fillout doesn't charge per team member. Every plan includes unlimited seats. Typeform charges extra for additional users starting at the Plus plan.

### Database and Integration Power

Fillout has native integrations with Airtable, Notion, Google Sheets, and Supabase — not just as destinations for responses, but as data sources for pre-filling and dynamic forms. You can build forms that pull data from your existing databases, which is powerful for internal tools and workflows.

### Scheduling and Payments

Fillout includes built-in scheduling (like Calendly-style booking) and payment collection (Stripe) on paid plans. Typeform offers payment collection too, but Fillout bundles more functionality without requiring separate tools.

### Conditional Logic

Both tools support conditional logic (showing/hiding questions based on previous answers). Fillout's implementation is slightly more flexible, allowing conditions based on external data sources, not just form responses.

## Integrations

### Fillout Integrations
Airtable, Notion, Google Sheets, Supabase, Slack, Zapier, Make, HubSpot, Mailchimp, Salesforce, Stripe, webhooks, and more. The Airtable and Notion integrations are particularly deep — you can use these databases as both data sources and response destinations.

### Typeform Integrations
HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, Google Sheets, Slack, Zapier, Make, Stripe, PayPal, and a large app marketplace. Typeform's integration ecosystem is more mature and has more pre-built options, especially for marketing automation.

**Winner**: Typeform has more integrations overall, but Fillout's database integrations (Airtable, Notion, Supabase) are deeper and more useful for operations-focused teams.

## What About Other Alternatives?

The form builder space is crowded. [Tally](/tools/tally/) is another free-tier-focused option worth considering — it's even simpler than Fillout with a generous free plan. [Jotform](/tools/jotform/) is the enterprise-grade option with 10,000+ templates and advanced features like PDF reports and app building, though it's pricier.

If your primary need is basic data collection without design requirements, Google Forms is still free and perfectly functional.

## When to Pick Fillout

- You need more than 10 responses per month without paying (so, basically everyone)
- Budget matters and you want the best value per dollar
- You use Airtable, Notion, or Supabase and want deep integration
- You need unlimited team members on forms
- You want both traditional and conversational form styles
- You're building internal tools or workflows that pull from existing databases

## When to Pick Typeform

- Brand presentation is critical and you want the most polished form experience
- You've tested both and your audience completes Typeform at higher rates
- You need the deepest marketing automation integrations (HubSpot, Salesforce workflows)
- You rely on Typeform's extensive template library
- Your budget comfortably covers $50+/month and the premium UX is worth it

## The Verdict

For most teams in 2026, [Fillout](/tools/fillout/) is the better deal. The free plan alone — 1,000 responses, unlimited forms, unlimited seats — outperforms what you get paying $25/month on [Typeform](/tools/typeform/). And the paid plans maintain that value gap at every tier.

Typeform still wins on polish. If your forms are a core brand touchpoint and you've measured that the conversational UX actually improves your conversion rates, the premium is justifiable.

But if you're honest about it, most forms don't need to be beautiful. They need to work, collect data reliably, and not cost a fortune. That's where Fillout quietly wins.
