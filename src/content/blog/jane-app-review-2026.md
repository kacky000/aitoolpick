Jane App has built a loyal following among allied-health and wellness clinics by doing one thing well: putting booking, charting, billing, and insurance claims into a single, genuinely usable platform. But no tool is right for everyone. This **Jane App review** covers what it does well, where it falls short, and who should choose it in 2026.

## What is Jane App?

Jane is practice management software for health and wellness clinics — physiotherapy, chiropractic, massage therapy, naturopathy, dietetics, and multidisciplinary teams. It handles online booking, charting, billing, insurance claims, and telehealth in one unified system, and it's known for a clean interface that staff actually enjoy using.

## Key features

- **Online booking** — a customizable booking site patients can use 24/7, with practitioner, room, and discipline filters.
- **Scheduling** — multi-practitioner, multi-room calendars with waitlists and group appointments.
- **Charting** — customizable templates per discipline, fast SOAP notes, and a strong mobile charting experience.
- **Billing & insurance** — invoicing, credit card processing, and insurance claim submission on the Insurance plan.
- **Telehealth** — built-in video visits without a separate Zoom subscription.
- **Reminders** — automated email reminders, with SMS available as a paid add-on.

## Jane App pricing

Jane runs three plans: **Base at $54/month**, **Insurance at $79/month**, and **Corporate at $99/month** — priced per location rather than per practitioner on the lower tiers. That model makes it unusually cost-effective for teams. For the full breakdown, see our [Jane App pricing guide](/blog/jane-app-pricing-2026/).

## Pros

- **Excellent UX** — consistently praised as one of the most intuitive platforms in healthcare software.
- **Per-location pricing** — scales affordably as you add practitioners.
- **Built-in telehealth** — no bolt-on video tool needed.
- **Strong support** — responsive, well-reviewed customer service.
- **Multidisciplinary by design** — handles mixed-discipline clinics that therapy-only tools can't.

## Cons

- **SMS costs extra** — text reminders are billed separately and add up for busy clinics.
- **Not behavioral-health specialized** — therapists wanting psychotherapy-specific note templates and workflows may prefer a dedicated tool.
- **Insurance plan required for claims** — US/Canada billing pushes most clinics to the $79 tier.

## Who should use Jane App?

Jane is the strongest pick for **multidisciplinary and allied-health clinics**, especially those with several practitioners and insurance billing. The per-location pricing and polished UX are hard to beat at scale.

## Verdict

Jane App earns its reputation. For clinics that need booking, charting, and insurance billing in one place — and value software their whole team will actually use — it's one of the best practice management platforms available in 2026. The main reasons to look elsewhere are behavioral-health specialization or a desire to avoid per-text SMS fees.
