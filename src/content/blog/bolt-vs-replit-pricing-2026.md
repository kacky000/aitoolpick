# Bolt vs Replit Pricing 2026: $25 Token Builder vs $20 Full-Stack Platform

Bolt and Replit both promise the same outcome — describe an app, get a working app — but they price it differently and run on different engines. Bolt.new is a token-metered AI builder, Pro at $25/month. Replit is a full-stack platform with an AI agent, Core at $20/month. The $5 gap is the least interesting part; what matters is that Bolt bills you by tokens consumed and Replit bills you by usage credits, and that changes who each one is cheaper for.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Bolt | Replit |
|---|------|--------|
| **Free** | $0, ~1M tokens to try | Starter — $0, limited daily |
| **Entry paid** | Pro — $25/mo, 10M tokens | Core — $20/mo, $20 credits |
| **Team tier** | Teams — $30/user/mo, 10M each | Pro — $100/mo flat (up to 15) |
| **Pricing model** | Token consumption (rollover) | Usage credits |
| **What it is** | AI app builder (prompt → app) | Full-stack build + host in-browser |

## The Free Tiers

**Bolt Free ($0)** includes roughly 1M tokens — enough to spin up a small project or test the prompt-to-app loop, but tokens disappear fast once you start iterating on a real build.

**Replit Starter ($0)** lets you write code, run projects, and try Replit Agent under tight daily limits. It's more of a "learn the platform" tier than a "ship something" tier.

Verdict on free: Bolt to feel the AI-app-builder magic on a quick idea; Replit to learn the full build-and-run environment. Both free tiers are short runways.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($25) vs Core ($20)

**Bolt Pro — $25/month.** Includes 10M tokens, and unused tokens **roll over** to the next month — a real plus if your usage is uneven. The entire model is token-based: every generation and iteration spends tokens, so heavy iterators burn through faster, while occasional builders benefit from rollover. At $25, Bolt competes with [Lovable and similar prompt-to-app builders](/blog/bolt-pricing-2026/) on the strength of how fast it turns a description into a deployable front end.

**Replit Core — $20/month.** Includes $20/month in usage credits covering Agent steps, generation, and hosting. Replit's value is the **complete loop** — prompt, build front *and* back end, then deploy, all in the browser with no terminal. Burn the credits and you can still code manually; Agent and premium AI pause until the next cycle or you buy more. (See [Replit's full pricing](/blog/replit-pricing-2026/).)

The real difference: Bolt is laser-focused on generating apps from prompts (with token rollover rewarding bursty use); Replit is a full development *platform* where AI is one feature among code, hosting, and collaboration. Want the fastest prompt-to-app flow? Bolt's $25. Want to build, host, and run a real product end-to-end? Replit's $20.

## Where They Scale

**Bolt Teams ($30/user/month)** adds $5/user over Pro and gives each member their own 10M token allocation plus collaboration features — costs scale linearly, so a 5-person team pays $150/mo and a team of 10 pays $300/mo. **Replit Pro ($100/month flat)** covers up to 15 builders under a single subscription — scaling by *headcount under one fee* rather than per seat. The math flips fast: for a 4-person team, four Bolt Teams seats (~$120) roughly match Replit Pro's flat $100; at 10+ builders, Replit's flat rate wins decisively over per-seat token plans. Like [Replit vs other builders](/blog/replit-pricing-2026/), the flat team tier is its strongest cost argument.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **You want the fastest prompt-to-app builder and your usage is bursty** → Bolt Pro ($25) with token rollover.
- **You want to build, host, and deploy a full product in one place** → Replit Core ($20).
- **Small team (2–4) where everyone builds** → roughly a wash; lean Replit Pro ($100 flat) if you'll grow.
- **Team of 5–15 builders** → Replit Pro's flat $100 crushes per-seat Bolt Teams on cost.

The $5 entry gap is noise next to the structural choice: Bolt is a focused AI app generator that rewards uneven use with rollover; Replit is a full platform whose flat team tier makes it the cheaper bet at scale. Match the billing model to how you actually build.
