Signing contracts by printing, scanning, and emailing PDFs is a relic. In 2026 the **best e-signature software** lets you send a document, collect a legally binding signature, and track the whole thing from your phone in minutes. But the market is crowded and pricing varies wildly — from free tiers to $40+/user/month. This guide compares the top options so you can pick the right one.

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Free plan | Entry paid price | Best for |
|------|-----------|------------------|----------|
| **DocuSign** | No | $10/mo (Personal) | Enterprises, broad integrations |
| **Dropbox Sign** | Yes | $15/user/mo | Simple, clean signing |
| **PandaDoc** | Yes | $19/user/mo | Sales proposals & quotes |
| **SignWell** | Yes | ~$10/user/mo | Budget-conscious teams |
| **Adobe Acrobat Sign** | No | ~$23/user/mo | PDF-heavy workflows |

## 1. DocuSign — The Default Standard

DocuSign is the name most people know, and for good reason: it's the most widely integrated e-signature platform and the safest "nobody got fired for choosing it" pick. It has no free plan, starting at **$10/month** for Personal (5 envelopes/month), then $25/user/month Standard and $40/user/month Business Pro.

**Choose it if:** you need enterprise-grade compliance, audit trails, and integrations with everything. See our [DocuSign pricing breakdown](/blog/docusign-pricing-2026/) for the full tier analysis.

## 2. Dropbox Sign — Simple and Clean

Formerly HelloSign, Dropbox Sign is the easiest tool to hand to a non-technical teammate. It has a **free plan** (3 signature requests/month) and paid plans from $15/user/month. The interface is uncluttered and the templates are straightforward.

**Choose it if:** you want a no-fuss signing experience without sales-doc overhead.

## 3. PandaDoc — Best for Sales Teams

PandaDoc is more than e-signature — it's a full proposal and quote builder with CRM integration, analytics, and a content library. There's a **free e-sign plan**, with paid tiers from $19/user/month (Essentials) to $49/user/month (Business).

**Choose it if:** you send proposals and quotes, not just contracts. Compare it head-to-head in [DocuSign vs PandaDoc](/blog/docusign-vs-pandadoc-2026/).

## 4. SignWell — The Budget Pick

SignWell (formerly Docsketch) delivers core e-signature features at a lower price point, with a usable free tier and affordable paid plans. It won't replace a full document-automation suite, but for straightforward signing it's hard to beat on cost.

**Choose it if:** you want DocuSign-like functionality without the DocuSign bill.

## 5. Adobe Acrobat Sign — For PDF Power Users

If your team already lives in Adobe Acrobat, Acrobat Sign integrates signing directly into your PDF workflow. It's powerful but pricier and more complex than purpose-built signing tools.

**Choose it if:** PDFs are central to your work and you're already in the Adobe ecosystem.

## How to Choose

- **Just need signatures?** Dropbox Sign or SignWell.
- **Send sales proposals?** PandaDoc.
- **Enterprise compliance & integrations?** DocuSign.
- **Tight budget?** SignWell or a [free e-signature tool](/blog/best-free-esignature-tools-2026/).

## Bottom Line

For most small teams, **PandaDoc** (if you send proposals) or **Dropbox Sign** (if you just need signatures) hit the sweet spot of price and usability. **DocuSign** remains the safe enterprise default, while **SignWell** wins on pure cost. Match the tool to your actual workflow rather than reaching for the most famous name.
