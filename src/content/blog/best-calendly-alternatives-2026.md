**Calendly alternatives** are worth exploring if you're hitting the one-event-type limit on the free plan, bothered by the Calendly branding, or looking for specific features — like open-source self-hosting, lifetime pricing, or better service business support.

Calendly is excellent, but it's not the only option. Here are the seven best alternatives in 2026.

## Why Look for Calendly Alternatives?

Calendly's free plan restricts you to one event type, which frustrates professionals who need separate links for different meeting lengths. The paid plans at $10-16/month are reasonable but add up across a team. Some teams also want:

- Open-source or self-hosted solutions
- Lifetime pricing instead of recurring fees
- Better integrations with specific CRMs
- More powerful service booking (intake forms, packages, payments)

## 1. Cal.com — Best Free Calendly Alternative

**Best for:** Individuals and teams wanting unlimited free scheduling

Cal.com is an open-source scheduling platform that offers unlimited event types and calendar connections on its free plan — no restrictions. For most individual users, Cal.com free matches or exceeds Calendly Standard.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | $0 (unlimited event types) |
| Team | $12/user/month |
| Enterprise | Custom |

**What Cal.com does better:** Open-source means self-hosting is an option for privacy-conscious organizations. The free tier is far more generous than Calendly's. The interface is clean and modern.

**What it lacks:** The integrations aren't as polished as Calendly's. HubSpot and [Salesforce](/tools/salesforce/) connections exist but require more setup. The mobile experience isn't as refined.

**Verdict:** If you're on Calendly's free plan and frustrated by the one-event-type limit, Cal.com free is the obvious upgrade at zero cost.

## 2. TidyCal — Best One-Time Purchase

**Best for:** Solopreneurs who hate recurring subscriptions

TidyCal offers a lifetime deal for $29 (one-time payment) that covers unlimited booking types, calendar connections, and group bookings. For individuals, this pays for itself in three months compared to Calendly Standard.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | Limited |
| Lifetime (individual) | $29 one-time |
| Lifetime (teams) | $79 one-time |

**What TidyCal does better:** Lifetime pricing is its defining advantage. If you use it for three or more years, you've saved significantly over any subscription tool.

**What it lacks:** The feature depth isn't as complete as Calendly. No advanced routing forms, limited workflow automation, smaller integration library. Works well for simple booking scenarios.

**Verdict:** Solo consultants and coaches who want to stop paying monthly should evaluate TidyCal seriously.

## 3. Acuity Scheduling — Best for Service Businesses

**Best for:** Health & wellness, beauty, coaching, and other appointment-based businesses

Acuity Scheduling (now part of [Squarespace](/tools/squarespace/)) is built for service businesses that need more than meeting links — intake forms, packages, gift certificates, class scheduling, and recurring appointments.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Emerging | $16/month |
| Growing | $27/month |
| Powerhouse | $49/month |

**What Acuity does better:** Service-specific features like packages (buy 5 sessions, use them over time), gift certificates, intake forms, and client-facing portal [make](/tools/make/) it better for coaches, therapists, and personal trainers than Calendly.

**What it lacks:** Less polished for pure B2B scheduling workflows. Routing and round-robin features aren't as strong.

**Verdict:** If you run a service business and need client management alongside scheduling, Acuity beats Calendly for your use case.

## 4. Doodle — Best for Group Scheduling

**Best for:** Finding meeting times for groups who don't share a calendar system

Doodle solves a different problem than Calendly — instead of sharing availability via a booking link, Doodle lets you poll a group to find the best meeting time. This is invaluable for external meetings with clients, partners, or committees who use different calendar systems.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free | Poll-based scheduling |
| Pro | $6.95/month |
| Team | $8.95/user/month |

**What Doodle does better:** Group polling is Doodle's superpower. For external meetings with multiple stakeholders who can't share calendars, Doodle eliminates the endless email chain.

**What it lacks:** Doodle isn't a Calendly replacement for individual booking links. It's a complement for specific use cases.

**Verdict:** Use Doodle alongside your primary scheduling tool, not instead of it.

## 5. SavvyCal — Best for Respecting Attendee Availability

**Best for:** Salespeople and consultants who want a more human scheduling experience

SavvyCal takes a different philosophical approach: instead of just showing your available times, it also asks attendees to overlay their own calendar, so both parties can see mutual free slots. This feels more considerate and reduces the "pick a time that works for me" dynamic.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Basic | $12/month |
| Premium | $20/month |

**What SavvyCal does better:** The mutual-availability feature and more human-feeling booking experience is a genuine differentiator for client-facing roles where relationship quality matters.

**What it lacks:** Smaller integration ecosystem than Calendly. Not ideal for high-volume sales teams that need routing and round-robin.

**Verdict:** Worth trying if you're in a relationship-driven sales or consulting role.

## 6. HubSpot Meetings — Best If You Already Use HubSpot

**Best for:** Teams using HubSpot [CRM](/category/crm/)

[HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) Meetings is a scheduling tool built directly into the HubSpot CRM. When someone books a meeting through a HubSpot meeting link, the booking is automatically logged in their contact record — no Zapier required.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free (1 link) | $0 |
| Starter ($15/month) | Multiple links |

**What [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) Meetings does better:** Native CRM integration means meetings, contacts, and deals are linked automatically. If you're already paying for HubSpot, this may eliminate the need for Calendly entirely.

**What it lacks:** The booking UX isn't as polished as Calendly's. Group features like round-robin require Teams plan. Overkill if you don't use HubSpot.

**Verdict:** If you use HubSpot, try HubSpot Meetings before paying for Calendly.

## 7. Chili Piper — Best for B2B Sales Teams

**Best for:** High-velocity B2B sales teams with inbound lead routing

Chili Piper is an enterprise scheduling and lead routing platform that handles complex sales workflows: inbound routing, territory assignment, hand-off scheduling between SDRs and AEs, and meeting completion tracking.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Instant Booker | $22.50/user/month |
| Form Concierge | $30/user/month |
| Distro | Custom |

**What Chili Piper does better:** The inbound routing and instant booking on web forms — where a prospect fills out a form and immediately books a call with the right rep — is best-in-class for B2B demand generation.

**What it lacks:** Expensive and complex. Overkill unless you have a significant inbound lead volume and a structured sales team.

**Verdict:** Enterprise B2B sales teams with 10+ reps and significant inbound volume should evaluate Chili Piper seriously.
