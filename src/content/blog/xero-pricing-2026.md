**[Xero](/tools/xero/)** is the cloud accounting platform that wins on one feature QuickBooks charges extra for: **unlimited users on every plan**. Here's exactly what Xero costs in the US in 2026, what each tier includes, and whether the cheapest plan is enough for your business.

## Xero Pricing in 2026

As of March 1, 2026, Xero's US monthly pricing is:

| Plan | Monthly cost | Invoice limit | Best for |
|------|-------------|---------------|----------|
| Early | **$25/mo** | 20 invoices, 5 bills | Freelancers, micro-businesses |
| Growing | **$55/mo** | Unlimited | Most growing small businesses |
| Established | **$90/mo** | Unlimited + multicurrency | Project tracking, global teams |

Every plan includes **unlimited users** — a major cost advantage over [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026), which caps users by tier. Annual prepayment typically saves around 10%.

## What Each Plan Actually Gets You

- **Early ($25)** — The catch is the hard caps: you can send only **20 invoices** and enter **5 bills** per month. Fine for a solo freelancer with a handful of clients, frustrating the moment you grow. Includes bank reconciliation, short-term cash flow, and the Xero mobile app.
- **Growing ($55)** — Removes the invoice and bill caps entirely. This is the right plan for most small businesses: unlimited transactions, bulk reconciliation, and quotes.
- **Established ($90)** — Adds the power features: **multicurrency**, project tracking, expense claims (Xero Expenses), and in-depth analytics with cash flow forecasting. Required the moment you bill in multiple currencies or track project profitability.

## The Hidden Costs

- **Xero Payroll (Gusto)** — US payroll runs through a Gusto integration, billed separately.
- **Payment processing** — Accepting card/ACH payments via Stripe or GoCardless carries standard processing fees.
- **Plan jumps** — If you exceed Early's 20-invoice cap, there's no middle option; you jump straight to Growing at more than double the price.

Xero's standout value is **unlimited users**. A 5-person team on Xero Growing pays $55 flat; the same team on [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026) needs Plus at $115. Xero also has a cleaner interface that many non-accountants prefer.

## Is Xero Worth It?

### It's worth it if:

- You have a **team** — unlimited users on every plan is the killer feature
- You want a **modern, clean UI** without the QuickBooks learning curve
- You bill in **multiple currencies** (Established)
- You're outside the US-accountant-defaults-to-QuickBooks bubble

### Look elsewhere if:

- You're a **pure freelancer** sending a few invoices — [FreshBooks](/blog/freshbooks-pricing-2026) or [Wave](/blog/wave-pricing-2026) fit better and cost less
- Your **accountant insists on QuickBooks** — switching creates friction
- You'd hit Early's 20-invoice cap but can't justify the jump to $55

## Tips to Lower Your Xero Cost

1. **Start on Early if you invoice lightly** — but track your invoice count; the cap sneaks up.
2. **Prepay annually** for roughly 10% off.
3. **Don't pay for Established** unless you genuinely need multicurrency or projects — Growing covers most needs.
4. **Use the free trial** to migrate your data before committing.

## The Bottom Line

Xero in 2026 is the best-value cloud accounting tool for **teams**, thanks to unlimited users at every tier. Most businesses belong on **Growing ($55)**; freelancers can start on Early ($25) if they watch the invoice cap. If your accountant lives in QuickBooks, the switching cost may outweigh the savings — but on raw value per user, Xero is hard to beat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does Xero cost in 2026?

Xero's US plans are Early $25/mo, Growing $55/mo, and Established $90/mo as of March 1, 2026. Every plan includes unlimited users.

### What's the catch with Xero's Early plan?

Early ($25) limits you to 20 invoices and 5 bills per month. Once you exceed that, you must upgrade to Growing ($55), which removes the caps.

### Is Xero cheaper than QuickBooks?

For teams, yes. Xero includes unlimited users on every plan, while [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026) caps users by tier and costs $115/mo for the comparable 5-user Plus plan versus Xero Growing at $55.
