[Clockify](/tools/clockify/) bills itself as the only truly free time tracking tool with no user limits — and in 2026, that claim still holds. Whether you're a solo freelancer or a 100-person team, the free plan offers unlimited time tracking without charging a cent. But is free good enough, and when should you upgrade? Here's our complete review.

## What Is Clockify?

Clockify is a time tracking application that lets you and your team log [hours](/tools/hours-tracker/) on projects and tasks. It offers timer-based tracking, manual time entry, and timesheet modes. Beyond basic tracking, paid plans add invoicing, scheduling, expense tracking, and advanced reporting.

Founded in 2017 by the CAKE.com team (who also [make](/tools/make/) Pumble), Clockify has grown to over 4 million users making it one of the most widely-used time trackers globally.

## Free Plan Deep Dive

Clockify's free plan includes:

- **Unlimited users** — no cap whatsoever
- **Unlimited projects and clients**
- Time tracking (timer, manual, timesheet)
- Basic reporting (summary, detailed, weekly)
- Browser extensions (Chrome, Firefox, Edge)
- Desktop apps (Windows, Mac, Linux)
- Mobile apps (iOS, Android)
- Idle detection
- Pomodoro timer
- CSV/PDF export

What you don't get: time rounding, bulk editing, custom fields, invoicing, scheduling, approvals, budgets, or advanced permissions.

For straightforward "track how much time I spend on things" use cases, the free plan is genuinely complete.

## Paid Features Worth Noting

### Invoicing (Pro - $7.99/user/mo)

Generate invoices directly from tracked time. Select a date range, client, and project — Clockify calculates totals based on billable rates and produces a professional invoice. Templates are customizable, and you can mark invoices as sent/paid.

### Scheduling (Standard - $5.49/user/mo)

A visual timeline for planning who works on what. Create assignments, set capacity limits, and compare scheduled vs actual hours. Useful for agency resource management.

### Budgets and Forecasting (Pro - $7.99/user/mo)

Set budgets per project (hours or money), receive alerts at thresholds, and see forecasts based on burn rate. Essential for client work where going over budget means eating costs.

### Kiosk Mode (Standard - $5.49/user/mo)

Turn any tablet into a time clock. Employees PIN in/out for shift-based time tracking. Popular with manufacturing, retail, and field service companies.

## Pros

- **Truly free** for unlimited users — unmatched in the market
- **Simple interface** that requires minimal training
- **Complete platform coverage** — web, desktop, mobile, browser extensions
- **Invoicing built-in** eliminates need for separate billing tool
- **Scheduling included** for resource planning
- **Offline tracking** works without internet, syncs when reconnected
- **Competitive paid pricing** across all tiers

## Cons

- **UI feels slightly dated** compared to [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/)
- **Reporting less visual** than premium competitors
- **No auto-tracking** (can't detect which apps/websites you're using)
- **Free plan lacks time rounding** which freelancers often need for billing
- **No project management** beyond basic task categorization
- **No Pomodoro stats** (timer exists but doesn't aggregate focus data like [TickTick](/tools/ticktick/))

## Who Should Use Clockify?

**Freelancers**: The free plan covers basic tracking needs. Upgrade to Pro ($7.99) when you want integrated invoicing.

**Small teams (5-20 people)**: Unbeatable value. Start free, add Standard ($5.49) when you need timesheets and scheduling.

**Agencies**: The Pro plan at $7.99/user gives you budgets, invoicing, and team scheduling at half the cost of [Harvest](/tools/harvest/) or [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) Premium.

**Enterprise**: If your primary need is time tracking (not full project management), Clockify Enterprise at $11.99 is far cheaper than tools like [Smartsheet](/tools/smartsheet/) or [Monday.com](/tools/monday/).

## The Verdict

Clockify earns a **4.5/5** in 2026. It delivers on its core promise — free, unlimited time tracking that actually works — and its paid plans add genuinely useful features at prices that undercut every major competitor.

The trade-off is aesthetics and polish. [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) looks better, reports better, and feels smoother. But Clockify does more (invoicing, scheduling, kiosk) for less money. For teams where budget matters more than design, Clockify is the clear winner.
