# Lovable vs Cursor Pricing 2026: $25 App Builder vs $20 AI Editor

Lovable and Cursor sit just $5 apart at entry — Lovable Starter at $25/month, Cursor Pro at $20/month — but they represent two opposite philosophies of AI building. Lovable (formerly GPT Engineer) turns a plain-language description into a working, hosted full-stack app. Cursor is a full AI code editor — a VS Code fork where you write and refactor a real codebase with AI alongside every keystroke. The $5 gap is noise next to that difference.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Lovable | Cursor |
|---|---------|--------|
| **Free** | $0, 5 credits/day (~30/mo) | Hobby — $0, limited |
| **Entry paid** | Starter — $25/mo, 100 credits | Pro — $20/mo, $20 pool |
| **Mid tier** | Pro — $50/mo, 400 credits | Pro+ — $60/mo, 3x credits |
| **Power tier** | Scale — higher, 800 credits | Ultra — $200/mo, 10x credits |
| **Team tier** | (via higher plans) | Teams — $40/user/mo, $40 pool |
| **What it is** | Prompt-to-app, hosted full-stack | Full AI code editor (IDE) |

## The Free Tiers

**Lovable Free ($0)** gives 5 credits per day (about 30 a month) — enough to test a few prompts and watch how it generates an app, but generation is deprioritized versus paid plans and projects stay public.

**Cursor Hobby ($0)** gives a limited number of Agent requests and Tab completions — enough to feel AI-native editing, but you'll hit limits in a day of full-time coding.

Verdict on free: Lovable to watch a whole app appear from a sentence; Cursor to feel what AI editing inside a real project is like.

## Entry Paid: Starter ($25) vs Pro ($20)

**Lovable Starter — $25/month.** Jumps you from ~30 monthly credits to 100 and unlocks private projects. Lovable's value is the all-in-one loop: describe an app, get a working full-stack prototype, hosted, without touching a terminal. It's aimed at founders, designers, and non-developers who want a shipped product fast. Credits are consumed per action, so heavy debugging burns through 100 faster than you'd expect.

**Cursor Pro — $20/month.** Gives a $20 monthly credit pool for frontier models (Claude Sonnet, GPT-4o, and others) inside a full IDE. Cursor is where developers *do the work*: multi-file edits, codebase-aware chat, agentic refactors, project-aware completion. It assumes you have — or are building — a real codebase and want AI to accelerate it.

The real difference: Lovable builds the app *for* you and hosts it; Cursor makes *you* a faster developer in your own environment. Want a product without managing code? Lovable's $25 fits. Live in an editor? Cursor's $20 fits.

## Where They Scale

Both offer a ladder, but for different reasons. **Lovable Pro ($50/mo, 400 credits)** and **Scale (higher tier, 800 credits)** scale by *generation volume* — more credits to build and iterate on bigger apps. **Cursor Pro+ ($60/mo)** triples the pool for developers who exhaust $20 mid-month, and **Ultra ($200/mo)** gives 10x credits for all-day agentic coding. So Lovable scales by *how much you build*; Cursor scales by *how intensely one developer codes*. For teams, Cursor adds **Teams at $40/user/month**.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Want a full-stack app built and hosted from a prompt?** Lovable Starter ($25/mo).
- **Developer who wants AI-native editing across a real codebase?** Cursor Pro ($20/mo).
- **Building and iterating on a bigger app?** Lovable Pro ($50/mo) or Scale.
- **Burning through your $20 pool monthly?** Cursor Pro+ ($60/mo) or Ultra ($200/mo).
- **Honest take?** Start in Lovable to scaffold and ship; move to Cursor when you need real engineering control.

## The Bottom Line

The $5 difference is irrelevant; the workflow is everything. Lovable builds and hosts a full-stack app from a description — ideal when you want a shipped product without managing code. Cursor is a full AI editor that makes a developer faster inside their own codebase. Pick Lovable if you want the app handed to you; pick Cursor if you want to build it yourself, faster. A common path: prototype in Lovable, then export into a Cursor workflow when the project outgrows prompt-only building.
