Email is still where most knowledge workers lose hours every week. A new wave of **AI email clients** promises to fix that — drafting replies, summarizing long threads, and triaging your inbox automatically. But they vary wildly on price, platform support, and how much the AI actually helps. This roundup ranks the best AI email clients in 2026 so you can pick the right one without a week of trials.

## How We Compared Them

We weighed four things that matter day to day:

- **AI quality** — drafting, summarizing, and search that actually saves time
- **Speed & keyboard control** — how fast you can move through a busy inbox
- **Platform support** — Gmail, Outlook, mobile, desktop
- **Price** — what you really pay once you need the useful tier

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Best for | Free plan | Starting price |
|---|---|---|---|
| Superhuman | Speed obsessives | No | $30/mo |
| Shortwave | Gmail power users | Yes | $8.50/mo |
| Spark | Teams & shared inboxes | Yes | ~$8/mo |
| Canary Mail | Privacy-first AI | Yes | ~$3/mo |
| Notion Mail | Notion-centric workflows | Yes | Bundled |
| Gmail + Gemini | Existing Google users | Yes | Bundled |
| Outlook Copilot | Microsoft 365 teams | No | Add-on |

## 1. Superhuman — Best for Speed

Superhuman built its reputation on raw speed and keyboard shortcuts, and its AI layer now adds Auto Drafts, Ask AI, and instant thread summaries. If your day is a firehose of email and you live in your inbox, nothing feels faster.

The catch is price: there's no free tier and the Starter plan is **$30/month** ($25 billed annually), with a Business plan at $40. That's premium positioning, justified only if shaving seconds off every email genuinely pays off for you.

See the full breakdown in our [Superhuman review](/blog/superhuman-review-2026/) and [Superhuman pricing guide](/blog/superhuman-pricing-2026/).

## 2. Shortwave — Best Value for Gmail

Shortwave is the strongest Gmail-native option. It bundles an AI executive assistant, smart summaries, and a clean keyboard-driven UI — and it has a genuinely usable **free plan**. Paid tiers start at **$8.50/month** (Personal), with Pro at $18 for heavy AI users.

For most people who want Superhuman-style productivity without the $30 price tag, Shortwave is the obvious first stop. We go deeper in [Superhuman vs Shortwave](/blog/superhuman-vs-shortwave-2026/).

## 3. Spark — Best for Teams

Spark by Readdle shines when email is a team sport. Shared inboxes, collaborative drafts, and inbox delegation make it ideal for support and small teams. Its "Smart Inbox" and AI compose features handle triage and drafting well, and there's a free tier plus affordable paid plans around **$8/month**.

If you collaborate on email rather than just process it, start with Spark. Compare it head to head in [Shortwave vs Spark](/blog/shortwave-vs-spark-2026/).

## 4. Canary Mail — Best for Privacy

Canary Mail pairs AI writing and summaries with a privacy-first stance, including end-to-end encryption options. It supports Gmail, Outlook, and IMAP, and its pricing is among the lowest here. A solid pick if you want AI assistance without sending everything through a big-tech model.

## 5. Notion Mail — Best for Notion Users

If your work already lives in Notion, Notion Mail keeps email in the same ecosystem with AI labeling and drafting. It's not the fastest standalone client, but the integration is the selling point for Notion-centric teams.

## 6. Gmail + Gemini — Best Free Default

You may not need a new app at all. Gmail's built-in Gemini features handle "Help me write," thread summaries, and smart search inside the inbox you already use. For Google Workspace users, it's the lowest-friction option.

## 7. Outlook + Copilot — Best for Microsoft Teams

For Microsoft 365 shops, Copilot in Outlook drafts replies, summarizes threads, and coaches your tone — all inside the Outlook you're locked into anyway. Pricing is an add-on per user, so factor that into your existing Microsoft bill.

## How to Choose

- **You want the fastest inbox, money no object** → Superhuman
- **You use Gmail and want value** → Shortwave
- **Your team shares an inbox** → Spark
- **Privacy is non-negotiable** → Canary Mail
- **You already pay for Google or Microsoft** → Gemini or Copilot

## Bottom Line

The best AI email client depends on your stack and your tolerance for price. Gmail users chasing value should try **Shortwave** first; speed-obsessed power users will love **Superhuman**; teams should look at **Spark**. Most offer free tiers or trials, so test two before committing.
