**[Framer](/tools/framer/)** prices differently from most website builders: site plans and workspace seats are billed separately. That trips people up, because the headline "$5/month" only covers one small site — the people editing it cost extra. Here's how Framer pricing actually works in 2026.

## Framer Pricing in 2026

Framer splits billing into two parts: **site plans** (per published site) and **workspace seats** (per editor). You pay for both.

### Site plans (per site, annual)

| Plan | Per month | Page views/mo | Best for |
|------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| **Free** | $0 | Limited, `.framer.website` domain | Drafts and learning |
| **Mini** | $5 | ~1,000 | One-page sites, link-in-bio |
| **Basic** | $15 | ~10,000 | Personal sites and portfolios |
| **Pro** | $30 | ~200,000 | Business sites and landing pages |

### Free — $0

You can design and publish, but on a Framer subdomain with Framer branding and tight page-view limits. Good for learning the editor or sharing a quick draft — not for a real brand.

### Mini — $5/month

- Connect a **custom domain**
- Single-page sites only (perfect for link-in-bio or a simple landing page)
- Removes most Framer branding

### Basic — $15/month

- **Multi-page** sites with a custom domain
- ~10,000 monthly page views
- CMS for blogs and dynamic content

Basic is the right entry point for a portfolio or small business site.

### Pro — $30/month

- ~200,000 monthly page views
- Advanced CMS, password protection, and analytics
- Best for marketing sites with real traffic

### Workspace seats (per editor)

On top of the site plan, each person who edits pays for a seat — typically around **$15–$20 per editor per month**. A solo founder pays one seat; an agency with five designers pays five. Always add seat cost to the site-plan cost to get your true bill.

## Is Framer Worth the Cost?

### It's worth it if:

- You're a **designer** who wants animation and polish without code
- You ship **landing pages and marketing sites** fast
- You value a modern editor over a traditional CMS

### Look elsewhere if:

- You need **deep CMS structure** — [Webflow](/blog/webflow-pricing-2026) is stronger
- You run a **large store** — Framer's commerce is limited
- You're cost-sensitive with a **big team** — per-seat billing adds up quickly

## Tips to Save on Framer

1. **Match the site plan to traffic** — don't pay Pro for a low-traffic portfolio
2. **Minimize editor seats** — only people who actually edit need a paid seat
3. **Use Mini for one-pagers** — link-in-bio and simple landers don't need Basic
4. **Pay annually** to cut the per-month rate

## The Bottom Line

Framer is the best builder for designers who want speed and motion, and its $5 Mini plan is genuinely cheap — but remember the two-part bill. A solo site on Basic with one seat is reasonable; a team site can cost more than it first looks once seats stack up. Add site plan + seats before you decide. For pure design-led marketing sites, Framer is hard to beat.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why does Framer pricing seem confusing?

Framer bills site plans and editor seats separately. The advertised price (e.g., $5 Mini) covers one site; each person editing pays an additional seat fee. Add both for your real cost.

### Is the free Framer plan usable?

For learning and drafts, yes. But it uses a Framer subdomain, shows branding, and has low page-view limits, so it isn't suitable for a live business site.

### Framer or Webflow for a real website?

Framer is faster for design-led marketing sites and animation. [Webflow](/blog/webflow-pricing-2026) offers deeper CMS and structural control. See our [Framer vs Webflow vs Wix](/blog/framer-vs-webflow-vs-wix-2026) breakdown.
