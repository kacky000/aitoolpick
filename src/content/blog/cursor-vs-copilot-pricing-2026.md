# Cursor vs GitHub Copilot Pricing 2026: $20 AI Editor vs $10 Code Assistant

Cursor and GitHub Copilot are the two most popular ways to put AI into your coding, and their entry plans sit $10 apart — Copilot Pro at $10/month, Cursor Pro at $20/month. That gap looks bigger than it is, because the two tools aren't the same kind of product. Copilot is an assistant that lives inside the editor you already use. Cursor is a full editor built around AI from the ground up. The price reflects which one you're buying into.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Cursor | GitHub Copilot |
|---|--------|----------------|
| **Free** | Hobby — $0, limited Agent + Tab | Free — $0, 2,000 completions + 50 chats/mo |
| **Entry paid** | Pro — $20/mo, $20 credit pool | Pro — $10/mo, unlimited completions |
| **Mid tier** | Pro+ — $60/mo, 3x credits | Pro+ — $39/mo, more premium requests |
| **Team tier** | Teams — $40/user/mo | Business — $19/user/mo |
| **What it is** | AI-native code editor (IDE) | AI assistant inside your IDE |

## The Free Tiers

**Cursor Hobby ($0)** gives a limited slice of Agent and Tab usage with no credit card — enough to feel how AI-native editing works, but you'll hit the ceiling in a real session quickly.

**Copilot Free ($0)** allows 2,000 code completions and 50 chat requests per month inside VS Code, JetBrains, and others. For a hobbyist who codes occasionally, the free tier alone can be enough — a genuinely generous offer that Cursor's free tier doesn't match on raw volume.

Verdict on free: Copilot Free if you just want autocomplete in your existing editor; Cursor Hobby if you want to test the AI-first editing experience before paying.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($20) vs Pro ($10)

**GitHub Copilot Pro — $10/month.** Unlimited completions plus a monthly allotment of premium model requests, working inside whatever IDE you already live in. The pitch is simple: keep your setup, add AI. For developers who don't want to switch editors, $10 is the lowest-friction way to get strong AI assistance. Verified students and open-source maintainers can get Pro free.

**Cursor Pro — $20/month.** Unlimited Tab completions, unlimited Auto mode, and a $20 credit pool for premium frontier models. The extra $10 buys a different category of tool — a forked VS Code where the Agent can read and edit across your whole codebase, not just suggest the next line. If you want the editor itself to be AI-native, that's what the $20 pays for. Like [Windsurf at $15](/blog/windsurf-pricing-2026/), Cursor is competing on the editing experience, not just completions.

The real difference: Copilot makes your current editor smarter for $10; Cursor replaces your editor with one designed around AI for $20. Coder who's happy in VS Code? Copilot's $10. Want the AI woven into every action? Cursor's $20.

## Where They Scale

**Copilot Pro+ ($39/month)** raises premium request limits for power users; **Cursor Pro+ ($60/month)** triples the credit pool. At the top, Cursor offers **Ultra at $200/month** (20x usage) for developers who run agents all day — Copilot has no direct equivalent at the individual level, pushing heavy users toward Business.

For teams: **Copilot Business ($19/user/month)** adds policy controls and centralized management; **Cursor Teams ($40/user/month)** adds SSO and admin on top of the full AI-native editor. A 5-person team pays ~$95/mo on Copilot Business vs ~$200/mo on Cursor Teams — Copilot wins on team cost, Cursor wins on capability per seat. Both knock ~20% off with annual billing.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **You code in VS Code or JetBrains and want AI for cheap** → Copilot Pro ($10).
- **You want the editor itself built around AI agents** → Cursor Pro ($20).
- **You run AI agents constantly on large codebases** → Cursor Ultra ($200) over stacking Copilot.
- **You manage a dev team on a budget** → Copilot Business ($19/user); spring for Cursor Teams ($40/user) only if AI-native editing is the priority.

The $10 gap isn't really about money — it's about whether you want an assistant or a new editor. Most developers happy with their current IDE should start at Copilot's $10 and only move to Cursor's $20 if they want AI in the driver's seat.
