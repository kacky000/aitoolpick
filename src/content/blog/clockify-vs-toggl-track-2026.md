[Clockify](/tools/clockify/) and [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) are the two most popular time tracking tools for freelancers and teams. Both offer free plans, both are well-designed, and both get the job done. But the differences matter — especially when you're choosing a tool your entire team will use daily. Here's how they compare in 2026.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Clockify | [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track-app/) |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Free plan users | Unlimited | Up to 5 |
| Free plan features | Time tracking, reports, projects | Time tracking, reports, projects |
| Starting paid price | $3.99/user/mo | $9/user/mo |
| Invoicing | Yes (paid) | No (use Toggl Invoice) |
| Scheduling | Yes (paid) | No |
| Kiosk/Time clock | Yes | No |
| Integrations | 80+ | 100+ |
| Best for | Budget-conscious teams | Freelancers & agencies |

**Cost difference**: For a 10-person team on the first paid tier, Clockify costs $39.90/month vs Toggl Track at $90/month. That's more than double the price for Toggl.

## Free Plans Compared

### Clockify Free

Clockify's free plan is extraordinarily generous:
- **Unlimited users** (no cap)
- Time tracking (timer + manual entry)
- Unlimited projects and clients
- Basic reports and exports
- Idle detection
- Browser extensions and apps

### Toggl Track Free

Toggl Track's free plan is good but limited:
- **5 users maximum**
- Time tracking (timer + manual entry)
- Unlimited projects and clients
- Basic reports
- Pomodoro timer
- Calendar integration

**Winner: Clockify** — Unlimited users on the free plan is a massive advantage for teams.

## Time Tracking Experience

### Clockify

Clockify offers a clean, functional interface for tracking time. You can use the timer, enter time manually, or fill out weekly timesheets. The interface is straightforward but can feel slightly dated compared to Toggl.

### Toggl Track

Toggl Track's one-click timer is legendary. The interface is more modern, with better visual design and a smoother experience. The auto-tracker (detects which apps you're using) and calendar view are exclusive to Toggl.

**Winner: Toggl Track** — The tracking experience is more polished and the auto-tracker adds genuine value.

## Reporting

### Clockify

Reports are functional on the free plan (summary, detailed, weekly) but become powerful on paid plans with custom fields, cost rates, and labor cost analysis.

### Toggl Track

Toggl Track's reporting is more visually appealing with better charts and the "Insights" feature on Premium that shows profitability per project and client.

**Winner: Toggl Track** — Better visualization and the Insights feature provides actionable data for agencies.

## Invoicing

### Clockify

Built-in invoicing on the Pro plan ($7.99/user/mo). Generate invoices directly from tracked time, customize templates, and send to clients. Simple but effective.

### Toggl Track

No built-in invoicing. Toggl offers a separate product (Toggl Invoice) but it's not integrated into Track's interface. You'd typically export time data to QuickBooks or [FreshBooks](/tools/freshbooks/).

**Winner: Clockify** — Having invoicing built into the same tool saves time and reduces errors.

## Scheduling and Resource Management

### Clockify

The Standard plan ($5.49/user/mo) includes scheduling with a visual timeline for assigning team members to projects and tasks. Useful for agencies managing workload.

### Toggl Track

No scheduling features. [Toggl Plan](/tools/toggl-plan/) exists as a separate product for project planning, but it's not integrated into Track.

**Winner: Clockify** — Scheduling in the same app means less context-switching.

## Integrations

Both integrate with popular project management tools:

- **Clockify**: 80+ integrations including [Asana](/tools/asana/), Trello, [Jira](/tools/jira/), [ClickUp](/tools/clickup/), GitHub
- **Toggl Track**: 100+ integrations including all the above plus [Notion](/tools/notion/), Salesforce, and more calendar apps

**Winner: Toggl Track** — Slightly broader integration ecosystem.

## Who Should Choose Clockify?

- Teams of any size wanting free time tracking
- Budget-conscious businesses (50%+ cheaper than Toggl on paid plans)
- Companies needing invoicing built into their time tracker
- Teams wanting scheduling and time tracking in one tool
- Organizations needing kiosk/time clock functionality

## Who Should Choose Toggl Track?

- Freelancers and small agencies (under 5 people) wanting the best free experience
- Teams that prioritize reporting and profitability insights
- Users who value polished design and smooth UX
- Companies with complex integration needs
- Anyone already using the Toggl ecosystem (Plan, Invoice)

## The Verdict

**Choose Clockify** if you want the best value — especially for teams. Unlimited free users, cheaper paid plans, built-in invoicing, and scheduling [make](/tools/make/) it the practical choice for businesses watching their budget.

**Choose Toggl Track** if you want the best experience — superior UX, better reporting visualizations, more integrations, and a more modern feel. The higher price reflects a more polished product.

For most teams over 5 people, [Clockify](/tools/clockify/) is the smarter financial choice. For freelancers and small agencies who value design and reporting, [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) justifies its premium.

Also consider [Harvest](/tools/harvest/) if you need time tracking with robust project budgeting and client invoicing in a mature, battle-tested package.
