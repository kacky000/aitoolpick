# Obsidian vs OneNote 2026: Local Knowledge Base vs Microsoft Notebook

Obsidian and OneNote attract very different users. Obsidian is a local-first, Markdown-based knowledge tool for connected thinking. OneNote is Microsoft's free-form digital notebook, deeply tied to the Office ecosystem. Here is how they compare in 2026.

## The Core Difference

**Obsidian** stores notes as plain Markdown files on your device and links them into a knowledge graph. It is structured, text-first, and private by design.

**OneNote** is a free-form canvas where you can type, draw, and drop content anywhere on a page, synced through your Microsoft account. It is flexible, visual, and cloud-based.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Obsidian | OneNote |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Storage** | Local Markdown files | Microsoft cloud |
| **Format** | Structured text | Free-form canvas |
| **Linking** | Backlinks + graph | Basic links |
| **Handwriting** | Limited | Excellent |
| **Privacy** | Data stays local | Cloud-synced |
| **Price** | Free (personal) | Free |
| **Office integration** | None | Deep |

## Where Obsidian Wins

- **Knowledge graphs.** Backlinks and graph view make it ideal for research and connected notes.
- **Data ownership.** Plain files you control, with no lock-in.
- **Extensibility.** A vast plugin ecosystem.
- **Markdown portability.** Your notes move anywhere.

## Where OneNote Wins

- **Free-form input.** Place text, images, and ink anywhere on the page.
- **Handwriting and stylus.** Outstanding on tablets and touch devices.
- **Office integration.** Seamless with Word, Outlook, and Teams.
- **Truly free.** Full-featured at no cost with a Microsoft account.

## Pricing

Both are free for individuals. Obsidian charges only for optional sync and publish add-ons; OneNote is bundled free with a Microsoft account, with storage tied to OneDrive limits. Cost is rarely the deciding factor here — workflow is.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Obsidian if** you want a private, structured knowledge base, think in linked notes, and value owning your files. It suits researchers, writers, and anyone building a personal knowledge system.

**Choose OneNote if** you want free-form, visual notes, rely on handwriting or a stylus, and live inside the Microsoft ecosystem.

If you are exploring alternatives more broadly, it is worth also weighing [Obsidian vs Notion](/blog/obsidian-vs-notion-2026/), since Notion is the other major contender for structured note-taking.

## Final Verdict

**Obsidian** is the pick for private, connected, text-first thinking. **OneNote** is the pick for visual, free-form notes and Microsoft-integrated workflows. Decide whether you want a structured knowledge graph or a flexible digital notebook, and the choice follows.
