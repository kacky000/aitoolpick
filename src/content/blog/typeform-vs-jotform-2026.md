**[Typeform](/tools/typeform/)** gives you a polished, one-question-at-a-time experience that people actually enjoy filling out. **[Jotform](/tools/jotform/)** gives you 10,000+ templates, drag-and-drop flexibility, and HIPAA compliance if you need it. Both are solid form builders — but they're built for different priorities.

This comparison breaks down exactly where each one wins and where it falls short, so you can stop guessing and pick the right tool.

## Pricing: Typeform Costs Less Per Month, Jotform Gives More Per Dollar

Typeform's plans look straightforward until you hit the response limits.

**Typeform:**
- Free: $0 — 10 responses/month, unlimited forms
- Basic: $25/month — 100 responses, 1 user
- Plus: $50/month — 1,000 responses, 3 users
- Business: $83/month — 10,000 responses, 5 users

**Jotform:**
- Free: $0 — 5 forms, 100 submissions, 100 MB storage
- Bronze: $34/month — 25 forms, 1,000 submissions
- Silver: $39/month — 50 forms, 2,500 submissions
- Gold: $99/month — 100 forms, 10,000 submissions, HIPAA
- Enterprise: Custom — unlimited everything

All prices are annual billing. Monthly billing runs roughly 15–25% higher on both platforms.

The math matters here. Typeform's Basic gives you 100 responses for $25. Jotform's Bronze gives you 1,000 submissions for $34. If response volume is your main concern, Jotform delivers ten times the capacity for $9 more per month.

But Typeform's free plan has unlimited forms — Jotform's free tier caps you at five. If you need many low-traffic forms, Typeform's free plan actually works. For a deep dive on Typeform's costs, see our [Typeform pricing breakdown](/blog/typeform-pricing-2026).

## Design and User Experience

This is the biggest difference between the two, and it's not close.

**Typeform** presents one question per screen. The form feels more like a conversation than a checklist. Users see a clean, focused interface with smooth transitions between questions. This format consistently produces higher completion rates — Typeform claims up to 2x higher than traditional forms, and independent tests back that up for consumer-facing surveys.

The downside: you can't deviate from the conversational format. Every form follows the same one-at-a-time structure. If you need a traditional multi-field form (like a detailed registration or order form), Typeform forces that content into a format it wasn't designed for.

**Jotform** uses a standard drag-and-drop builder. You place fields wherever you want on a canvas. It's closer to building a document than conducting a conversation. The output looks functional, not beautiful — but you can arrange fields side by side, create multi-column layouts, and build complex forms that would be awkward in Typeform's linear flow.

Jotform also supports a "card form" mode that mimics Typeform's one-at-a-time style. It works, but it doesn't feel as polished.

**Winner:** Typeform for customer-facing forms where completion rate matters. Jotform for internal forms, applications, and complex data collection where layout flexibility matters more than aesthetics.

## Templates

Jotform wins this category outright. Over 10,000 templates covering everything from medical intake forms to event registrations to job applications. Most are ready to use with minimal editing.

Typeform has around 800 templates. They're well-designed and optimized for the conversational format, but the selection is limited. If you need a niche form type — say, a HIPAA-compliant patient intake or a detailed insurance quote request — Jotform almost certainly has a template for it. Typeform probably doesn't.

## Integrations

Both platforms connect to the tools you'd expect: [Slack](/tools/slack/), Google Sheets, [Mailchimp](/tools/mailchimp/), [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/), [Zapier](/tools/zapier/), and [Notion](/tools/notion/).

Typeform's integrations feel slightly more polished in execution. The [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) integration, for example, maps form fields to CRM properties cleanly. Jotform has more total integrations (100+ native, plus Zapier), but some of the less popular ones feel bolted on.

For payment collection, Jotform has a clear edge — it supports PayPal, Stripe, Square, and others on all plans including free. Typeform limits payment integrations to paid plans.

## HIPAA Compliance

If you handle protected health information, this comparison gets simple. Jotform offers HIPAA compliance on the Gold plan ($99/month) and Enterprise. Typeform does not offer HIPAA compliance on any plan.

Medical practices, therapists, healthcare startups — if you need form submissions that meet HIPAA requirements, Jotform is one of your only options in the form builder space without going to a specialized healthcare platform.

## Conditional Logic and Advanced Features

Both platforms support conditional logic (show/hide fields, skip to different sections based on answers). Typeform calls it "Logic Jumps," Jotform calls it "Conditional Logic." In practice, they work similarly.

Typeform has a slight edge in calculator features and answer piping — pulling previous answers into later questions feels seamless. Jotform has stronger features for e-signatures, PDF generation, and form approvals — useful for business processes that go beyond data collection.

## Who Should Choose Typeform

Pick Typeform if:

- **Brand experience matters.** Customer-facing surveys, lead capture forms, and feedback forms where a polished experience directly affects completion rates.
- **You need high completion rates.** The conversational format genuinely converts better for short-to-medium surveys (under 15 questions).
- **You're already in the Typeform ecosystem.** The integrations and workflow are smooth if you commit to it.
- **Volume is low.** If you're collecting under 1,000 responses per month, the Plus plan at $50/month is reasonable.

## Who Should Choose Jotform

Pick Jotform if:

- **You need HIPAA compliance.** No workaround here — Typeform doesn't offer it.
- **Template variety matters.** 10,000+ templates mean you'll almost always find a starting point close to what you need.
- **You collect high-volume submissions.** Jotform's cost-per-submission ratio is dramatically better.
- **You need complex form layouts.** Multi-column, conditional sections, e-signatures, PDF output — Jotform handles complex business forms better.
- **You're a nonprofit or school.** Jotform offers 50% off for nonprofits and education discounts.

## The Bottom Line

Typeform is a better form experience. Jotform is a better form platform. If your primary goal is making forms people enjoy filling out — lead gen, customer surveys, brand interactions — Typeform's conversational format is hard to beat. If you need a form builder that handles a wider range of use cases with fewer restrictions and lower cost per response, Jotform is the more practical choice.

Neither is wrong. But they're built for different jobs.
