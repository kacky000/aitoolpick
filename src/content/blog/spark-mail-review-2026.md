Spark by Readdle has grown from a sleek personal email app into a collaboration-focused **AI email client** for teams. With shared inboxes, AI compose, and cross-platform support, it competes with both consumer apps and team tools. But is it worth it in 2026? This **Spark Mail review** covers the features, pricing, and who should use it.

## What Is Spark Mail?

Spark is an email client from Readdle that works across Gmail, Outlook, iCloud, and IMAP on Mac, Windows, iOS, and Android. Its signature features are the **Smart Inbox** (which auto-sorts mail by type and priority) and a strong set of **team collaboration** tools layered on top of standard email.

## Key Features

- **Smart Inbox** — groups newsletters, notifications, and personal mail automatically
- **AI compose & summaries** — drafts replies and condenses long threads
- **Shared inboxes** — teams handle a common address together
- **Collaborative drafts** — write and comment on emails before sending
- **Email delegation** — assign messages to teammates
- **Cross-platform** — true multi-account, multi-OS support

## Pricing

Spark has a **free plan** that covers individual use well. Paid plans start around **$8/month** for Premium (individuals) and scale to team tiers that unlock shared inboxes, delegation, and admin controls. Pricing is competitive with other AI email apps like Shortwave.

For a side-by-side on value, see [Shortwave vs Spark](/blog/shortwave-vs-spark-2026/).

## Pros

- **Excellent for teams** — shared inboxes and delegation are genuinely useful
- **True cross-platform** — best-in-class multi-account, multi-OS support
- **Free plan** — usable without paying
- **Clean, modern UI** — pleasant to use daily

## Cons

- **AI isn't the deepest** — capable, but less AI-first than Shortwave
- **Feature sprawl** — the collaboration tools can feel heavy for solo users
- **Premium needed** — best features sit behind paid tiers

## Who Should Use Spark?

- **Teams handling shared email** — support, sales, or small businesses
- **Multi-account users** — anyone juggling Gmail, Outlook, and iCloud together
- **Cross-platform households** — Mac plus Windows plus mobile

Solo users who only want the deepest AI assistant may prefer Shortwave, while speed obsessives will look at Superhuman.

## How It Compares

- **vs Shortwave** — Spark wins on teams and platform support; Shortwave wins on AI depth. See [Shortwave vs Spark](/blog/shortwave-vs-spark-2026/).
- **vs Superhuman** — Spark is far cheaper and team-oriented; Superhuman is faster and pricier.

## Bottom Line

Spark Mail is one of the best email clients for **teams and multi-account users** in 2026. Its collaboration features and cross-platform reach are genuinely strong, and the free plan makes it easy to try. If your priority is the deepest AI assistant rather than collaboration, compare it against the alternatives first.
