Miro's free plan gives you a taste of collaborative whiteboarding, but the limitations can sneak up on you. Here's what each tier actually delivers in 2026.

## Miro Free Plan

The free tier includes:

- **3 editable boards** — the biggest limitation
- **Unlimited team members** — anyone can view and comment
- **Core templates** — flowcharts, mind maps, wireframes
- **Basic integrations** — Slack, Google Drive, Jira
- **Sticky notes, shapes, and drawing** — the whiteboard essentials

**What's missing:** Unlimited boards, advanced templates, voting, timer, video chat, custom templates, and most admin controls.

## Miro Starter ($8/member/month)

Starter removes the most painful free-plan restriction:

- **Unlimited boards** — create as many as you need
- **Day passes for guests** — bring in external collaborators
- **Custom templates** — save and reuse your own board layouts
- **Board activity logs** — see who changed what
- **Voting and estimation** — built-in facilitation tools
- **Timer** — for timeboxed workshops and standups

This is where Miro becomes a real collaboration platform instead of a quick sketching tool.

## Miro Business ($20/member/month)

Business adds organizational control:

- **SSO (SAML)** — enterprise authentication
- **Guest access management** — fine-grained permissions
- **Smart diagramming** — AI-assisted flowcharts
- **Miro Assist (AI)** — generate content, summarize boards
- **Advanced attention management** — guide participants through boards
- **Organizational templates** — shared templates across teams
- **Priority support** — faster response times

## Who Should Stay Free?

Free Miro works for:

- **Individuals** brainstorming or mind-mapping solo
- **Occasional users** who need 3 or fewer active boards
- **Quick workshops** where you create, use, and archive boards

## Who Should Upgrade?

Upgrade to Starter if you **run recurring workshops** or need more than 3 boards active simultaneously. Business makes sense for **teams of 20+** that need SSO and organizational controls.

## The Verdict

Miro's free plan is surprisingly useful for solo work, but the 3-board limit kills it for teams. Starter at $8/member/month is excellent value — it's one of the more affordable collaboration tools.
