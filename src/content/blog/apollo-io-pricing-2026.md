**Apollo.io** has quietly become one of the most popular sales intelligence platforms on the market. It combines a massive contact database, email sequencing, a built-in dialer, and a lightweight CRM — all in one tool. The pricing reflects that ambition, but the free tier is generous enough that many solo reps never pay a dime.

Here's exactly what each plan costs and includes in 2026, so you can pick the right one without overpaying.

## Apollo.io Pricing Overview

| Plan | Monthly Price | Annual Price | Best For |
|------|--------------|--------------|----------|
| **Free** | $0 | $0 | Freelancers, early-stage founders |
| **Basic** | $59/user/mo | $49/user/mo | Solo reps doing outbound |
| **Professional** | $99/user/mo | $79/user/mo | Growing sales teams |
| **Organization** | $149/user/mo | $119/user/mo | Large teams needing full control |

All paid plans are billed per user. Annual billing saves roughly 20%, which adds up fast once you have five or more seats. For a deeper look at what the tool actually does, check out our [Apollo.io Review 2026](/blog/apollo-review-2026).

## Free Plan — Generous but Limited

Apollo's free tier is one of the best in the sales tool space. You get:

- **10,000 email credits per month** — enough to prospect at a serious volume
- Access to Apollo's full contact database (275M+ contacts)
- Basic email sequences (2 active sequences)
- LinkedIn Chrome extension
- Limited CRM features (Kanban board, basic deal tracking)
- 5 mobile number credits per month

The catch? You can't run A/B tests on sequences, there's no dialer, and reporting is basic. But for a founder who just needs to send cold emails and find prospect data, the free plan covers a lot of ground. Many users start here and only upgrade when they hit the sequence or credit limits.

## Basic Plan ($59/user/mo) — For Solo Reps

The Basic plan is where Apollo starts to feel like a real outbound tool:

- **Unlimited email credits** (with fair use limits)
- 75 mobile number credits per month
- A/B testing on email sequences
- Advanced filters and saved lists
- Intent signals (basic tier)
- Integration with major CRMs like [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) and [Salesforce](/tools/salesforce/)
- Tracking for email opens, clicks, and replies

At $59/month, Basic makes sense for individual sales reps or small teams of two to three people who rely on email-heavy outbound. The unlimited email credits alone justify the upgrade if you're sending more than a few hundred emails per week.

## Professional Plan ($99/user/mo) — For Growing Teams

Professional is the most popular plan and the one Apollo pushes hardest. It adds:

- **150 mobile number credits per month**
- Built-in dialer (power dialer with call recording)
- Advanced reports and dashboards
- Custom stages and fields in the CRM
- Buying intent signals (advanced tier)
- Manual tasks and workflows
- Priority support

The dialer is the biggest differentiator here. If your sales motion includes phone calls — and most B2B motions still do — Professional is the floor. The advanced intent data also helps teams prioritize accounts that are actively researching solutions, instead of spraying emails at cold lists.

For teams of 5-15 reps, Professional hits the right balance between cost and capability. You get a genuinely integrated platform without needing separate tools for calling, sequencing, and data.

## Organization Plan ($149/user/mo) — Full Power

Organization is Apollo's enterprise tier, though the price point is significantly lower than competitors like [ZoomInfo](/tools/zoominfo/):

- **200 mobile number credits per month**
- Advanced security (SSO, audit logs)
- Customizable user roles and permissions
- Advanced API access
- Dedicated customer success manager
- Call transcription and AI-powered conversation intelligence
- Custom integrations

The Organization plan is for teams that need governance — SOC 2 compliance, role-based access, and audit trails. If your company has 20+ reps and a security review process, this is the tier that clears procurement.

## The Credit System Explained

Apollo's pricing looks straightforward until you hit the credit system. Understanding it saves you from surprise costs:

| Credit Type | Free | Basic | Professional | Organization |
|-------------|------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Email Credits** | 10,000/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| **Mobile Credits** | 5/mo | 75/mo | 150/mo | 200/mo |
| **Export Credits** | 250/mo | 1,000/mo | 2,000/mo | 4,000/mo |

**Email credits** are consumed when you reveal a prospect's email address from the database. On paid plans, these are effectively unlimited.

**Mobile credits** are the real currency. Revealing a direct phone number costs one mobile credit. These run out fast if your team does heavy phone outreach. You can buy additional credits in packs, but they aren't cheap — expect to pay $0.30-0.50 per additional mobile credit depending on volume.

**Export credits** limit how many contacts you can export to CSV or push to your CRM per month. Teams doing large-scale list building hit this wall before anything else.

## Apollo.io vs HubSpot vs ZoomInfo — Price Comparison

| Feature | Apollo.io (Professional) | [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) Sales Pro | [ZoomInfo](/tools/zoominfo/) SalesOS |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Price** | $99/user/mo | $90/user/mo | ~$15,000+/year (team) |
| **Contact Database** | 275M+ contacts included | Not included (separate purchase) | 100M+ contacts included |
| **Email Sequences** | Yes | Yes | Limited |
| **Built-in Dialer** | Yes | Yes (paid add-on) | Yes |
| **CRM** | Basic, built-in | Full-featured CRM | No (requires integration) |
| **Intent Data** | Yes | No (needs third-party) | Yes |
| **Free Tier** | Yes (generous) | Yes (CRM only) | No |

Apollo sits in an interesting middle ground. It's significantly cheaper than ZoomInfo while offering a comparable contact database. Compared to [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/), Apollo bundles the database and intent data that HubSpot charges separately for — but HubSpot's CRM is far more mature.

If you primarily need prospecting data plus sequencing, Apollo gives you more for less. If you need a full CRM with marketing automation, HubSpot is the better foundation.

## Which Plan Is Right for You?

**Pick Free if** you're a solo founder or freelancer doing light outbound. You'll get enough email credits to validate whether cold outreach works for your business before spending anything.

**Pick Basic ($59/mo) if** you're an individual rep or a team of two to three who live in email. The unlimited email credits and A/B testing pay for themselves quickly.

**Pick Professional ($99/mo) if** you have a sales team that makes calls. The dialer, advanced intent data, and reporting make this the plan most growing teams land on.

**Pick Organization ($149/mo) if** your company requires SSO, audit logs, or has 20+ seats. The security and governance features are table stakes at that scale.

One more thing worth noting: Apollo offers a 14-day free trial on paid plans, so you can test Professional features before committing. Start there, and downgrade to Basic if you don't use the dialer.
