**[Jotform](/tools/jotform/)** has five pricing tiers, and the headline numbers don't tell the full story. Every plan except Enterprise is limited to a single user. Storage caps are tighter than you'd expect. And if you need HIPAA compliance, you're locked into the $99/month Gold plan — there's no cheaper option.

Here's every Jotform plan broken down with the actual limits, hidden costs, and when it makes sense to pay.

## Jotform Plans at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly (Annual) | Forms | Submissions/mo | Storage | Users |
|------|-------------------|-------|----------------|---------|-------|
| Free | $0 | 5 | 100 | 100 MB | 1 |
| Bronze | $34/month | 25 | 1,000 | 1 GB | 1 |
| Silver | $39/month | 50 | 2,500 | 10 GB | 1 |
| Gold | $99/month | 100 | 10,000 | 100 GB | 1 |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited |

These are annual billing prices. Monthly billing adds roughly 15–25% depending on the plan. All limits are hard caps — Jotform disables your forms when you hit a limit, not after a grace period.

## Free Plan: Functional but Tight

Jotform's free plan is more useful than [Typeform](/tools/typeform/)'s (which caps you at 10 responses), but the constraints add up:

- **5 forms** — not per project, total across your account
- **100 submissions/month** — shared across all forms
- **100 MB storage** — file uploads eat into this quickly
- **Jotform branding** on all forms
- **1,000 monthly form views** — a limit most other builders don't impose

The form view limit is the one that catches people off guard. If your form page gets 1,000 visits in a month — regardless of how many people actually submit — the form stops loading for new visitors. For a form embedded on a moderately trafficked website, 1,000 views is nothing.

That said, Jotform's free plan includes conditional logic, payment collection, and 100+ integrations. For internal or low-traffic forms, it's workable.

## Bronze Plan ($34/month)

Bronze is the entry-level paid plan, and it's where most individual users land.

**What you get:**
- 25 forms
- 1,000 submissions/month
- 1 GB storage
- 10,000 form views/month
- No Jotform branding
- All integrations
- Payment processing (Stripe, PayPal, Square)

**What to watch:** 1 GB of storage sounds fine until you accept file uploads. A form that collects resumes or images will burn through 1 GB in weeks. And the single-user limit means you can't add a teammate to help manage responses — you'd need Enterprise for that.

At $34/month, Bronze competes directly with Typeform's Plus plan at $50/month (1,000 responses, 3 users). Jotform gives you the same response capacity for $16 less, but Typeform includes 3 users while Jotform limits you to one.

## Silver Plan ($39/month)

Silver is only $5 more than Bronze but jumps to 2,500 submissions and 10 GB storage. Dollar for dollar, it's the best value in Jotform's lineup.

**What you get over Bronze:**
- 50 forms (up from 25)
- 2,500 submissions/month (up from 1,000)
- 10 GB storage (up from 1 GB)
- 25,000 form views/month
- Priority support

For $5 more than Bronze, you get 2.5x the submissions and 10x the storage. Silver is the obvious pick over Bronze.

## Gold Plan ($99/month)

Gold is Jotform's premium tier, and it's the only plan with HIPAA compliance.

**What you get:**
- 100 forms
- 10,000 submissions/month
- 100 GB storage
- 100,000 form views/month
- HIPAA compliance
- Priority support

**HIPAA is the big deal here.** If you're a medical practice, therapist, healthcare startup, or any organization handling protected health information, HIPAA compliance is non-negotiable. Among major form builders, Jotform is one of the few that offers it — and only on Gold and Enterprise.

The $99/month price tag is steep, but dedicated HIPAA-compliant form platforms typically charge more. For users who don't need HIPAA, Gold's main draw is the 10,000-submission limit and 100 GB storage.

## Enterprise Plan (Custom Pricing)

Enterprise removes all limits and adds multi-user access, SSO/SAML, dedicated support, custom domains, and SLA guarantees. Pricing isn't published — reports suggest it starts around $200–300/month.

The key point: if you need multiple users on Jotform, Enterprise is your only option. Every other plan is capped at one user, which pushes teams to Enterprise faster than submission limits do.

## Hidden Costs and Gotchas

### Every Plan Is Single-User (Except Enterprise)

This is Jotform's most surprising limitation. Free, Bronze, Silver, and Gold all allow exactly one user. No collaborators, no shared access, no view-only teammates. If two people on your team need to see form responses, you either share login credentials (against Jotform's terms of service) or pay for Enterprise.

Most competitors handle this differently. [Typeform](/tools/typeform/) allows 3 users on Plus ($50/month) and 5 on Business ($83/month). [Tally](/tools/tally/) Pro includes team workspaces for $29/month.

### Submission Limits Are Hard Caps

When you hit your monthly submission limit, Jotform deactivates your forms entirely. No overage billing, no grace period. For lead generation or customer intake forms, a deactivated form means lost revenue. Monitor your counts mid-month.

### Form View Limits and Storage

The free plan's 1,000 monthly form views counts page loads, not submissions. A form on a moderately trafficked page can exhaust this before anyone submits. Paid plans have higher view limits (10,000–100,000).

Bronze's 1 GB storage fills quickly if you accept file uploads. A single form collecting resumes or photos can consume it in weeks. Start on Silver (10 GB) if file uploads matter.

## Discounts

- **Nonprofits:** 50% off any paid plan. Apply through Jotform's nonprofit program with proof of 501(c)(3) status.
- **Education:** Discounted pricing for educational institutions. Details available through Jotform's education program.
- **Annual billing:** 15–25% savings compared to monthly billing across all plans.

## Which Jotform Plan Should You Pick?

- **Free:** Testing Jotform, internal forms with under 100 submissions/month, personal projects.
- **Silver (not Bronze):** The $5 difference from Bronze isn't worth debating. Silver gives you 2.5x submissions and 10x storage. Start here if you're paying.
- **Gold:** You need HIPAA compliance. That's the primary reason to jump to $99/month.
- **Enterprise:** You need multiple users or unlimited everything. Get a quote.

Skip Bronze. Either Free is enough or Silver is worth the extra $5.
