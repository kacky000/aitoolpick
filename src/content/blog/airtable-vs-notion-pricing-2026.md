# Airtable vs Notion Pricing 2026: $20 Database vs $10 Workspace

Airtable and Notion overlap enough to feel like rivals, but their paid plans tell the real story: Airtable's first serious tier is $20/seat/month, while Notion's is $10/user/month — exactly double. Airtable is a no-code database built for structured data at scale; Notion is an all-in-one workspace where databases are one feature among many. The 2x price gap is the clearest signal of where each tool puts its weight.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Airtable | Notion |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Free** | $0, 1,000 records/base | $0, generous personal use |
| **Entry paid** | Team — $20/seat/mo, 50,000 records | Plus — $10/user/mo, unlimited blocks |
| **Higher tier** | Business — $45/seat/mo, 125,000 records | Business — $18/user/mo |
| **What it is** | No-code relational database | All-in-one workspace |

## The Free Tiers

**Airtable Free ($0)** gives the core experience — multiple bases, multiple tables, grid/kanban/calendar/gallery views, and basic field types, with 1 GB attachments per base. The hard ceiling is **1,000 records per base** and 100 automation runs/month, which serious data outgrows fast.

**Notion Free ($0)** gives unlimited personal pages and blocks — plenty for notes, docs, and light databases. Limits are guest sharing (10 people), upload size, and page history, not record counts.

Verdict on free: Notion's free tier stretches further for individuals; Airtable's free tier is fine for testing but the 1,000-record cap bites quickly on real data.

## Entry Paid: Team ($20) vs Plus ($10)

**Airtable Team — $20/seat/month** (billed annually; ~20% more monthly). This is where Airtable earns its keep: records jump to 50,000 per base, 25,000 automation runs/month, custom branded forms, plus Gantt and timeline views. It's built for teams managing structured data — CRMs, inventories, content pipelines — where relational tables and views matter. At 5 seats that's $100/month or $1,200/year, so Airtable is positioned as a premium tool.

**Notion Plus — $10/user/month** (annual; $12 monthly). Half the price, and it unlocks unlimited file uploads, more guests, and full Notion AI on a workspace that already does notes, wikis, docs, and project boards. Notion's databases are flexible and great for many teams, but they're not built for the record volumes or relational depth Airtable handles.

The real difference: Airtable is a *database first* that happens to do collaboration; Notion is a *workspace first* that happens to do databases. You're paying double for Airtable because structured data at scale is its whole reason to exist.

## Where They Go Higher

**Airtable Business ($45/seat/month)** pushes to 125,000 records/base and 100,000 automation runs, with advanced admin and security — for data-heavy organizations. **Notion Business ($18/user/month annual)** adds advanced permissions, analytics, and collaboration for growing teams. Even at the higher tier, Airtable stays meaningfully pricier — the cost of a purpose-built database engine.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Managing structured/relational data — CRM, inventory, pipelines?** Airtable Team ($20/seat/mo).
- **Want one flexible tool for notes, docs, and light databases?** Notion Plus ($10/user/mo).
- **Data-heavy org needing 125k records and admin controls?** Airtable Business ($45/seat/mo).
- **Growing team needing permissions and analytics?** Notion Business ($18/user/mo).
- **On a budget?** Notion Free is far more forgiving than Airtable's 1,000-record cap.
- **Honest take?** Pay double for Airtable only if relational data at scale is the point; otherwise Notion does more for less.

## The Bottom Line

Airtable costs exactly twice Notion's entry plan, and the gap is honest. Airtable Team ($20/seat) is a true no-code database — 50,000 records, relational tables, branded forms — worth every dollar if structured data is the job. Notion Plus ($10/user) is an all-in-one workspace that does notes, docs, projects, and lighter databases for half the price. Pick Airtable when your data is the product; pick Notion when you want one flexible workspace and your databases are modest. Many teams run both: Airtable as the data backbone, Notion as the knowledge hub.
