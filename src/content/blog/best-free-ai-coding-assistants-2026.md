In 2026, "free" AI coding assistant means very different things depending on the tool. Some give you a genuinely usable tier forever; others offer a trial that throttles to near-uselessness after a week. Because frontier models running agentic tasks are expensive to serve, the free tiers are where vendors draw their sharpest lines. Here are the best free AI coding assistants in 2026 — and an honest look at where each one's limits actually bite.

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Free tier type | Best free use | Paid starts at |
|------|------|-----------|------|
| **GitHub Copilot** | Limited monthly completions | Everyday coding | $10/mo |
| **Windsurf** | Ongoing free tier | Agent-style help | $15/mo |
| **Cursor** | Hobby tier | Trial + light use | $20/mo |
| **Codeium** | Generous individual plan | Unlimited completion | Free core |
| **Gemini Code Assist** | Free individual tier | High completion limits | $19/mo |

## How to Read a "Free" Tier

Three questions cut through the marketing. **Is it forever or a trial?** A perpetual free tier you can rely on differs sharply from a 14-day taste. **What's metered — completions or premium model calls?** Most tools give generous basic completion and ration the expensive frontier-model agent requests. **Does it train on your code?** Some free tiers use your code to improve models; for proprietary work that's a dealbreaker. Judge free tiers on these, not on headline claims.

## The Best Free AI Coding Assistants

### 1. GitHub Copilot — Best free tier for everyday coding

Copilot now offers a free tier with a monthly allotment of completions and chat interactions — enough for light, regular use across VS Code and JetBrains. It's the best balance of quality and accessibility, and stepping up to the $10/month paid plan is the cheapest serious upgrade in the category. For most developers testing the waters, Copilot's free tier is the place to start. See our [Cursor vs Copilot pricing](/blog/cursor-vs-github-copilot-pricing-2026/) comparison for the upgrade math.

### 2. Windsurf — Best free agent-style experience

Windsurf's free tier is more generous than most for agentic features, letting you trial its AI-first editor and Cascade agent without paying. For developers who want to feel what multi-file AI editing is like before committing, it's the strongest free agent experience. Paid Pro is $15/month — the lowest entry among the premium editors. Compare it in our [Cursor vs Windsurf](/blog/cursor-vs-windsurf-2026/) breakdown.

### 3. Cursor — Best for trialing a premium editor

Cursor's free Hobby tier includes a limited number of premium model requests plus slower completions — enough to evaluate the editor and handle light work, though heavy users hit limits quickly. It's the best way to trial what many consider the leading AI editor before paying $20/month for Pro. Details in our [Cursor pricing guide](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/).

### 4. Codeium — Best genuinely-free completion

Codeium remains the standout for developers who want strong autocomplete at no cost, with a generous individual plan that doesn't meter basic completion the way others do. It won't match the frontier-model agent depth of paid Cursor or Claude Code, but for unlimited everyday completion across many editors, it's the most genuinely free option.

### 5. Gemini Code Assist — Best free completion limits

Google's Gemini Code Assist offers a free individual tier with notably high completion and chat limits, backed by Gemini models. For developers in the Google ecosystem or those wanting generous free usage without a hard monthly cap, it's a strong choice. Paid Standard runs around $19/month for team features.

## Where Free Tiers Fall Short

Free tiers are excellent for completion and light agent use, but they share predictable limits: premium frontier-model requests are tightly rationed, large agentic tasks (whole-repo refactors) need a paid plan, and some tiers train on your code. If your work is heavy, autonomous, or proprietary, budget for a paid plan — our [best AI coding assistants](/blog/best-ai-coding-assistants-2026/) roundup covers the paid leaders.

## The Verdict

For most developers, **GitHub Copilot's** free tier is the best starting point — quality help at no cost with the cheapest upgrade path. **Windsurf** offers the best free agent experience, **Codeium** the most genuinely unlimited completion, and **Cursor's** Hobby tier the best trial of a premium editor.
