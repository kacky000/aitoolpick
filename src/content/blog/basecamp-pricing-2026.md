[Basecamp](/tools/basecamp/) does something almost no project management tool does in 2026 — it offers a flat-rate plan where you pay one price regardless of team size. That pricing model is either a massive bargain or a bad deal, depending entirely on how many people are on your team.

This guide breaks down every Basecamp plan, calculates the exact break-even point, and compares costs against [Asana](/tools/asana/), [ClickUp](/tools/clickup/), and [Monday.com](/tools/monday/) so you can see where the math lands for your situation.

## Basecamp Plans at a Glance

| Plan | Price | Users | Storage | Best For |
|------|-------|-------|---------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited | 1 GB | Trying Basecamp out |
| Plus | $15/user/month | Per user | 500 GB | Small to mid-size teams |
| Pro Unlimited | $299/month (annual) | Unlimited | 5 TB | Large teams (20+) |

*Pro Unlimited costs $349/month if you pay monthly instead of annually.*

---

## Free Plan — Enough to Test, Not Enough to Use

Basecamp's free plan limits you to a single project with 1 GB of storage. You get the core features — to-dos, message boards, and Campfire chat — but with only one project, it's really just a trial.

There's no time limit on the free plan, which is nice. You can run a small side project on it indefinitely. But the moment you need a second project, you're upgrading.

**Verdict:** Use it to evaluate whether Basecamp's workflow fits your team. Don't plan on staying here.

---

## Plus Plan — $15/User/Month

The Plus plan is Basecamp's standard per-user tier. Every user costs $15/month, billed annually. You get:

- **Unlimited projects**
- **500 GB storage** (shared across the team)
- **All core features:** To-dos, message boards, Campfire chat, schedules, docs & files, Hill Charts
- **Guest access for free** — clients and contractors don't count toward your user limit
- **Priority support**

The free guest access is a genuine differentiator. Most competitors charge per seat regardless of whether someone is a full member or an occasional guest. In Basecamp, your client can check project status without adding to your bill.

At $15/user/month, here's what real teams pay:

| Team Size | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| 5 users | $75/month | $900/year |
| 10 users | $150/month | $1,800/year |
| 15 users | $225/month | $2,700/year |
| 20 users | $300/month | $3,600/year |
| 30 users | $450/month | $5,400/year |

Notice anything? At 20 users, you're paying $300/month — which is almost exactly what the Pro Unlimited plan costs.

---

## Pro Unlimited — $299/Month (Flat Rate)

This is the plan that makes Basecamp unusual. Pro Unlimited costs $299/month (billed annually) or $349/month (billed monthly), and it includes every user on your team. Five people or five hundred — same price.

What you get beyond the Plus plan:

- **Unlimited users** at no additional cost
- **5 TB storage** (10x the Plus plan)
- **Priority support with a 1-hour response guarantee**
- **Admin pro pack:** project templates, centralized admin controls, move projects between teams

---

## The Break-Even Point: 20 Users

The math here is straightforward:

- **Plus plan:** $15 × number of users
- **Pro Unlimited:** $299/month (annual) or $349/month (monthly)

At **20 users**, the Plus plan costs $300/month — essentially the same as Pro Unlimited at $299/month. Add a 21st person and Pro Unlimited is already saving you money.

| Team Size | Plus Cost | Pro Unlimited | Savings with Pro |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| 10 users | $150/month | $299/month | -$149 (Plus is cheaper) |
| 15 users | $225/month | $299/month | -$74 (Plus is cheaper) |
| 20 users | $300/month | $299/month | $1 (break-even) |
| 30 users | $450/month | $299/month | $151 saved |
| 50 users | $750/month | $299/month | $451 saved |
| 100 users | $1,500/month | $299/month | $1,201 saved |

The savings compound fast. A 50-person company saves over $5,400/year. A 100-person company saves over $14,400/year. No other major PM tool offers this kind of scaling economics.

## What's Included in Every Paid Plan

Basecamp keeps its feature set intentionally simple. Both Plus and Pro Unlimited include:

- **To-dos:** Task lists with assignments, due dates, and notes. No subtasks or custom fields — it's deliberately minimal.
- **Message Boards:** Long-form threaded discussions. Basecamp uses these instead of channels or real-time chat for most communication.
- **Campfire:** Real-time group chat within each project. Think of it as a lightweight Slack replacement.
- **Schedule:** Shared calendar for deadlines and events. Basic but functional.
- **Docs & Files:** Document storage and sharing within projects.
- **Hill Charts:** Basecamp's unique visual progress tracker. Teams plot tasks on a hill shape — uphill means figuring things out, downhill means executing.

---

## What Basecamp Doesn't Include

This is where Basecamp's philosophy of "less is more" cuts both ways:

- **No Gantt charts.** If you need dependency mapping and critical path analysis, Basecamp doesn't have it.
- **No built-in time tracking.** You'll need a third-party integration like Toggl or Harvest.
- **No advanced reporting.** There are no burndown charts, velocity metrics, or custom dashboards.
- **No custom fields or workflows.** You can't add dropdown menus, tags, or status columns to tasks.
- **No automations.** Unlike Asana or Monday.com, there's no "when X happens, do Y" automation engine.

For teams that want these features, the workaround is integrations — but that means additional cost and complexity.

## How Basecamp Compares to Competitors

| Tool | Entry Price | Per-User Cost | Flat Rate Option | Free Plan |
|------|------------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Basecamp** | $15/user/mo | $15/user/mo | $299/mo unlimited | 1 project |
| **Asana** | $10.99/user/mo | $10.99–$24.99/user/mo | No | 10 users |
| **ClickUp** | $7/user/mo | $7–$12/user/mo | No | Unlimited |
| **Monday.com** | $9/seat/mo | $9–$19/seat/mo | 2 users |  No |

At small team sizes, Basecamp is actually the most expensive option per user. ClickUp at $7/user and Monday.com at $9/seat both undercut it. But the dynamic reverses completely once you cross 20 users — no competitor can match $299/month for unlimited seats.

## Who Basecamp Works Best For

**Good fit:**
- **Teams of 20+ people** where the flat-rate plan saves real money
- **Teams that value simplicity** and don't want to configure complex workflows
- **Companies with many external collaborators** (free guest access is valuable)
- **Remote teams** that prefer async communication over constant meetings

**Not a good fit:**
- **Small teams (1–5 people)** — per-user tools like ClickUp or Monday.com will cost less
- **Teams needing advanced PM features** — no Gantt charts, time tracking, or custom fields
- **Software development teams** — no sprint planning, story points, or dev-specific workflows (look at Jira or Linear)
- **Data-driven managers** — minimal reporting compared to Asana or Monday.com

---

## Bottom Line

Basecamp's pricing makes the most sense at scale. Below 20 users, you're paying a premium for simplicity. Above 20 users, the $299/month flat rate becomes one of the best deals in project management software.

The real question isn't whether you can afford Basecamp — it's whether Basecamp's intentionally limited feature set matches how your team actually works. If you want a simple, opinionated tool and your team is large enough to benefit from flat-rate pricing, Basecamp is hard to beat.
