**Zapier** is a popular software tool used by individuals and teams for productivity and collaboration. In this review, we evaluate its features, pricing, pros, cons, and alternatives for 2026.

Zapier has been the go-to automation platform for years, connecting 7,000+ apps without code. But with [Make](/tools/make/) (formerly Integromat) gaining ground and AI automations becoming standard, is Zapier still the best choice in 2026?

## What Is Zapier?

Zapier connects your apps and automates workflows through "Zaps" — automated workflows triggered by events. When something happens in one app (a trigger), Zapier automatically does something in another app (an action).

**Example:** When you receive a Gmail attachment → save it to [Google Drive](/tools/google-drive/) → notify you on Slack.

## Pricing

| Plan | Price | Tasks/Month | Zaps |
|------|-------|-------------|------|
| Free | $0 | 100 | 5 |
| Professional | $29.99/mo | 750 | Unlimited |
| Team | $103.50/mo | 2,000 | Unlimited |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Unlimited |

**Important:** Pricing is based on tasks (individual actions), not Zaps. A 5-step Zap that runs once uses 5 tasks. This adds up fast.

## What's Good

### Massive App Library
7,000+ app integrations [make](/tools/make/) Zapier the largest automation ecosystem. If an app exists, Zapier probably connects to it.

### Easy to Use
The visual builder is intuitive. Non-technical users can create multi-step automations in minutes. No code required.

### AI Actions
Zapier's AI features can extract data, classify content, and generate text within your Zaps. Powered by GPT, these actions add intelligence to your automations.

### Tables & Interfaces
Zapier now includes Tables (a simple database) and Interfaces (form/page builder), turning it into a lightweight app platform beyond just automation.

### Reliability
Zaps are generally reliable with good error handling, retry logic, and detailed logs.

## What's Not Great

### Pricing Gets Expensive
The task-based model means costs scale quickly. A team running 50 multi-step Zaps can easily burn through thousands of tasks per month.

### Free Plan Is Very Limited
100 tasks and 5 Zaps per month is barely enough to test the platform. You'll hit limits fast.

### Complex Zaps Can Break
Multi-step Zaps with branching logic sometimes fail silently or become fragile when connected apps update their APIs.

### No Self-Hosting
For privacy-conscious organizations, there's no self-hosted option. All data flows through Zapier's servers.

## Zapier vs Make

The biggest alternative to Zapier is Make (formerly Integromat).

| Feature | Zapier | Make |
|---------|--------|------|
| App integrations | 7,000+ | 1,500+ |
| Ease of use | Easier | Steeper learning curve |
| Pricing model | Per task | Per operation |
| Complex workflows | Good | Better (visual branching) |
| Starting price | $29.99/mo | $10.59/mo |
| Free plan | 100 tasks | 1,000 operations |

**Bottom line:** Make is cheaper and more powerful for complex workflows. Zapier is easier and has more integrations.

## Who Is Zapier Best For?

**Great for:**
- Non-technical users who need simple automations
- Teams that use many different SaaS tools
- [Marketers](/best-for/marketers/) connecting forms, CRMs, and email tools
- Small businesses automating repetitive tasks

**Not ideal for:**
- Budget-conscious teams (Make is cheaper)
- Complex data transformations (Make handles these better)
- Privacy-sensitive organizations (no self-hosting)

## Final Verdict

Zapier remains the easiest way to automate workflows between apps. Its massive integration library is unmatched. But pricing is its weakness — if you're running complex automations at scale, Make offers better value.

**Recommendation:** Start with Zapier if you're new to automation. Switch to Make if you outgrow Zapier's pricing or need more complex workflows.
