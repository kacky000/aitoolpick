**n8n** and **Zapier** are two of the most popular workflow automation platforms in 2026 — but they're built for very different audiences. Zapier is the approachable no-code solution for non-technical teams, while n8n is the open-source powerhouse favored by developers who want full control without per-task pricing.

This guide breaks down every important difference so you can [make](/tools/make/) the right call.

## Quick Verdict

**Choose Zapier** if you want the easiest onboarding, the largest app library (7,000+ integrations), and you're comfortable paying more for convenience. It's perfect for marketers, ops teams, and anyone who just needs automations working fast.

**Choose n8n** if you're technically comfortable, want to self-host for free, need complex logic with JavaScript/Python nodes, or are building AI agent workflows. The cost savings alone can be massive at scale.

For a side-by-side feature breakdown, see our [n8n vs Zapier comparison page](/compare/n8n-vs-zapier).

## Pricing Comparison

| Plan | Zapier | n8n |
|------|--------|-----|
| Free | 100 tasks/mo, 5 Zaps | Free (self-hosted, unlimited) |
| Starter/Core | $19.99/mo (750 tasks) | $24/mo (cloud, 2,500 executions) |
| Professional/Pro | $49/mo (2,000 tasks) | $60/mo (cloud, 10,000 executions) |
| Team | $69/mo | $140/mo |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |

The key difference: **n8n self-hosting is completely free forever**. If you can run a [Docker](/tools/docker/) container, you pay nothing for unlimited workflows and executions. Zapier's free tier is limited to 100 tasks per month — easy to exhaust with a few active workflows.

For teams that run hundreds of automations monthly, Zapier's costs can spiral quickly. n8n's cloud pricing is also lower tier-for-tier once you compare execution volume.

## App Integrations

Zapier wins on breadth with **7,000+ integrations**. If you use obscure or niche SaaS tools, Zapier almost certainly supports them out of the [box](/tools/box/).

n8n supports **400+ native integrations** but comes with a killer advantage: every node can run custom JavaScript or Python code. Its HTTP Request node lets you connect to any REST API, and the community maintains hundreds of additional nodes. In practice, n8n can connect to almost anything — it just requires slightly more effort for non-standard tools.

**Winner: Zapier** for plug-and-play integrations. **n8n** for custom or technical connections.

## Ease of Use

Zapier's [linear](/tools/linear/) "Trigger → Action" model is the easiest automation interface in the industry. Most non-technical users build their first Zap in under five minutes.

n8n uses a **visual canvas-based workflow editor** where you connect nodes by dragging lines between them. It's more powerful and handles branching, loops, and error handling far better — but the learning curve is steeper. Budget a few hours to get comfortable if you're new to it.

If you've never used automation tools before, check out our [how to choose an automation tool guide](/blog/how-to-choose-automation-tool) first.

**Winner: Zapier** for beginners. **n8n** for technical users who want full control.

## AI Agent Capabilities

This is where n8n has surged ahead in 2026. n8n's **AI Agent nodes** let you build complex multi-step AI workflows — think: receive an email, extract data with an LLM, query a database, and reply automatically. You can wire up [Claude](/tools/claude/), GPT-4o, Gemini, or any local model directly into your workflows.

Zapier has added AI actions and a Copilot feature, but it remains more of a "trigger this AI tool" connector rather than a true AI workflow builder.

**Winner: n8n** for AI agent workflows. Zapier works for simple AI integrations.

## Self-Hosting and Data Privacy

n8n's self-hosting option is unmatched. Run it on your own server, control all your data, and never worry about per-task pricing or API keys leaving your infrastructure. This is a critical advantage for companies with compliance requirements (healthcare, finance, legal).

Zapier is cloud-only. All your workflow data flows through Zapier's servers. For most businesses this is fine, but regulated industries may have concerns.

**Winner: n8n** for privacy and self-hosting. Zapier for managed cloud simplicity.

## When to Choose Zapier

- Your team is non-technical and needs automations running today
- You rely on niche tools that only Zapier supports
- You want point-and-click reliability without maintenance overhead
- Your automation volume is relatively low (under 2,000 tasks/month)

## When to Choose n8n

- You're a developer or have technical team members
- You want to self-host and control costs
- You're building AI agent workflows or complex multi-step logic
- You need custom code execution inside your workflows
- You're scaling to thousands of workflow runs monthly

## Verdict

For most technical teams in 2026, **n8n is the better choice** — especially for AI-powered workflows and cost-sensitive scaling. The free self-hosted tier alone makes it worth evaluating seriously.

Zapier remains the gold standard for non-technical users who need speed and simplicity. If your team doesn't have a developer and you just need things connected, Zapier is still worth the price.
