# v0 vs Lovable Pricing 2026: ~$20 Code Export vs $25 Full-Stack

v0 and Lovable both generate working apps from a prompt, but they answer different questions. v0 (by Vercel) asks "what code do you want?" and hands you exportable Next.js + Tailwind + shadcn/ui you own outright. Lovable asks "what app do you want shipped?" and gives you a hosted, full-stack product with a database and auth baked in. Their entry prices sit $5 apart — v0 Premium at ~$20/month, Lovable Starter at $25/month — but what you walk away with is the real decision.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | v0 | Lovable |
|---|------|---------|
| **Free** | Limited monthly credits | 5 credits/day (~30/mo max) |
| **Entry paid** | Premium — ~$20/mo, ~$20 credits + higher limits | Starter — $25/mo, 100 credits |
| **Next tier** | Team — ~$30/user/mo, pooled credits | Pro — $50/mo, 400 credits |
| **Metering** | Credits | Credits (one per AI message/edit) |
| **You get** | Exportable Next.js code you own | Hosted full-stack app (Supabase-backed) |

## The Free Tiers

**v0 Free** gives a monthly credit allowance — enough to prototype a few screens. Since v0 emits standard React/Next.js, you can lift anything it makes into your own repo even on the free plan.

**Lovable Free** gives 5 credits per day (about 30/month). Each credit is roughly one AI message or edit, so you can stand up a small app and test whether the full-stack output matches your needs. It throttles daily, but the per-credit model makes it easy to see exactly how many moves remain.

Verdict on free: v0 if you want portable code, Lovable if you want to watch a complete, hosted app come together.

## Entry Paid: Premium (~$20) vs Starter ($25)

**v0 Premium — ~$20/month.** Adds ~$20 in credits, higher limits, and the strongest models. The defining trait is ownership: everything v0 generates is exportable Next.js you control. v0 is an accelerator for developers who already have a stack and want clean components fast — not a hosting platform.

**Lovable Starter — $25/month.** Gives 100 credits/month, where one credit ≈ one AI message or edit. Lovable's output is production-shaped full-stack: it provisions a Supabase backend, auth, and a database, then hosts the result. For a non-developer or a builder who wants the whole app shipped without wiring infrastructure, that's the appeal. Credits reset monthly and don't roll over, so heavy iteration hits the 100-credit ceiling fairly quickly.

The real difference: v0 gives you code to keep building with; Lovable gives you a deployed app you don't have to assemble. If you can code and want control, v0's ~$20 wins. If you want a working full-stack product without touching infrastructure, Lovable's $25 earns the premium.

## Going Heavier: v0 Team vs Lovable Pro

**v0 Team (~$30/user/month)** pools credits across members and adds collaboration — built for teams iterating on UI together. **Lovable Pro ($50/month)** isn't a team tier; it's the power-user single seat with 400 credits (4x Starter) plus collaboration. So v0's next step adds *people* with shared credits; Lovable's next step adds *capacity* for one heavy builder. Match the path to whether you're scaling a team or your own output.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Developer who wants to own clean Next.js code?** v0 Premium (~$20/mo).
- **Want a hosted full-stack app shipped without wiring a backend?** Lovable Starter ($25/mo).
- **Solo power user iterating on many projects?** Lovable Pro ($50/mo) for 4x credits.
- **UI team collaborating on components?** v0 Team (~$30/user/mo, pooled credits).
- **Just testing?** v0 free to keep the code, Lovable free for credit clarity.

## The Bottom Line

The $5 gap is irrelevant; ownership versus hosting is everything. v0 hands you exportable Next.js code to build with in your own stack. Lovable hands you a deployed, full-stack app you never have to assemble. Pick v0 if you're a developer who wants control of the code; pick Lovable if you want a finished, hosted product and would rather skip the infrastructure entirely.
