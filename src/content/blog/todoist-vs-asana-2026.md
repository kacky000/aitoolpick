Choosing between Todoist and Asana is one of the most common decisions for anyone looking to get organized. Both tools are excellent at what they do, but they serve different types of users.

In this comparison, we break down exactly where each tool shines — and where it falls short — so you can pick the right one for your workflow.

## Quick Overview

**Todoist** is a lightweight, fast task manager built for individuals and small teams who want simplicity. It costs $4/month for Pro and $6/user/month for Business.

**Asana** is a full-featured work management platform designed for teams that need project tracking, timelines, and cross-team visibility. It starts at $10.99/user/month for the Starter plan.

If you want a deeper look at each tool individually, check out our [Todoist review](/blog/todoist-review-2026) and [Asana review](/blog/asana-review-2026).

## Feature Comparison

### Task Management

Both tools handle basic task creation well, but the approach differs significantly.

Todoist uses natural language input — type "Submit report every Friday at 3pm" and it automatically parses the due date and recurrence. This makes it incredibly fast for personal task capture.

Asana treats tasks as part of a larger project structure. Each task can live in multiple projects, have subtasks, dependencies, and custom fields. This makes it better for team coordination but adds complexity.

**Winner: Todoist** for individual productivity, **Asana** for team task management.

### Project Views

Todoist offers list view, board (Kanban) view, and calendar view. It's clean and fast but limited in visualization options.

Asana provides list, board, timeline (Gantt), and calendar views, plus portfolios for tracking multiple projects. The timeline view alone is worth it for teams managing complex projects with dependencies.

**Winner: Asana** — more views, more flexibility for teams.

### Collaboration

Todoist's collaboration features are basic: you can share projects, assign tasks, and leave comments. It works for small teams but lacks the depth larger teams need.

Asana was built for collaboration from day one. Features like project status updates, workload management, forms for intake, and goals tracking make it a true team platform.

**Winner: Asana** by a wide margin for teams.

### Integrations

Todoist integrates with 80+ tools including Slack, Google Calendar, and Zapier. The integrations are solid but focused on the essentials.

Asana offers 200+ native integrations plus deep connections with tools like Salesforce, Adobe, and Microsoft Teams. For enterprise environments, Asana's integration ecosystem is significantly stronger.

**Winner: Asana** for breadth, though Todoist covers the essentials well.

### Mobile Experience

This is where Todoist truly excels. The mobile app is fast, beautiful, and full-featured. Quick add with natural language processing makes capturing tasks on the go effortless.

Asana's mobile app is functional but can feel sluggish with larger workspaces. It's improved significantly over the past year, but Todoist still leads here.

**Winner: Todoist** — one of the best mobile task management experiences available.

## Pricing Comparison

| Plan | Todoist | Asana |
|------|---------|-------|
| Free | 5 projects, 5 collaborators | 10 users, basic features |
| Personal/Starter | $4/month (Pro) | $10.99/user/month |
| Team/Advanced | $6/user/month | $24.99/user/month |
| Enterprise | — | Custom pricing |

Todoist is dramatically cheaper. For a 10-person team, you'd pay $60/month on Todoist Business vs $109.90/month on Asana Starter. That gap widens with Asana's Advanced plan.

## Who Should Choose Todoist?

- **Individuals** who want a fast, clean task manager
- **Freelancers** juggling multiple clients and personal tasks
- **Students** who need a free or affordable organization tool
- **Small teams** (under 5 people) who need basic collaboration
- Anyone who values **speed and simplicity** over feature depth

## Who Should Choose Asana?

- **Teams of 5+** that need project coordination
- **Marketing teams** managing campaigns with timelines and dependencies
- **Agencies** handling multiple client projects simultaneously
- **Organizations** that need goals, portfolios, and workload management
- Teams already using **enterprise tools** that integrate deeply with Asana

## The Verdict

Todoist and Asana aren't really competing for the same users. Todoist is the best personal task manager on the market — fast, affordable, and beautifully designed. Asana is one of the best team project management platforms — powerful, flexible, and built for organizational workflows.

**Choose Todoist** if you want a personal productivity powerhouse that can handle light team collaboration.

**Choose Asana** if your team needs a structured work management platform with advanced project tracking.

Still undecided? Both offer generous free plans, so try each for a week with your actual workflow before committing.
