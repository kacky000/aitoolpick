Looking for the best slack alternatives? We tested and compared the top options available in 2026, evaluating features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance.

Slack changed how teams communicate at work, but it's not the only option. If you're looking for better pricing, tighter integrations with your existing tools, or a simpler experience, these Slack alternatives deserve a look.

## 1. Microsoft Teams — Best for Microsoft 365 Users

**Pricing:** Free / $4/user/mo (Essentials) / $12.50/user/mo (Business Standard)

If your organization already uses Microsoft 365, Teams is the obvious choice. It combines chat, video meetings, and deep Office integration in one platform.

**Why choose Teams over Slack:**
- Included with Microsoft 365 subscriptions
- Better [video conferencing](/category/video-conferencing/)
- Native Office document collaboration
- Larger meeting capacity (up to 10,000 participants)

**Where Slack wins:** Better third-party integrations, cleaner UX, less resource-heavy.

→ [Compare Slack vs Microsoft Teams](/blog/slack-vs-microsoft-teams-2026)

## 2. Discord — Best for Communities & Small Teams

**Pricing:** Free / $2.99/mo (Nitro Basic) / $9.99/mo (Nitro)

Discord isn't just for gamers anymore. Many startups and open-source teams use it for real-time communication with excellent voice channels.

**Why choose Discord over Slack:**
- Generous free plan with unlimited message history
- Always-on voice channels
- Screen sharing included free
- Great for community building

**Where Slack wins:** Professional features, enterprise security, workflow automations.

## 3. Google Chat — Best for Google Workspace Users

**Pricing:** Included with Google Workspace ($7/user/mo+)

Google Chat is tightly integrated with Gmail, [Google Drive](/tools/google-drive/), Docs, and Meet. If your team lives in Google Workspace, it's the natural communication layer.

**Why choose Google Chat over Slack:**
- Included with Google Workspace
- Native [Google Drive](/tools/google-drive/) integration
- Seamless [Google Meet](/tools/google-meet/) video calls
- Spaces for topic-based discussions

**Where Slack wins:** More integrations, better threading, richer app ecosystem.

## 4. Rocket.Chat — Best Open-Source Option

**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted) / $4/user/mo (Pro cloud)

For organizations that need full control over their data, Rocket.Chat offers a self-hosted, open-source alternative with end-to-end encryption.

**Why choose Rocket.Chat over Slack:**
- Self-hosted option for full data control
- Open-source and customizable
- End-to-end encryption
- No vendor lock-in

**Where Slack wins:** Easier setup, larger integration ecosystem, better UX.

## 5. Lark — Best All-in-One Suite

**Pricing:** Free / Custom pricing for enterprise

Lark (by ByteDance) bundles messaging, video meetings, docs, calendar, and [project management](/category/project-management/) into one platform — all for free for small teams.

**Why choose Lark over Slack:**
- Messaging + docs + calendar + video in one app
- Generous free plan
- Built-in project management
- Real-time document collaboration

**Where Slack wins:** More third-party integrations, larger Western user base.

## 6. Twist — Best for Async Communication

**Pricing:** Free / $6/user/mo (Unlimited)

Built by the [Todoist](/tools/todoist/) team, Twist is designed for async-first teams. Instead of real-time chat that demands constant attention, Twist organizes conversations into threads.

**Why choose Twist over Slack:**
- Async-first design reduces interruptions
- Better thread organization
- Cleaner notification management
- Great for remote/distributed teams

**Where Slack wins:** Real-time collaboration, more integrations, larger ecosystem.

**Already in Microsoft 365?** → Microsoft Teams is free for you.
**Need async-first messaging?** → Twist reduces notification fatigue.
**Want everything in one app?** → Lark bundles it all.

Most teams should start with whatever integrates best with their existing tools. Try the free plans and see what sticks.
