Meetings generate decisions, action items, and context — and most of it used to evaporate the moment the call ended. AI meeting assistants fixed that. In 2026 they join your calls, transcribe everything, summarize the discussion, and pull out action items automatically. The category has matured to the point where the question is no longer "should I use one?" but "which one?" Here are the seven best AI meeting assistants in 2026.

## What to Look For

A good AI meeting assistant in 2026 should deliver four things reliably: **accurate transcription** (including with accents and crosstalk), **useful summaries** (not just a wall of text), **action item extraction** (who owns what), and **integrations** that push those outputs into the tools you already use — calendar, CRM, Slack, docs. Privacy and recording consent handling matter too, especially for client calls.

## The Best AI Meeting Assistants

### 1. Otter.ai — Best for live transcription and collaboration

Otter remains the most recognizable name for a reason. Its live transcription is fast and accurate, you can follow along in real time, and its collaborative notes let teammates highlight and comment during the meeting. Otter's automated summaries and action items are solid, and it integrates with the major calendar and conferencing platforms. It is the safe default for general business use.

### 2. Fireflies.ai — Best for integrations and search

Fireflies shines on what happens after the meeting. It boasts deep CRM integrations and a powerful conversation search that lets you find any moment across all your past meetings. For teams that live in their CRM and treat meeting data as a searchable knowledge base, Fireflies is the strongest pick, and its entry price is among the lowest.

### 3. Fathom — Best free tier

Fathom built its reputation on a genuinely generous free plan that includes unlimited recording and transcription. The summaries are fast and clean, and it integrates with CRMs on paid tiers. If you want a capable assistant without paying, start with Fathom.

### 4. Granola — Best for augmenting your own notes

Granola takes a different philosophy: instead of fully automating note-taking, it enhances notes you type yourself during the call, merging your shorthand with a full AI transcript into polished notes afterward. People who like to stay engaged and jot their own points — rather than reading an auto-summary later — tend to love it.

### 5. Avoma — Best for sales and revenue teams

Avoma is built for revenue teams. Beyond transcription and summaries, it adds conversation intelligence — talk-time ratios, topic tracking, and coaching insights — plus tight CRM workflows. For sales managers who want to analyze and improve call performance, it is more than a notetaker.

### 6. tl;dv — Best for multilingual teams and clip sharing

tl;dv supports a wide range of languages and makes it easy to clip and share specific moments from a recording. For distributed, multilingual teams that need to pass around highlights rather than full recordings, it is a strong, affordable option.

### 7. Zoom AI Companion — Best for native Zoom users

If your organization already runs everything through Zoom, the built-in AI Companion summarizes meetings and surfaces action items without adding another tool or bot to the call. It is not the most feature-rich option, but the zero-friction, already-included convenience is compelling for Zoom-centric teams.

## How to Choose

- **General business use:** Otter.ai
- **CRM-heavy teams:** Fireflies.ai
- **On a budget:** Fathom
- **You prefer taking your own notes:** Granola
- **Sales coaching:** Avoma
- **Multilingual / clip sharing:** tl;dv
- **All-Zoom shop:** Zoom AI Companion

## The Verdict

For most teams in 2026, **Otter.ai** and **Fireflies.ai** are the two safest choices — Otter for live collaboration, Fireflies for integrations and search. If cost is the deciding factor, **Fathom**'s free tier is unmatched. And if you are particular about how meeting notes get made, **Granola**'s augment-your-own-notes approach is worth a look.

The good news is that nearly every option offers a free tier or trial, so you can test two or three on real meetings before committing. Pick based on where your meeting data needs to go after the call — that integration fit matters more than transcription quality, which is excellent across the board.
