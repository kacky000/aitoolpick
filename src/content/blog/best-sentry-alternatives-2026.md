Sentry is an excellent error-monitoring platform, but it isn't the right fit for everyone. Its event-based billing can get expensive at scale, and some teams want broader observability or open-source control. If you're shopping around, these seven **Sentry alternatives** cover every budget and use case in 2026.

---

## Why Look for a Sentry Alternative?

- **Event-based billing** can spike unpredictably with high error volume
- You want **full-stack observability**, not just error tracking
- You prefer **open-source** or self-hosted control
- You need tighter pricing for a small team or side project

For context on what you'd be moving away from, read our [Sentry review](/blog/sentry-review-2026) and [Sentry pricing breakdown](/blog/sentry-pricing-2026).

---

## 1. Datadog — Best for Full-Stack Observability

[Datadog](/blog/datadog-review-2026) goes far beyond error tracking with infrastructure monitoring, APM, logs, and synthetics in one platform. It's the natural choice if you want everything in one pane — though it's pricier and more complex. See our [Sentry vs Datadog comparison](/blog/sentry-vs-datadog-2026).

## 2. New Relic — Best All-in-One Free Tier

[New Relic](/blog/new-relic-review-2026) offers a generous free tier (100GB/month ingest) and a full observability suite. Its user-based pricing model is different from Sentry's event model and can be cheaper for teams with predictable usage.

## 3. Grafana — Best Open-Source Stack

[Grafana](/blog/grafana-review-2026), paired with Loki and Tempo, gives you a powerful open-source observability stack you can self-host for free. Ideal for teams that want control and don't mind operating the infrastructure themselves.

## 4. PostHog — Best for Product + Error Monitoring

[PostHog](/blog/posthog-review-2026) combines product analytics, session replay, and error tracking. It's a strong pick for product teams that want user behavior and error data together, with a usable free tier.

## 5. Rollbar — Best Lightweight Error Tracking

Rollbar is a focused, developer-friendly error-monitoring tool that competes directly with Sentry's core use case. It's simpler and often easier to reason about on price for pure error tracking.

## 6. Bugsnag — Best for Mobile Apps

Bugsnag specializes in application stability monitoring with strong mobile (iOS/Android) support and release health features. A good fit if mobile crash reporting is your priority.

## 7. GlitchTip — Best Open-Source Sentry Clone

GlitchTip is an open-source, Sentry-compatible error tracker you can self-host. If you like Sentry's approach but want to avoid usage-based billing, it's the closest drop-in replacement.

## How to Choose

| If you want... | Choose |
|----------------|--------|
| Full-stack observability | Datadog |
| Generous free tier | New Relic |
| Open-source control | Grafana or GlitchTip |
| Product + errors together | PostHog |
| Mobile crash reporting | Bugsnag |
| Simple error tracking | Rollbar |

## Verdict

Sentry remains a great default, but the best alternative depends on your priorities. Pick **Datadog or New Relic** for breadth, **Grafana or GlitchTip** for open-source control, and **Rollbar or Bugsnag** for focused error tracking. Compare pricing carefully — see our [Sentry pricing guide](/blog/sentry-pricing-2026) and [Datadog pricing guide](/blog/datadog-pricing-2026) before deciding.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best free Sentry alternative?

New Relic offers the most generous free tier among hosted tools, while Grafana and GlitchTip are free and open-source if you self-host.

### Is there an open-source alternative to Sentry?

Yes. GlitchTip is a Sentry-compatible open-source error tracker, and the Grafana stack (Loki/Tempo) provides open-source observability.

### What's a cheaper alternative to Sentry for error tracking?

Rollbar and GlitchTip are often cheaper for pure error tracking, especially at higher event volumes where Sentry's usage billing adds up.
