**[Coda](/tools/coda/)** and **[Notion](/tools/notion/)** are the two leading all-in-one workspace tools, and on the surface their pricing looks almost identical. But the way each one counts billable users — and what triggers an upgrade — makes the real cost very different depending on how your team works.

This guide compares every plan, the hidden upgrade triggers, and which tool is actually cheaper for solo users, small teams, and growing organizations.

## The Key Difference: Who Counts as a Billable User

This is where the comparison actually matters.

**Coda only bills "Doc Makers."** Anyone who edits the structure of a doc is a Maker and is billed. Editors who only enter data, and all viewers, are **free**. A team where 3 people build docs and 20 people use them pays for 3 seats.

**Notion bills every member.** On Plus and Business, each member with edit access is a paid seat. Guests have limits, but your core team is billed per head.

For lopsided teams (a few builders, many consumers), Coda is dramatically cheaper. For teams where everyone edits equally, the two are close — and Notion's flat $10 Plus tier often wins.

## Cost Scenario: A 10-Person Team

Say you have 10 people, but only 3 actively build and structure docs.

- **Coda Pro**: 3 Makers × $10 = **$30/month** (7 editors/viewers free)
- **Notion Plus**: 10 members × $10 = **$100/month**

That's a **3.3x difference** for the same team — entirely because of how seats are counted.

Now flip it: 10 people who all build and edit equally.

- **Coda Pro**: 10 Makers × $10 = **$100/month**
- **Notion Plus**: 10 members × $10 = **$100/month**

Identical. The Maker model only saves money when your editor-to-consumer ratio is low.

## Free Plan Comparison

Both free plans are generous but capped differently:

- **Coda Free**: Unlimited docs and editors, but **1,000 objects per doc** (rows, columns, buttons). You hit this fast on any real tracker.
- **Notion Free**: Unlimited blocks for individuals, but **collaborative workspaces are limited** and the block allowance shrinks for teams.

For solo evaluation, both work. Coda's object cap bites sooner if you build data-heavy docs; Notion's team limits bite sooner if you collaborate.

## Where AI Pricing Differs

- **Coda AI** is bundled into the **Team plan ($30)** — no separate add-on.
- **Notion AI** is now included in paid plans, but historically was a **$10/user/mo add-on**, which inflated effective cost.

If AI is core to your workflow, Coda's bundled approach on Team can be simpler, while Notion Plus + AI lands in similar territory.

## Verdict

**Choose Coda** if you have a small group of builders serving a large group of consumers. The Maker-based pricing can cut your bill by 3x or more, and the Pro tier at $10 is excellent value for formula-heavy, automation-driven docs.

**Choose Notion** if your whole team edits equally, you want the cleaner writing experience, or you value the larger template ecosystem. Notion Plus at $10/user is simple and predictable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Coda or Notion cheaper?

It depends on your team structure. Coda only bills "Makers" (people who build docs), so teams with few builders and many viewers pay far less. Notion bills every member, so equal-editing teams pay the same on both.

### Do viewers cost money in Coda?

No. In Coda, viewers and editors who only input data are free. You only pay for Doc Makers who build and structure docs.

### Does Notion charge for guests?

Guests have access limits but aren't full paid seats. Your internal members with edit access are the billable seats on Plus and Business.

### Which is better value for a solo user?

Both free plans work for solo use. If you build data-heavy docs, Coda Pro ($10) removes the object cap; if you write and organize notes, Notion's free tier is often enough.
