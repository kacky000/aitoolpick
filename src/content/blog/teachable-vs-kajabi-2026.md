# Teachable vs Kajabi 2026: Which Online Course Platform Is Right for You?

Teachable and Kajabi are two of the biggest names in online course platforms, but they solve different problems. Teachable is a focused, affordable course builder that lets you get selling fast. Kajabi is a premium all-in-one business platform that bundles courses, email marketing, website hosting, and sales funnels into a single tool.

The right choice depends on what you actually need — and what you're willing to pay for. Here's the full comparison.

## Teachable Overview

Teachable has powered over 100,000 course creators since 2014. Its pitch: make it dead simple to create and sell a course online without technical skills.

The course builder is clean and functional. Upload videos, add text, attach files, organize into sections. It supports drip content, quizzes, completion certificates, and coaching products. Nothing fancy, but it works.

Where Teachable shines is accessibility. The free plan lets you publish one course and one coaching product with a 5% transaction fee — enough to validate an idea before committing money. Paid plans remove the transaction fee and unlock features like coupons, advanced reports, and affiliate marketing.

The trade-off: Teachable is not an all-in-one platform. You'll need separate tools for email marketing (ConvertKit, Mailchimp), website hosting, and sales funnels. That means more subscriptions, more integrations, and more things to manage.

For a deeper look, see our [Teachable review](/blog/teachable-review-2026).

## Kajabi Overview

Kajabi positions itself as the only platform you need to run an online knowledge business. Courses, memberships, email campaigns, landing pages, a full website, sales pipelines, community, podcasting — it's all built in.

The course builder offers more template variety than Teachable, with pre-built outlines and assessment tools. But the real differentiator is everything around the course. Kajabi's email marketing rivals standalone tools like ConvertKit. Its pipeline builder creates multi-step funnels without ClickFunnels. Its website builder produces professional sites with blog functionality.

The catch is price. Kajabi's cheapest plan costs $55/month (billed annually), and that plan caps you at one product and 250 contacts. To run a real business, most creators need the Growth plan at $149/month — which adds up to $1,788/year.

## Feature Comparison

### Course Creation

Both platforms handle the core job well. You can upload video, add text, organize into modules, and drip content over time. Teachable's editor is slightly simpler to learn. Kajabi offers more templates and assessment types out of the box.

**Edge: Tie** — both get the job done.

### Marketing & Email

This is where Kajabi pulls ahead decisively. Its built-in email tool supports sequences, broadcasts, segmentation, and automation triggers. Teachable offers basic email on paid plans, but nothing close to a standalone email platform.

**Edge: Kajabi**, by a wide margin.

### Website & Landing Pages

Kajabi includes a full website builder with blog, custom pages, and SEO settings. Teachable gives you a basic sales page per course and a simple school page, but not a full website.

**Edge: Kajabi.**

### Sales Funnels

Kajabi's pipeline builder handles opt-in funnels, webinar funnels, and product launch sequences natively. Teachable has no built-in funnel tool — you'd need a third-party tool like ClickFunnels.

**Edge: Kajabi.**

### Community

Kajabi's built-in community feature supports discussions, challenges, and content feeds. Teachable added basic community features recently, but they're more limited.

**Edge: Kajabi.**

### Payments & Checkout

Teachable processes payments through its own gateway (Teachable Payments) or PayPal. The free plan charges a 5% transaction fee; paid plans charge 0%. Kajabi uses Stripe and PayPal with 0% platform fees on all plans. Both support one-time payments, subscriptions, and payment plans.

**Edge: Slight Teachable advantage** for the free plan option; otherwise comparable.

## Who Should Choose Teachable

- **New course creators** testing an idea before investing heavily
- **Budget-conscious creators** who already use and like their existing email/website tools
- **Solo educators** who need a straightforward course-hosting solution without complexity
- Anyone who wants a **free starting point** with zero upfront cost

## Who Should Choose Kajabi

- **Established creators** ready to consolidate multiple tools into one platform
- **Business builders** who need email marketing, funnels, and a website alongside courses
- **Coaches and membership creators** who want community features baked in
- Anyone willing to pay a premium to **eliminate tool sprawl** and manage everything in one place

## The Verdict

Teachable is the better starting point. It costs less, it's simpler, and the free plan removes financial risk entirely. If your primary goal is to create a course and sell it, Teachable handles that without distractions.

Kajabi is the better scaling platform. Once you're generating revenue and spending $100+/month across multiple tools (email, funnels, website, community), consolidating into Kajabi can actually save money while giving you a more integrated workflow.

The most common path: start on Teachable, validate your course, build an audience — then migrate to Kajabi when the all-in-one platform justifies the cost. Neither is wrong. It depends on where you are in your business right now.
