**Grammarly** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

Grammarly is one of the most widely used writing assistant tools in the world, trusted by millions of students, professionals, and businesses. But with three distinct pricing tiers, it is not always obvious which plan is the right fit — or whether paying for Premium is actually worth it. In this guide, we break down every Grammarly plan in 2026, explain what each tier gets you, and help you decide where to put your money.

## Grammarly Pricing Overview

Grammarly offers three plans in 2026. Here is the quick comparison before we get into the details:

| Plan | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|---------|
| Free | $0/month | Casual writers, basic grammar checks |
| Premium | $12/month (billed annually) | Students, [freelancers](/best-for/freelancers/), power writers |
| Business | $15/seat/month (billed annually) | Teams of 3 or more |

Grammarly's Premium plan is $12/month when billed annually ($144/year). If you pay monthly, the price rises to $30/month — two and a half times more. The Business plan runs $15/seat/month annually, which includes centralized billing and team management features.

## Free Plan: Solid Baseline, Real Limitations

The Grammarly Free plan covers the fundamentals well. You get real-time spelling and grammar corrections, basic punctuation checks, and conciseness suggestions. It integrates with browsers, Google Docs, [Microsoft Word](/tools/microsoft-word/), and most desktop apps through the Grammarly desktop app.

For casual use — writing emails, filling out forms, or drafting quick messages — the Free plan is genuinely useful. It catches obvious errors and prevents embarrassing typos.

Where it falls short is everywhere beyond the basics. The Free plan does not include:

- **Clarity and readability improvements:** Suggestions to restructure unclear sentences
- **Tone detection:** Feedback on whether your writing sounds confident, formal, or aggressive
- **Plagiarism detection:** Side-by-side source comparisons
- **Advanced vocabulary suggestions:** Word choice upgrades beyond simple synonyms
- **Full-sentence rewrites:** The GrammarlyGO generative AI features
- **Style guide enforcement:** Custom rules for consistent writing

The free tier is also limited in its AI-powered suggestions. GrammarlyGO, Grammarly's generative writing assistant, is restricted to a small number of prompts per month on the free plan.

**Best for:** Casual users who need basic spell-check and grammar correction, or anyone testing Grammarly before committing to a paid plan.

## Premium Plan ($12/month): The Full Writing Assistant

The Premium plan is where Grammarly becomes a genuinely powerful writing tool. At $12/month billed annually, it unlocks the full suite of writing assistance features that Grammarly is known for.

Key features in Premium include:

- **Clarity-focused sentence rewrites:** Grammarly identifies hard-to-read sentences and suggests restructured versions that are clearer and more direct
- **Tone adjustments:** Real-time feedback on how your writing sounds to readers, with suggestions to adjust formality, confidence, or empathy
- **Vocabulary enhancements:** Contextually aware word upgrade suggestions that go beyond basic synonyms
- **Plagiarism detection:** Checks your text against billions of web pages and academic papers
- **Full GrammarlyGO access:** AI-powered prompt responses, drafting assistance, and rewriting tools with 2,000 prompts per month
- **Genre-specific writing style checks:** Tailored feedback for academic writing, business writing, casual communication, and more
- **Formatting suggestions:** Consistency checks for punctuation, capitalization, and formatting conventions

For students writing essays or professionals drafting reports, the Premium plan's plagiarism checker alone can justify the cost. The tone detection feature is particularly valuable for business communication — knowing that your email sounds passive-aggressive before you send it prevents a lot of headaches.

One important note: the $12/month price requires annual billing ($144 upfront). The month-to-month rate of $30/month makes little financial sense for regular users.

**Best for:** Students, freelancers, content creators, and professionals who write regularly and want comprehensive feedback beyond basic grammar correction. See our full [Grammarly review 2026](/blog/grammarly-review-2026) for a hands-on assessment.

## Business Plan ($15/seat/month): Teams and Organizations

The Business plan is built for teams of three or more people who need collaborative writing tools with administrative controls. At $15/seat/month billed annually, it includes everything in Premium plus:

- **Centralized billing and user management:** Add and remove seats from a single dashboard without managing individual subscriptions
- **Style guides:** Create organization-wide writing rules — specific terminology, tone requirements, forbidden phrases — that apply across all team members
- **Brand tone profiles:** Set a defined voice for your organization so all team members write consistently
- **Snippets (text shortcuts):** Save frequently used phrases, templates, and responses for reuse across the team
- **Analytics dashboard:** Track writing improvement trends and team-wide engagement with Grammarly's suggestions
- **Priority support:** Faster customer service response times for business accounts
- **SAML SSO:** Single sign-on integration for enterprise identity management (available on higher-tier business plans)

The style guide feature is genuinely valuable for content teams, marketing departments, and customer support organizations. Rather than each person getting individual feedback, the team enforces consistent terminology and tone through shared rules.

At $15/seat/month for a team of 10, the annual cost is $1,800. That is a meaningful investment, though it is competitive with other writing and editing tools at enterprise scale.

**Best for:** Marketing teams, content agencies, customer support departments, and any organization where consistent, on-brand writing matters. Teams smaller than 3 people should use Premium individual plans instead.

## Is Grammarly Premium Worth Paying For?

The honest answer depends on how much you write and what you write.

If you write professionally — as a content marketer, copywriter, student, or anyone producing more than a few thousand words per week — the Premium plan at $12/month is one of the easiest software subscriptions to justify. The combination of clarity suggestions, tone detection, and plagiarism checking saves real time and prevents real mistakes.

If you write occasionally — personal emails, the occasional document — the Free plan is probably enough. The grammar and spelling corrections it provides handle 80% of common writing errors.

The Business plan makes financial sense only when your team is large enough that centralized management and style consistency create measurable efficiency gains. For teams of 3-5 people, the per-seat cost adds up quickly relative to individual Premium subscriptions.

## How Grammarly Compares to Alternatives

Grammarly's $12/month Premium price is mid-range for writing tools. ProWritingAid offers similar functionality for $10/month or a one-time lifetime purchase [around](/tools/around/) $400. Wordtune is cheaper at around $8.33/month but lacks the depth of Grammarly's grammar engine.

The free tiers differ significantly. Grammarly Free is more capable than most alternatives for basic grammar correction, but it locks more advanced features behind Premium than competitors do.

## Final Verdict

Grammarly's pricing structure is logical: a functional free tier, a reasonably priced individual Premium at $12/month, and a team-focused Business plan at $15/seat/month. The biggest friction point is the price gap between annual and monthly billing — if you pay month-to-month for Premium, you are paying $30/month, which is hard to justify.

For most users who are considering upgrading, the right move is to commit to the annual Premium plan. The features are genuinely useful, the price is reasonable at $12/month, and the annual commitment keeps costs predictable.
