**Acuity Scheduling vs Calendly** is a question that splits on use case. Acuity is built for **service businesses** (coaches, therapists, wellness pros) that need intake forms, packages, and client management. Calendly is built for **meetings and sales workflows** (sales calls, demos, team routing). They're not really competing for the same user.

**Note**: Acuity pricing is per-account (not per-user), while Calendly is per-user. For a solo practitioner, Acuity at $16/month vs Calendly Standard at $10/month is a real difference. For a team of 6, Acuity Growing ($27/month total) beats Calendly Teams ($96/month for 6 users).

## Where Acuity Wins

### Intake Forms
Acuity's intake forms are meaningfully more powerful than Calendly's. You can build:
- Multi-field forms with conditional branching ("If you answered Yes to question 3, show these additional questions")
- Medical/health intake forms
- Coach intake questionnaires
- Pre-session prep documents

For a therapist or health coach, collecting this information at booking (before the first session) is workflow-critical. Calendly's basic intake questions don't match this.

### Package and Subscription Selling
Acuity natively sells session packages and recurring subscriptions. Examples:
- "Buy 10 coaching sessions, save 20%" — client purchases upfront, sessions deduct as used
- "Monthly yoga membership — 8 classes/month" — subscription, auto-renews, sessions replenish

Calendly has no equivalent. You can charge per-session via Stripe on Calendly, but there's no package or subscription logic.

### Per-Account Pricing for Teams
For practices with multiple practitioners, Acuity's Growing ($27/mo for 6 staff calendars) is dramatically cheaper than Calendly Teams ($16/user/month × 6 = $96/month). If you run a small practice with 3-6 practitioners, the cost difference is real.

## Where Calendly Wins

### Free Plan
Calendly's free plan (1 event type, unlimited bookings) lets you start without paying. Acuity's 7-day trial means you're committed to paying from day one.

### CRM Integrations
Calendly's Teams plan includes native HubSpot and Salesforce integrations that auto-log meetings, sync contact data, and trigger CRM sequences. For sales teams embedded in a CRM workflow, this is essential.

### Round-Robin and Lead Routing
Calendly Teams' routing forms qualify inbound leads and route to the right rep automatically. Round-robin distributes meetings evenly across a team. Acuity has no equivalent — it's designed for client self-selection, not SDR routing.

### Modern Interface
Calendly's UI — both the admin side and the client-facing booking page — is more polished than Acuity's. This matters if the booking experience is part of your brand presentation.

### Broader Ecosystem
Calendly integrates with Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, Salesforce, HubSpot, Marketo, Outreach, and hundreds of tools via Zapier. Acuity's native integrations are more limited.

## Decision Framework

**Choose Acuity Scheduling if:**
- You're a coach, therapist, trainer, or wellness professional
- You need detailed intake forms before the first session
- You sell session packages or subscriptions
- Your team has 3+ practitioners sharing a booking system (per-account pricing is cheaper)
- Your website runs on Squarespace

**Choose Calendly if:**
- You're in sales, recruiting, or customer success
- You need CRM integration (HubSpot, Salesforce) to auto-log meetings
- You need round-robin or lead routing for a team
- You want to start for free before committing
- You prioritize a modern, polished booking UX

**Consider Cal.com if:**
- You want Calendly-like features with unlimited event types on the free plan
- You want to self-host your scheduling infrastructure

## Verdict

**There's no universal winner** — these tools serve different customers. Acuity dominates for service-based practices where intake and packages matter. Calendly dominates for meeting-centric workflows, sales teams, and CRM-integrated organizations.

If you're reading this and unsure which applies to you: ask whether your bookings involve **client relationships and service delivery** (Acuity) or **meetings and sales conversations** (Calendly).
