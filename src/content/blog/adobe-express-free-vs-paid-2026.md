Adobe Express has a generous free tier — more generous than most people realize. But the free version has specific limits that start to bite once you use it for real work. Here's a straightforward breakdown of what's free, what's Premium-only, and whether the upgrade makes financial sense.

## Adobe Express Free Plan

The free tier is surprisingly capable for casual design work.

**What you get:**

- **100,000+ templates** across social media, flyers, logos, and video
- **1 million+ stock photos and icons** (Adobe Stock basics)
- **5 GB cloud storage**
- **25 generative AI credits per month** (Firefly-powered text-to-image, text effects, generative fill)
- **Social media scheduling** for 1 connected account
- **Basic video editing** with trimming, resizing, and simple transitions
- **2,000+ fonts** (Google Fonts + Adobe basics)
- **Remove background** (limited uses)

**What you don't get:**

- Premium templates and brand assets
- Most of the Adobe Stock library (200M+ assets are Premium-only)
- More than 1 social scheduling account
- Brand Kit (custom logos, colors, fonts saved across designs)
- Advanced video features like background removal in video
- Version history beyond 10 days
- Uploads larger than 300 MB per file

The 25 AI credit limit is the constraint that hits fastest. Each Firefly generation costs at least 1 credit, and iterating on a design can burn through 25 credits in a single session.

## Adobe Express Premium — $9.99/month

Premium unlocks everything Adobe Express can do.

**What you get (on top of Free):**

- **All Premium templates** (the full library, not the curated free subset)
- **200 million+ stock photos, videos, and design assets** from Adobe Stock
- **25,000+ fonts** including the full Adobe Fonts collection
- **250 generative AI credits per month** (10x the free tier)
- **100 GB cloud storage** (20x free)
- **Brand Kit** — save your brand colors, logos, and fonts for one-click consistency
- **Social scheduling** for unlimited accounts
- **Video background removal** (not available on free)
- **Full version history** (not capped at 10 days)
- **No file size cap** on uploads (free caps at 300 MB)

### Annual vs Monthly

| Billing | Price | Annual Cost |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Monthly | $9.99/mo | $119.88/yr |
| Annual | $8.33/mo ($99.99/yr) | $99.99/yr |

Annual billing saves about 17%, which works out to roughly $20/year. If you're going to use it for more than 2 months, annual makes more sense.

### Teams Plan

$9.99 per user per month (annual billing required, 2-user minimum). You get everything in Premium plus shared brand assets, admin controls, and team libraries. The per-seat cost is the same as individual Premium, so you're only paying for the collaboration layer.

## What Most People Miss: Bundled Premium Access

You might already have Adobe Express Premium and not know it.

**Creative Cloud All Apps ($22.99/month)** includes Adobe Express Premium at no extra cost. If you're paying for Photoshop, Illustrator, or the full Creative Cloud suite, you already have it.

**Photography Plan ($9.99/month)** — the popular Lightroom + Photoshop bundle — also includes Adobe Express Premium. At the same price as standalone Express Premium, you're getting Photoshop and Lightroom as a bonus.

Check your existing Adobe subscriptions before buying Express Premium separately. You might be about to pay for something you already own.

## Is Free Enough?

For most casual users — making the occasional Instagram post, resizing a photo, designing a birthday card — yes. The free tier handles that fine.

Free starts to break down when you:

- **Run a brand** that needs consistent colors, logos, and fonts across designs (Brand Kit is Premium-only)
- **Create content regularly** and need more than 25 AI generations per month
- **Manage multiple social accounts** (free only schedules to 1)
- **Work with video** and need background removal
- **Need more storage** than 5 GB

## How It Compares to Alternatives

Adobe Express occupies a middle ground between [Canva](/tools/canva/) and professional Adobe tools like Photoshop. Canva's free tier offers more templates and a more intuitive drag-and-drop editor, while Express Premium's edge is deeper Adobe ecosystem integration — especially if you already use Creative Cloud.

For UI/UX work rather than social media graphics, [Figma](/tools/figma/) is a different category entirely, but worth considering if your "design" needs are more product-focused than marketing-focused.

If you're already paying for any Creative Cloud plan, the decision is easy: you likely have Express Premium included. If not, the $9.99/month price only makes sense if you consistently hit at least two of the free tier's limits.

## Bottom Line

The free plan is one of the better free design tools available — no watermarks, real AI features, and decent template selection. Premium is worth it for brand-consistent content creation at volume. Skip it if you're a casual user who designs once or twice a month.
