Automation doesn't have to cost a dime. Every major workflow platform now offers a free tier, and some open-source options are entirely free forever. The question is which free plan actually lets you get real work done — and which one hits you with a paywall the moment you try anything useful.

We tested all seven tools below on their free plans. Here's what you actually get for $0 in 2026.

## 1. Zapier — Best Free Plan for Beginners

**What's free:** 100 tasks/month, 5 single-step Zaps, 15-minute polling intervals

Zapier's free tier is the easiest way to dip your toes into automation. The setup is dead simple — pick a trigger app, pick an action app, done. With 6,000+ integrations, you can connect almost anything.

**Key limitations:** The 100-task limit disappears fast. Each time a Zap fires, it counts as one task. A daily backup Zap burns 30 tasks/month alone. You're also locked to single-step Zaps, which means no filters, no formatters, and no branching logic.

**Best use case:** One or two simple automations, like sending Slack notifications when a Google Form is submitted.

For a deeper look at what each Zapier plan includes, see our [Zapier Free vs Paid breakdown](/blog/zapier-free-vs-paid-2026).

## 2. Make (formerly Integromat) — Best Free Plan for Power Users

**What's free:** 1,000 operations/month, 2 active scenarios, 10 MB data transfer

Make gives you ten times the volume of Zapier's free tier. The operations-based model is more generous because simple steps (like a filter or router) cost fewer ops than a full "task." You also get multi-step scenarios on the free plan — a huge advantage over Zapier.

**Key limitations:** Only 2 active scenarios at a time. The 10 MB data transfer cap means file-heavy automations (like moving PDFs or images) will hit the wall quickly. Polling is every 15 minutes.

**Best use case:** Complex multi-step workflows that don't involve large files, like syncing CRM data to a spreadsheet with conditional logic.

If you're choosing between these two, our [automation tool selection guide](/blog/how-to-choose-automation-tool) walks you through the decision framework.

## 3. n8n — Best Self-Hosted (Completely Free)

**What's free:** Everything — unlimited workflows, unlimited executions, all integrations (self-hosted)

n8n is the only tool on this list where "free" truly means free with no limits. Self-host it on a $5/month VPS and you have an enterprise-grade automation platform. No task caps, no scenario limits, no feature gates.

**Key limitations:** You need technical skills to set it up and maintain it. Self-hosting means you're responsible for updates, backups, and uptime. The cloud plan starts at $24/month, which isn't free.

**Best use case:** Developers and technical teams who want full automation power without any recurring SaaS costs.

## 4. IFTTT — Best for Smart Home & Simple Personal Automations

**What's free:** 2 applets (automations), standard connection speed

IFTTT pioneered the "if this, then that" model and remains the go-to for smart home integrations. It connects to Ring, Philips Hue, Nest, Alexa, and hundreds of IoT devices that other automation tools ignore.

**Key limitations:** Only 2 applets on the free plan is extremely restrictive. Each applet is a single-step automation. There's no multi-step logic, no filters, and no data transformation. The Pro plan at $3.49/month unlocks 20 applets with multi-step support.

**Best use case:** Personal smart home automations, like turning off your lights when you leave home or logging Fitbit data to a spreadsheet.

## 5. Microsoft Power Automate — Best for Microsoft 365 Users

**What's free:** Included with Microsoft 365 subscriptions, standard connectors, 6,000 runs/month (per-plan limits vary)

If your organization already pays for Microsoft 365, you likely have Power Automate included. It integrates deeply with Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, Excel, and the entire Microsoft ecosystem. Desktop flows (RPA) can automate legacy Windows applications.

**Key limitations:** Premium connectors (Salesforce, SAP, custom APIs) require a standalone license at $15/user/month. The visual builder is functional but less intuitive than Zapier or Make. Desktop flows require Power Automate Desktop, which is free on Windows 11 but limited without a premium license.

**Best use case:** Automating approvals, document routing, and data entry within the Microsoft ecosystem.

## 6. Pipedream — Best Free Plan for Developers

**What's free:** 10,000 invocations/month, 30-second execution time, 100 daily credits, all integrations

Pipedream is built for developers who want to write code alongside their automations. Every workflow step can include Node.js, Python, Go, or Bash code. The free tier is remarkably generous — 10,000 invocations/month dwarfs what Zapier and Make offer.

**Key limitations:** Not suitable for non-technical users. The UI assumes you're comfortable with code. Execution time is capped at 30 seconds per step, which can be tight for API-heavy workflows. Credit-based pricing for compute can be confusing.

**Best use case:** API integrations, webhook processing, and developer-oriented workflows that need custom logic.

## 7. Activepieces — Best Open-Source Alternative to Zapier

**What's free:** Unlimited flows and tasks (self-hosted), 1,000 tasks/month (cloud free tier)

Activepieces is the newest entrant and positions itself as a Zapier-like experience you can self-host. The UI is clean and beginner-friendly — closer to Zapier's simplicity than n8n's developer focus. The cloud free tier offers 1,000 tasks/month, matching Make's generosity.

**Key limitations:** Smaller integration library compared to established players (~150 integrations vs Zapier's 6,000+). Being newer means fewer tutorials and community resources. Some integrations are community-contributed and may lag behind official ones.

**Best use case:** Teams who want Zapier's ease of use with n8n's self-hosting freedom.

## Which Free Automation Tool Should You Pick?

**Pick Zapier** if you want the simplest possible setup and only need 1-2 basic automations.

**Pick Make** if you need multi-step logic on a free plan and want the best balance of power and usability.

**Pick n8n** if you're technical and want zero limits forever.

**Pick IFTTT** if your automations involve smart home devices.

**Pick Power Automate** if your company already uses Microsoft 365.

**Pick Pipedream** if you're a developer who wants to write code in your automations.

**Pick Activepieces** if you want Zapier's UX with self-hosting freedom.
