Asana's free plan is generous enough for individuals, but growing teams hit walls fast. Here's a no-fluff breakdown of what you get — and what you lose — on Asana's free tier in 2026.

## Asana Free (Personal) Plan

The Personal plan supports **up to 2 users** and includes:

- **Unlimited tasks and projects** — no cap on what you can create
- **List, Board, and Calendar views** — the basics are all there
- **Assignees and due dates** — standard task management
- **100+ integrations** — Slack, Google Drive, Microsoft 365
- **Mobile apps** — iOS and Android

**What's missing:** Timeline (Gantt), Portfolios, Goals, custom fields, forms, milestones, approvals, and automations.

## Asana Starter ($10.99/user/month)

The Starter plan unlocks the features most teams actually need:

- **Timeline view** — visual Gantt-style project tracking
- **Workflow Builder** — create simple automations
- **Forms** — intake requests from anyone
- **Custom fields** — add metadata to tasks
- **Milestones** — mark key project checkpoints
- **Admin console** — team management tools
- **Unlimited free guests** — collaborate with external partners

This tier is where Asana starts to feel like a real project management tool rather than a to-do list.

## Asana Advanced ($24.99/user/month)

Advanced adds power-user features:

- **Portfolios** — track multiple projects at a glance
- **Goals** — set and monitor team OKRs
- **Custom rules** — advanced automation with branching logic
- **Approvals** — built-in review and approval workflows
- **Proofing** — annotate images and PDFs directly
- **Advanced reporting** — cross-project dashboards
- **SAML/SSO** — enterprise-grade security

## Who Should Stay Free?

The free plan works if you're:

- A **solo user** or a team of exactly 2
- Managing **simple projects** with list or board views
- Not needing automations or Gantt charts

## Who Should Upgrade?

Pay for Starter if your team has **3+ people** or you need Timeline view. Jump to Advanced if you need **Portfolios, Goals, or custom automations**.

## The Verdict

Asana's free plan is functional but restrictive — the 2-user cap is the real dealbreaker. Most teams outgrow it within weeks. Starter at $10.99/user/month is the sweet spot for small teams.
