**Smartsheet** uses a three-tier pricing model in 2026 with several premium add-ons that can significantly increase your total cost. This guide breaks down the base plans, the hidden add-on expenses, and how Smartsheet stacks up against alternatives.

Unlike most project management tools, Smartsheet doesn't offer a permanent free plan — just a 30-day trial. That means you're paying from day one. Here's what each tier costs and what you actually get.

## Smartsheet Pricing Overview (2026)

| Plan | Price | User Limit | Best For |
|------|-------|-----------|----------|
| Free Trial | $0 (30 days) | 1 | Evaluating the platform |
| Pro | $9/user/mo | 10 max | Small teams and individual contributors |
| Business | $19/user/mo | Unlimited (3 min) | Growing teams with automation needs |
| Enterprise | Custom (~$15–20/user at scale) | Unlimited | Large orgs with governance requirements |

All prices reflect annual billing. Monthly billing is available on Pro and Business but costs roughly 25% more.

## Free Trial (30 Days)

Smartsheet gives you a 30-day trial of the Business plan — no credit card required. You get the full feature set during the trial, which is useful for a genuine evaluation. But there's no freemium tier to fall back on once it expires. You either pay or lose access.

If you're used to tools like [ClickUp](/tools/clickup/) or [Airtable](/tools/airtable/) that offer permanent free plans, this is worth noting upfront.

## Pro Plan — $9/user/month

The Pro plan is Smartsheet's entry point and comes with a hard cap of **10 users**. You get:

- **Unlimited sheets**, reports, and dashboards
- **Forms** for data collection
- **Gantt, grid, card, and calendar views**
- **Conditional formatting** and basic formulas
- **10 automations per month** per user
- **250 actions per automation**
- **20GB storage** total

The Pro plan is built for small teams or individuals who need Smartsheet's spreadsheet-meets-project-management model. At $9/user/month, it's competitively priced — but the 10-user cap means growing teams will hit a wall fast.

**Key limitation:** No resource management, no document builder, and no premium app access.

## Business Plan — $19/user/month

Business removes the user cap and adds the features that make Smartsheet genuinely useful at scale. It requires a **minimum of 3 seats** ($57/month minimum). You get everything in Pro, plus:

- **Unlimited users** (3 seat minimum)
- **Unlimited automations** per month
- **Resource management** (workload tracking)
- **Document builder** (branded reports and proposals)
- **Custom branding** for forms and portals
- **Activity log** for audit trails
- **1TB storage** total
- **Integrations**: Salesforce, Jira, ServiceNow, Tableau

For most teams, Business is the plan you'll land on. The jump from 10 automations/month (Pro) to unlimited automations is a significant upgrade for anyone building real workflows.

## Enterprise Plan — Custom Pricing (~$15–20/user at scale)

Enterprise adds governance, security, and IT administration features on top of Business:

- **Everything in Business**, plus:
- **Smartsheet Advance** (WorkApps, Data Shuttle, Bridge)
- **Admin center** with domain-level controls
- **Directory integration** (Azure AD, Okta, OneLogin)
- **SAML-based SSO**
- **Custom email domains**
- **IP allow-listing**
- **Dedicated support** with SLA
- **Unlimited storage**

Enterprise pricing isn't published, but based on market data, expect $15–20/user/month for organizations with 100+ seats. Volume discounts can push this lower for very large deployments.

## Premium Add-Ons (The Hidden Cost)

This is where Smartsheet's pricing gets complicated. Several features that competitors include in base plans are sold as paid add-ons:

| Add-On | What It Does | Extra Cost |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Dynamic View** | Controlled data sharing with external stakeholders | +20–30% of base plan |
| **Control Center** | Portfolio-level project templates and provisioning | +30–50% of base plan |
| **Data Shuttle** | Automated data imports/exports from external systems | Included in Enterprise, paid for Business |
| **Connectors** (Jira, Salesforce) | Two-way sync with enterprise tools | +20% of base plan |
| **Bridge** | Workflow automation across external apps | Included in Enterprise, paid for Business |

For a Business plan user at $19/month, adding Dynamic View and a connector could push the effective cost to $27–30/user/month. At that point, you're approaching enterprise-grade spending without enterprise-grade governance.

**Key takeaway:** Always calculate your total cost including add-ons, not just the base plan price.

## Which Plan Should You Choose?

- **Individual or very small team (1–5 people)**: Pro at $9/user/month works if you don't need automations beyond the basics. But consider whether the 10-user cap will become a problem within 6 months.
- **Growing teams (5–25 people)**: Business at $19/user/month is the clear choice. Unlimited automations and resource management are essential at this scale.
- **Large organizations (50+ people)**: Enterprise gives you governance controls and premium apps. The per-user cost actually drops at scale, so request a custom quote.
- **Budget-conscious teams**: If you need a free plan to start, Smartsheet isn't it. Look at [ClickUp](/tools/clickup/) or [Monday.com](/tools/monday/) instead.

## Watch Out For

1. **No permanent free plan.** You're committing to paid from the start.
2. **Premium add-on creep.** Dynamic View, Control Center, and connectors add 20–50% to your base cost.
3. **Pro's 10-user limit.** You'll outgrow it fast if you're onboarding a real team.
4. **Annual lock-in at Enterprise.** Multi-year contracts are common for the best rates.

## Bottom Line

Smartsheet's base pricing is competitive — $9/month for Pro and $19/month for Business are reasonable for a platform with this much depth. The catch is that the features many teams actually need (Dynamic View, connectors, portfolio tools) cost extra.

If you're coming from Excel or Google Sheets and want something that feels familiar but adds project management capabilities, Smartsheet is hard to beat. If you want an all-in-one platform where everything is included in the base price, [ClickUp](/tools/clickup/) or [Monday.com](/tools/monday/) will give you more out of the box.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Smartsheet free?

No, Smartsheet does not offer a permanent free plan. You get a 30-day free trial of the Business plan, after which you need to subscribe to Pro, Business, or Enterprise.

### Is Smartsheet worth paying for?

For teams that work heavily with spreadsheet-style data and need project management features on top, yes. The Business plan's unlimited automations and resource management provide strong value. For simpler task management, more affordable options exist.

### What is the cheapest Smartsheet plan?

The Pro plan starts at $9/user/month with annual billing and supports up to 10 users. Check the pricing table above for current details — plans and pricing may change.
