**Todoist** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

Todoist keeps its pricing refreshingly simple compared to most productivity tools. With just three plans — Free, Pro, and Business — it's easy to figure out what you need.

Here's everything you need to know about Todoist pricing in 2026.

## Todoist Pricing Overview

| Plan | Price (Annual) | Price (Monthly) |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 |
| Pro | $5/user/mo | $7/user/mo |
| Business | $8/user/mo | $10/user/mo |

Annual billing saves you about 20% on both paid plans.

## Free Plan — What You Get

Todoist's free plan is surprisingly capable for personal use:

- **5 active projects** (was previously unlimited, now limited)
- **5 collaborators** per project
- **5 MB file uploads**
- Natural language input
- Due dates and recurring tasks
- Priority levels (P1-P4)
- Available on all platforms (web, desktop, mobile)

**Best for:** Individuals with simple task management needs.

**Limitations:** The 5-project cap is the biggest restriction. If you need more projects, you'll need Pro.

## Pro Plan — $5/month

The Pro plan removes most limitations and adds power features:

- **300 active projects**
- **25 collaborators** per project
- **100 MB file uploads**
- **Reminders** (location + time-based)
- **Labels and filters**
- **Comments and file attachments**
- Calendar layout
- Task duration
- AI Assistant

**Best for:** Power users, freelancers, and anyone who's outgrown the free plan.

**Is it worth $5/month?** Absolutely. Reminders alone justify the upgrade for most users. The jump from 5 to 300 projects makes Pro essentially unlimited for individuals.

## Business Plan — $6/month

The Business plan adds team management features:

- Everything in Pro
- **500 active projects**
- **50 collaborators** per project
- **Team inbox**
- **Team billing** (centralized)
- **Admin and member roles**
- **Priority support**

**Best for:** Small teams (2-25 people) who want a shared task management system without the complexity of Asana or ClickUp.

**Is it worth it?** At $8/user/month, it's still one of the most affordable team productivity tools available. Compare that to Asana Starter at $10.99 or ClickUp Unlimited at $7.

## Who Should Choose Which Plan?

**Stick with Free if:**
- You have 5 or fewer projects
- You don't need reminders
- You're a student or casual user

**Upgrade to Pro if:**
- You need more than 5 projects
- Reminders are important to you
- You want the AI Assistant
- You use labels and custom filters

**Go with Business if:**
- You manage a team
- You need centralized billing
- You want admin controls

## Final Verdict

Todoist's pricing remains competitive. At $5/month for Pro (annual billing), it's still one of the best values in productivity software. It won't replace a full project management suite like ClickUp or Asana, but if you want a fast, clean task manager — Todoist delivers.
