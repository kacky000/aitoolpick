# How to Switch from Jasper to Copy.ai in 2026

If you're moving from Jasper to Copy.ai—usually to cut costs or simplify your workflow—a little planning prevents lost templates and inconsistent output. This guide walks through the switch step by step.

## Why People Switch

Common reasons teams move from Jasper to Copy.ai in 2026:

- **Pricing**: Copy.ai's plans can be more affordable for small teams.
- **Workflow automation**: Copy.ai's GTM and workflow features appeal to sales/marketing ops.
- **Simplicity**: A lighter interface for everyday copy tasks.

If your main need is long-form SEO content, weigh this carefully—compare both first in [Copy.ai vs Jasper](/blog/copy-ai-vs-jasper-2026/).

## Step 1: Inventory What You Use in Jasper

Before cancelling anything, list:

- The templates you use weekly (ads, emails, product descriptions)
- Your saved brand voice settings
- Any documents or campaigns you want to keep
- Team members and their roles

You can't export everything one-to-one, so know what actually matters.

## Step 2: Recreate Your Brand Voice

Copy.ai supports brand voice, but you'll set it up fresh:

1. Gather 3–5 samples of your best on-brand copy.
2. Create a brand voice in Copy.ai using those samples.
3. Run a test prompt and compare tone against your Jasper output.
4. Refine until the voice matches.

## Step 3: Map Templates to Workflows

Jasper templates and Copy.ai workflows don't map exactly:

| Jasper feature | Copy.ai equivalent |
|----------------|--------------------|
| Template (e.g., PAS, AIDA) | Prompt or workflow |
| Brand Voice | Brand Voice |
| Campaigns | Workflows / projects |

Rebuild your most-used templates as saved prompts or workflows so your team has consistent starting points.

## Step 4: Run Both in Parallel Briefly

Don't cancel Jasper on day one. For one to two weeks:

- Produce the same briefs in both tools.
- Compare quality and editing time.
- Confirm Copy.ai covers your highest-volume tasks.

This overlap costs a little but prevents a painful rollback.

## Step 5: Migrate Content and Cancel

Once you're confident:

1. Export or copy any documents you want to keep from Jasper.
2. Move your team fully to Copy.ai.
3. Cancel Jasper before the next billing cycle.
4. Document the new workflow so the team stays consistent.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Cancelling too early**: Always overlap before switching fully.
- **Skipping brand voice setup**: Output will feel off without it.
- **Assuming feature parity**: Long-form SEO workflows may differ—test them.

## The Bottom Line

Switching from Jasper to Copy.ai is straightforward if you **inventory first, recreate your brand voice, rebuild key templates, and overlap briefly** before cancelling. The biggest risk isn't the tool—it's losing your voice and templates in the move.
