# Is Grammarly Worth It for Students in 2026?

Grammarly is on every student's radar — but the free version is heavily restricted, and Premium costs $12/month. Is it worth it for essays, research papers, and academic writing?

Short answer: the free version is worth using. Premium is worth it only in specific situations.

For basic proofreading — catching grammar errors, typos, and awkward punctuation — the free plan does the job. Premium adds the higher-order feedback (style, clarity, vocabulary) and plagiarism checking.

## What Grammarly Premium Actually Adds for Students

### 1. Advanced Clarity Suggestions

Premium rewrites verbose or convoluted sentences into cleaner versions. This is genuinely useful for academic writing, where passive voice and overly complex sentences are common habits.

Example:
- **Draft:** "The results that were obtained from the experiment that was conducted show that..."
- **Grammarly suggestion:** "The experiment results show that..."

This teaches better writing habits over time, not just corrects current mistakes.

### 2. Plagiarism Checker

Grammarly's plagiarism checker compares your work against 16 billion web pages and academic databases. For students, this means:
- Catching accidental plagiarism (paraphrasing too closely from a source)
- Verifying citations are properly attributed
- Peace of mind before submitting

**Note:** Grammarly's plagiarism checker is not as comprehensive as Turnitin, which your institution likely uses. It's a self-check tool, not a guarantee.

### 3. Vocabulary Enhancement

Premium suggests more precise or academic-appropriate word choices. For non-native English speakers writing in academic contexts, this is particularly valuable.

### 4. Grammarly Go (AI Rewrites)

The newest Premium feature allows you to prompt Grammarly to rewrite sections: "Make this more concise," "Change to formal tone," "Simplify this paragraph." It's essentially an AI writing assistant built into the editor.

## When Grammarly Premium Is Worth It for Students

**Worth it if you:**
- Write long-form papers (dissertations, theses, research papers) regularly
- Are a non-native English speaker working in academic English
- Submit to instructors or journals that penalize style errors heavily
- Have the academic discount ($6.66/month for students — verify current pricing)
- Are actively trying to improve your writing, not just catch errors

**Not worth it if you:**
- Write mostly short assignments (< 500 words)
- Are a native English speaker with strong grammar fundamentals
- Your institution provides Turnitin for plagiarism checking
- You primarily need spell-check, not writing improvement

## Academic Discount

Grammarly offers a verified student discount — typically 20% off the annual plan. Go to Grammarly's website and look for "Student discount" or "Academic discount" — you'll need to verify with your .edu email. This brings Premium down significantly from the standard price.

### ChatGPT and Claude as Grammarly Alternatives

The rise of AI chatbots changes the equation significantly. For free:
- Paste your essay paragraph into ChatGPT: "Review this paragraph for grammar, clarity, and style"
- Get detailed feedback with explanations
- Ask for rewrites at different reading levels

This covers a lot of what Grammarly Premium offers — and often provides more context on *why* something should change.

**Important note on AI and academic integrity:** Many institutions have policies on AI-assisted writing. Using Grammarly for grammar correction is generally accepted. Using AI to rewrite significant portions of your work may not be. Check your institution's policy before using AI writing tools.

## The Verdict for Students

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Quick grammar check | **Grammarly Free** |
| Essay writing, native speaker | **Grammarly Free + Hemingway App** |
| Dissertation or thesis | **Grammarly Premium** (use student discount) |
| Non-native English speaker | **Grammarly Premium** |
| Budget-conscious, high volume | **ProWritingAid lifetime** |

Grammarly Free is worth installing and using for all students — there's no reason not to. Premium is worth the student-discounted price only if you write frequently enough to benefit from its deeper suggestions.
