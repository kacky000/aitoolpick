Todoist and Notion approach productivity from completely different angles. Todoist is a purpose-built task manager — fast, focused, and excellent at keeping your to-do list under control. Notion is a flexible workspace where task management is one of many things it can do. Here's how they compare for actually getting things done.

## Quick Verdict

**Choose Todoist** if you want the best dedicated task manager with natural language input, strong reminders, and a system that keeps your tasks separate from your thinking. **Choose Notion** if you want tasks integrated with your notes, projects, and documentation in a single flexible workspace.

## Pricing

| Plan | Todoist | Notion |
|------|---------|--------|
| Free | Up to 5 projects | Personal use, 10 guests |
| Pro | $5/month | $10/month |
| Business | $8/month | $18/month |

Todoist is significantly cheaper, especially at the Pro tier ($5 vs $10/month). For users who only need task management, this matters.

## Daily Task Management

This is Todoist's home turf, and it wins clearly. Todoist is designed around the daily task list — what needs to happen today, this week, and later. Its natural language input understands entries like "Buy groceries every Saturday" or "Submit report tomorrow at 9am" and converts them to scheduled tasks automatically.

Notion requires more manual setup for daily task management. You can build a powerful task database, but adding a task involves opening a database, creating a new entry, and filling in properties. For daily task capture, this friction adds up.

If you need to quickly capture 20 tasks while thinking, Todoist wins. Notion is better for thinking through tasks, not capturing them quickly.

## Project Management

Notion pulls ahead for project management. Because Notion is a database-driven workspace, you can link tasks to projects, connect projects to team docs, and build custom views (kanban, calendar, timeline) for different project types.

Todoist's projects are cleaner and simpler — essentially folders for tasks with optional kanban views. For software teams or complex multi-workstream projects, Todoist's project structure is limiting.

**Winner:** Notion for complex projects; Todoist for simple project task lists.

## Reminders and Notifications

Todoist handles reminders far better. You can set location-based reminders ("remind me when I arrive at the office"), notification-based reminders at specific times, and recurring reminders that automatically reschedule. The reminder system integrates with iOS and Android notifications reliably.

Notion's reminder system works but is less sophisticated. Reminders require a date property on a database entry, and notifications don't integrate as smoothly with mobile OS notification systems.

For people who rely on reminders to actually complete tasks, Todoist is the better tool.

## Note-Taking and Context

Notion wins decisively here. You can add rich notes, embedded files, tables, and sub-pages to any database entry in Notion. A task can include full meeting notes, a linked Figma design, and a decision log — all in one place.

Todoist tasks support comments and file attachments but lack the depth of Notion's block-based content. For knowledge workers who need context alongside tasks, Notion's approach is superior.

## Integrations

Both tools integrate with major services. Todoist connects with Google Calendar, Outlook, Slack, and 60+ other tools. Notion integrates with Slack, GitHub, Figma, and others.

For the specific workflow of calendar + tasks, Todoist's Google Calendar sync (two-way) is more polished than Notion's calendar integrations.

## Who Should Choose What

**Choose Todoist if:**
- You want the simplest, fastest daily task capture
- You rely heavily on reminders and notifications
- You use GTD (Getting Things Done) or Inbox Zero methodologies
- Your task management should be separate from your notes

**Choose Notion if:**
- You want tasks linked to notes, projects, and documentation
- You manage complex projects with multiple people
- You want custom properties and views for your task database
- You're already using Notion for other things

## Using Both Together

Many productive people use both: Todoist for personal task management and quick capture, Notion for project documentation and team work. The two tools complement each other when used this way.

Todoist's Notion integration (via Zapier or native connection) can sync tasks between the two systems if needed.
