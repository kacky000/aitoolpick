**Notion** and **Clickup** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

As a freelancer, your productivity tool isn't just nice-to-have — it's your entire operating system. Both Notion and ClickUp promise to be the all-in-one workspace freelancers need, but they take very different approaches.

After testing both tools extensively for freelance workflows, here's the honest breakdown.

## Quick Verdict

**Choose Notion** if you want a flexible workspace for notes, client wikis, and lightweight project tracking. **Choose ClickUp** if you juggle multiple clients with complex timelines and need built-in time tracking.

## Pricing for Freelancers

| Plan | Notion | ClickUp |
|------|--------|---------|
| Free | Unlimited pages, 5MB uploads | Unlimited tasks, 60MB storage |
| Paid | $10/mo (Plus) | $7/mo (Unlimited) |
| Best value | Free tier is generous | Unlimited plan packs more features |

For solo freelancers, both free plans are viable. ClickUp's paid plan is $3/month cheaper and includes time tracking — a critical feature for freelancers billing by the hour.

## Client Management

### Notion

Notion shines for creating polished client-facing spaces. You can build a client portal with shared pages, project briefs, and deliverable trackers. The database feature lets you create a CRM-style client tracker with custom properties.

**Strengths:**
- Beautiful client portals with shared pages
- Database-powered CRM for tracking leads and clients
- Templates for proposals, invoices, and contracts
- Clean, professional presentation

### ClickUp

ClickUp treats client work as projects with tasks, subtasks, and dependencies. Each client gets a Space or Folder, and you can track every deliverable with due dates, priorities, and custom statuses.

**Strengths:**
- Built-in time tracking per task
- Client-specific workspaces with permissions
- Gantt charts for project timelines
- Automations to streamline repetitive workflows

## Project Tracking

For freelancers managing 3-5 active clients, the difference matters.

**Notion** uses databases with views (Kanban, table, calendar) to track projects. It's flexible but requires setup time. You build your own system from scratch, which is powerful but can be overwhelming.

**ClickUp** gives you a structured [project management](/category/project-management/) system out of the box. Tasks, subtasks, dependencies, and multiple views come ready to use. Less customization, but faster to get started.

## Time Tracking

This is where ClickUp has a clear advantage. **ClickUp includes native time tracking** on every task — start a timer, log hours, and generate reports. For freelancers billing hourly, this eliminates the need for a separate tool like Toggl or [Harvest](/tools/harvest/).

**Notion has no built-in time tracking.** You'd need a third-party integration or manually log hours in a database.

## Note-Taking and Knowledge Base

Notion wins decisively here. Its document editor is best-in-class for freelancers who need to:
- Draft content and proposals
- Build a personal knowledge base
- Create SOPs and process docs
- Store reference materials and inspiration

ClickUp has Docs, but they feel secondary to the [task management](/category/task-management/) features.

## Mobile Experience

Both apps have mobile versions, but Notion's mobile app is more polished for quick note capture. ClickUp's mobile app is functional but can feel cluttered with all its features compressed into a small screen.

## The Freelancer Stack Test

## Our Recommendation

**For content freelancers** (writers, designers, marketers): Go with **Notion**. The document editor, client portals, and knowledge base capabilities align perfectly with creative work.

**For service freelancers** (developers, consultants, agencies): Go with **ClickUp**. The time tracking, task dependencies, and structured project management handle complex deliverables better.

**For budget-conscious freelancers**: ClickUp's Unlimited plan at $7/month delivers more project management features per dollar.
