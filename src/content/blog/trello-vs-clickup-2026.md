**Trello** and **ClickUp** both handle project management, but at very different scales. Trello is a beloved Kanban tool known for its radical simplicity. ClickUp is an all-in-one productivity platform that tries to replace Trello, Asana, Notion, and Docs all at once.

If you're choosing between them, here's everything you need to know.

## Quick Verdict

**Choose Trello** if you love Kanban boards, your projects are simple and visual, and you want something your whole team can learn in minutes. It's ideal for small teams, personal projects, and straightforward task tracking.

**Choose ClickUp** if you need multiple views (list, Gantt, calendar, mind map), advanced automation, time tracking, docs, and goals all in one place. It's the better choice for growing teams that are outgrowing simple Kanban.

## Pricing Comparison

| Plan | Trello | ClickUp |
|------|--------|---------|
| Free | Unlimited cards, 10 boards/workspace | Unlimited tasks, 100MB storage |
| Standard/Unlimited | $5/user/mo | $7/user/mo |
| Premium/Business | $10/user/mo | $12/user/mo |
| Enterprise | $17.50/user/mo | Custom |

Both have generous free plans. Trello's free plan limits workspaces to 10 boards, which is fine for small personal use but can feel constraining as you scale. ClickUp's free plan is remarkably full-featured for a free tier.

Trello's core feature set is intentionally lean. Nearly everything beyond basic Kanban requires Power-Ups (add-ons), and while the ecosystem is solid, it adds complexity and cost.

ClickUp includes almost all of these features natively — even on the free plan.

## Ease of Use

Trello's biggest competitive advantage is simplicity. The Kanban interface — lists, cards, drag-and-drop — is so intuitive that new users need zero onboarding. You can have your board running in under ten minutes.

ClickUp is more powerful but significantly more complex. The sheer number of features, views, and settings can overwhelm new users. Many teams report a 1-2 week learning curve before feeling comfortable. If you're not careful, ClickUp can become messy and unmaintainable without clear team conventions.

**Winner: Trello** for simplicity. **ClickUp** for power users who'll invest in learning it.

## Flexibility and Customization

Trello's flexibility is primarily delivered through **Power-Ups** — integrations and add-ons that extend core functionality. Want a timeline view? Add a Power-Up. Want time tracking? Add a Power-Up. The free plan only allows 1 Power-Up per board, which is a significant limitation.

ClickUp's flexibility is built-in. Custom fields, custom statuses, custom views, and automation rules are all available without add-ons. Every workspace can be configured differently to match how your team actually works.

**Winner: ClickUp** for out-of-the-box flexibility without add-on costs.

## Integrations

Trello supports 200+ Power-Ups including Slack, Google Drive, GitHub, and Jira. As an Atlassian product, it connects well with Confluence and the Atlassian ecosystem.

ClickUp integrates with 1,000+ tools including Slack, GitHub, Figma, HubSpot, Salesforce, and more. It also has a native email integration that lets you manage work directly from your inbox.

**Winner: ClickUp** for breadth of integrations.

## Team Size Fit

**Trello** works best for:
- Teams of 1-10 people
- Simple creative or content workflows
- Teams who just need "sticky notes on a wall"
- First-time project management tool users

**ClickUp** works better for:
- Teams of 5-100+ people
- Complex cross-functional projects
- Teams that want to consolidate multiple tools
- Agencies and client service businesses

## When to Stick With Trello

Trello's simplicity is a feature, not a limitation. If your team manages relatively simple workflows and everyone actually uses the tool (compared to a more powerful tool nobody understands), Trello wins. Many Trello users who switch to ClickUp eventually find the complexity isn't worth it for their needs.

## When to Switch to ClickUp

If you're constantly fighting Trello's limitations — adding Power-Ups for every basic feature, struggling to track time or goals, or needing more views than just Kanban — ClickUp is almost certainly the right upgrade.

## Verdict

**Trello** is still the king of simple Kanban in 2026. For teams that value speed, clarity, and zero learning curve, it's hard to beat.

**ClickUp** is the better platform for teams that have outgrown simple boards and want a single tool that handles tasks, docs, goals, and time tracking together.

The right answer depends on your team size, complexity, and how much time you're willing to invest in configuration.
