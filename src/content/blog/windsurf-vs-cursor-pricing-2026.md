# Windsurf vs Cursor Pricing 2026: $15 vs $20 AI Code Editor Showdown

Windsurf and Cursor are the closest comparison in AI coding: both are AI-native editors — forked from VS Code, agent at the center, built for developers working in a real codebase. Their entry plans land just $5 apart, Windsurf Pro at $15/month and Cursor Pro at $20/month. With the products this similar, the pricing *model* matters more than the headline number — one meters by daily quota, the other by a monthly credit pool.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Windsurf | Cursor |
|---|----------|--------|
| **Free** | $0, light daily quota | Hobby — $0, limited Agent + Tab |
| **Entry paid** | Pro — $15/mo, 50 premium/day | Pro — $20/mo, $20 credit pool |
| **Mid tier** | — | Pro+ — $60/mo, 3x credits |
| **Top tier** | — | Ultra — $200/mo, 20x usage |
| **Team tier** | Teams — $30/user/mo | Teams — $40/user/mo |
| **Pricing model** | Daily quotas (reset every 24h) | Monthly credit pool |

## The Free Tiers

**Windsurf Free ($0)** offers a light daily quota of AI interactions plus unlimited Tab completions — enough to test the Cascade agent, but you'll hit the daily ceiling in a focused session.

**Cursor Hobby ($0)** gives a limited slice of Agent and Tab usage with no credit card. Both free tiers are "try before you buy" rather than "use indefinitely." Pick whichever editor's feel you prefer — they're close enough that the free tier is mostly about UX taste.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($15) vs Pro ($20)

**Windsurf Pro — $15/month.** Windsurf moved off its old credit system to **daily quotas**: 50 premium AI interactions per day that reset every 24 hours, plus unlimited Tab. The daily reset is the headline feature — burn through a heavy morning and you're topped back up tomorrow, with no monthly cliff. At $15, it undercuts Cursor by $5 for a very similar editing experience. (Older Windsurf docs may still mention the previous credit tiers — the quota system is what's in effect now.)

**Cursor Pro — $20/month.** A **monthly credit pool**: $20 of premium-model credits, unlimited Tab, unlimited Auto mode. The pool model rewards uneven usage — a quiet week leaves headroom for a busy one, but run dry mid-month and premium models pause until you top up or the cycle resets. See [Cursor's full pricing breakdown](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/) for how the credits stretch.

The real difference: Windsurf's daily quota suits steady everyday coding (you can't bank it, but you can't run out for the month either); Cursor's monthly pool suits bursty work (bank quiet days for crunch time). Code a consistent amount daily? Windsurf's $15 and the daily reset. Work in unpredictable sprints? Cursor's $20 pool.

## Where They Scale

This is where Cursor pulls ahead on range. **Windsurf** keeps it simple — Pro at $15, then **Teams at $30/user/month** for centralized billing. **Cursor** offers a fuller ladder: **Pro+ ($60)** triples credits, **Ultra ($200)** gives 20x usage for all-day agent users, and **Teams ($40/user/month)** adds SSO and admin. If you're a power user who'll outgrow a daily quota, Cursor has somewhere for you to go that Windsurf doesn't. For a small team, three Windsurf seats (~$90) beat three Cursor seats (~$120) on cost. Both discount ~20% on annual billing — and like [GitHub Copilot](/blog/github-copilot-pricing-2026/), both offer free access to verified students.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **You code a steady amount most days and want the cheapest AI-native editor** → Windsurf Pro ($15).
- **Your usage is bursty and you want to bank quiet days** → Cursor Pro ($20).
- **You're a heavy daily agent user** → Cursor's Pro+/Ultra ladder; Windsurf tops out at Pro.
- **Small team on a budget** → Windsurf Teams ($30/user) over Cursor Teams ($40/user).

These two editors are close enough that you won't go wrong either way. Let the $5 and the metering model decide: steady coders save with Windsurf's daily quota; power users and bursty workflows get more room in Cursor's pool-and-ladder.
