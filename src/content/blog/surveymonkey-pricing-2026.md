# SurveyMonkey Pricing 2026: Every Plan Explained

SurveyMonkey's pricing isn't complicated, but it is confusing — mostly because the gap between the free plan and the first useful paid plan is enormous. The free tier limits you to 10 questions and 40 responses per survey. The cheapest plan that removes those limits costs $39/mo with annual billing.

Here's every plan, what it includes, and what the fine print says.

## Individual Plans

SurveyMonkey offers four individual tiers. One is free, one is month-to-month, and two require annual commitment.

### Basic (Free)

| | |
|---|---|
| Price | $0 |
| Questions per survey | 10 |
| Responses per survey | 40 |
| Surveys | Unlimited |
| Question types | All basic types |
| Skip logic | No |
| Custom branding | No |
| Data export | No |
| Support | Self-service only |

The free plan lets you create unlimited surveys, but each one caps at 10 questions and 40 responses. Responses beyond 40 are collected but hidden — you can't see them unless you upgrade. There's no data export, no custom branding, and no skip logic.

This is functional for a one-time internal poll (e.g., "Where should the team go for lunch?"). For anything professional, it's not enough.

### Standard Monthly — $99/mo

| | |
|---|---|
| Price | $99/mo (no annual option) |
| Questions per survey | Unlimited |
| Responses per month | 1,000 |
| Skip logic | Yes |
| Custom branding | Yes |
| Data export | Yes (CSV, PDF, XLS) |
| A/B testing | No |
| Sentiment analysis | No |

Standard is the only plan available on a true month-to-month basis. At $99/mo it's expensive, but it's the only option if you need survey capabilities for a short-term project without committing to a year.

The 1,000 responses per month limit resets each billing cycle. Unused responses don't roll over.

### Advantage Annual — $39/mo ($468/yr)

| | |
|---|---|

| Questions per survey | Unlimited |
| Responses per month | 5,000 |

| Sentiment analysis | No |

Advantage is the plan most individuals end up on. It removes the question cap, gives you 5,000 responses/mo, and unlocks features that matter: skip logic, A/B testing, SPSS export, custom branding, and multilingual surveys.

The catch: it requires annual billing. There's no monthly option. You're paying $468 upfront (or in annual installments depending on your payment method).

Premier doubles the response cap to 10,000/mo and adds sentiment analysis, block randomization, phone support, and advanced analytics tools. This tier makes sense for professional researchers or marketers who run large-scale surveys regularly.

At $1,428/yr, it's a significant investment. Most individuals won't need it unless they're consistently hitting the 5,000 response ceiling on Advantage.

## Team Plans

Team plans require a minimum of 3 users and annual billing. They share response pools across the team rather than giving each user a separate limit.

### Team Advantage — $30/user/mo

| | |
|---|---|
| Price | $30/user/mo (3+ users, annual billing) |
| Shared responses | 50,000/yr |
| Everything in Advantage | Yes |
| Shared asset library | Yes |
| Admin dashboard | Yes |
| Collaboration tools | Yes |

Team Advantage pools 50,000 responses per year across your team. Each member gets all Advantage features plus shared templates, a brand kit, and admin controls.

For a team of 3, that's $90/mo ($1,080/yr). The per-user cost is cheaper than Individual Advantage ($39/mo), and the shared response pool is generous for most team use cases.

### Team Premier — $92/user/mo

| | |
|---|---|
| Price | $92/user/mo (annual billing) |
| Shared responses | Unlimited |
| Everything in Premier | Yes |
| Admin dashboard | Yes |
| HIPAA compliance | Available |
| SSO (SAML) | Available |
| API access | Yes |

Team Premier removes the response cap entirely and adds enterprise-adjacent features: HIPAA compliance options, SSO, and API access. For a team of 5, the cost is $460/mo ($5,520/yr).

This tier targets organizations with heavy survey volume and compliance requirements. If you're running thousands of surveys across departments, the unlimited responses justify the cost.

### Enterprise — Custom Pricing

Enterprise adds dedicated customer success managers, custom integrations, advanced security controls, and priority support. Pricing is negotiated based on team size — expect north of $100/user/mo for mid-size deployments.

## Overage Fees

If you exceed your response limit, SurveyMonkey charges **$0.15 per additional response**. There's no automatic shutoff — your survey stays open and overages accumulate. A survey that collects 2,000 extra responses costs an additional $300.

**How to avoid overages:** Set a response cap in survey settings, close the survey manually at your target, or upgrade your plan before launching a high-volume survey.

## Which Plan Should You Pick?

Here's a quick decision framework:

**Free (Basic)** — You need a simple poll with fewer than 10 questions and fewer than 40 respondents. Office lunch votes, quick team check-ins, nothing serious.

**Standard ($99/mo)** — You need full survey features for 1-3 months and don't want an annual commitment. Conference feedback, event surveys, short-term research projects.

**Advantage ($39/mo annual)** — You run surveys regularly and need skip logic, branding, and decent response limits. This is the most popular individual plan for a reason.

**Premier ($119/mo annual)** — You're a researcher or marketer who consistently needs 5,000+ responses per month, sentiment analysis, and advanced question randomization.

**Team Advantage ($30/user/mo)** — Your team of 3+ runs surveys collaboratively and needs shared templates and a pooled response budget.

**Team Premier ($92/user/mo)** — Your organization needs unlimited responses, HIPAA compliance, SSO, and enterprise-level controls.

## Is SurveyMonkey Worth the Price?

That depends on what you're comparing it to. Google Forms is free and handles basic surveys perfectly well. Typeform starts at $25/mo and offers a better respondent experience for customer-facing forms. Tally gives you unlimited responses on its free plan.

SurveyMonkey's value lies in its analytics depth, certified question bank, enterprise integrations (especially Salesforce), and brand trust. If those matter to your workflow, the pricing is justifiable. If they don't, cheaper alternatives will serve you fine.
