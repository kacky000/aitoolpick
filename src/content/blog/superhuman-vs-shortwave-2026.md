Superhuman and Shortwave are the two most talked-about **AI email clients** in 2026. Both promise a faster, smarter inbox with AI drafting, summaries, and keyboard-first navigation. But they sit at very different price points and lean on different philosophies. This **Superhuman vs Shortwave** comparison shows which one fits your workflow.

## Quick Comparison

| | Superhuman | Shortwave |
|---|---|---|
| Free plan | No | Yes |
| Starting price | $30/mo ($25 annual) | $8.50/mo ($7 annual) |
| AI drafting | Auto Drafts, Ask AI | AI assistant, smart compose |
| Thread summaries | Yes | Yes |
| Platforms | Gmail, Outlook | Gmail-only |
| Best for | Speed obsessives | Gmail power users wanting value |

## Pricing

This is the clearest difference. Shortwave has a real **free plan** and starts at **$8.50/month** (Personal), with Pro at $18 for heavy AI users. Superhuman has **no free tier** and starts at **$30/month** ($25 billed annually), with Business at $40.

For the same core promise — a faster AI inbox — Shortwave costs a fraction of Superhuman. Unless raw speed is worth the premium to you, Shortwave wins on value alone.

**Winner: Shortwave.**

## Speed & Keyboard Control

Superhuman is built around speed. Every interaction is keyboard-driven, the UI is famously snappy, and the onboarding literally coaches you to hit "inbox zero" faster. If you process hundreds of emails a day, that responsiveness is the whole point.

Shortwave is fast and keyboard-friendly too, but it doesn't obsess over sub-100ms interactions the way Superhuman does.

**Winner: Superhuman.**

## AI Features

Both clients summarize threads, draft replies, and offer AI search. Superhuman's Auto Drafts pre-write replies in your voice, and Ask AI answers questions across your inbox. Shortwave's AI executive assistant does similar work — summaries, drafting, and natural-language search — and gives heavier AI usage on its Pro tier.

In practice the AI quality is close. Superhuman feels more polished; Shortwave gives you more AI value per dollar.

**Winner: Tie.**

## Platform Support

Superhuman supports both Gmail and Outlook, making it viable for Microsoft-based teams. Shortwave is **Gmail-only** — a dealbreaker if you're on Outlook, but a non-issue if you live in Google Workspace.

**Winner: Superhuman** (broader), but irrelevant if you're a Gmail user.

## Which Should You Choose?

- **Choose Superhuman if:** speed is everything, you process huge email volume, you need Outlook support, and price isn't a constraint.
- **Choose Shortwave if:** you use Gmail, want strong AI features, and care about value — especially with its free tier and $8.50 entry price.

For most Gmail users, Shortwave delivers 90% of the experience at a quarter of the cost.

## Bottom Line

Superhuman is the premium choice for speed-obsessed power users who'll pay $30/month for the fastest inbox available. Shortwave is the smarter value pick for Gmail users who want AI assistance without the premium price. Start with Shortwave's free plan; upgrade to Superhuman only if you find yourself wishing it were faster.
