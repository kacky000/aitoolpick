Dashlane is one of the most polished password managers available, but it's also one of the priciest. Here's exactly what each plan costs and whether it's worth the premium over free alternatives.

## Dashlane Plans & Pricing

| Plan | Price | Passwords | Devices | Key Features |
|------|-------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Free** | $0 | 25 | 1 device | Basic password management |
| **Premium** | $4.99/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited | VPN, dark web monitoring |
| **Friends & Family** | $7.49/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited | Up to 10 accounts |
| **Business** | $8/user/mo | Unlimited | Unlimited | Admin console, SSO, SCIM |

All paid plans are billed annually. Dashlane does not offer monthly billing — you'll pay for the full year upfront.

## Plan Details

### Free ($0)
Dashlane's free tier is functional but limited:
- 25 password limit
- 1 device only (no syncing)
- Password generator and autofill
- Security alerts for compromised passwords

With only 25 passwords on a single device, the free plan is essentially a trial. Most people have 80+ accounts, so you'll hit the limit fast.

### Premium ($4.99/month, billed annually at $59.88)
The core Dashlane experience:
- **Unlimited passwords** across unlimited devices
- **VPN for Wi-Fi protection** — a unique inclusion among password managers
- **Dark web monitoring** — alerts when your emails appear in breaches
- **Password Health Score** — identifies weak, reused, and compromised passwords
- **Secure notes and file storage** (1GB)
- **Passkey support** for passwordless login

The built-in VPN is Dashlane's standout feature. No other major password manager includes one. It's powered by Hotspot Shield and is decent for basic Wi-Fi protection, though it won't replace a dedicated VPN for streaming or heavy use.

### Friends & Family ($7.49/month, billed annually at $89.88)
Everything in Premium, plus:
- **Up to 10 separate accounts** under one subscription
- Each member gets their own private vault
- Family dashboard for managing invites

At $7.49/month for 10 accounts, that's $0.75/person — an excellent deal for families. Each person gets the full Premium feature set, including the VPN.

### Business ($8/user/month)
Built for teams and organizations:
- **Admin console** with user provisioning
- **SSO integration** (SAML-based)
- **SCIM directory sync** with Active Directory, Okta, etc.
- **Group sharing** with fine-grained permissions
- **Activity logs** and compliance reporting
- Free Friends & Family plan for each employee

## Is Dashlane Worth the Price?

### It's worth it if you:
- Want a VPN bundled with your password manager (saves buying separately)
- Value a polished, intuitive interface over maximum customization
- Have a family that needs 10 accounts on one plan
- Need dark web monitoring included (not a paid add-on)

### Consider alternatives if you:
- Want the cheapest option — **Bitwarden** at $1/month or free can't be beaten
- Need advanced features like secure sharing categories — **1Password** offers more organizational tools
- Already have a VPN subscription — Dashlane's VPN becomes redundant
- Are a solo user on a budget — 1Password at $2.99/month is 40% cheaper

## Hidden Costs & Gotchas

- **No monthly billing** — you commit to a full year ($59.88 minimum)
- **Auto-renewal** at full price — no returning-customer discounts
- **VPN is basic** — it works for Wi-Fi security but can't match dedicated VPN providers for speed, server selection, or streaming
- **Import limitations** — migrating from some password managers requires CSV export, which can be messy

## Best Way to Buy

1. **Try the free plan first** to test the interface
2. **Premium is the sweet spot** for individuals — skip the VPN if you already have one
3. **Family plan is the best value** per-person if you have 3+ people
