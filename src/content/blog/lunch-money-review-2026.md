Lunch Money is a web-based budgeting app built by a solo developer that's carved out a niche among tech workers, digital nomads, and anyone managing money across multiple currencies. It's not trying to be the next YNAB — it's building something different. Here's the full picture for 2026.

## What Is Lunch Money?

Lunch Money is a personal finance tracker with budgeting, transaction management, and multi-currency support. It connects to banks via Plaid (US/Canada) or manual CSV import, and runs entirely in the browser. There's no mobile app — just a responsive web interface.

What sets it apart: built-in multi-currency handling, a developer API, and a design philosophy that respects your intelligence. No gamification, no "financial wellness scores," no upsells. Just a clean tool for tracking where your money goes.

## What Lunch Money Does Well

### Multi-Currency Is First-Class

This is Lunch Money's killer feature. If you earn in USD but spend in EUR and JPY, Lunch Money handles it natively. You set a primary currency, and everything converts automatically using real exchange rates. Budgets can be set in any currency. Reports show totals in your primary currency.

YNAB and Monarch Money technically support multiple currencies, but it's an afterthought — manual conversion, workarounds, and constant friction. Lunch Money was built for this use case from the start.

### Developer-Friendly API

Lunch Money offers a full REST API with documented endpoints for transactions, categories, budgets, and recurring items. You can build custom integrations, automate transaction tagging, or pull data into your own dashboards.

This matters for the type of user who chooses Lunch Money — people who want to extend their tools, not just use them as-is.

### Clean, Focused Interface

The UI is minimal and fast. Dashboard shows your month at a glance: spending by category, budget progress, and account balances. There's no clutter, no dashboard widgets competing for attention, no onboarding wizards.

### Recurring Transaction Management

Lunch Money automatically detects recurring transactions and tracks them separately. You can see your fixed monthly costs in one view, which makes it easy to audit subscriptions and recurring charges.

### Crypto Tracking

Lunch Money supports cryptocurrency accounts. You can track crypto balances alongside traditional accounts, and they'll be included in your net worth calculations. It's basic — no detailed portfolio analysis — but it's more than most budgeting apps offer.

## Where Lunch Money Falls Short

### No Mobile App

There's no iOS or Android app. The web interface works on mobile browsers, but it's not optimized for quick on-the-go transaction entry. If you log expenses as they happen throughout the day, this is a real friction point.

### No Zero-Based Budgeting

Lunch Money uses simple category budgets: set a monthly limit, track spending against it. There's no envelope system, no "give every dollar a job" philosophy. If you want the discipline of zero-based budgeting, YNAB or Actual Budget serve that better.

### Limited Bank Coverage Outside US/Canada

Bank sync via Plaid works well in the US and Canada but coverage drops off internationally. For users in Europe, Asia, or elsewhere, you'll likely rely on CSV imports or the API. This is a common limitation, but given Lunch Money's multi-currency focus, it's worth noting.

### Solo Developer Risk

Lunch Money is built and maintained primarily by one developer. While that means fast, opinionated updates, it also means the product's future depends on one person's decisions and availability. The API and CSV export mitigate lock-in risk, but it's something to consider.

## Who Should Use Lunch Money?

**Great for:**
- Digital nomads and expats managing multiple currencies
- Developers who want API access to their financial data
- Tech-savvy users who prefer a clean, no-nonsense interface
- Anyone tracking crypto alongside traditional accounts

**Not ideal for:**
- Users who want a dedicated mobile app for daily tracking
- People who need zero-based envelope budgeting
- Families wanting shared household budgets
- Users outside US/Canada who need automatic bank sync

## The Verdict

Lunch Money is the best budgeting app for multi-currency users in 2026, and a strong choice for anyone who values simplicity and API access. It won't hold your hand with budgeting methodology — it assumes you know what you want to track and gives you clean tools to do it.

If you manage money across currencies or you're the type who'd build a custom Zapier workflow for your budget, Lunch Money is worth the 14-day trial.
