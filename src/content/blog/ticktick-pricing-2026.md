**Ticktick** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

TickTick has one of the simplest pricing structures in productivity software: free tier, one paid tier. No confusing business plan, no per-seat pricing, no feature gating across five plans.

Here's exactly what you get at each level and whether it's worth paying.

## TickTick Pricing Plans in 2026

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Annual Cost | Best For |
|------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Free | $0 | $0 | Evaluating the app |
| Premium | $2.99/month | $35.99/year | Serious personal use |

That's it. No team plan, no enterprise tier — TickTick is positioned as a personal productivity tool, not team project management software.

## Free Plan: What You Get (and What You Don't)

The free plan is functional enough to understand how TickTick works but too restricted for daily use:

**Included in Free:**
- 1 list
- 9 tasks per list (99 tasks total across sublists)
- Basic recurring tasks
- Due dates and reminders
- Mobile and desktop apps
- 2 devices

**Not in Free:**
- Calendar view
- Board view (Kanban)
- Habit tracker
- Pomodoro timer
- Custom filters
- Custom sorting
- Task activity history
- Priority inbox
- Unlimited task lists

The single-list and 9-task-per-list restriction makes the free plan nearly unusable for managing real work. Most users hit the ceiling on day one. That's intentional — TickTick wants you to upgrade.

## Premium Plan: Full Feature Breakdown

At $35.99/year ($2.99/month billed annually or $5.99 billed monthly), Premium unlocks:

**Task Management:**
- Unlimited lists and tasks
- Unlimited recurring task options (every X days, specific weekdays, custom patterns)
- Custom sorting and filters
- Smart date suggestions
- Task activity history

**Views:**
- **Calendar view** — Your tasks overlaid on a weekly/monthly calendar. Google Calendar sync included.
- **Board view** — Kanban-style columns for visual project tracking
- **Timeline view** — Gantt-style view for planning projects across time

**Productivity Features:**
- **Habit Tracker** — Define habits, track streaks, view completion history
- **Pomodoro Timer** — Built into each task; sessions auto-record
- **Focus statistics** — Total focus time, sessions per day, productive hours

**Collaboration (limited):**
- Share lists with up to 5 members
- Task assignment
- Comment threads on tasks

## How TickTick Premium Compares to Competitors

| Tool | Annual Price | Calendar View | Habit Tracker | Pomodoro |
|------|-------------|---------------|---------------|---------|
| TickTick Premium | $35.99 | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| [Todoist](/tools/todoist/) Pro | $48/year | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Things 3 (one-time) | $49.99 | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| [Notion](/tools/notion/) Personal | $192/year | ❌ (separate) | ❌ | ❌ |

At $35.99/year, TickTick is the cheapest of these options — and it includes more built-in productivity features than any of them. Todoist costs 33% more for fewer features. Things 3 is macOS only. Notion is 5x more expensive and doesn't replace a dedicated task manager.

## Is TickTick Premium Worth It?

**Yes, if:**
- You want tasks, habits, and Pomodoro in one app (saves paying for separate apps)
- You manage your schedule by calendar view
- You're already paying for Todoist Pro — TickTick gives more for less

**No, if:**
- You need team project management → [ClickUp](/blog/clickup-review-2026) or [Asana](/blog/asana-review-2026) are better suited
- You want tasks integrated with docs and databases → [Notion](/blog/notion-review-2026)
- You're Apple-only and want native design → Things 3 may fit better

## The Monthly vs Annual Calculation

Billed monthly: $5.99/month = $71.88/year  
Billed annually: $35.99/year = $3.00/month

The annual plan saves you $36 — 50% savings. If you're committing to TickTick, pay annually. The monthly option only makes sense if you're testing it before committing, and in that case the free plan is a better test anyway.

## Student Discount

TickTick doesn't currently advertise an official student discount program, but the $35.99/year price is already at student-budget levels. For comparison, Spotify Student is $5.99/month — TickTick Premium costs less per year than 6 months of Spotify.

## Verdict: Best Value in Task Management

For personal productivity, TickTick Premium offers the best feature-to-price ratio in the category. The calendar view and Pomodoro timer alone replace apps that cost more combined than TickTick's annual fee.

The free plan is good enough to test the UI and UX. Upgrade to Premium when you need the calendar view or want to track habits — which will happen within the first week for most users.
