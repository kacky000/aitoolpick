# Gumroad vs Lemon Squeezy 2026: Which Platform for Selling Digital Products?

If you sell digital products — ebooks, templates, courses, software — you've probably narrowed your options to Gumroad and Lemon Squeezy. Both let you set up a storefront, process payments, and deliver files without code. But they solve different problems and charge very differently.

Gumroad has been around since 2011, built on simplicity. Lemon Squeezy launched in 2021 and made its name handling global tax compliance as a Merchant of Record. That single difference shapes everything else.

## Gumroad Overview

Gumroad pioneered the "just paste a link and sell" model for digital creators, and millions of products have been sold through it.

The pitch is straightforward: upload your product, set a price, share the link. Gumroad handles payment processing and file delivery. You get a storefront, email tools, and sales analytics.

In 2026, Gumroad operates on a single plan with no monthly fees. You pay 10% of every sale, which includes payment processing. No tiered pricing, no feature gating — every seller gets the same tools.

That simplicity is both its strength and its limitation. Setting up your first product takes minutes. But if you need license key generation, SaaS billing, or VAT handling across 40 countries, you'll hit walls.

For a deeper look, read our full [Gumroad review](/blog/gumroad-review-2026).

## Lemon Squeezy Overview

Lemon Squeezy targets the exact pain points Gumroad doesn't solve: global tax compliance and software licensing.

The key differentiator is that Lemon Squeezy acts as your **Merchant of Record (MoR)**. This means Lemon Squeezy is technically the seller. They collect payments, handle VAT, sales tax, and GST filings in every jurisdiction, then pay you out minus fees. You never register for VAT in the EU or file sales tax in US states.

Lemon Squeezy also provides built-in license key management and a polished checkout experience with overlay and embedded options. The overall UX is clean and developer-friendly.

## Pricing and Fees

This is where the decision gets interesting, because the cheaper option depends on your price point.

### Gumroad

- **10% flat fee per transaction** (includes payment processing)
- No monthly fees
- No volume discounts

On a $10 sale, you pay $1.00. On a $100 sale, you pay $10.00.

### Lemon Squeezy

- **5% + 50¢ per transaction** on the standard tier
- Drops to **3.5% + 50¢** at higher volumes
- No monthly fees

On a $10 sale, you pay $1.00 (5% + 50¢). On a $100 sale, you pay $5.50.

**The math is clear at higher price points.** For a $50 product, Gumroad takes $5.00 while Lemon Squeezy takes $3.00. At $100, the gap doubles.

At very low price points (under $5), Lemon Squeezy's 50¢ fixed fee hurts more. A $3 ebook nets you $2.70 on Gumroad versus $2.35 on Lemon Squeezy.

**Bottom line on fees:** Products above $10 — Lemon Squeezy is cheaper. High volume of $1-5 micro-products — Gumroad's flat percentage works better.

## Feature Comparison

### Checkout Experience

Both platforms offer embeddable checkout and hosted checkout pages. Lemon Squeezy's checkout is newer and arguably more polished — it supports overlay modals, custom branding, and feels fast. Gumroad's checkout works fine but hasn't changed much visually in years.

### Analytics, Affiliates, and Email

Both platforms provide basic sales analytics, built-in affiliate programs, and simple email broadcasting tools. The implementations are roughly equivalent. Neither platform's email tools compete with dedicated services like ConvertKit, but they're good enough for product launch announcements and customer updates.

### License Keys

This is a Lemon Squeezy exclusive. If you sell software, Lemon Squeezy generates and validates license keys through their API — activation limits, expiration dates, and programmatic validation included. Gumroad doesn't offer this; software sellers on Gumroad build their own licensing layer or use a third-party service.

### SaaS and Subscription Billing

Both support recurring payments. Lemon Squeezy goes further with usage-based billing, trials, plan changes, and dunning management suited to SaaS businesses. Gumroad's subscription support is more basic.

## Tax and Compliance

This is the single biggest differentiator, and for many sellers, it's the entire reason to choose Lemon Squeezy.

**Gumroad** does not handle tax compliance for you. If you sell to customers in the EU, UK, Canada, Australia, or US states with digital goods tax, you're responsible for collecting and remitting the correct amount. For a solo creator, this can mean VAT registration in multiple countries, quarterly filings, or paying for a third-party tax service.

**Lemon Squeezy**, as Merchant of Record, handles all of this automatically. They calculate the correct tax at checkout, collect it, and file it with the relevant authorities. You receive your payout with taxes already deducted. You never register, file, or think about it.

For international sellers, this alone can save thousands in accounting fees and dozens of hours per year.

## Who Should Choose Gumroad

- You sell low-priced products (under $10) where the flat 10% fee is comparable or cheaper
- You want the simplest possible setup with zero learning curve
- You sell primarily to US customers and aren't worried about international tax
- You don't need license key management or advanced subscription features

## Who Should Choose Lemon Squeezy

- You sell internationally and don't want to deal with VAT, GST, or sales tax
- You sell software and need built-in license key generation
- Your products are priced above $10 where 5%+50¢ beats 10%
- You're building a SaaS product and need usage-based billing or plan management

## The Verdict

For most digital product sellers in 2026, **Lemon Squeezy is the stronger choice**. The Merchant of Record model solves a real, expensive problem. The fees are lower for anything above $10. And the feature set — license keys, better subscription management — is more complete.

Gumroad still wins on simplicity. If you're selling a $5 PDF to a mostly domestic audience, Gumroad gets you from zero to selling in five minutes. There's value in that.

But if you're building a digital products business, selling internationally, or shipping software, Lemon Squeezy handles the hard parts that Gumroad leaves to you.
