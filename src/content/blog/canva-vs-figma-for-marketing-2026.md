**Canva** and **Figma** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Marketing teams often face a choice: Canva's ease of use or Figma's design power. Both tools have grown dramatically in 2026. Here's how to decide which is right for your marketing team.

## At a Glance

| Factor | Canva | Figma |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Learning curve | Very easy | Moderate |
| Template library | Massive (millions) | Smaller (community-driven) |
| Collaboration | Real-time | Real-time |
| Brand consistency tools | Strong (Brand Kit) | Excellent (Design Systems) |
| Price (paid) | $15/user/mo | $15/editor/mo |
| Best for | Non-designers, social content | Designer-marketer hybrids, complex assets |

---

## Where Canva Wins for Marketing

**1. Speed for social media content**
Canva's pre-sized templates for every platform (Instagram Reels, LinkedIn posts, Twitter headers) let non-designers produce on-brand content in minutes. There's no equivalent in Figma for speed.

**2. Non-designer accessibility**
Any marketing manager can use Canva without training. Figma has a steeper learning curve — if your team doesn't have design experience, Canva is the realistic choice.

**3. AI content generation**
Canva's AI tools in 2026 include text-to-image, background removal, one-click resize, and AI copywriting suggestions. These features are directly built into the workflow.

**4. Print and export**
Canva handles print-ready exports, business cards, flyers, and presentations seamlessly. Figma is primarily a screen design tool.

---

## Where Figma Wins for Marketing

**1. Brand consistency at scale**
Figma's design system with shared components means any team member who updates a color or logo automatically updates it across all files. Canva's Brand Kit does this too, but Figma's version is more powerful for complex brands.

**2. Landing pages and UI assets**
If your marketing team creates web assets (hero images, email templates, landing page wireframes), Figma's pixel-precise controls and developer handoff mode are essential.

**3. Complex multi-format campaigns**
A coordinated campaign with a dozen ad sizes, landing page variations, and email banners is easier to manage in Figma with a structured component system.

**4. Design-developer handoff**
If your designs go to a developer, Figma's Dev Mode outputs exact CSS, spacing values, and assets. Canva doesn't have this workflow.

## Cost Comparison

Both tools start at $15/user/month for paid plans, but the value is different:

**Canva Pro ($15/mo per user):**
- Brand Kit
- Premium templates and elements
- Background Remover
- Content Planner
- 100GB cloud storage

**Figma Professional ($15/mo per editor):**
- Unlimited projects
- Shared libraries
- Dev Mode access
- Advanced prototyping
- Version history

Figma also charges per *editor*, not per viewer — team members who only view/comment are free. For larger marketing teams with many stakeholders who just need to review work, Figma can be more economical.

---

## The Hybrid Approach

Many marketing teams use both:
- **Canva** for day-to-day social posts, quick campaign assets, and content created by non-designers
- **Figma** for brand standards documentation, complex campaigns, and anything that needs developer handoff

This isn't redundant — the tools serve genuinely different workflows.

---

## Who Should Choose What

**Choose Canva if:**
- Most content creators on your team aren't trained designers
- You primarily make social media content
- You need speed above all else
- You want an all-in-one platform with scheduling, printing, and AI tools

**Choose Figma if:**
- Your team includes designers or design-adjacent marketers
- You're building web assets or need developer handoff
- Brand consistency at a system level is critical
- You're already in the product/design ecosystem

## Final Verdict

For most marketing teams, **Canva is the right starting point**. It's faster, more accessible, and handles 90% of marketing content needs. Add Figma if your team grows and needs design system management or developer handoff.
