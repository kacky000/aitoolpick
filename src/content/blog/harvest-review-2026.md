[Harvest](/tools/harvest/) has been a trusted name in time tracking since 2006 — making it one of the oldest tools in the category. While newer competitors like [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) and [Clockify](/tools/clockify/) have captured attention with slick interfaces and free plans, Harvest continues to serve a specific audience exceptionally well: professional services teams that bill clients for their time.

## What Is Harvest?

Harvest is a time tracking and invoicing platform designed for teams that sell their time. It combines simple time tracking with project budgeting, expense management, team capacity analysis, and native invoicing — all in one tool. The idea is straightforward: track time, set budgets, invoice clients, get paid.

## Harvest Pricing 2026

| Plan | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | 1 seat, 2 projects, basic time tracking |
| Pro | $10.80/seat/month | Unlimited projects, invoicing, budgets, reports |

Harvest keeps pricing simple with just two plans. The free plan is minimal — enough to test the product but not for real work. Pro at $10.80/seat/month includes everything with no feature gating between tiers.

This flat pricing structure means you get invoicing, budgets, expense tracking, and team capacity on the same plan. No deciding between tiers.

## Key Features

### Time Tracking

Harvest offers multiple ways to log time:

- **Timer**: One-click start/stop on any project and task
- **Manual entry**: Type hours directly into timesheets
- **Weekly timesheets**: Fill out an entire week at once
- **Reminders**: Nudge team members who haven't logged time

The interface is clean and uncluttered. You select a project, choose a task category, optionally add notes, and track. It works across web, desktop (Mac/Windows), mobile (iOS/Android), and browser extensions.

### Invoicing

This is where Harvest differentiates from [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) and [Clockify](/tools/clockify/). You can:

- Generate invoices directly from tracked time and expenses
- Customize invoice templates with your branding
- Set different billable rates per person, project, or task
- Accept online payments (via Stripe/PayPal integration)
- Track invoice status (draft, sent, paid, overdue)
- Set up recurring invoices for retainer clients

The invoicing-to-tracking pipeline is seamless. Select a date range and client, review line items, and send. No exporting to another tool needed.

### Project Budgets

Set budgets in hours or dollars for any project. Harvest shows:

- Budget consumed vs remaining (visual progress bar)
- Burn rate and projected completion
- Budget alerts at configurable thresholds (50%, 75%, 90%)
- Over-budget warnings

For agencies managing multiple client projects simultaneously, this prevents the common problem of going over budget before anyone notices.

### Team Capacity

The capacity report shows:

- How many hours each person has tracked vs their available hours
- Who is over-utilized and who has availability
- Historical utilization trends

Useful for resource planning, though it's not a full scheduling tool like what [Clockify](/tools/clockify/) offers on its Standard plan.

### Expense Tracking

Log expenses against projects with receipt attachments. Expenses can be marked as billable and included on client invoices. Basic but functional for most service businesses.

## Pros

- **Invoicing built-in**: Track time and bill clients in the same tool
- **Project budgets**: Prevent scope creep with real-time budget visibility
- **Simple pricing**: One paid plan with everything included
- **Mature and reliable**: 20 years of development means stability
- **Team capacity**: Understand utilization at a glance
- **Clean interface**: No feature overload — focused on what matters
- **Excellent integrations**: Works with [Asana](/tools/asana/), Trello, [Jira](/tools/jira/), QuickBooks, Xero, Slack

## Cons

- **Expensive for basic tracking**: $10.80/seat vs [Clockify](/tools/clockify/) free or Toggl at $9
- **Limited free plan**: Only 1 seat and 2 projects
- **No scheduling**: Can't plan future resource allocation
- **No project management**: Purely tracking and billing, no task boards or Gantt
- **UI feels dated**: Functional but not as modern as Toggl Track
- **No AI features**: No auto-categorization, predictions, or smart suggestions

## Who Should Use Harvest?

Harvest is purpose-built for:

- **Agencies** billing clients hourly with multiple active projects
- **Consultants** needing to track time, budget, and invoice from one platform
- **Professional services** firms (legal, accounting, design) requiring per-project profitability
- **Freelancers** who want tracking + invoicing without QuickBooks/FreshBooks

If you just need to track where your time goes (without billing clients), [Clockify](/tools/clockify/) or [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) are better values.

## The Verdict

Harvest earns a **4.4/5** in 2026. It's the best time tracking tool for teams that need integrated invoicing and project budgeting. The track-to-invoice pipeline is seamless, budget alerts prevent overruns, and the flat pricing keeps things simple.

The trade-off is price and modernity. [Clockify](/tools/clockify/) does more for less, and [Toggl Track](/tools/toggl-track/) looks better. But neither matches Harvest's invoicing maturity. If billing clients is your primary workflow, Harvest still makes the strongest case.
