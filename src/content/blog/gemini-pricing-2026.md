Google overhauled Gemini's pricing in 2026. The single "Gemini Advanced" tier is gone — replaced by six plans from free to enterprise, with prices starting at $7.99 per month. Here's what each plan costs and which one makes sense for you.

## Gemini Pricing Overview

| Plan | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Casual users, quick questions |
| Google AI Plus | $7.99/mo | Regular users who want fewer limits |
| Google AI Pro | $19.99/mo | Power users, professionals |
| Google AI Ultra | ~$42/mo ($124.99/3mo) | Heavy users, developers, teams of one |
| Business (Workspace) | $20/user/mo | Small-to-mid teams on Google Workspace |
| Enterprise (Workspace) | $30/user/mo | Large organizations with compliance needs |

Let's break down each tier.

## Free Plan

The free tier gives you access to Gemini's base model with daily usage limits. You can ask questions, generate text, analyze images, and get help with basic tasks.

**What's included:**
- Basic Gemini model access
- Daily query limits (roughly 30-50 queries per day depending on complexity)
- Google Search integration
- Basic image understanding
- Limited file upload support

**What's missing:**
- No access to Gemini 3 or Gemini 3 Pro models
- No AI credits for advanced features
- No expanded Google Workspace integration
- Stricter rate limits during peak hours

The free plan handles casual use well but you'll hit walls fast if you try to use it as a daily productivity tool.

## Google AI Plus — $7.99/mo

This is the new entry-level paid tier, and it's aggressively priced. At $7.99 per month, it's the cheapest paid AI chatbot from any major provider.

**What's included:**
- Everything in Free
- Expanded daily usage limits
- 2TB Google One storage (photos, Drive, Gmail)
- Priority access during high-traffic periods
- Extended conversation context

**What's missing:**
- No Gemini 3 model (still uses the standard model)
- No AI credits
- No advanced Workspace integration

The 2TB storage bundled in makes this interesting. If you're already paying $9.99/month for Google One's 2TB plan, switching to AI Plus actually saves you money while adding AI capabilities. That's a clever move by Google.

## Google AI Pro — $19.99/mo

This is Google's answer to [ChatGPT Plus ($20)](/blog/chatgpt-vs-gemini-2026/) and [Claude Pro ($20)](/blog/claude-vs-gemini-2026/). At $19.99, it's a penny cheaper — and it packs serious features.

**What's included:**
- Everything in AI Plus
- Gemini 3 model access (Google's most capable model)
- 1,000 AI credits per month for advanced tasks
- 1 million token context window
- Advanced image generation
- Voice conversation mode (Gemini Live)
- Deep Google Workspace integration (Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides)
- Web search with citations
- Advanced file analysis and code execution

**AI credits** are consumed by compute-intensive operations: long document analysis, complex code generation, image creation, and multi-step reasoning. Basic conversations don't consume credits. Most users won't burn through 1,000 in a month unless they're doing heavy image generation daily.

This is the sweet spot for most users. The Workspace integration is the real differentiator — AI natively inside Gmail, Docs, and Sheets eliminates the copy-paste workflow that plagues ChatGPT and Claude users in Google's ecosystem.

## Google AI Ultra — ~$42/mo ($124.99 per 3 months)

The Ultra tier is for power users who need maximum capability and volume.

**What's included:**
- Everything in AI Pro
- Gemini 3 Pro model (the largest, most capable version)
- 25,000 AI credits per month (25x the Pro allocation)
- Priority processing and fastest response times
- Extended API access
- Early access to experimental features

The 25x credit jump over Pro targets developers and researchers who use AI intensively throughout the day. Note that Ultra is billed quarterly only ($124.99/3 months) — no monthly option, so it requires a $125 upfront commitment.

## Business ($20/user/mo) and Enterprise ($30/user/mo)

Both Workspace plans add Gemini AI directly into Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Meet. The difference is compliance.

**Business** includes AI-assisted email drafting, document generation, formula writing in Sheets, presentation creation, meeting summaries, and admin controls. It's a per-user add-on to your existing Workspace subscription.

**Enterprise** adds data loss prevention (DLP), custom model fine-tuning, enhanced audit logs, data residency controls, and HIPAA/SOC 2 compliance options. The $10/user premium is about security and regulatory requirements — essential for healthcare, finance, and legal organizations.

## Which Plan Is Right for You?

**Stay on Free if:** You use AI a few times a week for quick questions or light brainstorming. The free tier handles casual use without issues.

**Get AI Plus ($7.99) if:** You already pay for Google One storage, or you want a cheap way to get more AI access without committing to a full-featured plan. The 2TB storage bundle alone makes this worthwhile.

**Get AI Pro ($19.99) if:** You use AI daily for work, you're in the Google ecosystem, and you want the best model with Workspace integration. This is the sweet spot for most professionals.

**Get AI Ultra (~$42) if:** You're a developer, researcher, or content creator who pushes AI tools to their limits daily. The 25,000 credit allocation and Gemini 3 Pro model are worth it if you consistently max out the Pro plan.

**Get Business/Enterprise if:** Your team runs on Google Workspace. Choose Enterprise ($30/user) over Business ($20/user) only if you need compliance certifications or data residency controls.

## Bottom Line

Google's new Gemini pricing is competitive. The $7.99 AI Plus tier undercuts every major competitor, and the AI Pro plan matches ChatGPT Plus and Claude Pro on price while offering deeper integration with the Google ecosystem. The Ultra tier is expensive but generous with credits.

The biggest deciding factor isn't price — it's ecosystem. If you live in Google Workspace, Gemini is the obvious choice. If you don't, [ChatGPT](/blog/chatgpt-vs-gemini-2026/) and [Claude](/blog/claude-vs-gemini-2026/) may serve you better.
