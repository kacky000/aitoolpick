**[Coda](/tools/coda/)** and **[ClickUp](/tools/clickup/)** both promise to be the one tool that replaces your docs, spreadsheets, and project tracker. But they charge for that promise in fundamentally different ways — and the gap between their bills can be 2x or more depending on how your team is shaped.

This guide compares every plan, the way each tool counts a billable user, and which one is actually cheaper for solo builders, lopsided teams, and teams where everyone works equally.

## The Key Difference: Who Counts as a Billable User

This is the single fact that decides the comparison.

**Coda only bills "Doc Makers."** Anyone who builds or structures a doc is a Maker and is billed. Editors who only enter data, and all viewers, are **free**. A team where 3 people build and 20 people consume pays for 3 seats.

**ClickUp bills every member with a paid seat.** There's no free-viewer model on Unlimited or Business — each person who logs in and works in your Workspace is a billable seat. The trade-off is that the per-seat price ($7) is among the lowest in the category.

So the math depends entirely on your editor-to-consumer ratio. Coda rewards teams with a few builders and many viewers; ClickUp rewards teams where most people actively work and you want the lowest flat per-seat rate.

## Cost Scenario: A 10-Person Team

Say you have 10 people, but only 3 actively build docs and dashboards.

- **Coda Pro**: 3 Makers × $10 = **$30/month** (7 editors/viewers free)
- **ClickUp Unlimited**: 10 seats × $7 = **$70/month**

Coda is **2.3x cheaper** for this lopsided team.

Now flip it: all 10 people build, edit, and manage work equally.

- **Coda Pro**: 10 Makers × $10 = **$100/month**
- **ClickUp Unlimited**: 10 seats × $7 = **$70/month**

ClickUp is now **30% cheaper**. The flat $7 seat wins whenever everyone is an active worker.

## Free Plan Comparison

- **Coda Free**: Unlimited docs and editors, but capped at **1,000 objects per doc** (rows, buttons, etc.). Data-heavy trackers hit this wall fast.
- **ClickUp Free Forever**: Unlimited members and tasks, but capped at **100 automations/month, 5 spaces, and 60MB storage**. Genuinely usable for a small team for a long time.

For collaborative project management, ClickUp's free plan stretches further because it never charges for additional members. For doc-building, Coda's free tier is fine until the object cap bites.

## Where AI Pricing Differs

- **Coda AI** is bundled into the **Team plan ($30)** — no separate line item.
- **ClickUp Brain** is a **$5/user/month add-on** on any paid plan, so it stacks on top of your seat cost (Unlimited + Brain = $12/user effectively).

If AI is core to your workflow, factor the Brain add-on into ClickUp's real cost — it narrows or erases ClickUp's price advantage at scale.

## Verdict

**Choose Coda** if a small group of builders serves a large group of viewers. The Maker model can cut your bill by more than half, and Pro at $10 is excellent value for formula-driven, automated docs.

**Choose ClickUp** if most of your team actively manages work, you want the deepest project-management feature set, and you value the lowest flat seat price. At $7/user it undercuts most rivals — just remember the Brain add-on if you need AI.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Coda or ClickUp cheaper?

It depends on your team shape. Coda only bills "Makers," so teams with few builders and many viewers pay far less. ClickUp bills every seat at a low flat $7, which wins when most of your team actively works.

### Do viewers cost money in ClickUp?

Yes. On Unlimited and Business, every member with access is a billable seat. ClickUp has no free-viewer tier like Coda's Maker model.

### Does ClickUp charge extra for AI?

Yes. ClickUp Brain is a $5/user/month add-on on top of your plan. Coda bundles its AI into the $30 Team plan instead.

### Which is better value for a solo user?

Both free plans work solo. If you build data-heavy docs, Coda Pro ($10) removes the object cap; if you want full project management features, ClickUp Unlimited ($7) is cheaper.
