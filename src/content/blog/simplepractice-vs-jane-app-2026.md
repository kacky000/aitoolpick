SimplePractice and Jane App are both top-tier practice management platforms — but they're built for slightly different practices. SimplePractice leans toward solo mental-health and wellness providers; Jane App targets multi-discipline clinics with heavy booking and insurance needs. This **SimplePractice vs Jane App** comparison shows which one fits in 2026.

## Quick Comparison

| | SimplePractice | Jane App |
|---|---|---|
| Starting price | $29/mo (Starter) | $54/mo (Base) |
| Insurance tier | Essential $69 | Insurance $79 |
| Free plan | No | No |
| Best for | Solo therapists & wellness pros | Multi-discipline clinics |
| Online booking | Strong | Excellent |
| Telehealth | Plus plan ($99) | Add-on |

## Pricing

SimplePractice starts lower at **$29/month**, but the tier most therapists need (Essential, with insurance billing) is **$69**. Jane App starts at **$54/month** for its Base plan and **$79** once you add insurance features.

For a budget-conscious solo therapist, SimplePractice is cheaper at entry. For a clinic that needs robust scheduling across many practitioners, Jane's per-practitioner model often scales more predictably.

## Booking & Scheduling

Jane App is famous for its online booking. Multi-practitioner, multi-location scheduling with treatment-specific durations is where Jane excels — it's a favorite of physiotherapy, massage, and multi-discipline clinics.

SimplePractice's scheduling is excellent for solo and small practices but isn't built around the same clinic-scale complexity.

**Winner: Jane App** for clinics, **SimplePractice** for solos.

## Discipline Fit

SimplePractice is optimized for mental health, therapy, and wellness coaching — its notes and workflows reflect that. Jane App is multi-discipline by design: physiotherapy, chiropractic, acupuncture, naturopathy, and mental health all coexist with discipline-specific charting.

**Winner: Jane App** for mixed clinics, **SimplePractice** for pure therapy.

## Insurance & Billing

Both handle claims and superbills. Jane's insurance handling (especially for North American clinics) is robust, with Base plus an Insurance tier. SimplePractice covers insurance from the Essential plan up and is very approachable for solo billers.

**Winner: Tie** — depends on volume and discipline.

## Telehealth & Client Experience

SimplePractice bundles a polished client portal and reserves telehealth for its Plus plan. Jane offers telehealth as an add-on and a clean patient-facing booking flow. Both feel modern; SimplePractice edges ahead on the solo-therapist client portal experience.

**Winner: SimplePractice** (slightly).

## Which Should You Choose?

- **Choose SimplePractice if:** you're a solo therapist or small wellness practice that wants the lowest entry price and a polished client portal.
- **Choose Jane App if:** you run a multi-practitioner or multi-discipline clinic and need best-in-class booking and charting across treatment types.

## Bottom Line

SimplePractice is the better pick for solo mental-health and wellness providers who want a low entry price and a great client experience. Jane App wins for multi-discipline clinics that live and die by scheduling and need discipline-specific charting at scale.
