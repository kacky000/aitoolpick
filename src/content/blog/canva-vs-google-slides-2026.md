Canva and Google Slides are both free presentation tools — but they serve very different needs. This comparison breaks down which one wins for your specific situation.

## Quick Verdict

| | Canva | Google Slides |
|---|---|---|
| **Best for** | Visual-first presentations | Team collaboration |
| **Free tier** | Generous templates | Unlimited, fully free |
| **Design quality** | High (250k+ templates) | Basic (limited templates) |
| **Collaboration** | Real-time (paid for full features) | Real-time (fully free) |
| **AI features** | Magic Design, Magic Write | Duet AI (Workspace only) |
| **Starting price** | Free / $15/mo Pro | Completely free |

**Bottom line:** Canva wins on design quality and template variety. Google Slides wins on collaboration, Google ecosystem integration, and being completely free with no limitations.

---

## What Is Canva?

Canva is an all-in-one design platform with a dedicated Presentations tool. It offers:

- **250,000+ presentation templates** across business, education, and creative styles
- **Magic Design** — generate a complete deck from a text prompt
- **Magic Write** — AI content generation for slide copy
- **Drag-and-drop editor** with charts, videos, animations, and brand kits
- **Presenter mode** with speaker notes and teleprompter

Canva presentations can be published as web links, downloaded as PDF or PPTX, or presented directly in the browser.

## What Is Google Slides?

Google Slides is the free presentation tool inside Google Workspace. Strengths include:

- **Deep Google integration** — links with Google Sheets, Docs, Drive
- **Real-time collaboration** for unlimited users (free)
- **Version history** — revert to any previous state
- **Offline editing** through Chrome browser
- **Duet AI** — AI writing and image features (requires Workspace subscription)

Google Slides exports to PPTX, PDF, or PNG — compatible with PowerPoint.

## Template Quality

**Winner: Canva (by a large margin)**

Canva's template library is one of its biggest strengths. You get:
- Professional, modern designs that look agency-quality
- Templates organized by industry, style, and color scheme
- Brand Kit to lock your fonts, colors, and logo

Google Slides offers around 20 built-in themes. They're functional but look dated. Third-party Slides templates exist but require manual importing.

---

## Collaboration

**Winner: Google Slides**

Google Slides was built for collaboration from day one:
- Comment on specific slides, tag teammates
- See everyone's cursor in real time
- Share with view/comment/edit permissions
- No account needed for guests to comment

Canva's free tier allows collaboration but with limitations. Full real-time team features require a Canva for Teams subscription ($10/user/month with a minimum of 3 users).

## AI Features

**Winner: Canva (for free users)**

Canva's Magic Studio is available on the free plan with limited credits:
- Generate complete presentations from a prompt with Magic Design
- Write slide copy with Magic Write
- Remove image backgrounds, generate AI images

Google's Duet AI for Slides requires a Google Workspace Business or Enterprise plan — not available on free Google Accounts.

---

## Pricing

**Canva:**
- Free: 250k+ templates, 5GB storage, limited AI credits
- Pro: $15/month — unlimited AI, Brand Kit, background remover, premium templates
- Teams: $10/user/month (min 3 users)

**Google Slides:**
- Free: Fully free, no feature limits for individuals
- Workspace Business Starter: $6/user/month (adds 30GB pooled storage, Meet features)
- Workspace Business Plus: $18/user/month (adds Duet AI features)

## When to Choose Canva

- You want visually polished decks without hiring a designer
- You're presenting to clients or external audiences
- You need brand consistency across your team
- You want AI to generate your first draft

## When to Choose Google Slides

- You collaborate constantly with a team
- You're already in the Google ecosystem (Gmail, Drive, Sheets)
- You need completely free with no limitations
- You present data linked to Google Sheets

## Final Recommendation

Both tools are free to start. Try **Canva** if you need impressive-looking slides fast — the templates alone justify the signup. Use **Google Slides** if your priority is seamless team collaboration and Google ecosystem integration.
