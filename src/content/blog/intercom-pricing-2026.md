**[Intercom](/tools/intercom/)** combines per-seat pricing with usage-based AI fees, which makes its real cost harder to predict than the sticker price suggests. The Fin AI agent in particular bills per resolution — and that's where bills surprise people. Here's exactly how the pieces add up.

## Intercom Pricing in 2026

| Component | Price | Billed as |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Essential | ~$39/seat/mo | Per seat, monthly |
| Advanced | ~$99/seat/mo | Per seat, monthly |
| Expert | ~$139/seat/mo | Per seat, monthly |
| Fin AI Agent | ~$0.99 per resolution | Usage-based, on top of seats |

Annual billing reduces seat costs. The critical thing to understand: **seats and Fin AI are separate charges**. Your total bill is (seats × plan price) + (Fin resolutions × $0.99).

## The Three Seat Plans

**Essential (~$39/seat/month)** — the shared inbox, basic messaging, help center, and ticketing. The entry point for small support teams.

**Advanced (~$99/seat/month)** — adds workflows/automation, multiple team inboxes, round-robin assignment, and richer reporting. Most growing teams land here.

**Expert (~$139/seat/month)** — adds SSO/SAML, advanced security, multi-brand, and the highest service-level controls. For larger orgs with compliance needs.

Every human agent who handles conversations needs a seat. This is where costs scale with team size — a 10-agent team on Advanced is ~$990/month before any AI usage.

## Fin AI Agent: The ~$0.99-per-Resolution Model

Fin is Intercom's AI support agent, and it's priced per **resolution** — roughly $0.99 each time Fin successfully answers a customer without human help. This is genuinely different from seat pricing:

- A resolution only counts when Fin actually resolves the issue
- Unresolved/escalated conversations don't incur the resolution fee
- High-volume support orgs can spend more on Fin than on seats

**The math that matters:** if Fin resolves 2,000 tickets/month, that's ~$1,980 in Fin fees alone — on top of your seat costs. For high-ticket-volume businesses, Fin can be cheaper than hiring agents. For low-volume teams, the per-resolution model stays affordable because you only pay when it works.

## The Hidden Cost: Usage Fees Stack on Seat Fees

The trap is treating the seat price as your total. A team on Advanced with heavy Fin usage and proactive outbound messaging can see a bill 2–3x the seat-only estimate. Before committing, model three numbers: number of agents, expected monthly Fin resolutions, and outbound message volume. The seat price is only the floor.

## Is Intercom Worth the Price?

**For AI-first, high-volume support: often yes.** If Fin can deflect thousands of tickets at ~$0.99 each, that's cheaper than the agents those tickets would otherwise require. Intercom is built around this bet, and it pays off at scale.

**For small teams under ~5 agents: evaluate carefully.** The seat prices are premium, and lighter-weight tools like Help Scout or Front cost less for basic shared-inbox support.

**For enterprises needing SSO and multi-brand:** the Expert plan is competitive with Zendesk's top tiers — compare both on your specific resolution volume, since AI usage often dominates the total bill.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does Intercom cost?

Intercom starts around $39/seat/month (Essential), rising to ~$99 (Advanced) and ~$139 (Expert). The Fin AI agent is billed separately at roughly $0.99 per resolution on top of seat costs.

### How does Fin AI pricing work?

Fin charges roughly $0.99 each time it successfully resolves a customer query without human intervention. Escalated or unresolved conversations don't incur the fee. High-volume teams can spend more on Fin than on seats.

### Is Intercom or Zendesk cheaper?

Zendesk's entry seats are cheaper (~$25 vs ~$39), but total cost depends heavily on AI resolution volume in both tools. Model your expected ticket and AI-resolution volume before choosing.
