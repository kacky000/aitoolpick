**[Tally](/tools/tally/)** does something almost no other form builder does: it gives you unlimited forms and unlimited responses on the free plan. No catch, no 14-day trial, no "upgrade to unlock" gate on core features. In a market where [Typeform](/tools/typeform/) charges $25/month for 100 responses and [Jotform](/tools/jotform/) caps free users at 5 forms, Tally's free tier is genuinely disruptive.

But is it actually good enough to replace paid form builders? After using it extensively, here's what works, what doesn't, and who should care.

## What Tally Is

Tally is a form builder created by a small team in Belgium. It uses a Notion-style block editor — you type your form like a document, adding question blocks as you go. The result looks clean and modern, with an optional conversational mode that presents one question at a time (similar to Typeform's signature format).

The pitch is simple: most form features should be free. Tally monetizes through a Pro plan that adds branding removal, team features, and file upload storage — not by gating basic functionality behind paywalls.

## Pricing

| Plan | Price | Forms | Responses | Key Features |
|------|-------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited | Unlimited | Logic, calculator, payments, integrations |
| Pro | $29/month | Unlimited | Unlimited | Remove branding, custom domains, file uploads (10 GB), team workspaces |

That's it. Two plans. Annual billing on Pro brings it to roughly $24/month.

Compare this to the competition:
- [Typeform](/tools/typeform/) Free: 10 responses/month. Basic at $25/month gets you 100 responses. See our [Typeform pricing analysis](/blog/typeform-pricing-2026) for the full cost breakdown.
- [Jotform](/tools/jotform/) Free: 5 forms, 100 submissions. Bronze at $34/month for 1,000.
- Google Forms: Free and unlimited, but no conditional logic, no conversational mode, no design flexibility.

Tally's free plan gives you more than what most competitors charge $25–50/month for.

## What Works Well

### The Free Plan Is Legitimately Generous

This isn't a stripped-down trial. Free Tally includes:

- Unlimited forms and submissions
- Conditional logic (show/hide questions based on answers)
- Calculator fields
- Hidden fields for tracking UTM parameters
- Payment collection via Stripe
- Integrations with [Notion](/tools/notion/), Google Sheets, [Slack](/tools/slack/), [Airtable](/tools/airtable/), and [Zapier](/tools/zapier/)
- Partial submissions (save progress)
- Closing forms on date or response count
- Custom thank-you pages

Most form builders lock conditional logic, payments, or integrations behind paid plans. Tally includes all of these at $0.

### Notion-Style Editor

If you've used [Notion](/tools/notion/), Tally's editor feels immediately familiar. You type a slash command (`/`) to add question blocks — short text, email, multiple choice, file upload, and so on. The form builds like a document, which makes it faster to create forms than dragging and dropping widgets on a canvas.

This approach is polarizing. People who love Notion find it intuitive. People who prefer visual builders (like Jotform's canvas) may find it limiting. There's no grid layout or multi-column option — forms are single-column, top to bottom.

### Conversational Mode

Tally includes a "respondent experience" toggle that switches any form to one-question-at-a-time mode. It looks and feels like Typeform's signature experience, with smooth transitions and keyboard navigation. The difference is that you don't pay $25/month for 100 responses to use it.

The conversational mode works well for short surveys (under 10 questions). For longer forms, the classic scrollable layout is usually faster for respondents.

### Conditional Logic Actually Works

Tally's logic system lets you show or hide blocks based on previous answers, redirect to different pages, and calculate scores. It covers 90% of what most users need from form logic. Setting it up is straightforward — click a block, add a condition, done.

It doesn't match Jotform's multi-step approval workflows or Typeform's advanced answer piping, but for standard "if answer is X, show question Y" logic, it's solid.

## What Doesn't Work Well

### Limited Template Library

Tally has a template gallery, but it's small — maybe 50-60 templates compared to Jotform's 10,000+. If you're starting from scratch and want a pre-built form for a specific use case (medical intake, insurance quote, event registration), you'll likely have to build it yourself.

### No Advanced Analytics

Tally shows you submission counts and individual responses. That's about it. There's no built-in response analysis, no charts, no cross-tabulation, no NPS tracking. If you need to analyze survey results beyond "read each response," you'll need to export to Google Sheets or a dedicated analytics tool.

SurveyMonkey and Typeform (on Business plan) offer significantly more built-in analysis.

### Team Features Are Pro-Only

The free plan is single-user. Sharing forms for collaboration, team workspaces, and role-based access all require Pro ($29/month). For solo users this doesn't matter, but teams need to budget for it.

### File Upload Storage Is Limited

Free plan file uploads exist but are capped at a low limit. Pro gives you 10 GB. If you're collecting resumes, portfolios, or media files at volume, you'll hit that ceiling. Jotform's paid plans offer more storage per dollar.

### No HIPAA Compliance

If you handle protected health information, Tally isn't an option. There's no HIPAA-compliant plan. Jotform (Gold plan at $99/month) and some specialized platforms handle this.

## Who Tally Is Best For

**Solo creators and freelancers.** If you need contact forms, feedback surveys, or intake forms and don't want to pay monthly fees, Tally is the obvious choice. The free plan covers everything a solo operator needs.

**Startups watching burn rate.** Early-stage companies that need functional forms without adding another SaaS subscription. You can run lead capture, customer feedback, and internal surveys on Tally's free plan indefinitely.

**Notion-heavy workflows.** The native Notion integration syncs form responses directly to a Notion database. If your team already runs on Notion, Tally slots in without friction. Responses appear as database entries you can filter, sort, and relate to other pages.

**Anyone who needs conversational forms but can't justify Typeform's pricing.** Tally's conversational mode delivers 80% of the Typeform experience at 0% of the cost. For most use cases, that's more than enough.

## Who Should Look Elsewhere

- **Enterprise teams** needing advanced security, SSO, or audit logs — Tally's Pro plan is basic on compliance features.
- **Healthcare organizations** needing HIPAA compliance — check Jotform Gold or a specialized platform.
- **Heavy analytics users** who want built-in survey analysis — SurveyMonkey or Typeform Business.
- **Complex multi-step workflows** with approvals, e-signatures, and PDF generation — Jotform handles these better.

## The Verdict

Tally is the best free form builder available in 2026. That's not a hedged statement — for the majority of individual users, freelancers, and small teams, there is no reason to pay for a form builder before trying Tally first.

The limitations are real: thin template library, no analytics depth, and no enterprise compliance. But those limitations only matter for specific use cases. For collecting responses, running surveys, and building clean forms with logic — the things most people actually need — Tally delivers at a price that's hard to argue with.

Start with Tally Free. If you outgrow it, then consider [Typeform](/tools/typeform/) for design polish or [Jotform](/tools/jotform/) for template variety and HIPAA. But most users won't outgrow it.
