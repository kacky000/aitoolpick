# v0 vs Bolt.new Pricing 2026: $20 Credits vs $25 Tokens

v0 and Bolt.new both turn a text prompt into running UI, but they sit at different ends of the "AI app builder" spectrum. v0 (by Vercel) generates clean Next.js + Tailwind + shadcn/ui code you own and export. Bolt.new builds and *hosts* full-stack apps entirely in the browser. Their entry prices land $5 apart — v0 Premium at ~$20/month, Bolt Pro at $25/month — but the metering and what you walk away with differ far more than the price.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | v0 | Bolt.new |
|---|------|----------|
| **Free** | Limited monthly credits | 1M tokens/mo, 300K daily cap |
| **Entry paid** | Premium — ~$20/mo, ~$20 credits + higher limits | Pro — $25/mo, 10M tokens |
| **Team tier** | Team — ~$30/user/mo, pooled credits | Teams — $30/user/mo, 10M tokens/user |
| **Metering** | Credits | Tokens (every action consumes) |
| **You get** | Exportable Next.js code you own | Hosted full-stack app, deployable in-browser |

## The Free Tiers

**v0 Free** gives a monthly credit allowance — enough to prototype a few screens and judge the quality of the generated components. Because v0 outputs standard React/Next.js code, even on free you can copy what it makes into your own repo.

**Bolt Free** hands you 1 million tokens per month capped at 300,000 daily, with Bolt branding on hosted sites, a 10 MB upload limit, and no custom domains. It's enough to test the build-and-host flow but the daily token cap throttles longer sessions.

Verdict on free: v0 if you want code to take with you; Bolt if you want to see a full app run end-to-end without leaving the browser.

## Entry Paid: Premium (~$20) vs Pro ($25)

**v0 Premium — ~$20/month.** Premium adds roughly $20 in monthly credits, higher rate limits, and access to the strongest models. The defining feature isn't the price — it's that you **own the code**. Everything v0 generates is exportable Next.js you drop into a Vercel project (or anywhere). That makes v0 less of a walled garden and more of an accelerator for developers who already have a stack. It's level on price with [Cursor Pro](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/), another $20 developer tool.

**Bolt Pro — $25/month.** Pro removes the daily token cap, giving 10 million tokens monthly with rollover, plus 100 MB uploads, custom domains, SEO controls, and no Bolt branding. Bolt's value is the all-in-one loop: prompt, build full-stack (front end *and* back end), and deploy without touching a terminal. Tokens are consumed by every action, so debugging-heavy sessions burn faster than expected.

The real difference: v0 is a code generator for people who'll keep building in their own environment; Bolt is a build-and-host platform for people who want to stay in one place. The $5 gap is trivial next to that choice.

## Team Tiers

Both land at ~$30/user/month. **v0 Team** pools credits across members and adds collaboration — good for design-and-dev teams iterating on UI together. **Bolt Teams** gives each member their own 10M-token allocation, better when every builder runs heavy, independent generation. v0's pooled model favors uneven usage across a team; Bolt's per-seat model favors consistently heavy users.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Developer with an existing Next.js/Vercel stack who wants to own the code?** v0 Premium (~$20/mo).
- **Want to build *and* host a full-stack app without a terminal?** Bolt Pro ($25/mo).
- **UI-focused team iterating on components together?** v0 Team (~$30/user/mo, pooled credits).
- **Team of heavy, independent builders?** Bolt Teams ($30/user/mo, per-seat tokens).
- **Just testing?** v0 free to keep the code, Bolt free to see the full app run.

## The Bottom Line

The $20-vs-$25 gap is noise. The signal is ownership and hosting: v0 gives you exportable Next.js code to build with in your own stack, while Bolt gives you a hosted full-stack app you never leave the browser to ship. Pick v0 if you're a developer who wants code; pick Bolt if you want the whole loop — build to deploy — in one tool.
