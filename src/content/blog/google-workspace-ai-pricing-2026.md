Google folded its **[Gemini](/tools/gemini/)** AI directly into Google Workspace, which changed how teams pay for AI at work. Instead of a separate per-seat AI subscription on top of everything, Gemini now comes **included in most Workspace business plans.** This guide explains exactly what you pay per user, what the AI unlocks, and whether it's worth it for your team.

## Google Workspace Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price (annual) | AI Included |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| Business Starter | ~$7/user/mo | Gemini features |
| Business Standard | ~$14/user/mo | Gemini features |
| Business Plus | ~$22/user/mo | Gemini features |
| Enterprise | Custom | Full Gemini + Gems |

The headline shift: **AI is no longer a separate line item.** Google moved Gemini into the core Workspace plans rather than charging the old $20–$30/user AI add-on on top. For teams, that effectively lowered the cost of AI at work.

## What "Gemini in Workspace" Includes

Bundled into Workspace, Gemini shows up across the apps your team already uses:

- **Gmail** — draft, summarize, and reply with AI
- **Docs** — write, refine, and proofread
- **Sheets** — formula help, analysis, and table generation
- **Slides** — generate images and content
- **Meet** — note-taking, summaries, and translated captions
- **Gemini app** — a standalone chat assistant with your work context

Higher tiers add **Gems** (custom AI agents), larger context, and admin controls for data governance.

## The Per-User Math

For a 10-person team, the difference between the old model and the new one is significant:

- **Old model:** Workspace (~$14/user) + separate AI add-on (~$20/user) = **~$34/user/mo**
- **New model:** Business Standard with Gemini included = **~$14/user/mo**

That's roughly a **60% reduction** in the cost of putting AI in front of your whole team — the single biggest reason Google Workspace AI has become competitive for cost-conscious businesses.

## Workspace AI vs Microsoft 365 Copilot

The natural comparison is **Microsoft 365 Copilot**, which charges **$30/user/month** as an add-on on top of an existing M365 license. Against Workspace's bundled-in approach, Google is meaningfully cheaper per seat for teams that don't specifically need Office. We break the numbers down in [Copilot vs Gemini pricing](/blog/copilot-vs-gemini-pricing-2026/).

## Is the AI Add-On Worth It?

**Yes, if your team:**
- Already runs on Google Workspace
- Writes and analyzes inside Docs, Sheets, and Gmail daily
- Wants AI without managing a second vendor and bill
- Values Meet's automatic notes and summaries

**Maybe not, if your team:**
- Lives entirely in Microsoft Office (Copilot fits better)
- Only needs AI for one or two people (individual [Gemini AI Pro](/blog/gemini-pricing-2026/) at $19.99 may be simpler)
- Barely uses the office suite at all

## The Bottom Line

Google's decision to bundle Gemini into Workspace rather than charge a separate AI seat is the story here. For teams already on Workspace, AI is now a built-in capability at roughly the price of the suite itself — a strong value position against the $30/user Copilot add-on. The question isn't really "is the AI worth $X" anymore; it's "which office ecosystem does your team already pay for."

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Gemini included in Google Workspace?

Yes. Google moved Gemini features into the core Workspace business plans rather than charging a separate AI add-on, so most plans now include AI across Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Meet.

### How much does Google Workspace cost per user?

Roughly $7/user/mo for Business Starter, ~$14 for Business Standard, and ~$22 for Business Plus (annual pricing), all including Gemini features. Enterprise is custom-priced.

### Is Workspace AI cheaper than Microsoft Copilot?

For most teams, yes. Microsoft 365 Copilot is a $30/user/mo add-on on top of M365, while Gemini is bundled into Workspace plans at a lower effective per-seat cost.

### Do I need the AI add-on for individual use?

No. Individuals can use [Google AI Pro at $19.99/mo](/blog/gemini-pricing-2026/) (with 2 TB storage) instead of a full Workspace business plan.
