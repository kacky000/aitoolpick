# Remote vs Deel 2026: Best Global HR Platform for Remote Teams?

Hiring internationally has never been easier — but it's still complex. Remote and Deel are the two dominant Employer of Record (EOR) and global payroll platforms, helping companies hire employees and contractors in 150+ countries without setting up local entities.

Both are excellent. Here's how to choose.

## What Do Remote and Deel Actually Do?

**Employer of Record (EOR)**: The platform becomes the legal employer of your international hire. They handle local labor law compliance, payroll, taxes, and benefits — you manage day-to-day work.

**Contractor Management**: For freelancers, both platforms generate compliant contracts, process payments, and handle currency conversion.

This matters because misclassifying employees as contractors can result in massive fines. Both platforms reduce that risk significantly.

## EOR Coverage: Deel Leads

Deel supports **150+ countries** for EOR; Remote supports **75+ countries**. If you're hiring in Southeast Asia, Africa, or emerging markets, Deel's broader coverage is a significant advantage.

However, Remote's 75+ country coverage handles the vast majority of common hiring destinations (US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia, India, Brazil, etc.).

## Contractor Management: Remote Is Cheaper

At $29/month per contractor vs Deel's $49/month, Remote saves you $240/year per contractor. For a company with 10 contractors, that's $2,400/year.

Both platforms:
- Generate locally compliant contracts
- Process payments in local currencies
- Handle tax documentation
- Provide contractor self-service portals

Deel's contractor experience is slightly more polished (better onboarding UX), but the $20/month premium is hard to justify for most companies.

## HRIS: Deel Wins with Free Plan

Deel's HRIS is **free for up to 200 employees** — and it's genuinely good. It includes:
- Employee database and org chart
- Time off management
- Document storage
- Basic reporting
- Performance reviews

Remote's HRIS is $35/employee/month, making it substantially more expensive for companies that just need headcount management without EOR services.

## Compliance & Legal

Both platforms employ local legal experts in every country they support. Key differences:

**Remote**:
- Known for deep compliance documentation
- Transparent local law guides available publicly
- Employee Misclassification Protection policy

**Deel**:
- 200+ in-house legal and HR specialists
- Built-in IP protection agreements
- Deel Shield: misclassification risk protection

For compliance-sensitive industries (financial services, healthcare), Deel's larger legal team provides marginally more coverage depth.

## Integration Ecosystem

Both platforms integrate with major HR and accounting tools:

**Remote integrations**: BambooHR, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, QuickBooks, Xero, Gusto

**Deel integrations**: BambooHR, Greenhouse, Ashby, Workday, NetSuite, QuickBooks, Rippling, Slack

Deel has a slightly broader integration library, and its Rippling integration is valuable for US-based companies already using Rippling for domestic HR.

## Who Should Choose Remote?

- You primarily need **contractor management** (lower cost)
- Your team is in major markets (US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia)
- You want transparent pricing with no hidden fees
- **Compliance documentation** is a top priority

## Who Should Choose Deel?

- You need **EOR in emerging markets** (150+ country coverage)
- You have **mixed US + global payroll** needs
- You want **free HRIS** for basic headcount management
- Contractors need **flexible payment options** (crypto, Revolut)
- You prefer faster support response times

## The Verdict

There's no clear universal winner — it depends on your company's hiring profile:

**For contractor-heavy companies in 50+ countries**: Remote is cheaper ($29 vs $49).

**For companies hiring full-time employees globally**: Tied on EOR price, but Deel wins on coverage breadth.

**For startups that want free HRIS + eventual global expansion**: Deel's free HRIS makes it the natural starting point.

Both offer free demos. Get quotes from both before committing — at $599/employee/month for EOR services, the right choice matters.
