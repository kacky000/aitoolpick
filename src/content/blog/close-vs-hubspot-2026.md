**Close** and **HubSpot** are both popular CRM platforms, but they solve different problems. Close is built for inside sales teams that live on the phone and in email. HubSpot is an all-in-one CRM with deep marketing automation. This guide breaks down pricing, features, and use cases so you can pick the right one.

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Close | HubSpot |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **Starting Price** | $9/user/mo (Solo) | Free (limited) |
| **Built-in Calling** | Yes (power dialer included) | Yes (limited minutes, paid add-on for power dialer) |
| **Email Sequences** | Yes, all plans | Yes, Starter and above |
| **Marketing Automation** | No | Yes (Marketing Hub) |
| **Free Plan** | No | Yes |
| **Best For** | Inside sales teams, startups | Marketing + sales alignment, scaling businesses |

## Pricing Breakdown

### Close Pricing

Close keeps it straightforward with four tiers, all billed annually:

- **Solo**: $9/user/month — 1 user only, basic pipeline, built-in calling and email
- **Essentials**: $35/user/month — multi-user, call recording, sequences, custom fields
- **Growth**: $99/user/month — power dialer, call coaching, advanced reporting
- **Scale**: $139/user/month — predictive dialer, priority support, custom onboarding

Add-ons can push costs higher. Extra phone numbers, additional calling minutes, and SMS credits are billed separately.

### HubSpot CRM Pricing

HubSpot's CRM starts free but the paid tiers climb fast:

- **Free CRM**: Unlimited users, 1M contacts, basic deal tracking and email
- **Starter**: $20/user/month — removes branding, adds simple automation and sequences
- **Professional**: $100/user/month — full automation, custom reporting, forecasting
- **Enterprise**: $150/user/month — custom objects, predictive scoring, advanced permissions

The real cost of HubSpot shows up when you add the Marketing Hub or Service Hub. A full-stack Professional plan can easily reach $1,000+/month for a small team.

## Feature Comparison

### Built-in Calling & SMS

This is where Close genuinely stands apart. Every Close plan includes a built-in dialer with call logging, recording, and voicemail drops. The Growth plan adds a power dialer that auto-calls through lists. The Scale plan includes predictive dialing. SMS is built in too.

HubSpot offers calling, but it's more limited. Free and Starter plans get basic calling with capped minutes. Power dialing requires a paid add-on or a third-party integration like Aircall.

**Winner: Close** — calling is a core feature, not a bolt-on.

### Marketing Automation

HubSpot was built as a marketing platform first, and it shows. The Marketing Hub includes landing pages, forms, email campaigns, ad management, social scheduling, and multi-step workflows. It's one of the most complete marketing automation suites available.

Close has no marketing features. No landing pages, no forms, no ad tracking. It's a sales-only tool by design.

**Winner: HubSpot** — if you need marketing and sales under one roof, there's no contest.

### Sales Pipeline Management

Both platforms handle pipeline management well. Close gives you a clean, fast pipeline view with drag-and-drop deals, custom stages, and revenue forecasting. Everything feels snappy because the interface is intentionally lightweight.

HubSpot's pipeline is more visual and includes deal scoring, automated stage transitions, and multi-pipeline support. It's more configurable but also more complex.

**Winner: Tie** — Close is faster to use day-to-day; HubSpot is more configurable.

### Ease of Use

Close is one of the simplest CRMs to set up. Import contacts, connect email, and you're making calls within an hour. The interface is clean and focused — there aren't dozens of menus to navigate because the tool only does sales.

HubSpot is intuitive for a platform of its size, but the sheer number of features means there's more to learn. Teams often underuse HubSpot because they never fully explore its capabilities.

**Winner: Close** — minimal setup, steep productivity from day one.

### Reporting & Analytics

HubSpot's reporting is significantly more powerful. Custom dashboards, attribution reports, cross-object reporting, and real-time data visualization are all available on Professional and above.

Close has solid sales reporting — leaderboards, activity metrics, pipeline velocity — but it can't match HubSpot's depth for cross-functional analytics.

**Winner: HubSpot**

### Integrations

HubSpot has 1,500+ integrations in its App Marketplace plus connectivity through [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) and [Make](/tools/make/). Close has 100+ integrations and also connects through Zapier, but the ecosystem is much smaller.

If you need tight integrations with tools like [Salesforce](/tools/salesforce/), Marketo, or Shopify, HubSpot's ecosystem is considerably broader.

**Winner: HubSpot**

## Who Should Choose Close?

- Inside sales teams that make 50+ calls per day
- Startups and SMBs (under 50 reps) that need a CRM built around outbound
- Teams that want built-in calling without managing a separate phone system
- Sales orgs that value speed over feature breadth
- Companies already using a separate marketing tool and just need a sales CRM

## Who Should Choose HubSpot?

- Teams that want marketing and sales in one platform
- Companies scaling from 10 to 500+ employees
- Marketing-driven businesses focused on inbound lead generation
- Organizations that need a free CRM to start and grow into paid features
- Teams that want deep reporting across sales, marketing, and service

## How They Compare to Alternatives

If neither fits perfectly, consider [Pipedrive](/tools/pipedrive/) — it sits between Close and HubSpot with strong pipeline management at a mid-range price point. For enterprise needs, [Salesforce](/tools/salesforce/) remains the most customizable option, though it requires significantly more setup and administration.

## Verdict

**Close** is the better CRM if your team's primary job is making calls, sending emails, and closing deals. The built-in dialer alone saves $30–50/user/month compared to adding a phone system to HubSpot. For a 10-person sales team doing outbound, Close is hard to beat.

**HubSpot** is the better choice if you need marketing automation alongside your CRM, if you're building an inbound lead engine, or if you want a platform that scales from startup to enterprise without switching tools.

Pick Close for pure sales speed. Pick HubSpot for cross-functional reach.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Close or HubSpot better?

It depends on your workflow. Close is purpose-built for outbound sales teams that rely on calling and email. HubSpot is better for teams that need marketing and sales working together in one system.

### Can I use Close and HubSpot together?

Yes. Some teams use HubSpot for marketing and lead capture, then push qualified leads into Close for the sales team to work. Both platforms support integrations through Zapier and native connectors.

### Which is cheaper, Close or HubSpot?

HubSpot's free plan is hard to beat on cost. But once you compare paid tiers for a sales team, Close's Essentials at $35/user/month often costs less than HubSpot Starter plus a calling add-on. Total cost depends on team size and which features you actually need.
