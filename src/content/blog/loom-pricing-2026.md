**Loom** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

Loom makes async video messaging feel effortless — but is the free plan enough, or do you need to pay? This guide breaks down every Loom pricing tier in 2026 so you can decide without guessing.

## Loom Pricing Overview

| Plan | Price | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0/month | Individuals and light users |
| Business | $15/creator/month | Growing teams |
| Business+ | $25/creator/month | Power users and larger teams |

*Prices are per creator per month, billed annually. Monthly billing costs slightly more.*

---

## Free Plan — What You Actually Get

Loom's free plan is genuinely useful for occasional use, but it comes with meaningful restrictions:

- **25 videos** maximum in your library (older videos get locked, not deleted)
- **5-minute recording limit** per video
- Unlimited viewers (anyone with the link can watch)
- Basic editing: trim, chapters, CTAs
- Slack and [Notion](/tools/notion/) integration
- Auto-generated transcripts

**Verdict:** The free plan works well for one-off demos or simple team updates. Once you hit 25 videos or need longer recordings, you'll need to upgrade.

---

## Business Plan — $15/creator/month

The Business plan removes the biggest free-tier pain points:

- **Unlimited videos** in your library
- **Unlimited recording length** (record as long as you need)
- Custom branding (logo, colors)
- Engagement insights (who watched, how far)
- Password-protected videos
- Priority support

At $15/creator/month (billed annually), a 5-person team pays $75/month or $900/year. That's the real cost to budget for.

**Verdict:** If you use Loom regularly for customer-facing content, sales demos, or team documentation, the Business plan pays for itself quickly.

---

## Business+ Plan — $25/creator/month

Business+ adds enterprise-grade features:

- Everything in Business
- **Advanced analytics** (funnel-style drop-off data)
- [CRM](/category/crm/) integrations (HubSpot, Salesforce)
- [Salesforce](/tools/salesforce/) activity logging
- Custom video pages
- SSO (SAML)
- Advanced admin controls

**Verdict:** Business+ is best for customer success or sales teams who need CRM sync and deeper viewer analytics. Most teams won't need it unless they're using Loom as a core sales tool.

---

## Is Loom Worth It in 2026?

Loom's biggest value is **reducing unnecessary meetings**. A 2-minute Loom can replace a 30-minute [Zoom](/tools/zoom/) call for the right type of communication.

**When Loom is worth paying for:**
- You record demos, walkthroughs, or feedback regularly
- You want viewers to watch asynchronously (different time zones)
- You're sending videos to customers or prospects

**When it's not worth paying:**
- You only send the occasional screen recording
- Your team prefers real-time calls anyway
- You're happy with tools like [Zoom](/blog/zoom-review-2026) or [Google Meet](/blog/google-meet-review-2026) for sync communication
