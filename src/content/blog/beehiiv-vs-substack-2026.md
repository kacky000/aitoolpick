# Beehiiv vs Substack 2026: Which Newsletter Platform Is Better?

Beehiiv and Substack are the two newsletter platforms that come up in every conversation about starting a newsletter in 2026. They both let you publish and grow an email audience — but they take fundamentally different approaches to how you do it.

Substack is the simpler, writer-first platform that handles everything for you. Beehiiv is the operator-first platform that gives you more control over growth, monetization, and branding. This comparison breaks down what actually matters so you can pick the right one.

## Beehiiv Overview

Beehiiv was built by early Morning Brew employees, and it shows. The platform is designed around newsletter growth — not just writing. If you think of your newsletter as a business rather than a blog, Beehiiv is built for you.

**Key strengths:**

- **Growth tools**: Referral programs, recommendation networks, and subscriber segmentation are built in. These aren't add-ons — they're core to the platform. The referral system alone (similar to Morning Brew's viral growth engine) is something Substack simply doesn't offer.
- **Beehiiv Ad Network**: You can monetize through Beehiiv's own ad marketplace, even if you have zero paid subscribers. This is a real differentiator for creators who don't want to gate content behind paywalls.
- **Custom domains and SEO**: Every plan supports custom domains. Blog posts are indexable, and you get proper SEO controls (meta descriptions, slugs, structured data). If search traffic matters to you, Beehiiv takes this seriously.
- **Website builder**: Your newsletter doubles as a full website with customizable pages, navigation, and branding. No need for a separate landing page tool.

For a deeper look, see our full [Beehiiv review](/blog/beehiiv-review-2026).

## Substack Overview

Substack is deliberately simple. You sign up, you write, you hit publish. There's no referral engine to configure, no ad network to manage, no website builder to set up. That's the point.

**Key strengths:**

- **Simplicity**: The editor is clean, the publishing flow is straightforward, and there's almost nothing to configure. You can go from zero to published newsletter in under ten minutes.
- **Built-in audience**: Substack has a discovery network — the Substack app, recommendation features, and "Notes" (their social feed). Readers actively browse Substack looking for new writers. This built-in audience doesn't exist on Beehiiv.
- **Paid subscriptions as a core model**: Substack was designed around reader-pays. The paid subscription flow is seamless: readers subscribe, pay through Stripe, and get access to paywalled posts. It just works.
- **Community features**: Substack offers chat, discussion threads, and Notes — turning your newsletter into more of a community hub. Beehiiv is more transactional by comparison.

## Pricing Comparison

This is where the two platforms diverge the most.

### Beehiiv Pricing

| Plan | Price | What You Get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Launch | Free | Up to 2,500 subscribers, unlimited sends, custom domain, basic analytics |
| Scale | $42/mo | Up to 10K subs, ad network access, referral program, advanced analytics, automations |
| Max | $85/mo | Up to 100K subs, priority support, full API access, advanced segmentation |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited subs, dedicated support |

Beehiiv takes **0% of your revenue** on all plans. You keep everything you earn from paid subscriptions and ads.

Full breakdown: [Beehiiv pricing 2026](/blog/beehiiv-pricing-2026).

### Substack Pricing

| Plan | Price | What You Get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | Unlimited subscribers, unlimited posts, basic customization |
| Substack Pro | $0 upfront | Additional support, but still 10% cut |

Substack charges **10% of your paid subscription revenue** (plus Stripe's payment processing fees of ~2.9% + 30 cents). There are no monthly fees.

This means if you earn $5,000/month from paid subscribers, Substack takes $500. On Beehiiv's $42/month Scale plan, you'd keep all $5,000 minus the flat $42.

The math is simple: once your newsletter generates more than about $420/month in subscription revenue, Beehiiv's flat fee is cheaper than Substack's percentage cut.

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Editor Experience

**Substack** wins on simplicity. The editor is distraction-free and feels like writing in a clean document. Drag-and-drop for images, easy embedding, and a familiar formatting toolbar.

**Beehiiv** offers a more powerful editor with reusable content blocks, dynamic content (show different content to different segments), and better HTML/CSS control. But the extra options add complexity.

**Pick**: Substack if you want to just write. Beehiiv if you want layout control and personalization.

### Analytics

**Beehiiv** is significantly stronger here. You get 3D analytics (opens, clicks, and engagement heatmaps), UTM tracking, subscriber attribution (know exactly where each subscriber came from), and cohort analysis. The Scale plan adds revenue analytics and ad performance data.

**Substack** gives you open rates, subscriber counts, and basic post-level stats. It's enough to know what's working, but you won't get the granular data that growth operators want.

**Pick**: Beehiiv, clearly.

### Monetization

Both platforms support paid subscriptions. The difference is in the options beyond that.

**Beehiiv** gives you three monetization paths: paid subscriptions (0% cut), the Beehiiv Ad Network (get paid to run ads from vetted sponsors), and Boosts (get paid when you recommend other newsletters). You can stack all three.

**Substack** focuses on one: reader-pays subscriptions (10% cut). There's no built-in ad marketplace. You can run your own sponsorships manually, but there's no platform support for it.

**Pick**: Beehiiv if you want monetization flexibility. Substack if you prefer the clean reader-pays model.

### Design and Branding

**Beehiiv** lets you build a full branded website with custom colors, fonts, navigation menus, and multiple page types. Your newsletter URL can live on your own domain and look like your own site.

**Substack** keeps things intentionally uniform. You can add a logo, pick an accent color, and write a bio — but every Substack looks roughly like every other Substack. The trade-off is zero design decisions to make.

**Pick**: Beehiiv if brand identity matters. Substack if you'd rather not think about design.

### Integrations

**Beehiiv** integrates with Zapier, Make, and has a full API. You can connect it to your CRM, ad platforms, or analytics tools. Webhooks are available on paid plans.

**Substack** has limited integrations. No public API, no Zapier connection, no webhooks. You can export your subscriber list as CSV, and that's about it. Substack wants to be your whole stack, not one piece of it.

**Pick**: Beehiiv if your newsletter is part of a larger tech ecosystem. Substack if it's standalone.

## Who Should Choose Which?

**Choose Beehiiv if:**
- You're building a newsletter as a business, not just a creative outlet
- Growth tools (referrals, recommendations, segmentation) are important to you
- You want to monetize through ads and sponsorships, not just paid subscriptions
- You need strong analytics and attribution data
- You want a custom-branded website, not a Substack subdomain
- Your subscription revenue is (or will be) over $500/month

**Choose Substack if:**
- You're a writer first and want the simplest possible publishing experience
- Substack's built-in audience and discovery network matters to you
- You prefer the reader-pays model and don't want to deal with ads
- You're starting out and want zero upfront costs with no risk
- Community features (chat, Notes, discussion threads) are valuable
- You don't need advanced analytics or integrations

## The Verdict

Beehiiv and Substack aren't really competing for the same person, despite looking similar on the surface.

**Substack is for writers.** If you want to sit down, write something good, and build a paying audience around your ideas — Substack makes that remarkably easy. The built-in network gives you a discovery advantage that Beehiiv can't match, and the zero-config approach means you spend your time writing, not configuring.

**Beehiiv is for operators.** If you think about your newsletter in terms of growth rates, subscriber acquisition costs, and revenue per subscriber — Beehiiv gives you the tools to optimize all of that. The ad network, referral engine, and analytics suite are built for people who treat their newsletter like a product.

The pricing question is straightforward: if you're earning meaningful subscription revenue, Beehiiv's flat fee beats Substack's 10% cut. If you're just starting out or your newsletter is primarily free, Substack's simplicity and built-in audience make it the safer starting point.

Neither platform is objectively better. The right choice depends on whether you identify more as a writer or a newsletter operator.
