# OpenAI Codex Free vs Paid 2026: What You Actually Get

OpenAI Codex is bundled with ChatGPT, which means there's a free way in — but the free tier and the paid tiers behave very differently once you start using the agent for real work. Since the move to token-based credits in April 2026, the question isn't just "free or paid," it's "how much will my actual usage cost." Here's the honest breakdown.

## What the Free Tier Gets You

The ChatGPT Free plan ($0) includes limited Codex access. You can try the agent, run small tasks, and see how the cloud workflow feels. The catches:

- **Strict rate limits.** You'll hit caps quickly on anything beyond light use.
- **Minimal credit allocation.** Token-heavy tasks exhaust your monthly credits fast.
- **No parallel agents in practice.** Running multiple agents burns through limits almost immediately.

The free tier is a genuine trial, not a sustainable workflow. It's enough to decide whether Codex fits how you work — and little more.

## What Paid Tiers Add

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Codex Access | Rate Limits | Best For |
|------|-------------|--------------|-------------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Limited | Strict | Trying it out |
| Plus | $20/mo | Standard | Standard | Regular individual use |
| Pro | $200/mo | Full | Minimal | Heavy, full-time use |
| Business | Per seat | Standard | Standard | Teams + admin |
| Enterprise | Custom | Full | Custom | Compliance, SSO, SLA |

### Plus ($20/mo)
The entry point for real use. You get a meaningful monthly credit allocation and standard rate limits — enough for a few tasks a day. Heavy sessions can trigger overage charges, so your effective bill often lands around $20–40/month.

### Pro ($200/mo)
Built for developers who treat Codex as a primary tool. Pro removes most rate limits, making parallel agents, CI/CD automation, and scheduled tasks practical without hitting caps mid-workflow. Effective cost typically runs $150–300/month depending on volume.

### Codex-Only Seats ($0/user/month)
For teams that want the agent without full ChatGPT, OpenAI offers Codex-only seats at $0/user — but with no included credits. All usage bills against workspace credits you buy separately. It's pure pay-as-you-go.

## The Token Credit Factor

Since April 2, 2026, every Codex task consumes credits based on the model selected and the work performed. This means:

- Two developers on the same plan can have very different bills.
- Light users may never exceed their Plus allocation.
- Heavy users on Plus can pay overage that approaches Pro territory anyway.

The practical takeaway: estimate your task volume before assuming a tier. For a full numbers breakdown, see the [OpenAI Codex pricing guide](/blog/openai-codex-pricing-2026).

## Is Paid Worth It?

- **Stay free** if you're evaluating Codex or only need it occasionally.
- **Upgrade to Plus** once you use it most days — the rate limits on free will frustrate you fast.
- **Jump to Pro** if you run parallel agents, automate pipelines, or use Codex 2–3+ hours daily. At that intensity, the time saved usually justifies the $200.
