Notion and Trello take very different approaches to project management. Trello keeps it simple with Kanban boards. Notion gives you an entire workspace. Which one should you pick?

## Quick Verdict

- **Choose Trello** if you want dead-simple Kanban boards that anyone can learn in 5 minutes.
- **Choose Notion** if you need tasks, docs, wikis, and databases in one place.

## Pricing Comparison

| Plan | Notion | Trello |
|------|--------|--------|
| Free | ✅ Generous | ✅ Great |
| Starter/Standard | $10/user/mo | $5/user/mo |
| Business/Premium | $18/user/mo | $10/user/mo |
| Enterprise | Custom | $17.50/user/mo |

Trello is consistently cheaper at every tier. If budget matters, Trello wins on price.

## Features Head-to-Head

### Project Management

**Trello:** Kanban boards are Trello's bread and butter. You get lists, cards, labels, due dates, and checklists. Simple, visual, effective.

**Notion:** Offers Kanban views plus table, calendar, timeline, gallery, and list views — all from the same database. More flexible, but more complex.

**Winner:** Notion for flexibility, Trello for simplicity.

### Documentation

**Trello:** No built-in docs. You can add descriptions to cards, but there's no wiki or document management.

**Notion:** Full document editor with nested pages, databases, and templates. It's one of the best documentation tools available.

**Winner:** Notion by a mile.

### Automation

**Trello:** Butler automation is surprisingly powerful on the free plan. Rule-based triggers, scheduled commands, and card/board buttons.

**Notion:** Automations are more limited. Basic database automations exist, but nothing as intuitive as Butler.

**Winner:** Trello.

### Integrations

**Trello:** Power-Ups ecosystem with 200+ integrations. Slack, Google Drive, GitHub, and more connect seamlessly.

**Notion:** Growing integration library, plus a powerful API. Fewer native integrations but the API compensates.

**Winner:** Trello for plug-and-play, Notion for API-driven workflows.

### Learning Curve

**Trello:** 5 minutes to get started. Drag cards between lists. Done.

**Notion:** Days to weeks to master. The flexibility that makes Notion powerful also makes it complex.

**Winner:** Trello.

## Best For

| Use Case | Better Choice |
|----------|--------------|
| Simple task boards | Trello |
| Team wiki + tasks | Notion |
| Quick onboarding | Trello |

| Small team projects | Trello |
| All-in-one workspace | Notion |
| Budget-conscious teams | Trello |
| Startups & freelancers | Notion |

## When to Choose Trello

- Your team needs a simple, visual task board
- You don't want to spend time setting up a workspace
- Budget is a key concern
- You need great automations out of the box

## When to Choose Notion

- You want to replace multiple tools (tasks + docs + wiki)
- You need flexible database views
- Your workflow is complex or custom
- You're okay investing time in setup

## Can You Use Both?

Yes — and many teams do. Trello for quick project boards, Notion for documentation and knowledge management. The Trello Power-Up for Notion helps bridge the gap.

## Final Take

Trello and Notion aren't really competing for the same user. If you know exactly what you need (simple Kanban), Trello is perfect. If you're not sure what you need yet and want room to grow, Notion's flexibility will serve you better long-term.
