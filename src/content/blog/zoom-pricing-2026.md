**Zoom** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

Zoom's pricing has expanded significantly beyond video calls. In 2026, Zoom sells a full communications platform — phone, email, team chat, events, and AI features — and understanding which tier you actually need requires untangling multiple product lines.

Here is every Zoom pricing number that matters.

## Zoom Meetings Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Key Limits |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Basic (Free) | $0 | 40-min limit on group meetings, 100 participants |
| Pro | $13.32/user/month | 30-hour meetings, 100 participants, 5 GB cloud recording |
| Business | $18.32/user/month | 300 participants, SSO, managed domains |
| Business Plus | $22.49/user/month | Translated captions, workspace reservations, unlimited recordings |
| Enterprise | Custom | 500-1,000 participants, unlimited cloud recording, dedicated CSM |

All prices billed annually. Monthly billing adds ~20%.

## What Each Plan Gets You

### Basic (Free)
The free plan is genuinely useful for 1:1 calls — no time limit on those. Group meetings (3+ participants) cut off at 40 minutes, which forces upgrades for most business use cases. 100-participant cap, local recording only, no cloud storage.

**Best for:** Occasional meetings, personal use, testing Zoom.

### Pro ($13.32/user/month)
Pro removes the 40-minute limit and adds cloud recording (5 GB). This is the minimum tier for any business using Zoom regularly. Zoom AI Companion (meeting summaries, action items, whiteboard AI) is included at no extra cost.

**Best for:** Freelancers, small teams under 10 people.

### Business ($18.32/user/month)
Business requires a minimum of 10 users and adds SSO, managed domains, and increased participant limits (300). The managed domains feature is important for IT admins who need to ensure all company email addresses route to one account.

**Best for:** SMBs with IT requirements, teams needing SSO or compliance controls.

### Business Plus ($22.49/user/month)
Business Plus adds translated captions in 36 languages (important for global teams) and workspace reservation features for hybrid offices. Unlimited cloud recording is the headline feature here.

**Best for:** Global teams, hybrid offices managing desk/room booking.

## Zoom AI Companion: Now Included

In 2024, Zoom made AI Companion free for all paid accounts. This includes:
- Meeting summaries and next steps
- In-meeting questions ("What did I miss?")
- Whiteboard AI
- Chat compose and thread summarization

This is significant — competitors like [Microsoft Teams](/tools/microsoft-teams/) charge extra for Copilot ($30/user/month), while Zoom includes equivalent features in Pro ($13.32).

## How to Reduce Your Zoom Bill

1. **Audit named users:** Zoom charges per named user. Many orgs have licenses for employees who left or use Zoom rarely.
2. **Use the free plan for external-only calls:** If a portion of your team only joins external Zoom calls (never hosts), they may not need paid licenses.
3. **Evaluate Microsoft Teams or Google Meet:** If your organization already pays for Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace, you may have equivalent meeting functionality at no additional cost.
4. **Negotiate annual contracts:** Zoom's list prices are discounts from rack rates. Volume pricing for 50+ users is negotiable.
