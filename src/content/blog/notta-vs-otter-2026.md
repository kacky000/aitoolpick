# Notta vs Otter 2026: Which AI Transcription Tool Wins?

Picking a transcription tool in 2026 means choosing between two solid options that approach the same problem from different angles. Notta bets on **multilingual coverage and translation**, while Otter.ai doubles down on **English-language depth and team collaboration**. Both record meetings, generate transcripts, and produce AI summaries — but the details matter when you're about to hand one of them the keys to every conversation you have.

Here's a practical breakdown to help you decide.

## At a Glance

| Feature | Notta | Otter.ai |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Languages supported** | 58 | English (+ limited beta for others) |
| **Real-time translation** | Yes — live bilingual subtitles | No |
| **Speaker identification** | Yes | Yes (industry-leading) |
| **Meeting integrations** | Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, Webex | Zoom, Teams, Google Meet |
| **Slack integration** | Limited | Native |
| **AI Chat on transcripts** | Basic Q&A | OtterPilot AI Chat (strong) |
| **Mobile app** | iOS & Android | iOS & Android |

## Where Notta Wins

### 58-Language Transcription

Notta's standout feature is breadth. It transcribes in 58 languages, including Japanese, Mandarin, Korean, Spanish, French, German, and Arabic. If your meetings regularly involve participants speaking different languages, Notta handles code-switching better than anything else in this price range.

### Real-Time Translation

Beyond transcription, Notta offers live bilingual subtitles during calls. A Japanese speaker and an English speaker can each see real-time translated captions on the same call. For international teams, this alone can justify the subscription — no separate interpreter needed for routine syncs.

### Zoom, Teams, Meet, and Webex

Notta covers the four major video conferencing platforms, including Webex, which Otter still doesn't support natively. If your organization runs on Cisco infrastructure, this matters.

### Audio and Video File Import

Both tools handle file uploads, but Notta is more flexible with input formats and processes longer files on its free plan (up to 5 minutes per transcription vs. Otter's tighter caps on the free tier).

## Where Otter Wins

### Speaker Identification Accuracy

Otter's speaker diarization — figuring out who said what — remains the best in the category. It learns voice profiles over time, so after a few meetings, it correctly labels speakers without manual correction. For meeting minutes where attribution matters (legal, sales calls, board meetings), this is significant.

### AI Chat That Actually Helps

Otter's AI Chat lets you ask questions about your transcripts naturally. "What did Sarah say about the Q3 budget?" returns a direct answer with a timestamp link. It's built on top of a solid retrieval system and feels closer to having a searchable meeting memory than a simple transcript search.

### Slack Integration

Otter pushes summaries, action items, and key highlights directly to Slack channels. For teams already living in Slack, this keeps meeting takeaways visible without anyone needing to open a separate app. Notta's Slack integration exists but is more limited.

### Collaborative Editing

Otter lets multiple team members highlight, comment on, and edit transcripts in real time. It's designed as a team tool from the ground up — shared workspaces, permission controls, and a transcript library that the whole org can search.

## Pricing Compared

| Plan | Notta | Otter.ai |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Free** | 120 min/mo, 5 min per session | 600 min/mo, 40 min per session |
| **Pro** | $14.99/mo (billed monthly) | $16.99/mo (billed monthly) |
| **Business** | $27.99/user/mo | $30/user/mo |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Custom |

A few things jump out:

**Otter's free plan is more generous** — 600 minutes per month with 40-minute sessions vs. Notta's 120 minutes and 5-minute cap. If you want to test-drive a tool before paying, Otter gives you a much longer runway.

**Notta's Pro is slightly cheaper** at $14.99/mo vs. $16.99/mo. The $2/month difference adds up to $24/year — not life-changing, but Notta gives you multilingual support at a lower price point.

**Business tiers are close** — both land around $28-30/user/month. At this level, the decision should be about features, not price.

Annual billing drops both by roughly 20%, so if you're committing, pay yearly.

## Accuracy and Reliability

In English, both tools deliver transcription accuracy above 90% in clean audio conditions. Otter edges ahead slightly in noisy environments and with overlapping speakers, thanks to its more mature speaker model.

For non-English languages, Notta is the only real option. Its accuracy varies by language — major languages like Spanish, French, and Japanese perform well (85-90%), while less common languages may dip lower. Still, it's far better than having no transcription at all.

Otter's CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) make it the stronger pick for sales teams who need call notes flowing into their pipeline automatically. Notta focuses more on the transcription itself and relies on Zapier for downstream connections.

## Who Should Choose Notta?

- **Multilingual teams** where meetings happen in more than one language
- **International businesses** that need real-time translation during calls
- **Webex users** who need native integration
- **Budget-conscious users** who want a capable Pro plan under $15/month
- **Content creators** working with audio/video in non-English languages

## Who Should Choose Otter?

- **English-first teams** that want the deepest transcript features
- **Sales organizations** using Salesforce or HubSpot
- **Slack-heavy workplaces** that want meeting summaries pushed automatically
- **Anyone who values speaker identification** accuracy above all else
- **Teams who want AI Chat** to query across all their past meetings

## The Verdict

If your work crosses language boundaries — international clients, bilingual team members, content in multiple languages — **Notta is the clear pick**. Its 58-language support and real-time translation are unmatched at this price.

If you operate primarily in English and want the most polished meeting-notes experience with strong team collaboration features, **Otter wins**. Its speaker identification, AI Chat, and CRM integrations make it the more complete tool for English-language teams.
