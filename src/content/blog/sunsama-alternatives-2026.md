[Sunsama](/tools/sunsama/) is a great daily planner. The guided morning planning ritual, the daily shutdown review, the clean interface — it all works. But at $20/month on an annual plan ($25 monthly), it's one of the most expensive individual productivity tools you can buy.

That price point sends a lot of people looking for alternatives. Some want similar features for less money. Others want a fundamentally different approach — AI-driven scheduling instead of manual daily planning. Here are 7 alternatives worth considering, with honest takes on what each does better and worse than Sunsama.

For full pricing context, see our [Sunsama Pricing 2026](/blog/sunsama-pricing-2026/) breakdown.

## 1. Motion — Best if You Want AI to Replace Manual Planning

**Price:** $12.73/month (annual) / $19/month (monthly)
**Free tier:** No (7-day trial)

[Motion](/tools/motion/) is the philosophical opposite of Sunsama. Where Sunsama asks you to manually plan your day each morning, Motion's AI builds the plan for you. Add your tasks with deadlines and priorities, and Motion schedules them into available calendar slots automatically. When meetings shift, everything reshuffles.

**Why choose Motion over Sunsama:**
- No daily planning session required — AI handles it
- Automatic rescheduling when priorities change
- Built-in meeting scheduler (replaces basic Calendly)
- Project management features for teams

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- Sunsama's daily ritual builds mindfulness about your time
- Motion requires trusting an algorithm with your day
- Sunsama's weekly review is more structured
- Motion can feel chaotic if you like predictable routines

**The bottom line:** If you find Sunsama's daily planning session tedious rather than therapeutic, Motion eliminates it entirely. You give up intentionality for efficiency. See our [Sunsama vs Motion](/blog/sunsama-vs-motion-2026/) comparison for a deeper dive.

## 2. Reclaim.ai — Best Free Alternative with AI Features

**Price:** $10/month Starter (annual) / Free Lite plan
**Free tier:** Yes

Reclaim sits between Sunsama's manual approach and Motion's full automation. It syncs tasks from tools like Todoist, Asana, and Jira, then automatically finds time for them in your calendar. But unlike Motion, it's more of an assistant than an autopilot — you maintain more control.

The free Lite plan is the big differentiator. You get smart time blocking, habit scheduling, and basic calendar defense at no cost. That's a hard offer to beat when Sunsama charges $20/month with no free tier.

**Why choose Reclaim over Sunsama:**
- Free tier that actually works
- Paid plan ($10/mo) is half the cost of Sunsama
- Deep integrations with Todoist, Asana, Jira, ClickUp, Linear
- Habit tracking built into your calendar (lunch, exercise, focus time)

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- Sunsama's planning ritual is more structured and guided
- Better task consolidation from multiple sources in one view
- Daily shutdown and weekly review features
- More polished UX for the daily planning workflow

**The bottom line:** If cost is a factor, start with Reclaim's free tier. It won't replicate Sunsama's daily planning ritual, but it handles the time blocking and task scheduling that most people are actually paying for.

## 3. Todoist — Best Budget Option for Task Management

**Price:** $5/month Pro (annual) / Free tier available
**Free tier:** Yes (up to 5 active projects)

Todoist doesn't try to be a calendar tool or a daily planner. It's a task manager — arguably the best pure task manager available. If what you really need from Sunsama is a place to organize and prioritize tasks, Todoist does that at a quarter of the price.

**Why choose Todoist over Sunsama:**
- $5/month vs $20/month
- Best-in-class task organization (labels, filters, priority levels)
- Natural language input ("Meeting with John tomorrow at 3pm")
- Works everywhere (web, desktop, mobile, browser extensions)
- Generous free tier

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- No calendar integration or time blocking
- No daily planning ritual
- No visual calendar view of your day
- Requires more discipline — Todoist doesn't structure your time

**The bottom line:** Todoist is for people who realize they don't actually need calendar-based planning — they need better task management. If you used Sunsama mainly for its task list and rarely engaged with the calendar view, Todoist at $5/month is the pragmatic choice.

## 4. TickTick — Best Value All-in-One

**Price:** $35.99/year (~$3/month) / Free tier available
**Free tier:** Yes

TickTick combines task management, calendar, habit tracking, and Pomodoro timer in one app. It's the Swiss Army knife of productivity tools, and at $36/year, it's absurdly cheap for what you get.

**Why choose TickTick over Sunsama:**
- $3/month vs $20/month — enormous price gap
- Built-in calendar view with tasks
- Habit tracker included
- Pomodoro timer for focus sessions
- Available on every platform

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- Sunsama's daily planning workflow is more guided
- Better integration with external tools (Jira, Asana, Slack)
- More intentional daily shutdown process
- TickTick's calendar is functional but not as polished

**The bottom line:** TickTick is the value pick. If you want tasks + calendar + habits without paying $20/month, TickTick delivers roughly 70% of Sunsama's value at 15% of the price. The trade-off is a less guided daily planning experience.

## 5. Akiflow — Most Similar Workflow to Sunsama

**Price:** $19/month (annual) / $34/month (monthly)
**Free tier:** No (7-day trial)

Akiflow is the closest thing to a Sunsama clone in terms of workflow. It has a universal inbox that pulls tasks from email, Slack, Todoist, Asana, and other tools. You drag tasks onto your calendar for time blocking. There's a daily planning view and a command bar for quick task creation.

**Why choose Akiflow over Sunsama:**
- Universal inbox consolidates tasks from more sources
- Faster keyboard-driven interface
- Better command bar for power users
- Native time blocking with calendar view

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- Sunsama is $20/mo annual vs Akiflow's $19/mo — basically identical pricing
- Sunsama's daily planning ritual is more structured
- Better mobile experience
- More established with a larger user community

**The bottom line:** Akiflow is a lateral move, not a downgrade. Choose it if you prefer a keyboard-first, speed-oriented interface over Sunsama's guided ritual. But don't switch for cost savings — the annual pricing is nearly identical.

## 6. Morgen — Best for Multi-Calendar Users

**Price:** $9/month / Free tier (limited)
**Free tier:** Yes

Morgen's standout feature is true multi-calendar support. It unifies Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud into a single view — something surprisingly few tools do well. It also integrates tasks from Todoist, Things 3, and Microsoft To Do.

Since [Clockwise](/tools/clockwise/) shut down in March 2026, Morgen has become the go-to for people who need cross-platform calendar management.

**Why choose Morgen over Sunsama:**
- $9/month vs $20/month
- Supports Google + Outlook + iCloud simultaneously
- Works on macOS, Windows, Linux, iOS, Android
- Scheduling links included (replaces basic Calendly needs)

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- Sunsama's daily planning ritual is unique
- Better task integration depth (Jira, Asana, Trello, Gmail)
- Daily shutdown and weekly review features
- More mature product with established workflows

**The bottom line:** Morgen is the pick if you manage calendars across multiple providers. It doesn't replicate Sunsama's planning philosophy, but it handles the calendar + task integration piece at less than half the price.

## 7. Notion + Calendar — Best DIY Option

**Price:** $10/month Plus / Free tier available
**Free tier:** Yes

This isn't a single tool — it's a workflow. Build your own daily planning system in Notion using databases, templates, and calendar views. Pair it with Google Calendar or any calendar app. Some people create Sunsama-like daily planning templates with morning reviews, time-blocked schedules, and end-of-day reflections.

**Why choose this over Sunsama:**
- Fully customizable to your exact workflow
- Notion's free plan is generous
- No subscription specifically for daily planning
- Can grow into a full second brain / project management system

**Why Sunsama might still be better:**
- Building and maintaining a Notion system takes effort
- No guided daily planning automation
- No native calendar time blocking (requires switching between apps)
- Easy to over-engineer and spend more time on the system than your work

**The bottom line:** The Notion DIY route is for people who enjoy building systems. If setting up databases and templates sounds fun to you, this works. If you want something that works out of the box tomorrow morning, it's not the move.

## Which Alternative Should You Pick?

**Want AI to plan your day:** [Motion](/tools/motion/). Skip the manual planning, let the algorithm work.

**Want something similar but cheaper:** Reclaim.ai (free or $10/mo) for AI-assisted scheduling. TickTick ($3/mo) for the budget all-in-one.

**Want the same workflow but different UX:** Akiflow. Nearly identical concept, keyboard-first design.

**Just need task management:** Todoist at $5/month. Don't pay for calendar features you don't use.

**Manage multiple calendars:** Morgen at $9/month. Nothing else handles cross-provider calendars as well.

**Like building your own system:** Notion. Maximum flexibility, maximum setup effort.

The honest truth: Sunsama's daily planning ritual is hard to replicate exactly. If that ritual is what you love, no alternative will feel the same. But if you're paying $20/month and mostly using it as a task list with a calendar view, several of these alternatives deliver that for a fraction of the cost.
