# Suno Pricing 2026: Free vs Pro vs Premier — Complete Breakdown

Suno has turned "type lyrics, get a song" from a novelty into a genuinely useful creative tool. Whether you're scoring a YouTube video, prototyping a jingle for a client, or just having fun making music you couldn't make before, the question eventually becomes: which Suno plan do you actually need?

This guide breaks down every tier, what the credits mean in practice, and where each plan makes sense.

## Suno Pricing at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly Price | Annual Price | Daily Credits | Monthly Credits (est.) | Commercial Use |
|------|--------------|--------------|---------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Free** | $0 | $0 | 50 | ~1,500 | No |
| **Pro** | $10/mo | $8/mo | — | 2,500 | Yes |
| **Premier** | $30/mo | $24/mo | — | 10,000 | Yes |

*Annual billing saves 20% on Pro and Premier.*

## What Are Suno Credits?

Every song generation on Suno costs credits. The exact cost depends on the generation type:

- **Standard song** (up to 2 minutes): ~5 credits
- **Extended song** (up to 4 minutes): ~10 credits
- **Remix or variation**: ~5 credits
- **Instrumental only**: ~5 credits

So on the **Pro plan** with 2,500 monthly credits, you can generate roughly **500 standard songs** or **250 extended tracks** per month. On **Premier** with 10,000 credits, that scales to about **2,000 standard songs** or **1,000 extended tracks**.

For the **Free plan**, 50 daily credits means you can make about **10 songs per day**. That's enough to experiment, but the non-commercial restriction means you can't use any of those songs in monetized content.

## Free Plan — $0/month

The free tier is surprisingly functional for testing. You get 50 credits per day (refreshed daily, not banked), access to all generation models, and the ability to create songs from text prompts or lyrics.

**What you get:**
- 50 credits per day
- All music generation models
- Text-to-music and lyrics-to-song
- Public sharing
- MP3 downloads

**What you don't get:**
- Commercial usage rights — anything you generate is for personal, non-commercial use only
- Priority queue — free users wait longer during peak times
- Custom mode features may be limited
- No watermark removal on shared tracks

**Best for:** Trying Suno before committing, personal projects, learning the tool, and having fun making music with zero risk.

### The Daily Credit Reset Catch

Free plan credits reset daily. You get 50 fresh credits each day, but unused credits don't roll over. This means you can't save up credits for a bigger project. If you need 200 credits for a batch of songs, you'll need to spread the work across four days.

## Pro Plan — $10/month

The Pro plan is where Suno becomes a real production tool. At $10/month ($8/month on annual billing), it's aggressively priced compared to most AI creative subscriptions.

**What you get (on top of Free):**
- 2,500 credits per month (pooled, not daily)
- Commercial usage rights — use generated music in YouTube videos, podcasts, ads, games, client work
- Priority generation queue — faster results during busy periods
- No watermark on downloads
- Higher quality audio output options
- Ability to create private (non-public) songs

**2,500 credits in practice:**
- ~500 standard songs (2 min each)
- ~250 extended songs (4 min each)
- Realistically, expect to generate 300-400 songs factoring in remixes and variations

**Best for:** Content creators who need background music for videos, podcasters wanting custom intros/outros, indie game developers, small agencies producing client content, and musicians using AI for demos and inspiration.

### Is Pro Worth $10/month?

For anyone monetizing content that needs music, yes — almost certainly. A single royalty-free music subscription (Epidemic Sound, Artlist) costs $13-17/month and gives you access to a library. Suno Pro gives you *custom-generated* music for less. The math works as long as the output quality meets your standards, which in 2026, it does for most use cases.

## Premier Plan — $30/month

Premier is the volume tier. You get 4x the credits of Pro at 3x the price, which makes it the better value per credit if you're generating heavily.

**What you get (on top of Pro):**
- 10,000 credits per month
- Everything in Pro
- Priority generation (highest tier)
- Early access to new models and features

**10,000 credits in practice:**
- ~2,000 standard songs
- ~1,000 extended songs
- Enough for a production studio, music library creator, or agency producing daily content

**Best for:** Music production studios using AI for volume work, agencies managing multiple client accounts, content farms producing background music at scale, and power users who generate dozens of tracks daily.

### Pro vs Premier: The Credit Math

Premier costs 3x more than Pro but gives you 4x the credits — a 25% per-credit discount. If you're consistently hitting your Pro limit before month's end, upgrading saves money. Both plans offer annual billing at a 20% discount (Pro drops to $8/mo, Premier to $24/mo).

## Commercial Usage Rights

This is the most important distinction between Free and paid plans. Free-tier generations are **non-commercial only** — no monetized YouTube videos, no client work, no ads. Pro and Premier give you full commercial rights to everything you generate. If you're making money with content that uses AI music, you need Pro at minimum.

## The Bottom Line

Suno's pricing is straightforward and competitive. The **Free plan** is the best way to explore AI music generation with no commitment. **Pro at $10/month** unlocks commercial rights and enough credits for most solo creators. **Premier at $30/month** is for volume users who need thousands of generations monthly.

For most people reading this, Pro is the right call. It costs less than a streaming subscription and gives you unlimited creative control over custom music.
