Zapier is the most popular no-code automation platform, connecting over 6,000 apps with an intuitive interface. But with its Professional plan at $29.99/month and task limits that add up quickly, many teams are searching for alternatives that offer better value or more advanced capabilities.

Whether you need a cheaper option, more technical flexibility, or self-hosted control, here are the 7 best Zapier alternatives in 2026.

## 1. Make (formerly Integromat) — Best Overall Alternative

**Price:** Free / Core $10.59/mo / Pro $18.82/mo

Make is the most direct Zapier competitor and often the first tool people switch to. Its visual scenario builder lets you design complex automations with branching logic, loops, and error handling — capabilities that require workarounds in Zapier.

**Best for:** Teams that need complex, multi-step automations at a lower cost

**Pros:**
- Visual drag-and-drop workflow builder with branching paths
- Significantly cheaper than Zapier (Core plan is $10.59 vs Zapier's $29.99)
- Built-in data transformation tools (JSON, XML, text parsing)
- Operations-based pricing gives more flexibility than Zapier's task model
- HTTP module for connecting any API without a native integration

**Cons:**
- Steeper learning curve than Zapier
- Smaller app library (~1,800 vs Zapier's 6,000+)
- Debugging complex scenarios can be frustrating
- Some integrations feel less polished

**Zapier vs Make:** Make offers more value per dollar, especially for technical teams. Zapier wins on simplicity and app coverage. For a detailed breakdown, check our [Zapier vs Make comparison](/blog/zapier-vs-make-automation-comparison/).

## 2. n8n — Best Self-Hosted Option

**Price:** Free (self-hosted) / Cloud from $24/mo

n8n is an open-source workflow automation tool that you can host on your own servers — a massive advantage for teams with strict data privacy requirements. The visual editor is similar to Make, with a node-based workflow builder.

**Best for:** Developers and privacy-conscious teams who want full control

**Pros:**
- Free forever when self-hosted
- Full source code access and customization
- 400+ integrations with custom node support
- JavaScript/Python code nodes for advanced logic
- No task limits on self-hosted plans

**Cons:**
- Self-hosting requires technical setup and maintenance
- Cloud pricing can exceed Zapier for high-volume usage
- Community integrations vary in quality
- Documentation could be more beginner-friendly

**Zapier vs n8n:** n8n is the clear winner if you want self-hosted automation with no task limits. Zapier is better for non-technical users who want zero infrastructure management.

## 3. Pipedream — Best for Developers

**Price:** Free (100 credits/day) / Basic $29/mo / Advanced $79/mo

Pipedream is a code-first automation platform designed for developers. You can write workflows in Node.js, Python, Go, or Bash while still using pre-built triggers and actions for popular apps.

**Best for:** Developers who want code-level control with pre-built integrations

**Pros:**
- Write real code (Node.js, Python) in workflow steps
- Generous free tier with 100 daily credits
- Built-in key-value store and SQL database
- Event-driven architecture with real-time webhooks
- Excellent GitHub and developer tool integrations

**Cons:**
- Not suitable for non-technical users
- Smaller app library than Zapier or Make
- Complex pricing model based on credits
- UI is functional but not visually polished

**Zapier vs Pipedream:** Pipedream gives developers far more power and flexibility. Zapier is the better choice for business users who need a no-code experience.

## 4. Pabbly Connect — Best Budget Option

**Price:** Free (100 tasks/mo) / Standard $16/mo / Pro $33/mo (lifetime deals available)

Pabbly Connect stands out with lifetime deal pricing and no task limits on paid plans. If you're tired of watching your Zapier bill climb with every additional task, Pabbly's flat-rate pricing is refreshing.

**Best for:** Small businesses and solopreneurs who want predictable costs

**Pros:**
- No task limits on paid plans (unlike Zapier's per-task pricing)
- Lifetime deal frequently available
- Multi-step workflows with filters and routers
- 1,000+ app integrations
- Built-in webhook support

**Cons:**
- Interface feels dated compared to Zapier and Make
- Some integrations are less reliable
- Limited error handling options
- Smaller community and fewer tutorials

**Zapier vs Pabbly:** Pabbly wins on pricing, especially for high-volume automations. Zapier offers a more polished experience and broader app support.

## 5. Integrately — Best for Simplicity

**Price:** Free (100 tasks/mo) / Starter $19.99/mo / Professional $39/mo

Integrately focuses on one-click automations. Instead of building workflows from scratch, you browse pre-built templates and activate them with a single click. It's even simpler than Zapier.

**Best for:** Non-technical users who want the fastest setup possible

**Pros:**
- 8 million+ pre-built automations ready to activate
- Simpler than Zapier for basic use cases
- Competitive pricing with higher task limits
- Smart Connect feature for quick app linking
- Good customer support

**Cons:**
- Limited customization for complex workflows
- Pre-built automations may not match your exact needs
- Fewer advanced features (no code steps, limited branching)
- Less well-known, so community resources are limited

**Zapier vs Integrately:** Integrately is simpler for basic automations. Zapier offers far more flexibility and customization for complex workflows.

## 6. IFTTT — Best for Personal Automations

**Price:** Free (2 applets) / Pro $3.49/mo / Pro+ $14.99/mo

IFTTT (If This Then That) pioneered simple automation with its trigger-action model. While it's less powerful than Zapier for business use, it excels at personal automations and smart home integrations.

**Best for:** Personal use, smart home automation, and simple app connections

**Pros:**
- Extremely simple "if this, then that" logic
- Excellent smart home integrations (Alexa, Google Home, Philips Hue)
- Very affordable Pro plan at $3.49/month
- Location-based triggers
- Clean, mobile-friendly interface

**Cons:**
- Limited to simple, single-trigger automations
- Not suitable for complex business workflows
- Slow execution speed compared to Zapier
- Free plan limited to just 2 applets

**Zapier vs IFTTT:** IFTTT wins for personal and smart home automation. Zapier is vastly superior for business workflows.

## 7. Microsoft Power Automate — Best for Microsoft Ecosystem

**Price:** Included with Microsoft 365 / Standalone from $15/user/mo

If your organization already uses Microsoft 365, Power Automate is essentially free and deeply integrated with Teams, SharePoint, Outlook, and Dynamics 365. It offers both cloud-based and desktop automation (RPA).

**Best for:** Organizations invested in the Microsoft ecosystem

**Pros:**
- Included with Microsoft 365 subscriptions
- Deep integration with all Microsoft products
- Desktop automation (RPA) for legacy app automation
- AI Builder for document processing and predictions
- Enterprise-grade security and compliance

**Cons:**
- Complex interface with a steep learning curve
- Non-Microsoft integrations are limited and clunky
- Licensing can be confusing
- Performance issues with large, complex flows

**Zapier vs Power Automate:** Power Automate is the obvious choice for Microsoft-heavy organizations. Zapier is better for connecting diverse third-party apps.

## How to Choose the Right Zapier Alternative

**Choose Make if** you want the closest Zapier experience with more power and lower cost. See our [Make vs Zapier pricing breakdown](/compare/zapier-vs-make/) for a detailed cost analysis.

**Choose n8n if** data privacy matters and your team has technical skills to self-host.

**Choose Pipedream if** you're a developer who wants to write code in your automations.

**Choose Pabbly if** budget is your top priority and you need unlimited tasks.

**Choose IFTTT if** you primarily need personal and smart home automations.

**Choose Power Automate if** your organization already uses Microsoft 365.

## Final Thoughts

Zapier is still an excellent tool, but it's no longer the only game in town. Make offers the best overall value for most teams, while n8n and Pipedream cater to technical users. Budget-conscious teams should look at Pabbly, and Microsoft shops should leverage Power Automate.

The best choice depends on your technical skills, budget, and which apps you need to connect. Many of these tools offer generous free plans, so test a couple before committing.
