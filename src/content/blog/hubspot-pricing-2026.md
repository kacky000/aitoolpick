**HubSpot pricing** is famously complex. Unlike most SaaS tools with three simple tiers, HubSpot sells modular "Hubs" — Marketing, Sales, Service, CMS, and Operations — each with their own Free, Starter, Professional, and Enterprise tiers. The free [CRM](/category/crm/) is genuinely free. Everything beyond it gets expensive fast.

This guide cuts through the complexity to show you exactly what you pay and what you get at each level in 2026.

## HubSpot's Structure: Hubs + Bundles

HubSpot is organized [around](/tools/around/) five product hubs:

| Hub | What It Does |
|-----|--------------|
| **Marketing Hub** | Email, landing pages, ads, SEO, social media |
| **Sales Hub** | CRM pipeline, email sequences, meetings, deals |
| **Service Hub** | Help desk, tickets, knowledge base, live chat |
| **CMS Hub** | Website builder, blog, A/B testing |
| **Operations Hub** | Data sync, automation, programmable ops |

You can buy hubs individually or bundle them. HubSpot also offers **CRM Suite** bundles that combine all hubs at a discounted rate.

## Free CRM: What You Actually Get

HubSpot's free tier is legitimately useful and used by hundreds of thousands of businesses:

- Unlimited users
- 1,000,000 contacts (non-marketing)
- Contact and company management
- Deal pipeline (1 pipeline, up to 1M deals)
- Email tracking (200 notifications/month per user)
- Meeting scheduling links (1 link per user)
- Live chat and basic chatbots
- Forms and landing pages (HubSpot-branded)
- Basic [email marketing](/category/email-marketing/) (2,000 sends/month)
- 5 email templates

The free CRM is a real product, not a stripped-down teaser. Small teams can run their sales process entirely on the free tier.

## Marketing Hub Pricing

| Plan | Price | Key Features |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Forms, email (2K/month), basic analytics |
| Starter | $15/month (1K contacts) | Remove HubSpot branding, email sends ×5 contacts |
| Professional | $800/month (2K contacts) | Automation, A/B testing, SEO tools, social media |
| Enterprise | $3,600/month (10K contacts) | Custom events, multi-touch attribution, teams |

**Important**: Marketing Hub pricing scales with contact count. At 10K contacts, Starter costs ~$45/month; Professional jumps to ~$1,050/month.

The jump from Starter to Professional is the most significant in HubSpot's lineup — $15 to $800/month is a 53× increase. Professional is where HubSpot becomes a serious marketing automation platform.

## Sales Hub Pricing

| Plan | Price | Key Features |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Basic CRM, deal tracking, email templates |
| Starter | $15/user/month | Email sequences, meeting links, 1 pipeline |
| Professional | $90/user/month | Multiple pipelines, sales automation, forecasting |
| Enterprise | $150/user/month | Custom objects, advanced permissions, predictive lead scoring |

Sales Hub Professional at $90/user/month is the sweet spot for sales teams that have outgrown basic CRM tools. The automation and pipeline management at this tier replaces significant manual work.

## Service Hub Pricing

| Plan | Price | Key Features |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Free | $0 | Shared inbox, live chat, tickets |
| Starter | $15/user/month | Ticket routing, customer portal |
| Professional | $90/user/month | Knowledge base, customer feedback, SLAs |
| Enterprise | $130/user/month | Custom objects, conversation intelligence |

## CRM Suite Bundle (All Hubs)

HubSpot offers bundle pricing that's often more economical:

| Plan | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|----------------|
| Starter | $30/month | All hubs at Starter level |
| Professional | $1,600/month | Marketing Pro + Sales Pro + Service Pro |
| Enterprise | $5,000+/month | All hubs at Enterprise level |

The Starter bundle is the best value for small businesses that want all hubs at the lowest paid tier.

## Hidden Costs to Watch

**Onboarding fees**: HubSpot requires paid onboarding for Professional and Enterprise plans — $3,000 for Marketing Professional, $750 for Sales/Service Professional. These are non-optional.

**Contact limits**: Marketing Hub charges per contact in your marketing database. Adding 5K more contacts to Starter adds ~$75/month. Costs scale faster than expected.

**Integrations**: Many valuable integrations ([Salesforce](/tools/salesforce/) sync, some data enrichment tools) require Professional or higher.

**Add-ons**: Dedicated IP for email ($300/month), increased API calls, additional users on restrictive plans.

## Which HubSpot Plan Do You Need?

**Free tier** is enough if: You have fewer than 5 sales reps, do minimal email marketing, and just need contact and deal management.

**Starter** ($15-30/month) is right for: Small teams wanting HubSpot branding removed, basic sequences, and cleaner workflows.

**Professional** ($800-1,600/month) makes sense if: You have a marketing team sending regular campaigns, running automations, and need A/B testing and SEO tools.

**Enterprise** ($3,600-5,000+/month) is only justified for: Companies with 50+ person marketing/sales teams, complex attribution needs, custom objects, and a budget to match.

## Verdict

HubSpot's free CRM is one of the best free tools in software. The paid plans — especially Professional and above — are expensive but comprehensive. The key question isn't whether HubSpot is worth it, but whether you'll use enough features to justify the price jump from Starter to Professional.
