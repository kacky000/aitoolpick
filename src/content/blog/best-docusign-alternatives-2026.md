DocuSign is the e-signature standard, but it's also one of the priciest — with no free plan and **envelope limits** that quietly inflate your bill. If you're hitting those caps or just want a simpler, cheaper tool, here are the best **DocuSign alternatives** in 2026.

## Why Look for an Alternative?

- **No free plan** — DocuSign only offers a trial.
- **Envelope limits** — Standard and Business Pro cap at ~100 envelopes/user/year on annual plans.
- **Per-user cost** — scales fast for growing teams.
- **Overkill** — many users only need basic signing, not enterprise tooling.

See exactly where the costs add up in our [DocuSign pricing breakdown](/blog/docusign-pricing-2026/).

## 1. Dropbox Sign — Best for Simplicity

Formerly HelloSign, Dropbox Sign is the cleanest, easiest alternative. It has a **free plan** (3 requests/month) and paid plans from $15/user/month with no fiddly envelope math. The interface is genuinely simpler than DocuSign's.

**Best for:** teams that want straightforward signing without the learning curve.

## 2. PandaDoc — Best for Sales Teams

PandaDoc bundles e-signature into a full proposal and quote builder, with a **free e-sign plan** and paid tiers from $19/user/month. No envelope caps, plus document analytics and CRM workflows.

**Best for:** anyone sending proposals and quotes. See [DocuSign vs PandaDoc](/blog/docusign-vs-pandadoc-2026/).

## 3. SignWell — Best Budget Pick

SignWell (formerly Docsketch) offers DocuSign-style features at a fraction of the price, with a usable free tier and cheap paid plans. Reminders, templates, and audit trails included.

**Best for:** cost-conscious small businesses.

## 4. Adobe Acrobat Sign — Best for PDF Workflows

If your team already uses Adobe Acrobat, Acrobat Sign folds signing into your existing PDF workflow. Pricier than the budget picks but seamless if you're in the Adobe ecosystem.

**Best for:** PDF-heavy teams already paying for Adobe.

## 5. SignNow — Best for High Volume

SignNow offers competitive flat-rate pricing without the envelope anxiety, plus a solid API for embedding signing into your own product. Good value for teams that send a lot.

**Best for:** high-volume senders and developers.

## 6. Signaturely — Best Free Option

Signaturely keeps things minimal with a free plan for occasional signing and affordable upgrades. Not feature-rich, but perfect for light, infrequent use.

**Best for:** freelancers and very low volume.

## Bottom Line

If you just need to **sign documents simply**, switch to **Dropbox Sign**. If you send **sales proposals**, **PandaDoc** is the upgrade. On a **tight budget**, **SignWell** delivers the most for the least. All three skip DocuSign's envelope limits and offer a free way to start.
