**Logseq** is one of the most generous note-taking tools on the market when it comes to pricing. The entire feature set is free for local use, with optional paid plans for cloud sync and supporting the project. But what exactly do you get at each tier, and how does it compare to Obsidian and Notion?

This guide breaks down every Logseq pricing option in 2026, explains what you actually need to pay for, and helps you decide whether the paid plans are worth it.

## Logseq Pricing Overview

Here is the full pricing breakdown for 2026:

| Plan | Price | What You Get |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Free (Local) | $0 | Full app, unlimited pages, all features, local storage |
| Logseq Sync | ~$5/month | End-to-end encrypted cloud sync across devices |
| Sponsor Tier | ~$15/month | Early access to experimental features + sync |

Unlike most productivity tools, Logseq does not gate features behind paid plans. You are not paying for functionality. You are paying for convenience (sync) or to support development (sponsor).

## Free Tier: Everything You Need, No Strings Attached

The free version of Logseq is not a limited trial. It is the complete product. You get:

- **Unlimited pages, blocks, and graphs** with no storage caps
- **Bidirectional linking** and graph view
- **Daily journals** for frictionless capture
- **Advanced queries** to pull data across your knowledge base
- **Plugin ecosystem** with hundreds of community-built extensions
- **Whiteboards** for visual thinking
- **PDF annotation** built into the app
- **Full offline access** since everything runs locally

Your data is stored as plain Markdown and Org-mode files on your local machine. You own every file. There is no vendor lock-in, no account required, and no internet connection needed to use the app.

This is possible because Logseq is open-source software. The project is funded through its paid sync service and sponsor contributions, not by restricting core features.

**Best for:** Anyone who is comfortable managing their own file sync, solo users, privacy-focused individuals, and anyone who wants to try Logseq without committing a dollar.

## Logseq Sync (~$5/month): Seamless Cross-Device Access

The Logseq Sync plan adds cloud synchronization with end-to-end encryption. Your notes sync across desktop and mobile devices without you having to set up any third-party service.

Key details:

- **End-to-end encrypted**: Logseq cannot read your data on their servers
- **Real-time sync**: Changes propagate within seconds across devices
- **Mobile support**: Full sync with the iOS and Android apps
- **Conflict resolution**: Handles simultaneous edits gracefully
- **Storage included**: Sufficient for most knowledge bases (including images and assets)

The encryption is the main selling point here. If you use iCloud or Dropbox for sync, your cloud provider can technically access your files. Logseq Sync ensures that only your devices can decrypt the data.

**Best for:** Users who work across multiple devices (laptop, phone, tablet) and want encrypted sync without technical setup.

## Sponsor Tier (~$15/month): Support the Project, Get Early Access

The Sponsor tier is part support, part early access. You get everything in the Sync plan, plus:

- **Early access to experimental features** before they reach the general release
- **Priority in the community feedback pipeline**
- **Badge and recognition** in the Logseq community
- **Direct support of open-source development**

This tier is not about getting more features long-term. Experimental features eventually roll out to everyone. You are paying to support the team and to be first in line for new capabilities.

**Best for:** Power users who want to shape the product's direction, open-source supporters, and anyone who relies on Logseq professionally and wants to ensure its continued development.

## Free Self-Sync Alternatives

If you want cross-device access without paying for Logseq Sync, four options work well:

- **iCloud (macOS/iOS):** Automatic sync between Apple devices. Does not work natively with Android or Windows, and occasional file conflicts can occur.
- **Dropbox:** Cross-platform with 2 GB free storage, more than enough for text-based graphs. Risk of sync conflicts with simultaneous edits.
- **Git:** The most robust option for technical users. Full version history and conflict resolution, but not real-time and requires command-line comfort.
- **Syncthing:** Free, open-source, peer-to-peer sync with no cloud server. Both devices must be online simultaneously.

**Bottom line:** If you are on a single platform, free sync options work fine. If you need reliable cross-platform sync with zero setup, the $5/month Logseq Sync plan saves real headaches.

## Logseq vs Obsidian Pricing

Obsidian is the most common comparison point for Logseq. Here is how pricing stacks up:

| Feature | Logseq | Obsidian |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Base app | Free | Free |
| Cloud sync | ~$5/month | $4/month (Obsidian Sync) |
| Publishing | Not available | $8/month (Obsidian Publish) |
| Commercial use | Free | $50/year per user |
| Encryption | E2E (sync plan) | E2E (sync plan) |

Obsidian charges $50/user/year for commercial use (Logseq does not), and offers an $8/month Publish service with no Logseq equivalent. Sync pricing is nearly identical at ~$5/month for both. For a deeper comparison, see our [Logseq vs Obsidian](/blog/logseq-vs-obsidian-2026) breakdown.

## Logseq vs Notion Pricing

Notion takes a completely different approach to pricing:

| Plan | Notion | Logseq Equivalent |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| Free | $0 (limited blocks for guests) | $0 (unlimited, local) |
| Plus | $10/month | ~$5/month (sync only) |
| Business | $20/month | N/A |
| Enterprise | Custom | N/A |

Notion's free tier is limited for team use, while Logseq's is the full product. Notion is cloud-first with your data on their servers. At scale, Notion gets expensive fast (a 10-person team on Business pays $200/month), while Logseq has no per-seat pricing. If you prefer owning your data, Logseq is dramatically cheaper. If you need team collaboration features, Notion fills a gap Logseq does not cover.

## Who Should Pay for Logseq?

**Pay for Sync (~$5/month) if you:**
- Use Logseq on multiple devices daily
- Want encrypted sync without any technical setup
- Use both desktop and mobile and need real-time updates
- Value convenience over DIY configuration

**Pay for Sponsor (~$15/month) if you:**
- Use Logseq as a core tool in your workflow
- Want early access to new features
- Want to support open-source development financially
- Can afford it and believe in the mission

**Stay on Free if you:**
- Use Logseq on a single device
- Are comfortable with iCloud, Dropbox, Git, or Syncthing for sync
- Are evaluating Logseq before committing
- Prefer spending zero on note-taking tools

## The Bottom Line

Logseq is one of the best deals in the note-taking space. The free tier is genuinely free with no feature restrictions, no usage caps, and no data lock-in. The $5/month sync plan is competitively priced against Obsidian Sync and far cheaper than Notion's paid tiers for individual use.

The only scenario where Logseq pricing becomes a consideration is if you need cross-device sync and do not want to manage it yourself. Even then, $5/month for end-to-end encrypted sync is reasonable by any standard.
