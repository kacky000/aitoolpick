# Windsurf vs GitHub Copilot Pricing 2026: $15 AI Editor vs $10 IDE Assistant

Windsurf and GitHub Copilot sit just $5 apart at the entry level — Copilot Pro at $10/month, Windsurf Pro at $15/month — but they're not the same kind of product. Copilot adds AI to the editor you already use. Windsurf is an AI-native editor built around its Cascade agent. That $5 buys a different way of coding, not just more of the same. Here's the full breakdown.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Windsurf | GitHub Copilot |
|---|----------|----------------|
| **Free** | $0, light daily quota + unlimited Tab | Free — $0, 2,000 completions + 50 chats/mo |
| **Entry paid** | Pro — $15/mo, 50 premium interactions/day | Pro — $10/mo, unlimited completions |
| **Mid tier** | — | Pro+ — $39/mo, more premium requests |
| **Team tier** | Teams — $30/user/mo | Business — $19/user/mo |
| **What it is** | AI-native code editor (IDE) | AI assistant inside your IDE |

## The Free Tiers

**Windsurf Free ($0)** gives a light daily quota of premium AI interactions plus unlimited Tab completions — genuinely usable for light sessions, but you'll hit the quota ceiling fast if you lean on the Cascade agent.

**Copilot Free ($0)** allows 2,000 code completions and 50 chat requests per month inside VS Code, JetBrains, and others. For an occasional coder, Copilot's free tier alone can cover real work — a generous floor Windsurf doesn't match on raw completion volume.

Verdict on free: Copilot Free for autocomplete in your existing editor; Windsurf Free to test the AI-first editing flow.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($15) vs Pro ($10)

**GitHub Copilot Pro — $10/month.** Unlimited completions plus a monthly allotment of premium requests (now metered as AI Credits, $10 included on Pro), working inside whatever IDE you already use. The pitch: keep your setup, add AI, pay the least. Verified students and OSS maintainers get it free.

**Windsurf Pro — $15/month.** A daily quota of 50 premium AI interactions plus unlimited Tab completions, inside Windsurf's own editor where Cascade can read and edit across your whole project — not just suggest the next line. At $15 it undercuts [Cursor's $20 Pro](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/) while offering the same tier of frontier models, and annual billing brings the effective cost closer to $12–13/month.

The real difference: Copilot makes your current editor smarter for $10; Windsurf gives you an editor built around an AI agent for $15. Happy in VS Code? Copilot's $10. Want the AI woven into every action? Windsurf's $15.

## Where They Scale

Copilot's individual ceiling is **Pro+ ($39/month)** for higher premium-request limits. Windsurf keeps individual pricing simple at one paid tier ($15), pushing heavier users toward Teams.

For teams: **Copilot Business ($19/user/month)** adds policy controls and deep GitHub ecosystem integration; **Windsurf Teams ($30/user/month)** adds team management on top of the AI-native editor. A 5-person team pays ~$95/mo on Copilot Business vs ~$150/mo on Windsurf Teams — Copilot wins on team cost and GitHub integration, Windsurf wins on editing experience per seat. Both discount with annual billing.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **You code in VS Code or JetBrains and want AI for the least money** → Copilot Pro ($10).
- **You want an editor built around an AI agent** → Windsurf Pro ($15).
- **You only need autocomplete occasionally** → Copilot Free.
- **You manage a dev team on a budget with heavy GitHub use** → Copilot Business ($19/user).
- **You want AI-native editing for your team** → Windsurf Teams ($30/user).

The $5 gap isn't really about money — it's whether you want an assistant in your editor or an editor built around the assistant. Most developers content with their IDE should start at Copilot's $10; move to Windsurf's $15 only if you want the AI in the driver's seat.
