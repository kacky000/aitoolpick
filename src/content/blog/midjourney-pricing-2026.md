Midjourney's pricing has stayed relatively stable since its launch, but the feature differences between plans have grown. If you're deciding which tier makes sense for your usage, here's everything you need to know about Midjourney's four plans in 2026.

## All Plans at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) | Fast GPU Time | Concurrent Jobs | Stealth Mode | Relax GPU |
|------|---------|-------------------|---------------|-----------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Basic** | $10 | $8 | 3.3 hr/mo | 3 | No | No |
| **Standard** | $30 | $24 | 15 hr/mo | 3 | No | Unlimited |
| **Pro** | $60 | $48 | 30 hr/mo | 12 | Yes | Unlimited |
| **Mega** | $120 | $96 | 60 hr/mo | 12 | Yes | Unlimited |

All plans include access to the Midjourney web app, the full model library (V6, Niji), commercial usage rights, and the member gallery.

Annual billing saves you 20% across every tier.

## Basic ($10/month)

The entry-level plan gives you 3.3 hours of fast GPU time per month. That translates to roughly 200 image generations, depending on resolution and the model version you use.

**What you get:**
- 3.3 hours of fast GPU time
- 3 concurrent jobs
- Commercial usage rights
- Access to all models and features

**What you don't get:**
- No Relax mode — once your fast hours run out, you wait until next month
- No Stealth mode — your images appear in the public gallery

Basic is fine for casual users who generate a handful of images per week. If you're experimenting with AI art or making the occasional social media graphic, this covers it. But if you're running any kind of regular production workflow, you'll burn through 3.3 hours faster than expected.

## Standard ($30/month)

Standard triples the fast GPU time to 15 hours and adds unlimited Relax mode — a significant upgrade.

**What you get:**
- 15 hours of fast GPU time
- Unlimited Relax GPU time
- 3 concurrent jobs
- Commercial usage rights

**The Relax mode difference:** When your fast hours run out (or when speed isn't critical), Relax mode queues your jobs behind paying fast-mode users. Generation times range from 1 to 10 minutes instead of the usual 30-60 seconds. For batch work where you don't mind waiting, it's effectively unlimited generation.

Standard is the most popular plan for a reason. Fifteen fast hours handles most professional workflows, and Relax mode means you never truly hit a wall. Freelance designers, content creators, and small teams typically land here.

## Pro ($60/month)

Pro doubles the fast GPU time again and unlocks two features that matter for professional use: Stealth mode and 12 concurrent jobs.

**What you get:**
- 30 hours of fast GPU time
- Unlimited Relax GPU time
- 12 concurrent jobs (up from 3)
- Stealth mode
- Commercial usage rights

**Stealth mode explained:** By default, every image you generate on Midjourney is visible in the public member gallery and can be found via search. Stealth mode hides your generations from public view. Your prompts and outputs stay private.

This matters if you're working on client projects, developing a product line, or creating images you don't want competitors to see. For agencies and businesses, Stealth mode alone can justify the upgrade from Standard.

**12 concurrent jobs** means you can queue up far more work at once. Instead of submitting three prompts and waiting, you can fire off twelve variations simultaneously — useful when you're exploring different directions or iterating on a concept.

## Mega ($120/month)

Mega is Pro with double the fast GPU time. Everything else stays the same.

**What you get:**
- 60 hours of fast GPU time
- Unlimited Relax GPU time
- 12 concurrent jobs
- Stealth mode
- Commercial usage rights

Sixty hours of fast GPU is a lot. To put it in perspective, that's roughly 3,600+ standard-resolution images per month at full speed. Mega exists for studios and power users who generate hundreds of images daily and can't afford to rely on Relax mode for any of it.

If you're not consistently maxing out Pro's 30 fast hours, Mega is overkill. Check your usage stats before upgrading.

## How GPU Time Actually Works

Fast GPU time is consumed differently depending on what you're doing:

- **Standard generation (V6, 1024x1024):** ~1 minute of GPU time per image
- **Upscaling:** Uses additional GPU time (varies by resolution)
- **Variations and remixing:** Each counts as a new generation
- **Niji model:** Similar consumption to V6

The 3.3 hours on Basic doesn't mean 3.3 hours of wall-clock time. It's actual GPU compute time. A single image might take 30-60 seconds of real time but only consume ~1 minute of your GPU allocation.

Track your usage in the Midjourney account settings. The dashboard shows remaining fast hours, total generations, and Relax mode availability.
