**Linear** offers multiple pricing tiers ranging from a free plan to enterprise options. This guide breaks down every plan, what's included, and which one offers the best value for your needs.

Linear has become the go-to issue tracking tool for modern engineering teams. But is it worth the price? Here's a complete breakdown of Linear's pricing in 2026, including what you get at each tier and who each plan is designed for.

For a full feature overview, see our [Linear review](/blog/linear-review-2026).

## Linear Pricing Plans

### Free — For Small Teams

**Price**: $0 forever

Linear's free plan is surprisingly generous for an enterprise-quality tool:

- Unlimited issues
- Unlimited members
- All core views (board, list, timeline)
- Git integration (GitHub, GitLab)
- Cycles and projects
- API access
- 250MB file storage

The free plan works well for teams up to about 10 people. You get the full Linear experience — the speed, the keyboard shortcuts, the beautiful UI — without paying anything.

**Best for**: Startups, open-source teams, and small engineering teams.

### Basic — $8/user/month

**Price**: $8/user/month (annual), $10/user/month (monthly)

Everything in Free, plus:

- Unlimited file storage
- Admin roles and permissions
- Priority support
- Custom workflows
- Advanced filters and views
- Guest access

The jump from Free to Basic mainly unlocks administrative controls and unlimited storage. For teams where file attachments and permissions matter, it's worth the upgrade.

**Best for**: Growing engineering teams (10-50 people) that need role management.

### Business — $14/user/month (annual)

**Price**: $14/user/month (annual), $16/user/month (monthly)

Everything in Basic, plus:

- SAML SSO
- SCIM provisioning
- Audit logs
- Advanced security controls
- Private teams
- SLA support

The Business plan is primarily about security and compliance. If your organization requires SSO, audit trails, or enterprise security features, this is the tier you need.

**Best for**: Mid-size to large engineering organizations with security requirements.

### Enterprise — Custom Pricing

Everything in Business, plus:

- Dedicated account manager
- Custom integrations
- Advanced compliance certifications
- Uptime SLA
- Custom data residency

Enterprise pricing is negotiated based on team size and requirements. Expect significant per-seat discounts for teams over 100.

**Best for**: Large organizations with specific compliance or support requirements.

## Is Linear Worth the Cost?

### It's worth it if:

- Your team values **speed and keyboard-driven workflows**
- You're tired of Jira's complexity and slow performance
- You need **tight Git integration** without configuration headaches
- You want a tool that **developers actually enjoy using**
- Your team is growing and needs better issue tracking

### Stick with free/other tools if:

- You need features beyond issue tracking (docs, wikis, CRM)
- Your team isn't primarily engineering-focused
- You need extensive customization (Jira's flexibility is unmatched)
- Budget is extremely tight (the free plan covers most needs)

## Tips to Save on Linear

1. **Start with Free** — it's legitimately usable for most small teams
2. **Pay annually** — save 20% on Basic ($8 vs $10) and 12.5% on Business ($14 vs $16)
3. **Only upgrade for what you need** — if you don't need SSO or audit logs, Basic is enough
4. **Startup program** — Linear offers discounts for early-stage startups (check their website)

## The Bottom Line

Linear's pricing is fair for what you get — the fastest, most polished issue tracking tool on the market. The free plan is generous enough to start with, and the paid plans are competitively priced compared to alternatives.

For most engineering teams under 10 people, the free plan is all you need. Scale up to Basic when you need admin controls, and Business when compliance requires it.
