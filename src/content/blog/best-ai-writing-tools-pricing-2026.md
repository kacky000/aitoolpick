# Best AI Writing Tools Pricing 2026: Jasper, Copy.ai, Writesonic & Grammarly Compared

The AI writing market has split into clear price bands, and picking the right tool is now mostly about matching the *job* to the *tier* — not chasing the lowest sticker. This guide lays out what every major AI writing tool actually costs in 2026, what the price buys, and which one wins for your specific use case.

## The Full Pricing Table

| Tool | Free tier | Entry paid | Top published tier |
|------|-----------|------------|--------------------|
| **Grammarly** | Yes (grammar + basics) | Pro $12/mo (annual) | Business $15/seat/mo |
| **Writesonic** | Yes | Individual $16/mo | Standard $79/mo |
| **ChatGPT** | Yes | Plus $20/mo | Pro $200/mo |
| **Jasper** | No (trial only) | Creator $49/mo | Pro $69/mo (+ Business custom) |
| **Copy.ai** | Yes (2,000 words/mo) | Starter $49/mo | Advanced $249/mo |

Three things jump out: Grammarly is the cheapest entry that does a *complete* job; ChatGPT is the value generalist; and Copy.ai has the widest spread ($0 to $249) because its top tier sells automation, not writing.

## The Price Bands, Explained

**$12–$20 — Edit & general-purpose.** Grammarly Pro ($12) polishes writing you produce yourself. ChatGPT Plus ($20) generates and does everything else, but you supply the structure. These two cover most individual writers.

**$16–$79 — Volume content.** Writesonic spans this band, optimizing for cost-per-word. Cheap to start, with quality that improves as you climb. See [Jasper vs Writesonic pricing](/blog/jasper-vs-writesonic-pricing-2026/) for how it stacks against the premium option.

**$49–$69 — Brand-voice content.** Jasper lives here, selling consistency and marketing scaffolding rather than raw volume. Worth it when re-editing time is your real cost.

**$249 — GTM automation.** Copy.ai Advanced is a different product entirely — workflow automation for sales and marketing ops, not blog drafts.

## Pick by Job

**"I write my own words and want them clean."** → Grammarly Pro $12. The clearest value in the market. Details in [Grammarly vs Jasper pricing](/blog/grammarly-vs-jasper-pricing-2026/) — they're complements, not rivals.

**"I want a cheap, flexible all-rounder."** → ChatGPT Plus $20. Generates content plus handles every other task. See [Jasper vs ChatGPT pricing](/blog/jasper-vs-chatgpt-pricing-2026/) for when the $20 generalist quietly costs more than the $49 specialist.

**"I need high volume on a budget."** → Writesonic Individual $16, free tier to test first.

**"I produce on-brand marketing content at scale."** → Jasper Creator $49, Pro $69 for teams. Compare with [Jasper vs Copy.ai pricing](/blog/jasper-vs-copy-ai-pricing-2026/).

**"I'm automating sales/GTM workflows."** → Copy.ai Advanced $249. Nothing else here does this job — see [Copy.ai vs Writesonic pricing](/blog/copy-ai-vs-writesonic-pricing-2026/).

## Common Pricing Mistakes

- **Paying Jasper's $49 to clean up emails.** That's a Grammarly $12 job.
- **Paying Copy.ai's $249 for blog drafts.** Writesonic does volume writing for $16–$79.
- **Assuming cheapest entry = cheapest outcome.** ChatGPT at $20 can cost more than Jasper at $49 once you count prompt-setup time on a content team.
- **Buying one when you need two.** Generating and editing are different jobs; Jasper + Grammarly is a common, sensible pairing.

## Free Tiers Worth Using First

Four of the five have a free tier: Grammarly (genuinely useful), ChatGPT (powerful), Writesonic (volume test), and Copy.ai (2,000 words). Only Jasper is trial-only. Start free, hit the real limits, *then* pay for the tier that removes the limit that's actually blocking you.

## The Bottom Line

There's no single "best" — there's the best for your job. Editing → Grammarly $12. General use → ChatGPT $20. Volume → Writesonic $16. Brand content → Jasper $49. GTM automation → Copy.ai $249. Match the tier to the work and you'll never overpay.
