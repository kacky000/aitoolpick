For students, the choice between **[ChatGPT](/tools/chatgpt/)** and **[Gemini](/tools/gemini/)** comes down to two things most guides skip: which one is actually free enough for school work, and which has the better **student deal**. This guide compares both on price and on what matters for studying — research, writing, and not blowing your budget.

## The Short Answer

- **On a strict $0 budget:** Both free tiers handle most coursework. Gemini's free tier is generous, and ChatGPT's free tier covers everyday writing and Q&A.
- **For the best student value:** Both companies have run **free or discounted plans for students** — check current offers before paying full price.
- **If you'd pay:** Gemini AI Pro ($19.99) bundles **2 TB of storage** (useful for backing up all your school files); ChatGPT Plus ($20) is pure AI with strong reasoning.

## What Matters for School Work

### Writing essays and assignments
Both are strong. ChatGPT has a slight edge in tone control and structured drafting; Gemini is fast and integrates with Google Docs, where a lot of student writing already happens. If you write in Docs, Gemini saves you copy-pasting.

### Research
Neither is a citation engine by default (for that, see [Perplexity vs ChatGPT for research](/blog/perplexity-vs-chatgpt-for-research-2026/)). Between these two, Gemini's tight Google Search integration helps for quick fact-finding, while ChatGPT is better at synthesizing and explaining.

### Math and problem-solving
Both handle step-by-step explanations well. Gemini's free tier gives you generous access to capable models; ChatGPT's reasoning models (on paid tiers) are excellent for harder STEM problems.

### Storage and files
This is Gemini's quiet advantage: AI Pro includes **2 TB of Google storage**. For a student already using Google Drive for notes, slides, and backups, that's real value bundled with the AI.

## The Bottom Line

For most students, **start with the free tier of either** — they're genuinely capable for coursework. If you're going to pay, **check the student offer first** (free plans appear regularly), then choose based on ecosystem: Gemini if you live in Google Docs and want bundled storage, ChatGPT if you want the strongest reasoning for tougher subjects.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is ChatGPT or Gemini better for students?

Both are excellent for coursework. Gemini wins on Google Docs integration and bundled storage; ChatGPT wins on reasoning depth for STEM. On a free budget, either works.

### Do students get ChatGPT or Gemini for free?

Both OpenAI and Google have offered students free or discounted access to their paid tiers. Promotions change, so check the current student page before paying. Both also have permanent free tiers.

### What's the cheapest paid AI plan for students?

Gemini AI Plus at $7.99/mo is the lowest standing paid tier. ChatGPT's entry tier (Go) is $8/mo. Student promos can make the flagship tiers free temporarily.

### Which AI is better for writing essays?

Both are strong. ChatGPT edges ahead on tone and structure; Gemini is more convenient if you write inside Google Docs.
