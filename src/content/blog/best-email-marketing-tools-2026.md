# Best Email Marketing Tools for Small Business in 2026

Email marketing delivers one of the highest ROIs of any digital channel — around $36 for every $1 spent, according to industry benchmarks. But with dozens of tools available, choosing the right platform for your small business can be overwhelming.

This guide compares the five best email marketing tools for small businesses in 2026, covering pricing, deliverability, automation, and ease of use.

---

## Quick Comparison

| Tool | Free Tier | Starting Price | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mailchimp | 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/mo | $13/month | Beginners, all-in-one |
| ConvertKit | 10,000 subscribers | $25/month | Creators, bloggers |
| Brevo | 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts | $9/month | Budget-conscious |
| MailerLite | 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/mo | $10/month | Simplicity |
| Constant Contact | 60-day trial | $12/month | Events, nonprofits |

---

## 1. Mailchimp — Best All-in-One Platform

Mailchimp is the most recognized email marketing tool in the world, and for good reason. It combines email marketing with landing pages, basic CRM, and even social media ads — all in one dashboard.

**Strengths:**
- Drag-and-drop email builder with 100+ templates
- Audience segmentation and behavioral targeting
- A/B testing on subject lines, content, and send times
- Integrates with 300+ apps including Shopify, WordPress, and Salesforce
- Marketing CRM to track customer journeys

**Weaknesses:**
- Pricing scales quickly as your list grows
- Automation is limited on lower-tier plans
- Interface has become cluttered with added features

**Pricing:**
- **Free:** 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month
- **Essentials:** from $13/month (500 contacts)
- **Standard:** from $20/month — best for automation
- **Premium:** from $350/month — advanced segmentation

**Best for:** Small businesses that want one platform for email, landing pages, and basic ads without juggling multiple tools.

---

## 2. ConvertKit — Best for Creators and Bloggers

ConvertKit (now rebranding as Kit) was built specifically for content creators — bloggers, podcasters, YouTubers, and newsletter writers. Its approach to subscriber management using tags and sequences is more flexible than traditional list-based platforms.

**Strengths:**
- Tag-based subscriber management (no duplicate contacts across lists)
- Visual automation builder with branching logic
- Built-in landing page and signup form creator
- Commerce features to sell digital products directly
- Generous free plan: up to 10,000 subscribers

**Weaknesses:**
- Email template library is limited
- Less suited for e-commerce or product-based businesses
- Reporting is simpler compared to Mailchimp

**Pricing:**
- **Free:** Up to 10,000 subscribers (limited automation)
- **Creator:** from $25/month — automated sequences, integrations
- **Creator Pro:** from $50/month — advanced reporting, Facebook custom audiences

**Best for:** Bloggers, newsletter creators, and solopreneurs who grow their audience through content and want to monetize directly.

---

## 3. Brevo — Best Budget Option

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) takes a different pricing approach: instead of charging per subscriber, it charges based on emails sent per month. This makes it highly cost-effective for businesses with large lists that send infrequently.

**Strengths:**
- Contact-count-agnostic pricing (pay per email sent, not per subscriber)
- Built-in SMS marketing, WhatsApp, and live chat
- Transactional email support (order confirmations, receipts)
- CRM included in all plans
- Strong deliverability reputation

**Weaknesses:**
- Free plan limits you to 300 emails per day
- Email builder is functional but less polished than competitors
- Automation workflows have a steeper learning curve

**Pricing:**
- **Free:** 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts
- **Starter:** from $9/month — 5,000 emails/month, no daily limit
- **Business:** from $18/month — marketing automation, A/B testing, advanced reporting
- **Enterprise:** Custom

**Best for:** Small businesses with large contact lists that don't email frequently, or those needing SMS alongside email.

---

## 4. MailerLite — Best for Simplicity

MailerLite consistently ranks as the easiest email marketing tool to use. The interface is clean, the feature set is focused, and the pricing is among the most transparent in the industry.

**Strengths:**
- Clean, intuitive drag-and-drop builder
- Excellent deliverability rates
- Landing pages, pop-ups, and signup forms included
- Digital product sales (like ConvertKit)
- Honest, predictable pricing

**Weaknesses:**
- Fewer native integrations than Mailchimp
- Advanced segmentation is limited
- No built-in CRM

**Pricing:**
- **Free:** 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails/month
- **Growing Business:** from $10/month — unlimited emails, 3 users
- **Advanced:** from $20/month — unlimited users, custom HTML editor, AI writing
- **Enterprise:** Custom

**Best for:** Small business owners who want a clean, no-nonsense email marketing tool without a steep learning curve.

---

## 5. Constant Contact — Best for Events and Nonprofits

Constant Contact has been around since 1995 and remains a solid choice for businesses that run events or work in the nonprofit space. Its event management features and social media integration stand out.

**Strengths:**
- Event marketing and registration management
- Social media scheduling included
- Strong phone and chat support
- 60-day free trial (no credit card required)
- Nonprofit discounts available

**Weaknesses:**
- More expensive than competitors for equivalent features
- Email builder feels dated compared to MailerLite or Mailchimp
- Automation is basic on entry-level plans

**Pricing:**
- **Lite:** from $12/month — 1 user, 10x contacts email sends/month
- **Standard:** from $35/month — automation, segmentation
- **Premium:** from $80/month — advanced features, custom automations

**Best for:** Small businesses, nonprofits, and event organizers that prioritize support and event management features.

---

## How to Choose the Right Tool

**Budget is tight?** Start with Brevo (free for up to 300 emails/day with unlimited contacts) or MailerLite (free up to 1,000 subscribers).

**Building a content business?** ConvertKit's creator-first features and 10,000-subscriber free plan are unbeatable.

**Want everything in one place?** Mailchimp's ecosystem is the most comprehensive for small businesses running multiple marketing channels.

**Just need simplicity?** MailerLite is the easiest to learn and use.

**Running events or a nonprofit?** Constant Contact's specialized features justify the higher price.

---

## Key Features to Evaluate

Before choosing, evaluate these factors:

1. **List size and growth rate** — Will you hit paid tiers quickly?
2. **Email volume** — How often do you send? (Matters for Brevo's model)
3. **Automation needs** — Do you need welcome sequences, drip campaigns, or complex workflows?
4. **Integrations** — Does it connect to your e-commerce platform, CRM, or other tools?
5. **Deliverability** — Check independent inbox placement rates before committing
