**[Squarespace](/tools/squarespace-builder/)** quietly replaced its old plan lineup in late 2025. The familiar Personal / Business / Commerce tiers are gone, replaced by four cleaner plans: Basic, Core, Plus, and Advanced. Here's exactly what each costs in 2026 and which one you actually need.

## Squarespace Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Annual (per mo) | Monthly | Transaction fee |
|------|-----------------|---------|-----------------|
| **Basic** | $16 | $25 | 2% on sales |
| **Core** | $23 | $36 | 0% |
| **Plus** | $39 | $56 | 0% |
| **Advanced** | $99 | $139 | 0% |

Every annual plan includes a **free custom domain for the first year** and SSL. Annual billing saves up to ~36% versus paying monthly.

### Basic — $16/month (annual)

The entry plan covers a complete website:

- Unlimited pages, bandwidth, and storage
- SSL security and basic website analytics
- Selling enabled — but with a **2% transaction fee** on each sale

Basic is right for portfolios, brochure sites, and creators who sell only occasionally. If you process meaningful sales volume, that 2% fee makes upgrading cheaper than staying.

### Core — $23/month (annual)

Everything in Basic, plus:

- **0% transaction fees**
- Advanced selling tools and customer accounts
- Point-of-sale and basic merchandising

Core is the sweet spot for most small online stores. Dropping the 2% fee usually offsets the price jump once you're selling regularly.

### Plus — $39/month (annual)

Everything in Core, plus:

- Advanced shipping and inventory tools
- Subscriptions and advanced commerce analytics
- Limited API access

Plus suits growing stores that need subscriptions or deeper logistics control.

### Advanced — $99/month (annual)

Everything in Plus, plus:

- Advanced discounts and abandoned-cart recovery
- Full commerce APIs
- Advanced shipping integrations

Advanced is for serious merchants where recovered carts and automation justify the cost.

## Is Squarespace Worth the Cost?

### It's worth it if:

- You want **design-led templates** that look professional with zero effort
- You sell occasionally (Basic) or run a **small-to-mid store** (Core/Plus)
- You value an all-in-one platform — hosting, domain, and store in one bill

### Look elsewhere if:

- You need a **free plan** to start — [Wix](/blog/wix-pricing-2026) offers one
- You want **pixel-level design control** — [Webflow](/blog/webflow-pricing-2026) goes deeper
- You're a high-volume merchant — [Shopify](/blog/shopify-pricing-2026)'s ecosystem fits better

## Tips to Save on Squarespace

1. **Pay annually** — it saves up to 36% and includes a free domain year one
2. **Skip Basic if you sell regularly** — the 2% fee on Basic often costs more than upgrading to Core
3. **Watch the renewal domain price** — the free year reverts to standard pricing after 12 months
4. **Start on Core, not Plus** — only upgrade for subscriptions or advanced logistics

## The Bottom Line

The 2026 plan overhaul made Squarespace simpler: Basic for sites, Core for most stores, Plus and Advanced for scaling merchants. There's no free tier, but the design quality and Core's 0% transaction fee make it strong value for anyone who cares how their site looks. If you sell anything regularly, start at Core — the 2% fee on Basic erases its savings fast.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Did Squarespace change its plans?

Yes. In late 2025 Squarespace replaced Personal, Business, Commerce Basic, and Commerce Advanced with four new plans: Basic, Core, Plus, and Advanced. The rollout reached most markets by early 2026.

### Does Squarespace charge transaction fees?

Only on the Basic plan, which charges 2% per sale. Core, Plus, and Advanced have 0% Squarespace transaction fees (your payment processor's fees still apply).

### Is there a free Squarespace plan?

No. Squarespace offers a free trial but no permanent free tier. If you need a free plan, [Wix](/blog/wix-pricing-2026) is the closest alternative.
