**Bardeen** is a browser-based automation tool that lets you build workflows without writing code — right from your Chrome tab. It handles everything from scraping web data to connecting apps and running AI-powered agents. But how much does it actually cost?

This guide covers every Bardeen plan in 2026, what each tier includes, and how it stacks up against alternatives like [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) and [Make](/tools/make/).

## Bardeen Pricing Overview

| Plan | Price | Credits/Month | Key Features |
|------|-------|---------------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | Limited | Basic automations, browser extension, 50+ integrations |
| Professional | $10/user/mo | Generous | Unlimited runs, premium integrations, AI actions |
| Business | $15/user/mo | Higher limits | Team management, shared workflows, priority support |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | SSO, audit logs, dedicated support, custom onboarding |

All paid plans are billed per user per month. Annual billing is available with discounts.

## Free Plan — What's Included

Bardeen's free tier is surprisingly usable. You get the full browser extension, access to the visual workflow builder, and connections to over 50 apps including Google Sheets, Slack, Notion, and LinkedIn.

Here's what you can do without paying anything:

- **Basic automations** — build and run simple multi-step workflows
- **Browser scraping** — pull data from web pages into spreadsheets
- **50+ integrations** — connect popular apps out of the box
- **Community playbooks** — use pre-built automations shared by other users
- **Manual triggers** — run automations on demand from your browser

The catch? You'll hit credit limits quickly if you're running automations frequently. There's no scheduling on the free plan either, so everything has to be triggered manually. For occasional use — say, scraping a list of leads once a week or automating a repetitive form fill — the free plan works fine. But once you need things running on autopilot, you'll want to upgrade.

## Professional Plan ($10/mo) — The Sweet Spot

At $10 per user per month, the Professional plan removes most of the friction. This is where Bardeen becomes a genuine productivity tool rather than a toy.

What you get over Free:

- **Unlimited automation runs** — no more worrying about credit caps
- **Scheduled automations** — set workflows to run on a timer without opening your browser
- **Premium integrations** — access to advanced connectors for CRMs, databases, and enterprise tools
- **AI-powered actions** — use built-in AI to classify data, summarize text, or generate responses inside your workflows
- **Priority execution** — your automations run faster and more reliably

For solo users, freelancers, and small teams, this plan covers virtually every use case. The AI actions alone justify the $10 — you can build workflows that read incoming emails, categorize them with AI, and route them to the right Slack channel, all without touching a line of code.

If you've been comparing [Zapier's pricing](/blog/zapier-pricing-2026), you'll notice that Bardeen's Professional plan at $10/month undercuts Zapier's equivalent tier by a wide margin. The trade-off is that Bardeen runs inside your browser rather than in the cloud, which matters for always-on workflows.

## Business Plan ($15/mo) — Built for Teams

The Business plan adds $5 per user per month on top of Professional, and the extra cost is almost entirely about team collaboration and administrative control.

Additional features over Professional:

- **Team workspaces** — share automations across your organization
- **Role-based access control** — decide who can edit, run, or view specific workflows
- **Shared playbooks** — build a library of approved automations that anyone on the team can use
- **Admin dashboard** — monitor usage, manage seats, and track automation performance
- **Priority support** — faster response times from Bardeen's support team

This plan makes sense when you have 3+ people who all need to automate browser-based tasks. The shared playbooks feature is particularly useful — instead of every team member building their own version of "scrape LinkedIn profiles and add to Google Sheets," you build it once and share it.

For a team of 5, you're looking at $75/month total. That's less than what a single Zapier Team plan costs.

## Enterprise — Custom Pricing

Bardeen's Enterprise tier is designed for larger organizations that need compliance features and dedicated support. Pricing isn't published — you'll need to contact their sales team.

Enterprise typically includes:

- **SSO (Single Sign-On)** via SAML or OKTA
- **Audit logs** — full visibility into who ran what and when
- **Custom onboarding** — dedicated sessions to set up workflows for your team
- **Dedicated account manager** — a named contact for support and strategy
- **Custom integrations** — connectors built specifically for your internal tools
- **SLA guarantees** — uptime and response time commitments

If your company requires SOC 2 compliance or has strict data handling policies, the Enterprise tier is the only option. For everyone else, Business or Professional will do the job.

## Bardeen vs Zapier vs Make — Price Comparison

How does Bardeen stack up against the two biggest automation platforms? Here's a side-by-side look at comparable tiers:

| Feature | Bardeen Professional | Zapier Professional | Make Core |
|---------|---------------------|--------------------|-----------| 
| Price | $10/user/mo | $29.99/mo | $10.59/mo |
| Pricing Model | Per user | Per workspace | Per workspace |
| Automation Runs | Unlimited | 750 tasks/mo | 10,000 credits/mo |
| Scheduling | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| AI Features | Built-in | Add-on | Limited |
| Browser Automation | Native | No | No |
| Min Check Interval | N/A (browser-based) | 1 minute | 1 minute |

A few things stand out. Bardeen is the cheapest option per seat, and it's the only one with native browser automation. Zapier and Make run entirely in the cloud, which means they're better for server-to-server integrations that need to run 24/7 without a browser open. Bardeen shines when your workflows involve web pages — scraping, form filling, browser-based data entry.

For a deeper comparison of the other two, see our [Zapier vs Make breakdown](/blog/zapier-vs-make-2026).

If you're primarily automating cloud apps (Slack to Google Sheets, CRM updates, email sequences), Zapier or Make might be the better fit. If your workflows touch the browser — and many do — Bardeen fills a gap that neither of those tools covers well.

## Which Plan Is Right for You?

**Stick with Free** if you're just experimenting with browser automation or only need to run a few manual workflows per week.

**Go Professional ($10/mo)** if you're a solo user or freelancer who wants scheduled automations, AI-powered actions, and unlimited runs. This is the plan most people should start with.

**Go Business ($15/mo)** if you have a team of 3+ people who need shared workflows, admin controls, and collaborative automation management.

**Talk to Sales for Enterprise** if your organization requires SSO, audit trails, or custom compliance features.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Bardeen free?

Yes, Bardeen offers a free plan that includes basic automations, browser scraping, and 50+ integrations. See the pricing breakdown above for the full list of limitations.

### Is Bardeen worth paying for?

For most users who automate browser-based tasks regularly, yes. The Professional plan at $10/month removes credit limits and unlocks scheduling, which makes a significant difference in daily workflow.

### How does Bardeen compare to Zapier on price?

Bardeen's Professional plan ($10/user/mo) is substantially cheaper than Zapier's Professional plan ($29.99/mo). However, they serve slightly different use cases — Bardeen excels at browser automation while Zapier is stronger for cloud-to-cloud integrations.
