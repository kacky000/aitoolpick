# Bolt.new vs Cursor Pricing 2026: $25 App Builder vs $20 AI Editor

Bolt.new and Cursor both put AI at the heart of building software, and their entry plans land just $5 apart — Bolt Pro at $25/month, Cursor Pro at $20/month. But they're built for different builders. Bolt.new is a prompt-to-app platform: describe a full-stack app, watch it build and deploy entirely in the browser, no terminal required. Cursor is a full AI code editor — a VS Code fork where you write, refactor, and navigate a real codebase with AI alongside you. The $5 gap is trivial next to that difference.

## The Free Tiers

**Bolt Free** gives 1 million tokens per month capped at 300,000 daily, with Bolt branding on hosted sites, a 10 MB upload limit, and no custom domains. Enough to test the build-and-host flow, but the daily token cap throttles longer sessions.

**Cursor Free** gives a limited number of AI completions and slow-pool requests — enough to feel the editor, but you'll hit limits in a day of real coding.

Verdict on free: Bolt to see a whole app run end-to-end; Cursor to feel what AI-native editing is like inside a real project.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($25) vs Pro ($20)

**Bolt Pro — $25/month.** Removes the daily token cap, giving 10 million tokens monthly with rollover, plus 100 MB uploads, custom domains, SEO controls, and no Bolt branding. Bolt's value is the all-in-one loop: prompt → full-stack build (front end *and* back end) → deploy, without ever touching a terminal. It's aimed at people who want a working, hosted product fast — including non-developers. Tokens are consumed by every action, so heavy debugging burns faster than expected.

**Cursor Pro — $20/month.** Gives a $20 monthly credit pool for premium models inside a full IDE. Cursor is where developers *do the work*: multi-file edits, codebase-aware chat, agentic refactors, and project-aware tab completion. It assumes you already have (or are building) a real codebase and want AI to accelerate every keystroke.

The real difference: Bolt builds the app *for* you and hosts it; Cursor makes *you* a faster developer in your own environment. If you want to ship a product without managing code, Bolt's $25 fits. If you're a developer who lives in an editor, Cursor's $20 fits.

## Where Cursor Scales

Cursor offers a ladder Bolt doesn't: **Pro+ at $60/month** triples the credit pool for developers who exhaust $20 mid-month, and **Ultra at $200/month** gives 10x credits for all-day agentic coding. Bolt scales differently — its next step is **Teams at $30/user/month**, adding $5 over Pro for per-seat 10M-token allocations and collaboration. So Cursor scales by *intensity* (more credits for one developer), Bolt by *headcount* (more builders on a team).

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Want a full-stack app built and hosted without a terminal?** Bolt Pro ($25/mo).
- **Developer who wants AI-native editing across a real codebase?** Cursor Pro ($20/mo).
- **Burning through your $20 pool every month?** Cursor Pro+ ($60/mo) or Ultra ($200/mo).
- **Team of builders shipping apps together?** Bolt Teams ($30/user/mo).
- **Engineering team coding daily?** Cursor Teams ($40/user/mo).
- **Honest take?** They're not rivals — Bolt scaffolds and hosts; Cursor is where you keep building.

The $5 gap is noise; the job is everything. Bolt.new builds and hosts a full-stack app from a prompt — ideal when you want a shipped product without managing code. Cursor is a full AI editor that makes a developer faster inside their own codebase. Pick Bolt if you want the app handed to you; pick Cursor if you want to build it yourself, faster. And if you start in Bolt and later need real engineering control, exporting into a Cursor workflow is a natural next step.
