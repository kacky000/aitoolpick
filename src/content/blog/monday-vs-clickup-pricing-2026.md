**[Monday.com](/tools/monday/)** and **[ClickUp](/tools/clickup/)** are two of the biggest work-management platforms, and both look affordable on paper. But Monday's **seat minimums** and seat-tier bundling make its real cost very different from ClickUp's straight per-user pricing.

This guide compares both pricing models, the seat rules that catch teams off guard, and which platform is cheaper at the sizes most teams actually run.

## The Key Difference: Seat Minimums and Tier Bundling

Two quirks make Monday cost more than its per-seat price suggests.

**Monday enforces seat minimums and bundles.** Paid plans require a **minimum of 3 seats**, and seats are often sold in **bundles (3, 5, 10…)**. A 4-person team frequently pays for 5 seats. So Monday's "$9 Basic" is really "$27 minimum," and growth happens in jumps, not one seat at a time.

**ClickUp bills per actual user with no minimum.** Three people cost exactly 3 seats. You add one seat at a time, with no bundle rounding.

The result: ClickUp is almost always cheaper, and the gap is largest for small or oddly-sized teams that get rounded up by Monday's bundles.

## Cost Scenario: A 7-Person Team

Monday often rounds 7 up to a 10-seat bundle:

- **Monday Standard**: ~10 seats × $12 = **$120/month** (rounded up from 7)
- **ClickUp Business**: 7 × $12 = **$84/month**

That's a **~$36/month** difference for the same headcount, purely from seat rounding.

Even matched seat-for-seat at the entry tier:

- **Monday Basic**: 7 × $9 = **$63/month**
- **ClickUp Unlimited**: 7 × $7 = **$49/month**

ClickUp stays cheaper because of the lower base price *and* no bundle rounding.

## Free Plan Comparison

- **Monday Free**: Capped at **2 seats** — effectively a solo/duo trial, not a team plan.
- **ClickUp Free Forever**: **Unlimited members and tasks**, with capped advanced features.

ClickUp's free tier is genuinely usable by a small team; Monday's 2-seat cap forces a paid upgrade almost immediately.

## Where Monday Justifies Its Price

Monday isn't just more expensive for no reason:

- **Visual, color-coded boards** that non-technical teams adopt fast
- **Polished onboarding** and a gentler learning curve than ClickUp
- **Strong CRM and marketing templates** out of the box

If ease of adoption matters more than per-seat cost, Monday's premium can pay for itself in faster team buy-in.

## Verdict

**Choose ClickUp** if you want the lowest real cost and flexible, per-user billing with no minimums. For small and odd-sized teams especially, it avoids Monday's bundle rounding.

**Choose Monday.com** if visual simplicity and fast, non-technical adoption are worth a premium — and your team is large enough that seat minimums don't sting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is ClickUp cheaper than Monday.com?

Usually yes. ClickUp Unlimited is $7/user vs Monday Basic at $9, and ClickUp has no seat minimum, while Monday requires at least 3 seats and often sells seats in bundles.

### Does Monday.com have a seat minimum?

Yes. Paid Monday plans require a minimum of 3 seats, and seats are frequently sold in bundles (3, 5, 10), so small teams get rounded up.

### How many users does Monday's free plan allow?

Monday Free is capped at 2 seats, making it a solo or duo trial rather than a true team plan. ClickUp's free tier allows unlimited members.

### Which is easier to learn?

Monday.com generally has a gentler learning curve with its visual boards, while ClickUp offers more features at the cost of added complexity.
