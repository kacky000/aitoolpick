Whether you're a freelancer billing by the hour, an agency tracking project profitability, or a team manager monitoring workloads, accurate time tracking is essential. The right tool makes it effortless — the wrong one makes it a chore your team ignores.

Here are the best time tracking tools in 2026, organized by use case.

## Best Overall: Toggl Track

**Price**: Free for up to 5 users / $9/user/mo (Starter) / $18/user/mo (Premium)

Toggl Track is the gold standard for time tracking. One-click timers, clean reporting, and integrations with 100+ tools make it painless for individuals and teams alike.

**Best for**: Teams that need simple, reliable time tracking without complexity.

**Key features**:
- One-click timer + manual entry
- Project and client tracking
- Detailed reports and dashboards
- Calendar integration
- Browser extension and desktop app
- Pomodoro timer built in

## Best for Freelancers: Harvest

**Price**: Free (1 seat, 2 projects) / $10.80/seat/mo

Harvest combines time tracking with invoicing — a perfect combo for freelancers and consultants. Track time, generate invoices from tracked hours, and accept online payments.

**Best for**: Freelancers and consultants who bill by the hour.

**Key features**:
- Timer + manual time entry
- Invoicing from tracked time
- Expense tracking
- Budget monitoring per project
- QuickBooks and Xero integration
- Team capacity planning

## Best Free Option: Clockify

**Price**: Free (unlimited users) / $3.99/user/mo (Basic) / $5.49/user/mo (Standard)

Clockify offers unlimited users and unlimited tracking on its free plan — a rare deal. The interface is clean, reports are solid, and it covers the basics well.

**Best for**: Teams on a tight budget that need basic time tracking for everyone.

**Key features**:
- Unlimited free tracking
- Project and task categorization
- Weekly timesheets
- Reports and exports
- Kiosk mode for on-site teams
- GPS tracking (mobile)

## Best for Agencies: Teamwork

**Price**: Free (5 users) / $13.99/user/mo (Deliver) / $25.99/user/mo (Grow)

Teamwork combines project management with built-in time tracking, making it ideal for agencies that need to track billable hours across multiple clients and projects.

**Best for**: Agencies managing multiple client projects.

**Key features**:
- Time tracking tied to tasks
- Billable vs non-billable hours
- Client-level reporting
- Resource management
- Profitability tracking
- Budget alerts

## Best Built into a PM Tool: ClickUp

**Price**: Free / $7/user/mo (Unlimited)

If you already use [ClickUp for project management](/blog/clickup-review-2026), its built-in time tracking means one less tool to manage. Track time directly on tasks, view time reports, and set estimates.

**Best for**: Teams already using ClickUp that want native time tracking.

**Key features**:
- Timer on every task
- Time estimates vs actuals
- Billable time flagging
- Time reports by team member
- Integrations with Toggl and Harvest

## Best for Remote Teams: Hubstaff

**Price**: $4.99/user/mo (Starter) / $7.50/user/mo (Grow) / $10/user/mo (Team)

Hubstaff includes activity monitoring (screenshots, app tracking, URL tracking) alongside time tracking. It's designed for remote teams that need visibility into how work time is spent.

**Best for**: Remote teams that need accountability and activity monitoring.

**Key features**:
- Automatic time tracking
- Optional screenshots and activity levels
- GPS location tracking
- Payroll automation
- Project budgets
- Idle time detection

## Best for Automatic Tracking: Timely

**Price**: $11/user/mo (Starter) / $20/user/mo (Premium) / $28/user/mo (Unlimited)

Timely runs in the background and automatically records everything you work on — apps, websites, documents, meetings. You review and approve the AI-generated timesheet instead of manually starting timers.

**Best for**: People who hate manually tracking time.

**Key features**:
- Automatic time capture
- AI-powered timesheet drafts
- Memory timeline (private activity log)
- Project budgets
- Team dashboards
- No manual timers needed

## Best for Developers: WakaTime

**Price**: Free / $11.99/mo (Premium)

WakaTime integrates directly into your code editor (VS Code, JetBrains, Vim, etc.) and automatically tracks coding time by project, language, and file.

**Best for**: Developers who want effortless coding metrics.

**Key features**:
- Automatic tracking in 60+ IDEs
- Language and project breakdowns
- Daily/weekly coding reports
- Goal setting
- Team leaderboards
- GitHub integration

## Best for Billing: FreshBooks

**Price**: $6.80/mo (Lite) / $11.80/mo (Plus) / $22.40/mo (Premium)

FreshBooks is primarily an accounting tool, but its time tracking is excellent — especially because tracked time flows directly into invoices and financial reports.

**Best for**: Small businesses that need time tracking integrated with accounting.

**Key features**:
- Timer and manual entry
- Automatic invoice generation
- Expense tracking
- Tax-ready reports
- Client portal
- Proposal creation

## Best for Simple Timesheets: Everhour

**Price**: Free (up to 5 users) / $8.50/user/mo (Team)

Everhour integrates directly into tools like [Asana](/blog/asana-review-2026), [Trello](/blog/trello-review-2026), [ClickUp](/blog/clickup-review-2026), and Jira. Track time inside the PM tool you're already using.

**Best for**: Teams that want time tracking embedded in their existing project management workflow.

**Key features**:
- Native integration with PM tools
- Budgets and estimates
- Invoicing
- Timesheets and approvals
- Resource planning
- Detailed reporting

## How to Choose

| Need | Best Pick |
|------|-----------|
| Simple tracking for a team | Toggl Track |
| Freelancer + invoicing | Harvest |
| Free for unlimited users | Clockify |
| Agency client management | Teamwork |
| Already using ClickUp | ClickUp |
| Remote team monitoring | Hubstaff |
| Hate manual tracking | Timely |
| Developer metrics | WakaTime |
| Accounting integration | FreshBooks |
| Embed in existing PM tool | Everhour |

## The Bottom Line

The best time tracking tool is the one your team will actually use. Start with the simplest option that fits your needs — you can always upgrade later. For most teams, Toggl Track or Clockify is the right starting point. Freelancers should look at Harvest. Agencies should evaluate Teamwork.
