# Notion vs Obsidian 2026: Which Note App Should You Use?

Notion and Obsidian are the two most-debated note-taking tools of 2026—but they're built on opposite philosophies. Notion is an all-in-one cloud workspace with databases; Obsidian is a local-first Markdown vault built around linked thinking.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Notion** if you want structured databases, collaboration, and an all-in-one workspace. Choose **Obsidian** if you want fast, private, local Markdown notes you fully own.

## Pricing Compared

| Plan | Notion | Obsidian |
|------|--------|----------|
| Free | Generous personal use | Full app, free |
| Paid | Plus $10/user/mo | Sync $4/mo, Publish $8/mo |
| AI add-on | Notion AI bundled/extra | Bring your own via plugins |

Obsidian's core app is free; you only pay for optional Sync and Publish. Notion charges per seat for teams and AI. See the full [Notion pricing breakdown](/blog/notion-pricing-2026) for what each tier includes.

## Where Notion Wins

- **Databases**: Tables, boards, and relations turn notes into real systems.
- **Collaboration**: Shared workspaces, comments, and permissions built in.
- **All-in-one**: Docs, wikis, tasks, and projects in one tool.

## Where Obsidian Wins

- **Local-first**: Your notes are plain Markdown files on your disk—you own them.
- **Speed & privacy**: No cloud round-trip; works fully offline.
- **Linked thinking**: Backlinks and graph view excel for personal knowledge.
- **Plugins**: A massive community plugin ecosystem.

## Head-to-Head

| Factor | Notion | Obsidian |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Data ownership | Cloud | Local files |
| Collaboration | Excellent | Limited |
| Offline | Partial | Full |
| Best for | Teams & systems | Personal knowledge |

## Who Should Choose What?

**Choose Notion if you:**
- Need databases and structured workspaces
- Collaborate with a team
- Want everything in one cloud tool

**Choose Obsidian if you:**
- Want to own your notes as local files
- Value privacy, speed, and offline access
- Are building a personal knowledge base

If you're still weighing options, see [Best Notion Alternatives](/blog/best-notion-alternatives-2026).

## The Bottom Line

**Notion** wins for structured, collaborative workspaces; **Obsidian** wins for private, local-first knowledge. Pick Notion for systems and teams, Obsidian for ownership and thinking.
