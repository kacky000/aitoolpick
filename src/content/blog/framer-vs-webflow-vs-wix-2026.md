# Framer vs Webflow vs Wix: Best No-Code Website Builder in 2026

Three platforms dominate the no-code website market in 2026, and they target very different users. Here is the side-by-side comparison.

## TL;DR

| Goal | Pick |
|------|------|
| Beautiful marketing site shipped this weekend | **Framer** |
| Production-grade site with CMS and custom code | **Webflow** |
| Simple business site, no design skills required | **Wix** |

## Pricing

- **Framer**: Free site, Mini $5, Basic $15, Pro $25/mo per site.
- **Webflow**: Starter free, Basic $14, CMS $23, Business $39/mo.
- **Wix**: Light $17, Core $29, Business $36, Studio $159/mo.

Framer is cheapest at the entry tier. Wix is the most expensive when you factor in apps.

## AI Features

- **Framer AI**: Generates entire pages from a prompt. Best UX of the three.
- **Webflow AI**: Solid for copy and image generation, but the builder itself stays manual.
- **Wix AI**: Most aggressive — Wix AI Builder generates the whole site from a few questions. Quality is OK but homogeneous.

## Design Freedom

Webflow wins for pixel-perfect control. Framer is close behind and faster. Wix is templated — most Wix sites look like Wix sites.

## SEO and Performance

- **Webflow**: Best technical SEO. Clean HTML, fast loads, CMS-friendly URLs.
- **Framer**: Improved significantly in 2026 — now passes Core Web Vitals out of the box.
- **Wix**: Better than its 2018 reputation, but still slower than the other two.

## CMS

Webflow's CMS is the most powerful. Framer added a usable CMS in 2025 — fine for blogs and small content sites. Wix Studio caught up on this front but lags in developer features.

## Who Should Pick What

- **Designers and indie hackers**: Framer.
- **Marketing teams, agencies, scale-up SaaS**: Webflow.
- **Small businesses, restaurants, local shops**: Wix.

## Migration Reality

Migrating between these three is painful. Pick once based on a 2-year horizon, not just today's needs.

## Bottom Line

Framer is the rising star, Webflow is the professional standard, and Wix owns the SMB market. There is no "best" — only "best for your use case."
