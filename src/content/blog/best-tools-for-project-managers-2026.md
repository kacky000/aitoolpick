Looking for the best tools for project managers? We tested and compared the top options available in 2026, evaluating features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance.

Project managers need tools that balance planning, execution, and communication. After testing dozens of options, here are the 8 best tools for PMs in 2026 — ranked by how well they handle the actual job of managing projects.

## 1. Asana — Best for Structured Team Workflows

**Price:** Free (up to 10 users) | Starter $10.99/user/mo | Advanced $24.99/user/mo

Asana is built for project managers. Timeline view for Gantt-style planning, workload management for capacity planning, and portfolios for multi-project oversight. The automation builder handles repetitive tasks like status updates and approvals.

**Best for:** Teams with defined processes who need structure without complexity.

**Pros:** Clean timeline view, great portfolios, reliable automations
**Cons:** Gets expensive fast, limited free plan, no built-in docs

## 2. Monday.com — Best for Visual Project Tracking

**Price:** Free (2 users) | Basic $9/seat/mo | Standard $12/seat/mo | Pro $19/seat/mo

Monday.com's visual dashboards are its killer feature. Every status update, timeline shift, and workload change is visible at a glance. The automation and integration capabilities on Standard and Pro plans [make](/tools/make/) it powerful for cross-functional teams.

**Best for:** PMs who want visual dashboards and automated reporting.

**Pros:** Beautiful dashboards, powerful automations, CRM add-on
**Cons:** 3-seat minimum, expensive at scale, free plan is very limited

## 3. ClickUp — Best Value for Feature-Rich PM

**Price:** Free (unlimited users) | Unlimited $7/user/mo | Business $12/user/mo

ClickUp packs more features per dollar than any competitor. Docs, whiteboards, goals, time tracking, dashboards — it's genuinely all-in-one. The free plan supports unlimited users, making it unbeatable for small teams on a budget.

**Best for:** PMs who want everything in one tool without paying enterprise prices.

**Pros:** Feature-rich, affordable, generous free plan
**Cons:** Can be overwhelming, occasional performance issues, mobile app is basic

## 4. Linear — Best for Engineering Project Managers

**Price:** Free (up to 250 issues) | Basic $8/user/mo | Business $16/user/mo

Linear is the fastest issue tracker on the market. Purpose-built for software teams, it handles sprints, cycles, roadmaps, and bug tracking with a keyboard-first interface that engineers love. If you manage engineering projects, nothing else comes [close](/tools/close/).

**Best for:** Engineering PMs and technical program managers.

**Pros:** Blazing fast, keyboard shortcuts, beautiful Git integration
**Cons:** Developer-focused only, limited customization, no docs feature

## 5. Notion — Best for Flexible Project Documentation

**Price:** Free | Plus $10/user/mo | Business $20/user/mo

Notion isn't a traditional PM tool, but many project managers use it for project documentation, meeting notes, knowledge bases, and lightweight task tracking. Its database feature can create custom project trackers tailored to any workflow.

**Best for:** PMs who need project documentation alongside task management.

**Pros:** Extremely flexible, great free tier, powerful databases
**Cons:** Not a dedicated PM tool, slow with large pages, limited automations

## 6. Trello — Best for Simple Kanban Workflows

**Price:** Free | Standard $5/user/mo | Premium $10/user/mo

Trello's simplicity is its strength. If your project management needs are straightforward — track tasks across stages — Trello does it with zero learning curve. Power-Ups add calendar views, time tracking, and integrations.

**Best for:** PMs managing simple, visual workflows.

**Pros:** Dead simple, great free plan, mobile-friendly
**Cons:** Limited views, not for complex projects, basic reporting

## 7. Airtable — Best for Custom PM Workflows

**Price:** Free | Team $20/seat/mo | Business $45/seat/mo

Airtable is a spreadsheet-database hybrid that lets PMs build custom project management systems. Create your own views, automations, and interfaces. It's more work to set up than Asana or Monday.com, but the result is perfectly tailored to your process.

**Best for:** PMs who need custom workflows beyond what standard PM tools offer.

**Pros:** Infinitely customizable, great API, interface designer
**Cons:** Expensive, requires setup time, row limits on lower plans

## 8. Miro — Best for Planning and Brainstorming

**Price:** Free (3 boards) | Starter $8/user/mo | Business $16/user/mo

Miro complements your PM tool rather than replacing it. Use it for sprint planning, retrospectives, brainstorming sessions, and stakeholder mapping. The infinite canvas and real-time collaboration make remote planning sessions feel natural.

**Best for:** PMs who run frequent planning workshops and team sessions.

**Pros:** Infinite canvas, great for workshops, templates library
**Cons:** Not a task manager, can be slow with large boards, learning curve
