Looking for the best gantt chart software? We tested and compared the top options available in 2026, evaluating features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance.

Gantt charts are one of the most useful tools in project management — and nearly every major PM platform has built one in. The question isn't whether you need Gantt charts; it's which tool gives you the best ones for your situation.

This guide covers the best Gantt chart software in 2026, from dedicated tools to PM platforms with built-in timeline views.

## 1. ClickUp — Best Free Gantt Chart Option

**Best for:** Teams that want Gantt charts alongside tasks, docs, and dashboards

ClickUp includes a Gantt chart view on the free plan — a rarity among PM tools. The free version is limited in some ways (fewer automations, storage), but the timeline view itself is fully functional.

The Gantt view in ClickUp lets you drag tasks to reschedule them, set dependencies so one task can't start until another finishes, and see resource allocation across your team. It connects directly to your task list, so updates flow in both directions.

**What it does well:**
- Drag-and-drop rescheduling
- Task dependencies with visual connectors
- Critical path highlighting on paid plans
- Connects to everything else in ClickUp

**What it lacks:**
- Baseline tracking only on Business plan
- Can feel overwhelming if you only want Gantt charts

## 2. Asana — Best for Teams Needing Robust Timelines

**Best for:** Marketing, operations, and product teams who live in Asana

Asana's Timeline view is one of the cleanest Gantt implementations available. Available on the Starter plan ($10.99/user/month), it shows tasks on a timeline with dependencies, milestones, and color-coded workload indicators.

The key advantage over ClickUp is polish. Asana's interface is consistently clean, and Timeline feels like a first-class feature rather than one of fifty views crammed in.

**What it does well:**
- Clean, intuitive drag-and-drop
- Workload view to avoid overloading team members
- Milestones and dependencies
- Portfolio-level timelines (Advanced plan)

**What it lacks:**
- No Gantt on the free plan
- Expensive for large teams

## 3. Monday.com — Best for Visual Teams

**Best for:** Client-facing teams and agencies who present timelines regularly

Monday.com's timeline view is highly visual and easy to customize. You can color-code by status, group, or team member, making it easy to build a Gantt chart that communicates well in a client presentation or stakeholder meeting.

Like Asana, it doesn't offer Gantt on the free plan. The Standard plan ($12/seat/month, minimum 3 seats) is the entry point.

**What it does well:**
- Excellent visual design
- Easy to share with external clients
- Automations that update timeline automatically
- Dashboard widgets for overview

**What it lacks:**
- Minimum 3 seats — expensive for small teams
- Baseline tracking missing from lower tiers

[See Monday.com pricing →](/tools/monday)

## 4. Notion — Best If Your Team Is Already in Notion

**Best for:** Teams using [Notion](/tools/notion/) for docs and wikis who want light project tracking

Notion doesn't have a traditional Gantt chart, but its Timeline view (available on the Plus plan at $10/user/month) provides similar functionality for teams who don't need heavy PM features.

If your team already uses Notion for documentation, adding a timeline database is a natural extension. The limitation is that Notion's timeline is relatively simple — no resource allocation, no critical path, no milestones in the traditional sense.

**What it does well:**
- Integrated with your docs and wikis
- Clean, customizable views
- Filters and grouping
- Good for solo or small team use

**What it lacks:**
- No dependencies until you build a workaround
- Not suitable for complex multi-project management

[Compare Notion vs Asana →](/compare/notion-vs-asana)

## 5. TeamGantt — Best Dedicated Free Option

**Best for:** Freelancers and solo users who need a real Gantt chart for free

TeamGantt is one of the few dedicated Gantt tools with a genuinely useful free plan (1 manager, 3 guests, 1 project). If you just need to build a single project timeline and share it with a client or team, TeamGantt's free tier covers it.

The interface is purpose-built for Gantt charts — simpler and more focused than ClickUp or Asana. The paid plans start at $24/month for small teams.

## 6. GanttPro — Best Standalone Gantt Tool

**Best for:** Project managers who want a dedicated tool with no distractions

GanttPro starts at $7.99/user/month (annual) and focuses entirely on Gantt charts. It includes baselines, resource management, time tracking, and a workload chart — features that take dedicated PM platforms multiple plan tiers to match.

If you manage complex multi-project timelines and don't need the full suite of a PM platform, GanttPro is worth considering.

## How to Choose

**Choose ClickUp** if you want Gantt charts for free and don't mind a larger platform.

**Choose Asana or Monday.com** if your team is large, you manage complex dependencies, and you can afford a paid plan.

**Choose Notion** if your team already lives in Notion and you need light timeline tracking.

**Choose TeamGantt or GanttPro** if you specifically want a Gantt-first tool, not a full PM platform.

## The Bottom Line

For most teams, the Gantt chart views in [ClickUp](/tools/clickup), [Asana](/tools/asana), or [Monday.com](/tools/monday) are more than enough — and you get the rest of the platform's features alongside them. Dedicated Gantt tools make sense if Gantt charts are your entire workflow and you don't want to pay for features you won't use.
