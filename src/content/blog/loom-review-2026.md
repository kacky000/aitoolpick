**Loom** is a popular software tool used by individuals and teams for productivity and collaboration. In this review, we evaluate its features, pricing, pros, cons, and alternatives for 2026.

Loom was the breakout tool of the remote work era. Record your screen, share a link, and skip the meeting. Then Atlassian acquired it in 2023, and a lot of users started wondering: is it still the best tool for async video communication in 2026?

Short answer: yes — for most use cases. Here's the full picture.

## What Loom Actually Does

Loom lets you record your screen, webcam, or both simultaneously, then [instantly](/tools/instantly/) generates a shareable link. The recipient watches in their browser with no downloads required. They can comment at specific timestamps, react with emoji, and respond in-kind with their own Loom.

It replaces a surprising number of meetings:
- "Can you walk me through this bug?" → Loom it
- "Here's feedback on your design" → Loom it
- "Let me show you how to use this feature" → Loom it

The async nature is the entire value proposition. Your New York colleague records at 9 AM; your London colleague watches at 2 PM. Nobody needs to be online at the same time.

## What Atlassian Changed (and What They Didn't)

Since the acquisition, Loom has deepened its integration with [Jira](/tools/jira/), Confluence, and the broader Atlassian ecosystem. If you're an Atlassian shop, Loom is now built into your workflow rather than bolted on.

The core recording experience hasn't changed. The free plan is still free. Pricing held steady.

What did change: the enterprise push. Loom is increasingly marketed to larger teams through Atlassian's sales channels. [Small teams](/best-for/small-teams/) and solo users still get a great product, but the roadmap prioritizes enterprise features.

## Key Features

### Recording Quality
Loom captures up to 4K at 60fps on Business plans. The "picture-in-picture" webcam overlay is clean — you appear in the corner while your screen is the focus. Audio quality depends on your microphone, but Loom's noise reduction is better than average.

### Auto-Transcription
Every Loom gets an automatic transcript. Viewers can search the transcript, jump to any timestamp, and read without audio (useful in open offices). Transcription accuracy is solid for English and improving for other languages.

### Editing
Basic trimming, chapter markers, and call-to-action buttons are available on Business plans. You can cut out dead air and "umm" moments. It's not full video editing, but it's enough to polish a recording before sharing.

### Analytics
See who watched your video, how much they watched, and when they stopped. For sales and customer success teams, this is genuinely useful — you know if a prospect watched your product demo.

### Integrations
Loom embeds natively in:
- [Slack](/tools/slack/) (auto-previews in messages)
- [Notion](/tools/notion/) (renders inline)
- Jira and Confluence
- [Linear](/tools/linear/)
- [GitHub](/tools/github/)

For [remote teams](/best-for/remote-teams/) already living in Slack and Notion, Loom fits into the existing workflow with minimal friction.

## Who Should Use Loom

**Engineering teams**: Great for async code reviews, bug reproduction steps, and explaining architectural decisions without scheduling a call. The screen share + code walkthrough use case is where Loom genuinely shines.

**Customer success and sales**: Demo recordings, onboarding walkthroughs, and support explanations are faster and more personal than text-only responses.

**Managers**: Feedback on documents, design, or presentations. "I left comments in the doc" is less effective than watching someone walk through their specific reactions in real time.

**Educators and trainers**: Tutorial creation is fast. Record once, share many times.

Loom's free plan is the most useful of the group. Vidyard's single-video free tier is nearly useless. Zoom Clips works if your org is all-in on Zoom, but the integration depth doesn't match Loom's.

## What Loom Doesn't Do Well

**Long-form content**: Loom isn't a video hosting platform. For courses, webinars, or anything longer than 15-20 minutes, purpose-built tools are better.

**Complex editing**: You can trim and add chapters, but if you need captions, B-roll, or multi-track editing, you need dedicated software.

**Large organizations without Atlassian**: The Atlassian acquisition benefits Atlassian customers specifically. If you're not using Jira or Confluence, the integration advantage disappears.

## Verdict

Loom remains the best async video messaging tool for remote teams in 2026. The Atlassian acquisition hasn't degraded the product, and for teams already using Atlassian tools, the integration improvements are a genuine bonus.

The $15/creator/month Business plan is the sweet spot. Unlimited recordings, no time cap, and enough editing features to ship polished content without switching to a full video editor.

If you're evaluating async communication tools for your team, start with the free plan. You'll know within a week if it fits.
