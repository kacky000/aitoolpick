# Copilot Money Review 2026: Is It Worth It?

**Copilot Money** has earned a reputation as the most beautiful budgeting app on iPhone — and one of the smartest, thanks to its AI-powered transaction categorization. But it's Apple-first and subscription-only. In this review, we cover whether Copilot deserves a spot on your home screen in 2026.

## What is Copilot Money?

Copilot Money is a premium personal-finance app focused on the Apple ecosystem (iPhone, iPad, and Mac). It connects your accounts, automatically categorizes spending using machine learning, and presents everything in a famously polished interface. Like its rivals, it makes money from subscriptions rather than ads or data sales.

## Key Features

- **AI categorization**: Learns from your corrections and gets more accurate over time.
- **Beautiful, fast UI**: Widely considered the best-designed budgeting app on iOS.
- **Investment and net worth tracking** alongside budgeting.
- **Custom rollover budgets** and recurring transaction detection.
- **Apple-native experience**: Widgets, Face ID, and deep iOS integration.

## Copilot Money Pricing

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Free Trial | 1 month |
| Monthly | $13/month |
| Annual | $95/year (~$7.92/mo) |

There's no free tier, but the one-month trial is generous. At $95/year, Copilot is slightly cheaper than [Monarch Money](/blog/monarch-money-pricing-2026) ($99.99) and [YNAB](/blog/ynab-pricing-2026) ($109).

## Pros

- **Best-in-class design** and user experience on Apple devices
- **Smart AI categorization** that genuinely reduces manual cleanup
- **Cheapest of the premium trio** on the annual plan
- **One-month trial** — longer than most competitors

## Cons

- **Apple-only** — no Android or full web app
- **No free tier** after the trial
- **Less couples-focused** than Monarch

## Who Should Use Copilot Money?

Copilot is the obvious pick for **iPhone users who value design and automation** and want minimal manual work. If you're on Android or need shared couples' access, [Monarch Money](/blog/monarch-money-review-2026) is the better fit, and strict budgeters should look at [YNAB](/blog/ynab-review-2026).

## Verdict

Copilot Money is the best budgeting app for the Apple ecosystem in 2026 — beautiful, smart, and the cheapest of the premium options at $95/year. The only real catch is platform lock-in: if you live on iPhone, it's hard to beat; if you don't, look elsewhere.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Copilot Money free?

No. Copilot offers a one-month free trial, then costs $13/month or $95/year.

### Does Copilot Money work on Android?

No. Copilot is Apple-only — iPhone, iPad, and Mac. Android users should consider Monarch Money instead.

### Is Copilot Money worth it?

For iPhone users who want the best design and AI-powered categorization, yes. At $95/year it's the cheapest premium budgeting app.
