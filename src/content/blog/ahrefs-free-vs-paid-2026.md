Ahrefs gives away a surprising amount of functionality for free. Between Ahrefs Webmaster Tools and the free versions of its keyword and backlink checkers, you can do real SEO work without spending anything. But the free tools have hard limits — and understanding exactly where those limits kick in is the difference between wasting time and making a smart purchasing decision.

Here's a no-fluff breakdown of what's free, what's paid, and which plan actually makes sense for your situation.

## What You Get for Free

### Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (AWT)

AWT is the core free offering. You verify your own site (via DNS or HTML file) and get access to two tools:

- **Site Audit** — Crawls up to 5,000 pages per project. Finds technical SEO issues (broken links, missing meta tags, slow pages, redirect chains). Reports update every 7 days.
- **Site Explorer (limited)** — View your own site's backlink profile, referring domains, top pages, and organic keywords. Data is limited to your verified site only — you cannot check competitors.

**What's missing:** You can't analyze competitor sites, research keywords you don't already rank for, or access historical data beyond 3 months.

### Free Web Tools (No Account Required)

Ahrefs also offers browser-based tools that anyone can use without signing up:

- **Backlink Checker** — Shows top 100 backlinks for any domain. Enough to get a rough picture, not enough for competitive analysis.
- **Keyword Generator** — Returns up to 150 keyword ideas for a seed term, with search volume and keyword difficulty. No click data, no SERP analysis.
- **SERP Checker** — Shows the current top 10 results for a keyword with basic metrics.
- **Website Authority Checker** — Domain Rating (DR) for any URL.
- **Broken Link Checker** — Scans a page for broken outbound links.

These free tools are useful for quick checks, but they cap output at a fraction of what paid users see.

## What Paid Plans Unlock

The jump from free to paid isn't incremental — it's a different product. Here's what opens up:

### Full Site Explorer

The heart of Ahrefs. On paid plans, you can analyze any domain on the internet, not just your own. See complete backlink profiles, organic keyword rankings, traffic estimates, top pages, content gaps, and competing domains. This is the tool that makes Ahrefs worth paying for.

### Keywords Explorer

The paid version covers 10 search engines (not just Google), click metrics, SERP feature tracking, keyword clustering, and parent topic identification — everything you need to build a content strategy.

### Content Explorer

Not available on any free plan. Content Explorer lets you search a database of billions of web pages to find content by topic, filter by metrics (traffic, referring domains, word count), and identify link-building opportunities. It's exclusive to Standard ($249/month) and above.

### Rank Tracker

Track your keyword positions daily. Not available on free or Starter plans. Lite gets 750 tracked keywords, Standard gets 2,000, and Advanced gets 5,000.

### API Access

Available on Standard and above. Required if you want to pull Ahrefs data into dashboards, scripts, or automated reports.

## Free vs. Starter ($29/month): Is It Worth the Upgrade?

The Starter plan launched in January 2026, cutting the entry price from $99/month (old Lite) to $29. It's an easy upgrade for anyone who's outgrown the free tools.

**What Starter adds over free:**
- Site Explorer for *any* domain (limited to 1,000 rows per report)
- Keywords Explorer with basic filters
- 5 projects instead of 1 verified site
- Competitor analysis (the single biggest unlock)

**What Starter still lacks:**
- Content Explorer
- Rank Tracker
- API access
- Batch analysis
- Reports beyond 1,000 rows

If you find yourself constantly hitting the 100-backlink wall on the free checker, or if you need to look at competitor sites at all, $29/month is worth it. For a full pricing breakdown of every plan, check our [Ahrefs pricing guide](/blog/ahrefs-pricing-2026).

## Lite ($129/month): The Freelancer Plan

Lite is where Ahrefs becomes a serious work tool. Full Site Explorer, full Keywords Explorer, Site Audit, and 750 tracked keywords. If you're a freelancer managing a couple of client sites, this covers your needs.

The main pinch point is the keyword tracking limit. 750 keywords sounds like a lot until you're tracking 3 client sites across 50 keywords each — that's only 5 clients before you max out.

## Standard ($249/month): The Content Team Plan

Standard unlocks Content Explorer and bumps Rank Tracker to 2,000 keywords. It also gives you 2 years of historical data and API access, which matters for reporting.

Content Explorer alone can justify the upgrade from Lite — searching billions of pages, filtering by traffic, and finding unlinked brand mentions. This is the plan most in-house SEO teams land on.

## Advanced ($449/month) and Enterprise ($1,499/month)

Advanced is built for agencies: 5 user seats, 5,000 tracked keywords, Looker Studio integration, and 5 years of historical data. Enterprise scales everything further and adds dedicated support, SSO, and custom data limits.

## Who Should Stay on Free?

Free Ahrefs makes sense if:

- You run a single website and only need to audit your own technical SEO
- You do occasional keyword checks (fewer than a few times per week)
- You're learning SEO and want to understand the basics before investing
- Your site gets under 5,000 organic visits/month and SEO isn't your primary channel

## Who Should Pay?

You need a paid plan if:

- **You analyze competitors** — Free tools only show your own site. Even Starter at $29/month opens up competitor research.
- **You build content strategies** — Free keyword data is too shallow for editorial planning. Lite or Standard gives you the depth.
- **You do link building** — Full backlink data, Content Explorer for prospecting, and batch analysis are paid-only.
- **You report to clients or stakeholders** — Rank Tracker, historical data, and API exports require Standard or above.
- **You manage multiple sites** — Free AWT covers one site. Paid plans support 5 to 100+ projects.

## The Bottom Line

Ahrefs' free tools are better than what most SEO platforms offer at their lowest paid tier. But the moment you need competitor analysis or keyword research at scale, you're into paid territory.

The $29 Starter plan is the smart entry point — it removes the biggest limitation (competitor data) at a fraction of what Ahrefs used to cost. From there: Lite for freelancers, Standard for content teams, Advanced for agencies.
