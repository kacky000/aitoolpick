# Monarch Money Review 2026: Is It Worth It?

When Mint shut down, millions of users went looking for a replacement — and **Monarch Money** became the most recommended landing spot. But at $99.99/year with no free tier, it's a real commitment. In this review, we cover what Monarch does well, where it falls short, and who should actually pay for it.

## What is Monarch Money?

Monarch Money is a subscription personal-finance app that combines budgeting, net worth tracking, investment monitoring, and goal planning in one privacy-first dashboard. Unlike free apps that sell data or show ads, Monarch's only revenue is your subscription — which shapes the entire experience.

## Key Features

- **Account aggregation**: Syncs unlimited banks, cards, loans, and investment accounts.
- **Flexible budgeting**: Category budgets with rollover, so unused money carries forward.
- **Net worth tracking**: A clean timeline of assets and liabilities over time.
- **Goals**: Savings, debt payoff, and purchase goals tied to real accounts.
- **Collaboration**: A partner gets full access at no extra cost — a standout for couples.
- **Customizable dashboard**: Rearrange widgets to match how you think about money.

## Pros

- **Genuinely private** — no ads, no data selling
- **Best-in-class couples experience** — shared access included
- **Polished, fast interface** on both web and mobile
- **Strong net worth and investment views** alongside budgeting

## Cons

- **No free plan** — you must pay after the trial
- **Bank syncing can occasionally drop connections** (an industry-wide issue)
- **More expensive than some rivals** like [Copilot Money](/blog/copilot-money-review-2026)

## Who Should Use Monarch Money?

Monarch is ideal for **couples and households** who want one shared, private dashboard for budgeting and net worth. It's also a natural Mint replacement for anyone who valued the all-in-one view. If you're a strict zero-based budgeter, [YNAB](/blog/ynab-review-2026) may suit you better — see our [YNAB vs Monarch Money comparison](/blog/ynab-vs-monarch-money-2026) for a head-to-head.

## Verdict

Monarch Money earns its price for the right user. It's the most complete, private, couples-friendly budgeting app available in 2026, and the 7-day trial removes most of the risk. If you only need free envelope budgeting, look at our [best budgeting apps guide](/blog/best-budgeting-apps-2026) first — but if you want a true Mint successor, Monarch is the safest bet.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Monarch Money safe?

Yes. Monarch uses bank-level encryption and read-only connections, and its subscription model means it doesn't sell your data.

### Is Monarch Money worth $99 a year?

For couples and anyone wanting a private, all-in-one budgeting and net worth app, yes. Strict budget-only users may prefer cheaper or free alternatives.

### Does Monarch Money have a free version?

No. There's a 7-day free trial, after which it costs $99.99/year or $14.99/month.
