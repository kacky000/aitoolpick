**HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely powerful** — it's not a crippled trial. But the paid tiers (Starter, Professional, Enterprise) lock away features that determine whether HubSpot can actually run your marketing or sales operation. Here's exactly where the lines are drawn.

## HubSpot Pricing Tiers (2026)

| Tier | Monthly Cost | Best For |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Free | $0 | Small teams, CRM basics |
| Starter | $18/mo (2 seats) | Growing teams needing automation basics |
| Professional | $800-1,600+/mo | Full marketing/sales automation |
| Enterprise | $3,600+/mo | Large orgs, custom reporting |

Note: HubSpot sells separate Hubs (Marketing, Sales, Service, Operations, CMS). Prices above reflect Marketing Hub as reference. Bundles exist but are complex.

## HubSpot Free: What's Actually Included

HubSpot's free plan is not fake-free. You get:

### CRM (Always Free, No Expiry)
- **Unlimited contacts** (no contact cap)
- Contact, company, and deal records
- Activity timeline (emails, calls, meetings)
- Pipeline management (1 pipeline)
- Task management and activity scheduling
- Basic email templates (5)
- Meeting scheduling link (1 link)
- Live chat widget (HubSpot-branded)
- Forms (HubSpot-branded)
- Basic reporting dashboard

### What's Locked on Free

The moment you want to do anything automated or multi-touch, you hit a wall:

- ❌ No email automation sequences
- ❌ No workflows (automated actions based on triggers)
- ❌ No list segmentation based on behavior
- ❌ No A/B testing
- ❌ No custom reporting
- ❌ No social media publishing
- ❌ No ads management
- ❌ HubSpot branding on forms, emails, chat widget
- ❌ Only 1 sales pipeline
- ❌ Email sending via HubSpot limited to 2,000/month

## Starter Plan: $18/month (2 seats)

Starter removes HubSpot branding and unlocks a few critical baseline features:

- Remove HubSpot branding from all touchpoints
- **Email sending**: Up to 5x contact limit/month (vs 2,000 on free)
- **1:1 email personalization** with tokens
- **Simple automation**: Basic if-then workflows (limited)
- **Calling**: 500 minutes/month
- 2 pipelines (vs 1 on free)
- 10 email templates (vs 5)
- Conversation inbox
- Payment collection (U.S. only)

**The honest Starter assessment**: Starter at $18/month does less than competitors charge $50+/month for. It's best understood as "free + branding removal + slightly more room." It's not a true marketing automation tier.

## Professional Plan: $800-1,600+/month

This is where HubSpot becomes a real marketing/sales automation platform — and where the price jumps sharply:

### Marketing Hub Professional ($800/month, 3 seats)
- **Marketing automation** (full workflow builder)
- **Email campaigns** with A/B testing
- SEO recommendations
- Social media publishing and monitoring
- Smart content (personalize pages/emails by contact)
- Campaign analytics
- Custom reporting dashboards
- Blog, landing pages, CTAs (full suite)
- 10,000 marketing contacts included

### Sales Hub Professional ($100/user/month)
- **Sequences** (automated outreach cadences)
- **Forecasting** (deal probability, predicted revenue)
- Multiple sales pipelines
- Custom reporting
- Playbooks for reps
- E-signature

**The price jump from Starter to Professional is real and steep.** Most small businesses comparing HubSpot to Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or monday CRM will find Professional-tier pricing difficult to justify without significant contact volume or deal size.

## Is HubSpot Free Worth Using?

**Yes, for CRM basics.** HubSpot free is one of the best free CRMs available. If you need:
- Contact management for a small team
- A single pipeline to track deals
- Meeting booking links
- Live chat on your website

...then HubSpot free competes directly with paid tools like Pipedrive and Monday CRM.

**No, for marketing automation.** If you need email sequences, list segmentation, or behavioral triggers, HubSpot free gives you nothing. You're either paying $800/month for Professional or you're using a different tool.

## HubSpot vs Alternatives by Budget

| Budget | Better Option |
|--------|-------------|
| $0 | HubSpot Free (best free CRM) |
| $10-50/mo | Mailchimp, ActiveCampaign, or Brevo (email); Pipedrive (sales) |
| $100-300/mo | ActiveCampaign, Close, or Pipedrive (better per-feature value) |
| $500+/mo | HubSpot Professional becomes competitive at scale |

## Who Should Pay for HubSpot

**HubSpot Professional is worth it when:**
- You have a large inbound content engine (blog, SEO) and need the full attribution loop
- Your marketing team is 3+ people managing multi-channel campaigns
- You're on Salesforce or want to replace it with something more integrated
- Deal size is large enough that attribution and automation ROI is measurable

**Stick with free (or switch tools) when:**
- You need just email sequences → use Apollo, Instantly, or Close
- You need just email marketing → use Mailchimp or Brevo
- You need just a CRM → use Pipedrive or Notion CRM

## Bottom Line

HubSpot free is the best free CRM on the market — genuinely useful, no time limit, no contact cap. The paid tiers are strong but expensive. The Starter plan ($18/mo) offers minimal additional value. The jump to Professional ($800+/mo) is significant enough that smaller teams should evaluate ActiveCampaign, Close, or Brevo as alternatives before committing.
