# Notion vs Monday.com Pricing 2026: Complete Cost Breakdown

Notion and Monday.com both look affordable on their pricing pages. Then you add three team members, discover minimum seat requirements, and realize you're paying double what you expected. 

This breakdown covers the real cost of each platform in 2026 — not just the headline numbers, but per-seat math, forced minimums, annual vs monthly traps, and the hidden costs that only show up after you commit.

## Pricing at a Glance

| | Notion | Monday.com |
|---|--------|------------|
| Free Plan | Yes (unlimited blocks, 10 guests) | Yes (up to 2 seats only) |
| Cheapest Paid | $10/user/mo (Plus) | $9/seat/mo (Basic) — but min 3 seats = **$27/mo** |
| Mid Tier | $18/user/mo (Business) | $12/seat/mo (Standard) — min 3 = $36/mo |
| High Tier | Custom (Enterprise) | $19/seat/mo (Pro) — min 3 = $57/mo |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom |
| Billing | Monthly or annual | Monthly or annual |
| Annual Discount | ~20% | ~18% |

The first thing to notice: Monday.com requires a minimum of 3 seats on every paid plan. So while the per-seat price looks competitive, the actual starting cost is 3x the listed number.

## Notion Pricing Breakdown

### Free — $0

Notion's free plan is genuinely usable for individuals. You get unlimited pages and blocks, which is the core product. The main limitations:

- 10 guest collaborators (not full members)
- 7-day page history
- File uploads capped at 5MB per file
- No advanced permissions or SAML SSO

**Good for:** Solo users, students, personal knowledge management. You can run a serious personal workspace indefinitely on this plan.

### Plus — $10/user/month (annual) | $12/user/month (monthly)

The entry point for teams. Key upgrades over Free:

- Unlimited team members
- Unlimited file uploads (no size cap)
- 30-day page history
- Custom automations (250/month)
- Basic analytics

**Real cost for a 5-person team:** $50/month (annual) or $60/month (monthly).

### Business — $18/user/month (annual) | $21/user/month (monthly)

For teams that need governance and more power:

- Advanced permissions and private teamspaces
- 90-day page history
- Bulk PDF export
- Advanced automations (250/month per member)
- SAML SSO
- Admin tools and audit log

**Real cost for a 10-person team:** $180/month (annual).

### Enterprise — Custom Pricing

Adds SCIM provisioning, unlimited page history, advanced security, dedicated customer success manager, and custom contracts. Pricing is negotiated — typically starts around $25-28/user/month for 50+ seats based on public reports.

## Monday.com Pricing Breakdown

### Free — $0 (Maximum 2 Seats)

Monday's free plan is far more restrictive than [Notion](/tools/notion/)'s:

- Hard cap at 2 seats (not 2 users *per workspace* — 2 total)
- Up to 3 boards
- Unlimited docs
- 500MB storage
- No timeline, calendar, or Gantt views
- No integrations

**Good for:** Testing the interface. That's about it. A 2-seat, 3-board limit makes this impractical for real work.

### Basic — $9/seat/month (annual) | $12/seat/month (monthly) — Minimum 3 Seats

This is where Monday's pricing gets tricky. The minimum seat requirement means:

- **Listed price:** $9/seat/month
- **Actual minimum cost:** $27/month (3 seats x $9)
- **Monthly billing minimum:** $36/month (3 seats x $12)

What you get:
- Unlimited boards and items
- 5GB storage
- 1 dashboard (based on 1 board)
- iOS/Android apps
- No automations, no integrations, no timeline view

**The catch:** No automations and no integrations on Basic. For most teams, that makes this plan a non-starter. You're paying $27+/month for a tool you can't connect to anything.

### Standard — $12/seat/month (annual) | $14/seat/month (monthly) — Minimum 3 Seats

The first plan most teams actually consider:

- **Actual minimum cost:** $36/month (3 seats x $12)
- Timeline, Calendar, and Gantt views
- Guest access
- 250 automations/month
- 250 integrations/month
- 20GB storage

**Real cost for a 5-person team:** $60/month (annual).

### Pro — $19/seat/month (annual) | $22/seat/month (monthly) — Minimum 3 Seats

For teams that need reporting and time tracking:

- **Actual minimum cost:** $57/month (3 seats x $19)
- Private boards
- Chart view
- Time tracking
- Formula columns
- 25,000 automations/month
- 25,000 integrations/month
- 100GB storage

**Real cost for a 10-person team:** $190/month (annual).

### Enterprise — Custom Pricing

Advanced security, multi-level permissions, enterprise-scale automations, premium support. Requires annual contract.

## Hidden Costs to Watch

### Notion Hidden Costs
- **AI add-on:** Notion AI costs an additional $8/member/month (Plus) or $10/member/month (Business). It's not included in any plan.
- **Guest overages:** Free plan limits guests to 10. Paid plans include unlimited guests, but guests with editing access count toward your member billing.
- **Automation limits:** 250 automations/month on Plus. Heavy automation users may need Business ($18/user) just for higher limits.

### Monday.com Hidden Costs
- **Minimum seats:** The 3-seat minimum applies even if only 1 person uses the tool. You're paying for empty seats.
- **Seat increments:** Monday sells seats in packs (3, 5, 10, 15, etc.). If you have 6 people, you buy 10 seats. That's 4 wasted seats at full price.
- **Automations on Basic:** There are none. Zero. You need Standard ($36/mo minimum) for any automation at all.
- **Monday AI:** Available on Pro and Enterprise only. Standard users can't access AI features regardless of willingness to pay extra.
- **Storage caps:** 5GB on Basic, 20GB on Standard. If your team shares large files, you'll hit these quickly.

## Which Costs Less for Your Situation?

**Solo or 2-person team:** [Notion](/tools/notion/) wins decisively. The free plan is actually useful, and Plus at $10-20/month total crushes Monday's forced $36/month minimum.

**Small team (3-10 people):** Notion Plus is still cheaper than Monday Standard at every point. The only reason to choose [Monday](/tools/monday/) is if your team specifically needs Gantt charts, time tracking, or a visual board-centric workflow that Notion's databases don't replicate well.

**Mid-size team (10-50 people):** The per-seat savings with Notion compound here. At 50 people, you're saving $100/month ($1,200/year) on comparable tiers. But Monday's Pro plan includes native time tracking that Notion lacks — if you'd otherwise buy a separate time tracking tool, factor that cost into the comparison.

**Enterprise (50+ people):** Both require custom quotes. Negotiate hard and compare total cost of ownership including training, onboarding, and integration costs. For a deeper feature comparison, see our [Notion vs Monday 2026](/blog/notion-vs-monday-2026/) breakdown.

## Bottom Line

Notion is the cheaper option at every team size and tier, and it's not particularly close for small teams. Monday.com's minimum 3-seat requirement and higher per-seat pricing mean you're paying a premium for its more structured, visual project management approach.

That premium is worth it if your team relies on Gantt charts, time tracking, and workflow automations that Notion's flexible-but-less-structured approach doesn't handle as well. But if you're choosing purely on cost, Notion wins this one clearly.
