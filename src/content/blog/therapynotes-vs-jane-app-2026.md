TherapyNotes and Jane App are two of the most recommended practice management platforms in health and wellness — but they're built for different practices. TherapyNotes is laser-focused on behavioral health, while Jane App spans the whole allied-health spectrum. This **TherapyNotes vs Jane App** comparison breaks down pricing, features, and fit so you can pick the right one in 2026.

## Quick verdict

- **Choose TherapyNotes** if you're a therapist, counselor, or behavioral-health group that wants psychotherapy-specific notes and workflows.
- **Choose Jane App** if you run a multidisciplinary or allied-health clinic and want booking, charting, and insurance billing in one place — especially with several practitioners.

## Pricing compared

| | TherapyNotes | Jane App |
|---|---|---|
| Entry plan | **Solo $49/clinician** | **Base $54/location** |
| Mid plan | Group $59/clinician | Insurance $79/location |
| Top plan | Enterprise (custom) | Corporate $99/location |
| Pricing model | Per clinician | Per location (lower tiers) |

This is the single biggest difference. TherapyNotes charges **per clinician**, so costs rise linearly with team size. Jane prices **per location** on Base and Insurance, so a five-practitioner clinic pays one flat rate. For solo providers the two land close together; for teams, Jane is usually cheaper per head.

## Features compared

### TherapyNotes strengths
- Psychotherapy-specific note templates (intake, progress, treatment plans)
- Built-in behavioral-health billing and a patient portal
- Specialized compliance workflows for mental-health practices
- Simple, focused interface tuned to one discipline

### Jane App strengths
- Multidisciplinary scheduling (multiple disciplines, rooms, practitioners)
- Insurance claim submission on the Insurance plan
- Built-in telehealth without a separate video tool
- A polished, widely praised booking site and UX

## Insurance & billing

Both handle billing, but differently. TherapyNotes is tuned for behavioral-health claims and a single discipline. Jane's Insurance plan ($79) handles allied-health claims across multiple disciplines, making it the better fit for clinics that mix physiotherapy, massage, and nutrition under one roof.

## Which should you choose?

- **Solo behavioral-health therapist** → TherapyNotes. The specialized templates and per-clinician pricing fit a one-person psychotherapy practice.
- **Behavioral-health group practice** → TherapyNotes, unless you're adding non-therapy disciplines.
- **Multidisciplinary / allied-health clinic** → Jane App. Per-location pricing and discipline flexibility win.
- **Cost-sensitive growing team** → Jane App, thanks to flat per-location pricing.
