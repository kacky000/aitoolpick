**Acuity Scheduling** (now owned by Squarespace) is appointment scheduling software built specifically for service businesses — coaches, therapists, personal trainers, photographers, and wellness professionals. Unlike Calendly, which focuses on meetings and sales calls, Acuity focuses on **service appointments**: intake forms, package selling, and client management.

## Who Acuity Is Built For

Acuity targets a specific type of business:
- Health and wellness professionals (therapists, trainers, yoga instructors)
- Coaches (life coaches, business coaches, career coaches)
- Service providers (photographers, tutors, consultants charging by session)
- Anyone who needs to sell session packages or subscriptions alongside scheduling

If you're booking **sales calls or team meetings**, Calendly is probably a better fit. If you're booking **paid appointments with clients who also fill out intake forms and buy packages**, Acuity is built for that workflow.

## Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Calendar Limit | Staff Calendars |
|------|-------|---------------|----------------|
| Emerging | $16/mo | 1 | 1 |
| Growing | $27/mo | 6 | 6 |
| Powerhouse | $49/mo | 36 | 36 |

**Important**: Acuity has no free plan — only a 7-day free trial. This is the biggest competitive disadvantage vs Calendly (which has a free tier) and Cal.com (which has a generous free plan).

All plans include:
- Unlimited appointment types
- Client self-scheduling page
- Automated reminders and follow-ups
- Payment collection (Stripe, Square, PayPal)
- Time zone detection
- Basic intake forms

## Key Features

### Intake Forms on Booking
Acuity's standout differentiator: clients fill out a form **at the time of booking**, before the appointment. You can collect:
- Health history (for wellness professionals)
- Goals and background (for coaches)
- Project details (for photographers)
- Custom questions specific to your service

Calendly's equivalent (intake questions) is less powerful — Acuity's form builder is more feature-rich with conditional logic.

### Packages and Gift Certificates
Sell bundles of sessions directly through your booking page:
- "5-session coaching package" — client buys upfront, each booking deducts from the package
- "10-class yoga pass" — pre-paid, tracks remaining sessions
- Gift certificates for services

This feature alone makes Acuity meaningfully different from Calendly for service businesses. Calendly charges per session; Acuity supports pre-purchased packages natively.

### Subscriptions (Growing and Powerhouse)
Create recurring subscription products (e.g., "Monthly coaching retainer — 4 sessions/month, $299/month"). Clients subscribe and sessions auto-replenish each billing cycle.

### Multiple Staff Calendars
Each staff member gets their own calendar with individual availability. Clients can book with a specific staff member or the next available. This scales across Growing ($27/mo for up to 6 calendars) and Powerhouse ($49/mo for up to 36).

### Squarespace Integration
Acuity is a Squarespace product. If your website runs on Squarespace, Acuity embeds natively — no iframes or workarounds. This is seamless for Squarespace users.

## What Acuity Does Not Do Well

### No Free Plan
The 7-day trial converts to a paid plan or cancels. For solopreneurs testing the market or bootstrapped service businesses, this is a meaningful barrier. Calendly free and Cal.com free both work indefinitely.

### Dated Interface
Acuity's admin UI has not changed substantially in years. Compared to Calendly's clean, modern interface, Acuity feels functional but not polished. The client-facing booking page is fine; the back-end is cluttered.

### Limited Meeting/Team Use Cases
Acuity's routing and team collaboration features are weaker than Calendly's Teams plan. For sales teams, SDR routing, and revenue operations, Calendly or Cal.com Teams are stronger.

### No Meaningful CRM Integration
Acuity integrates with Mailchimp and some Zapier workflows, but native HubSpot, Salesforce, or Close integrations are limited. For teams embedding scheduling in a CRM-driven sales process, this is a gap.

## Verdict

**Acuity Scheduling is the right tool for service-based businesses** where the booking process includes intake, packages, and client relationships. For coaches, therapists, photographers, and fitness professionals, its forms and package features are meaningfully better than Calendly.

**The lack of a free plan is a real disadvantage** — it means you're paying $16/month minimum before you've proven product-market fit. Cal.com and Calendly both have free options that let you start without commitment.

**Rating: 4.1/5 — Strong for its niche. Not the right tool outside that niche.**
