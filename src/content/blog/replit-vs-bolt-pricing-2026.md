# Replit vs Bolt.new Pricing 2026: Credits vs Tokens, $20 vs $25

Replit and Bolt.new both promise the same thing: describe an app, watch the AI build it, deploy it from the browser. Their paid plans even land within $5 of each other — Replit Core at $20/month, Bolt Pro at $25/month. But the two price their AI completely differently. Replit meters in usage credits; Bolt meters in tokens. That difference, not the $5 gap, decides which one is cheaper for the way you build.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Replit | Bolt.new |
|---|--------|----------|
| **Free** | Starter — limited daily Agent credits, 1,200 min/mo dev time | Free — 1M tokens/mo, 300K daily cap, Bolt branding |
| **Entry paid** | Core — $20/mo, $20 usage credits included | Pro — $25/mo, 10M tokens, no daily cap |
| **Team tier** | Pro — $100/mo flat, up to 15 builders | Teams — $30/user/mo, 10M tokens/user |
| **Metering** | Usage credits (Agent steps, generation) | Tokens (every action consumes) |
| **Rollover** | Credits expire monthly (rollover only on Pro) | Token rollover on all paid plans |

## The Free Tiers

Both free plans exist to let you evaluate, not ship.

**Replit Starter** gives you limited daily Agent credits that reset every 24 hours, plus 1,200 minutes of monthly dev time. You can start a project with Agent, but the daily cap exhausts before you finish anything beyond a prototype.

**Bolt Free** hands you 1 million tokens per month with a 300,000 daily limit. That's enough for a few small projects, but every hosted site carries Bolt branding, uploads cap at 10 MB, and there are no custom domains. The 300K daily token ceiling is the real bottleneck — a complex iteration session can blow through it before you're done.

Verdict on free: Bolt's monthly token pool feels more generous for short bursts, but both throttle you daily. Neither is a production tool.

## Entry Paid: Core ($20) vs Pro ($25)

This is the comparison that matters for most solo builders.

**Replit Core — $20/month.** Replit dropped Core from $25 to $20 in late 2025, putting it level with [Cursor's Pro tier](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/). You get full Replit Agent access, $20 of usage credits included, and autonomous long-running builds — Agent can scaffold, install dependencies, fix errors, and deploy while you're away. When the $20 credit allowance runs out, Agent pauses until next cycle or you buy more credits. You can still code manually.

**Bolt Pro — $25/month.** Pro lifts the daily token cap entirely — you get 10 million tokens monthly to use however you like, burst or spread. Upload limit rises to 100 MB, and you unlock custom domains, SEO controls, private sharing, and the removal of Bolt branding. Token rollover means unused tokens carry forward.

The real difference: Replit's $20 credit allowance is consumed by AI interactions and can run dry mid-month, after which you pay overage or code by hand. Bolt's 10M tokens with rollover gives more predictable headroom for heavy generation sessions. If you iterate intensely, Bolt's no-daily-cap-plus-rollover model is friendlier; if your AI use is moderate and you value Replit's full hosting environment, Core's $20 edges it on price.

## Team Tiers: Where They Diverge

Here the two pricing philosophies split hard.

**Replit Pro — $100/month flat** covers up to 15 builders under one subscription. Per-user cost drops under $7/month, and Pro adds credit rollover. For a startup, bootcamp cohort, or small agency, the flat rate is dramatically cheaper than buying individual seats.

**Bolt Teams — $30/user/month** scales linearly: each seat gets its own 10M tokens. Five users cost $150/month vs Replit Pro's flat $100 for up to 15.

For teams, Replit's flat $100 is the clear value winner — unless your team needs the per-user token guarantees Bolt provides.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Solo builder, moderate AI use, want full hosting + IDE?** Replit Core at $20/mo.
- **Solo builder, heavy generation, want token rollover and no daily cap?** Bolt Pro at $25/mo.
- **Team of 3–15 builders?** Replit Pro at $100 flat beats Bolt Teams on cost decisively.
- **Just testing?** Try both free tiers — Bolt's monthly token pool for short experiments, Replit's Agent for full-stack prototypes.

## The Bottom Line

The $5 gap between Core and Pro is noise. The signal is the metering model: Replit's usage credits can run dry and reset, while Bolt's tokens roll over with no daily cap. For solo heavy-iterators, Bolt's model is more forgiving. For teams, Replit's flat $100-for-15 crushes Bolt's per-seat pricing. Match the metering to your build rhythm, not the headline price.
