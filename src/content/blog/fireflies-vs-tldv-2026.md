Both Fireflies.ai and tl;dv sit in the same category — AI meeting assistants that record, transcribe, and summarize your calls — but they serve different workflows. Fireflies leans into post-meeting intelligence with deep CRM connections and cross-meeting search. tl;dv focuses on making meetings immediately actionable with timestamps, clips, and a free tier that actually lets you use the product. Here's how they compare in 2026.

## Quick Comparison

| | Fireflies.ai | tl;dv |
|---|-------------|-------|
| **Core strength** | Search + CRM automation | Free tier + clips |
| **Platform support** | Zoom, Meet, Teams, Webex, + dialers | Zoom, Google Meet, Teams |
| **Free tier** | Limited (800 min storage) | Unlimited recordings |
| **AI summaries** | Yes (custom templates) | Yes (multi-meeting reports) |
| **CRM integration** | Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, Copper, Freshsales | HubSpot, Salesforce (Business plan) |
| **Languages** | 60+ | 30+ |
| **Starting price** | $18/seat/month | $25/user/month |

## Transcription Accuracy

Both tools produce solid transcripts, but they handle edge cases differently. Fireflies performs well with American and British English accents and handles multi-speaker identification reliably once you've trained it on a few calls. Its speaker labeling sticks across meetings, which matters for sales teams reviewing calls weeks later.

tl;dv's transcription is comparable in quality for its supported languages. Where it pulls ahead is the timestamping — you can click any moment in the transcript and jump straight to that point in the recording. For teams that review specific segments rather than full transcripts, this saves real time.

Neither tool handles heavy background noise or thick accents perfectly. If your calls regularly have poor audio quality, accuracy drops for both. For a broader look at how Fireflies stacks up against other transcription tools, see our [Fireflies.ai review](/blog/fireflies-ai-review-2026).

## Platform and Integration Support

This is where Fireflies has a clear edge. It connects to Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, GoTo Meeting, and several phone dialers. If your team uses different platforms across clients and internal calls, Fireflies covers more ground without asking you to think about it.

tl;dv supports Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams — the big three. For most teams, that's enough. But if you're on Webex or use a dialer-based sales workflow, tl;dv won't join those calls.

On the integration side, Fireflies connects to 40+ tools out of the box: Slack, Notion, Asana, Trello, and a deep bench of CRMs including Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho, Copper, and Freshsales. It can automatically push meeting notes, action items, and call metrics into your CRM after every call — no manual step needed.

tl;dv integrates with Slack, Notion, HubSpot, and Salesforce, but the CRM connections are gated behind the Business plan ($59/month). At the free and Pro tiers, you're mostly working within tl;dv's own interface. For more on what each plan includes, see our [tl;dv pricing breakdown](/blog/tldv-pricing-2026).

## AI Summaries and Search

Fireflies lets you build custom summary templates — you can define what sections the AI should extract (action items, decisions, questions raised, next steps) and apply them consistently across calls. Its "AskFred" feature lets you query your entire meeting history: "What did the client say about the Q3 timeline?" pulls relevant snippets from past calls. For teams running 20+ meetings a week, this searchability is genuinely useful.

tl;dv takes a different approach. Its multi-meeting reports aggregate insights across several calls, which is handy for product managers tracking feedback themes or sales leaders reviewing a week of demos at once. The AI summaries are clean and well-structured, though you get less control over their format compared to Fireflies' templates.

Both tools generate action items automatically. Fireflies tends to be more granular (listing specific follow-ups per person), while tl;dv keeps them concise.

## Free Tier Comparison

This is tl;dv's strongest card. Its free plan includes unlimited meeting recordings, AI summaries, and timestamps — you can genuinely use it as your daily meeting tool without paying. The limits show up in CRM integration, advanced analytics, and team admin features, but for individual users or small teams just getting started, the free tier is remarkably complete. Read the full [tl;dv review](/blog/tldv-review-2026) for more details on what's included.

Fireflies' free plan is more restrictive: 800 minutes of storage, limited transcription credits, and basic summaries. It's enough to evaluate the product, but most teams outgrow it within a month or two. Check the [Fireflies pricing page](/blog/fireflies-ai-pricing-2026) for the full plan breakdown.

## Pricing: Paid Plans

| Plan tier | Fireflies.ai | tl;dv |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Starter/Pro** | $18/seat/month | $25/user/month |
| **Business** | $29/seat/month | $59/user/month |
| **Enterprise** | $39/seat/month | Contact sales |

Fireflies is cheaper at every paid tier. Its Pro plan at $18/seat gives you unlimited transcription, custom AI summaries, and CRM integrations — features that tl;dv reserves for its $59 Business plan. For teams of 10+, that price gap adds up fast.

tl;dv's counterargument is its free tier. If you only need 2-3 seats on a paid plan and can keep the rest of your team on free, the total cost might be comparable. But if your whole team needs CRM sync, Fireflies is the more economical choice.

## Video Clips and Sharing

tl;dv makes it dead simple to clip a 30-second segment from a meeting and share it via link. Product teams use this to send user feedback clips to engineers; sales managers use it for coaching moments. The clip-and-share workflow is faster and more polished than anything Fireflies offers in this area.

Fireflies supports Soundbites (audio/video clips) and sharing, but the experience isn't as smooth. Its sharing strength is more in full transcripts and summaries pushed to other tools automatically, rather than quick clips for Slack or email.

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose Fireflies.ai if:**
- Your team runs a CRM-heavy workflow and needs automatic call logging
- You want to search across months of meeting history
- You need support for platforms beyond Zoom, Meet, and Teams
- Budget matters and you want more features per dollar on paid plans

**Choose tl;dv if:**
- You want a genuinely usable free tier for your team
- Video clips and shareable meeting moments are part of your workflow
- You primarily use Zoom, Google Meet, or Teams
- You need multi-meeting summary reports for stakeholder updates
