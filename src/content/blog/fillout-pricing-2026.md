# Fillout Pricing 2026: Free Plan, Paid Tiers, and What Each Gets You

Fillout has one of the most generous free plans in the form builder space. While competitors gate basic features behind paywalls, [Fillout](/tools/fillout/) gives you unlimited forms, unlimited seats, and 1,000 responses per month — for free.

But when does it make sense to upgrade? Here's the full pricing breakdown for 2026.

## Fillout Pricing at a Glance

| Plan | Monthly Price | Responses/mo | Forms | Seats |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|-------|
| Free | $0 | 1,000 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Starter | $15/mo | 2,000 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Pro | $40/mo | 5,000 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Business | $75/mo | Higher limits | Unlimited | Unlimited |

Every plan includes unlimited forms and unlimited team members. The main things that change as you move up are response limits, branding options, and access to advanced features.

## Free Plan — $0/month

Fillout's free tier is genuinely usable. This isn't a "free trial in disguise" situation — it's a real plan you can stay on indefinitely.

**What you get:**
- Unlimited forms
- Unlimited seats (invite your whole team)
- 1,000 responses per month
- Standard question types (text, multiple choice, dropdowns, ratings, etc.)
- Basic conditional logic
- Integrations with Google Sheets, Airtable, Notion
- Fillout branding on forms
- Basic analytics

**What you don't get:**
- Advanced question types (payment, scheduling, file uploads on some types)
- Custom themes and full branding control
- Priority support
- Ability to remove Fillout branding

**Who it's for:** Solo creators, small teams, anyone collecting surveys or feedback who doesn't need more than 1,000 responses a month. For context, that's about 33 responses per day — plenty for most small businesses.

**How it compares:** [Typeform](/tools/typeform/)'s free plan caps you at 10 responses per month. [Tally](/tools/tally/) also has a generous free tier. [Jotform](/tools/jotform/) gives you 100 submissions/month for free. Fillout's 1,000 responses put it at or near the top of free form tools.

## Starter Plan — $15/month

The first paid tier. A reasonable upgrade when you outgrow the free plan's response limit or need more professional-looking forms.

**What you get (on top of Free):**
- 2,000 responses per month
- All question types including payment collection, scheduling, and advanced inputs
- Custom themes and styling
- Custom thank-you pages
- Redirect on completion
- Basic webhooks

**What you still don't get:**
- Remove Fillout branding
- Priority support
- Advanced compliance features

**Who it's for:** Growing startups, freelancers with multiple client forms, or anyone who wants custom branding without the Fillout logo on their forms. Wait — you actually can't remove branding at this tier. If that matters, you need Pro.

**Is it worth upgrading from Free?** Only if you're consistently hitting the 1,000 response limit or you need the advanced question types (especially payment collection via Stripe). If you're under 1,000 responses and don't need payments, the free plan is enough.

## Pro Plan — $40/month

The tier where Fillout removes itself from your forms and you get full control over the experience.

**What you get (on top of Starter):**
- 5,000 responses per month
- Remove Fillout branding completely
- Custom domains
- Priority support
- Advanced analytics
- More integration options

**Who it's for:** Businesses using forms as a core part of their customer experience — lead capture, onboarding flows, checkout processes. If your forms are client-facing and brand matters, this is the tier.

**Is it worth upgrading from Starter?** The main reasons to jump to Pro are branding removal and the response limit increase. If you're between 2,000 and 5,000 responses per month and want clean, unbranded forms, Pro is the right move. The priority support is also valuable if forms are business-critical.

## Business Plan — $75/month

The top tier for teams with higher volume and compliance needs.

**What you get (on top of Pro):**
- Higher response limits
- Advanced security and compliance features
- Priority onboarding support
- Custom contract options (for larger organizations)

**Who it's for:** Mid-size businesses with high form volume, teams in regulated industries that need compliance documentation, or organizations that want dedicated support.

**Is it worth it?** For most teams, Pro covers everything you need. Business makes sense when you're consistently over 5,000 responses monthly or your organization requires specific compliance certifications.

Contact Fillout's sales team directly for discount details. These aren't self-serve — you'll need to reach out and verify eligibility.

## Which Plan Should You Pick?

**Stay on Free if:**
- You collect fewer than 1,000 responses per month
- You don't need payment collection or scheduling in forms
- Fillout branding on your forms doesn't bother you
- You're testing Fillout before committing

**Go Starter ($15/mo) if:**
- You need 1,001-2,000 responses monthly
- You want payment collection via Stripe
- You need custom themes but don't mind the Fillout badge

**Go Pro ($40/mo) if:**
- You need clean, unbranded forms
- Volume is between 2,000 and 5,000 responses
- Forms are a core part of your customer-facing experience
- You want priority support

**Go Business ($75/mo) if:**
- You consistently exceed 5,000 responses monthly
- Your organization has compliance or security requirements
- You want dedicated onboarding support

## The Verdict

Fillout's pricing structure is straightforward and unusually generous. The free plan alone handles what many competitors charge $25-50/month for. And unlike some tools that technically have a free plan but make it unusable, Fillout's free tier includes unlimited forms and unlimited seats — it's designed to be a real, long-term option.

For most small teams, the free plan is enough. When you outgrow it, the paid tiers are priced fairly without dramatic jumps. Start free, upgrade when you hit a real limit — not before.
