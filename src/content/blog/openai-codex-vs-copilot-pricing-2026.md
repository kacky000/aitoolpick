# OpenAI Codex vs GitHub Copilot Pricing 2026: $20 ChatGPT Agent vs $10 IDE Assistant

OpenAI Codex and GitHub Copilot both put AI into your coding workflow, but they price it in fundamentally different ways. Copilot is a flat-fee assistant — $10/month and you know your bill. Codex rides on your ChatGPT subscription and bills usage on top, so what you pay depends on how hard you push it. Here's how the two stack up in 2026.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | OpenAI Codex | GitHub Copilot |
|---|--------------|----------------|
| **Free** | Bundled with ChatGPT Free — $0, strict limits | Free — $0, 2,000 completions + 50 chats/mo |
| **Entry paid** | ChatGPT Plus — $20/mo | Pro — $10/mo, unlimited completions |
| **Mid tier** | — | Pro+ — $39/mo, more premium requests |
| **Heavy tier** | ChatGPT Pro — $200/mo | — |
| **Team tier** | Business / Enterprise (custom) | Business — $19/user/mo |
| **Billing model** | Subscription + token-based usage | Flat fee + AI credits included |

## How Codex Bills (The Catch)

Since the shift to token-based billing in April 2026, Codex isn't a standalone plan — it's bundled into ChatGPT. **ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo)** includes Codex with standard rate limits, enough for a few tasks a day. Push harder and you'll hit caps or rack up overage, pushing the effective cost to ~$20–40/month. **ChatGPT Pro ($200/mo)** removes most limits for full-time Codex users.

OpenAI also offers **Codex-only seats at $0/user/month** for organizations — but with no included credits, all usage is billed against workspace credits you buy separately. The headline "$0" hides a usage meter.

## How Copilot Bills (Predictable)

**GitHub Copilot Pro — $10/month** gives unlimited code completions plus a monthly allotment of premium requests, now measured as **AI Credits** (1 credit = $0.01, with $10 of credits included on Pro). Completions and Next Edit Suggestions stay unlimited and don't touch credits. The point: your floor is $10 and your completions never run out. Verified students and open-source maintainers get Pro free.

## Entry Tier: $20 Codex vs $10 Copilot

At the entry level Copilot is half the price — $10 vs $20 — and the cheaper plan also gives you a flatter, more predictable bill. So why pick Codex? Because the $20 ChatGPT Plus subscription isn't only buying a coding tool. You also get the full ChatGPT app, GPT-5-class models for everything else, and Codex's agentic task execution in the same plan. If you already pay for ChatGPT Plus, Codex is effectively free to start using.

Copilot, by contrast, is purpose-built to live inside VS Code, JetBrains, and Visual Studio. If you want AI completions in your existing editor and a bill that never surprises you, $10 Copilot is the lowest-friction option — much like [Cursor at $20](/blog/cursor-pricing-2026/) competes on the editor experience while Copilot competes on price and ubiquity.

## Where They Scale

For heavy individual use, Codex pushes you to **ChatGPT Pro ($200/mo)** to escape rate limits — the same tier ceiling you'd hit running [Claude Code on Max 20x ($200)](/blog/claude-code-pricing-2026/). Copilot's individual ceiling is **Pro+ ($39/mo)** for more premium requests; beyond that, heavy and team users move to **Business ($19/user/mo)** with policy controls and central management.

A 5-person team on Copilot Business pays ~$95/mo with a known total. A team on Codex pays per ChatGPT seat plus variable usage — cheaper if light, unpredictable if heavy.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **You already pay for ChatGPT Plus** → Codex is already included; start there for free.
- **You want a flat, predictable coding bill** → Copilot Pro ($10).
- **You code occasionally in VS Code/JetBrains** → Copilot Free or Pro ($10).
- **You run agentic tasks all day and want one subscription for chat + code** → ChatGPT Pro ($200) for Codex.
- **You manage a team and want predictable cost** → Copilot Business ($19/user).

The real divide: Copilot sells predictability for $10, Codex sells an all-in-one ChatGPT + agent bundle where your bill scales with usage. Light coders who want certainty pick Copilot; ChatGPT power users get Codex thrown in.
