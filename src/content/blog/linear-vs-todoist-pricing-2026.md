**[Linear](/tools/linear/)** and **[Todoist](/tools/todoist/)** both track work, but they aim at different jobs. Linear is a polished issue tracker built for software teams; Todoist is a fast personal task manager that stretches to small teams. On price they're closer than you'd expect — the real question is which model fits how you work.

## Pricing Overview

### Linear Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price (Annual) | Right For |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Individuals, small projects |
| Basic | $8/user/mo | Small teams |
| Business | $14/user/mo | Growing product teams |
| Enterprise | Custom | Large organizations |

### Todoist Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price (Annual) | Price (Monthly) |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| Free | $0 | $0 |
| Pro | $5/user/mo | $7/user/mo |
| Business | $8/user/mo | $10/user/mo |

The headline: **Todoist is cheaper at every tier.** Todoist Business ($8/user) matches Linear's *entry* Basic plan, and Todoist Pro ($5) is the cheapest paid option of all.

## The Real Difference: Issue Tracker vs Task Manager

**Linear** is purpose-built for software development: issues, sprints/cycles, roadmaps, Git integration, and a famously fast UI. Even its Basic plan assumes you're running engineering workflows. You pay for structure designed around shipping product.

**Todoist** is a focused task manager — projects, due dates, labels, filters. It's brilliant for personal productivity and lightweight team task lists, but it has no concept of sprints, cycles, or engineering roadmaps.

The honest framing: this isn't "cheaper vs pricier version of the same thing." It's *what kind of work are you tracking?* Code and product → Linear. Tasks and to-dos → Todoist.

## Cost Scenario: Solo vs 10-Person Team

**Solo user:**
- **Todoist Pro:** $5/month
- **Linear Free:** $0 (the free plan is genuinely usable solo)

For an individual, Linear's free tier or Todoist Pro both work — Linear free is fine for a solo developer, Todoist Pro for a power task-manager.

**Team of 10:**
- **Todoist Business:** 10 × $8 = **$80/month**
- **Linear Basic:** 10 × $8 = **$80/month**
- **Linear Business:** 10 × $14 = **$140/month**

At the entry tier the two cost **exactly the same ($80)** — so price isn't the deciding factor. Choose on fit: an engineering team gets far more from Linear Basic; a team that just needs shared task lists gets it cheaper and simpler from Todoist.

## Free Plan Comparison

- **Linear Free:** Generous for small teams — unlimited members on limited issues, core issue tracking, GitHub/GitLab integration.
- **Todoist Free:** 5 personal projects, recurring dates, 3 filters. Great solo, tight for teams.

Linear's free plan is unusually capable for a small dev team; Todoist's is best as a personal starting point.

## Verdict

**Choose Linear** if you're a software or product team — issues, cycles, and roadmaps are worth the price, and its free plan is strong for small teams.

**Choose Todoist** if you want an affordable, fast task manager for yourself or a non-engineering team. Pro at $5 is the cheapest serious option here.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Todoist cheaper than Linear?**
Todoist Pro ($5/user) is cheaper than any Linear paid plan, but Linear Basic and Todoist Business cost the same at $8/user/month.

**What's the difference between Linear and Todoist?**
Linear is an engineering issue tracker with sprints and roadmaps; Todoist is a personal/team task manager. They track different kinds of work.

**Which has the better free plan?**
For small dev teams, Linear's free plan is more capable. For solo personal use, Todoist Free is the better fit.

**Can Todoist replace Linear for a dev team?**
Not really — Todoist lacks sprints, cycles, and engineering roadmaps. It's a task list, not an issue tracker.
