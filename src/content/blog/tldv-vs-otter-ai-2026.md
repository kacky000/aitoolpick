But they solve different problems. tl;dv is built around video recordings, clips, and CRM sync. Otter.ai is built around real-time transcription and live collaboration. Here's how they compare in 2026 and which one fits your workflow better.

## tl;dv Overview

tl;dv started as a meeting recorder and evolved into a meeting intelligence platform. Its standout feature is the free tier — you get unlimited meeting recordings, transcripts, and AI summaries without paying anything. That alone makes it worth trying before committing to a paid tool.

The video-first approach matters. Every meeting is recorded with full video, and you can timestamp key moments during the call, then clip and share 30-second segments via link afterward. Product teams use this to send user feedback directly to engineers. Sales managers use it for coaching. The clip workflow is fast and polished.

On paid plans, tl;dv connects to HubSpot and Salesforce to automatically push meeting notes, action items, and deal-relevant insights into your CRM. The multi-meeting report feature aggregates themes across several calls — useful for product managers tracking feature requests or sales leaders reviewing a week of demos.

The tradeoff: tl;dv's CRM integrations are gated behind the Business plan, and its language support (30+) is narrower than some competitors. For a deeper look, read our [tl;dv review](/blog/tldv-review-2026).

## Otter.ai Overview

Otter.ai built its reputation on real-time transcription, and that's still where it shines. The live transcript appears as the meeting happens — fast, readable, and accurate enough to follow along without listening. If you've ever been in a meeting while multitasking, Otter makes that workable without missing key points.

OtterPilot is the AI agent that joins your calls automatically, captures the transcript, generates a summary, and extracts action items. It works across Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams. The summaries land in your Otter workspace immediately after the call ends, and you can share them with teammates or export to other tools.

Otter's collaboration features are where it differentiates. Team members can highlight, comment on, and react to specific parts of a transcript in real time. For teams that treat meeting notes as living documents rather than archives, this is genuinely useful.

The limitation: Otter's free tier caps at 300 minutes per month (roughly 7-8 hours of meetings), which many professionals hit within their first week. Its integrations beyond calendar sync are limited compared to tl;dv's CRM connections. See the full [Otter.ai review](/blog/otter-ai-review-2026) for details.

## Feature Comparison

### Transcription Accuracy

Both tools produce reliable transcripts for clear audio in English. Otter has a slight edge for real-time accuracy — the words appear faster and with fewer corrections needed as the speaker talks. tl;dv's transcripts are comparable in final quality but optimized more for post-meeting review than live reading.

Neither handles heavy background noise or strong accents perfectly. For non-English meetings, tl;dv supports 30+ languages while Otter primarily targets English with limited support for 6 other languages.

### AI Summaries

tl;dv generates structured summaries and lets you run multi-meeting reports — pulling themes across 5, 10, or 20 calls into a single digest. This is valuable for spotting patterns (recurring objections in sales calls, repeated feature requests in user interviews).

Otter's OtterPilot summaries are clean and immediate, with action items extracted automatically. The summaries are good for individual meetings but lack the cross-meeting aggregation that tl;dv offers.

### Integrations

tl;dv connects to Slack, Notion, HubSpot, Salesforce, and several project management tools. The CRM integrations (Business plan) automatically log meeting data to deal records — a significant time saver for sales teams.

Otter integrates with Google Calendar, Zoom, and Slack. Its integration depth is shallower. If CRM automation matters to your team, tl;dv wins this category clearly.

### Video and Clips

tl;dv records full video and makes it easy to clip, timestamp, and share specific moments. Otter focuses on audio transcription — video recording is available but the clip-and-share experience is not as refined.

## Who Should Choose Which

**Choose tl;dv if:**
- You want a free tier that doesn't cap your meeting minutes
- Video clips and shareable highlights are part of your workflow
- Your team uses a CRM (HubSpot or Salesforce) and wants automatic call logging
- You need transcription in non-English languages
- You review meetings asynchronously rather than live

**Choose Otter.ai if:**
- Real-time transcription quality is your top priority
- You want the cheapest paid plan for a full team rollout
- Your team collaborates inside meeting notes (highlights, comments, reactions)
- Most of your meetings are in English
- You prefer a simple tool focused on transcription over a full meeting intelligence platform

## Verdict

tl;dv gives you more for free and more depth at the high end. Its unlimited free recordings, video clips, and CRM integrations make it the stronger choice for sales teams, product teams, and anyone who reviews meetings after the fact. The generous free tier means you can evaluate it thoroughly before spending anything.

Otter.ai is the better pick for teams that prioritize the live experience — following along in real time, collaborating in the transcript as it's written, and getting clean summaries fast. Its paid plans are cheaper per seat, which matters when you're rolling it out across a large team that primarily works in English.

Neither tool is a bad choice. The decision comes down to whether you get more value from live transcription (Otter) or post-meeting intelligence (tl;dv).
