**Ticktick Free** and **Paid** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

TickTick is one of the most popular task management apps, and for good reason — it works beautifully across every platform. But the question many users face is: **is the free plan enough, or do you need Premium?**

After using both versions extensively, here's an honest breakdown.

## TickTick Free Plan: What You Get

The free plan is genuinely useful and more generous than competitors like Todoist's free tier. Here's what's included:

- **Up to 9 lists** (enough for most personal users)
- **Up to 99 tasks per list**
- Basic recurring tasks (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Cross-platform sync (iOS, Android, Web, Mac, Windows)
- Basic calendar view
- Collaboration on up to 2 shared projects with 2 collaborators each
- Basic widgets and integrations

For someone managing personal to-dos, grocery lists, or a light project, the free tier covers the essentials well.

## TickTick Premium: What You Unlock

TickTick Premium costs **$35.99/year** (about $3/month) — one of the most affordable premium tiers among major task apps.

Premium adds:

| Feature | Free | Premium |
|---------|------|---------|
| Lists | 9 | Unlimited |
| Tasks per list | 99 | Unlimited |
| Calendar view | Basic | Full (subscribe to external calendars) |
| Custom filters | ❌ | ✅ (save complex queries) |
| Habit tracking | Basic | Unlimited habits + heatmap |
| Tags | Basic | Unlimited tags |
| Task duration & timeline | ❌ | ✅ |

| File attachments | 5 MB/file | 100 MB/file |

| Reminder options | Basic | Location-based, time-range reminders |
| Pomo timer | Basic | Statistics + focus reports |
| Kanban view | ❌ | ✅ |

## The Features That Actually Matter

**Custom Filters** are the biggest premium differentiator. Being able to save queries like "high priority + due this week + tagged #work" transforms how you use TickTick. Power users who manage multiple projects will feel this limitation hard on the free plan.

**Calendar integration** — Premium lets you subscribe to Google Calendar, Outlook, or iCal inside TickTick. For people who live in their calendar, this alone justifies the upgrade.

**Habit tracking with heatmaps** is genuinely best-in-class for a task app. If you're working on building streaks (exercise, reading, writing), the visual heatmap is highly motivating.

**Kanban view** surprises many users — TickTick isn't just a list app. The premium Kanban is perfect for small project management without switching to a heavier tool.

## Who Should Upgrade?

**Upgrade to Premium if you:**
- Have more than 9 active lists
- Want to combine your calendar and tasks in one app
- Use habit tracking seriously
- Need saved filters for complex task views
- Collaborate on 3+ shared projects
- Want Kanban boards for project management

**Stay on Free if you:**
- Manage fewer than 9 lists of personal tasks
- Don't need calendar integration
- Use TickTick only for simple daily to-dos
- Are already paying for another productivity app

## Verdict

**TickTick Premium is worth it** — but mainly for power users. At $3/month, the cost is trivially small, and the feature unlock is substantial. The custom filters, unlimited lists, and calendar sync alone justify the upgrade for anyone using TickTick as their main productivity system.

If you're on the fence, start with the free plan. You'll know within a week whether you're hitting the limits.
