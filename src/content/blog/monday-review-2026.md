Monday.com has positioned itself as more than just a project management tool — it calls itself a "Work OS." But does it live up to the hype in 2026? We tested Monday.com extensively to give you an honest, detailed review.

## What Is Monday.com?

Monday.com is a visual work management platform that helps teams plan, track, and deliver work across projects, workflows, and everyday tasks. Founded in 2012 (originally as dapulse), it has grown into one of the most popular PM tools with millions of users worldwide.

What sets Monday.com apart is its visual, colorful interface and its flexibility. The platform includes dedicated products for project management, CRM, development, and marketing — all built on the same core platform.

## Key Features

### Boards and Views
Monday.com's boards are the foundation of everything. Each board represents a project or workflow, with items (rows) representing tasks. You can view your boards in multiple ways:

- **Main Table**: The default spreadsheet-like view
- **Kanban**: Drag-and-drop cards grouped by status
- **Timeline/Gantt**: Visualize task schedules and dependencies
- **Calendar**: See deadlines at a glance
- **Chart**: Built-in reporting and analytics
- **Map**: Geo-based views for location data
- **Workload**: See who's overloaded or underutilized

### Automations
Monday.com's automation builder is one of the best in the industry. Using simple "when-then" logic, you can automate:

- Status changes and notifications
- Date-based reminders
- Item creation and assignment
- Cross-board updates
- External integrations (Slack, email, etc.)

The free plan includes basic automations, with higher tiers offering more actions per month. The automation recipes are pre-built and customizable, making them accessible to non-technical users.

### Monday CRM
The built-in CRM is a standout feature. Sales teams can manage leads, deals, and customer communications without a separate tool like HubSpot or Salesforce. It includes:

- Deal stages with pipeline views
- Contact management
- Email tracking and templates
- Activity logging
- Revenue forecasting

### Dashboards
Create visual dashboards pulling data from multiple boards. Widgets include charts, numbers, timelines, and battery gauges that update in real-time. These dashboards are excellent for executive reporting.

## Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Price (per seat/mo) | Key Features |
|------|-------------------|-------------|
| Free | $0 (up to 2 seats) | 3 boards, basic views, 200+ templates |
| Basic | $9 | Unlimited boards, 5GB storage, priority support |
| Standard | $12 | Timeline, Gantt, calendar, automations (250/mo) |
| Pro | $19 | Time tracking, formula column, chart view, 25K automations |
| Enterprise | Custom | Advanced security, audit log, SCIM, tailored onboarding |

**Important note**: Monday.com requires a minimum of 3 seats on paid plans, making the true starting price $27/month (3 x $9) for the Basic plan.

## Pros

- **Visually stunning interface**: The color-coded boards make project tracking intuitive and even enjoyable
- **Excellent automations**: One of the most powerful automation builders available
- **Built-in CRM**: No need for a separate sales tool
- **Flexible board structure**: Adapts to almost any workflow or industry
- **Strong templates**: 200+ templates cover everything from marketing campaigns to software sprints
- **Great customer support**: Responsive and helpful, with extensive documentation

## Cons

- **Expensive at scale**: The 3-seat minimum and per-seat pricing adds up quickly
- **Very limited free plan**: Only 2 seats and 3 boards — barely enough to evaluate the platform
- **Can become cluttered**: With many boards and automations, the workspace can feel overwhelming
- **Steep learning curve for advanced features**: Formula columns and complex automations require time to master
- **Storage limits on lower plans**: 5GB on Basic feels tight for teams sharing files

## Who Is Monday.com Best For?

Monday.com works best for:
- **Sales teams** that want PM + CRM in one platform
- **Marketing teams** managing campaigns with multiple stakeholders
- **Operations teams** that need visual workflow management
- **Mid-to-large companies** (10-500+ employees) with budget for paid plans
- **Teams that value aesthetics** and visual project tracking

It's less ideal for solo users (due to the 3-seat minimum), budget-conscious startups, or software development teams (where [Linear](/blog/linear-review-2026/) is a better fit).

## The Verdict

Monday.com is a powerful, visually impressive work management platform that genuinely delivers on its "Work OS" promise. The combination of project management, CRM, automations, and dashboards makes it a compelling choice for teams that want a centralized platform.

The main drawbacks are pricing (the 3-seat minimum and higher per-seat costs vs competitors like ClickUp) and the limited free plan. If budget isn't a primary concern and you value visual, intuitive project management, Monday.com is one of the best options available in 2026.
