[Loom](/tools/loom/) changed how teams communicate with async video. But in 2026, the free plan caps you at 25 videos and 5 minutes per recording — and the Business plan costs $15/user/month. If those limits feel tight, you have solid options.

I tested the main contenders across recording quality, sharing workflow, editing features, and price. Here are the seven worth considering.

## Why People Leave Loom

Loom's core product is still good. The reasons people look elsewhere usually come down to three things:

- **Free plan got worse.** The 25-video library cap and 5-minute limit weren't always there. Older users remember unlimited recordings.
- **$15/user/month adds up.** A 10-person team pays $150/month just for screen recordings. That's $1,800/year.
- **Limited editing.** Loom lets you trim and add chapters, but anything beyond that requires exporting to another tool.

If any of those hit home, one of these alternatives will probably fit better.

## 1. Vidyard — Best for Sales Teams

**Price:** Free (25 videos) / Pro $59/month / Business custom pricing

[Vidyard](/tools/vidyard/) started as a video analytics platform for sales teams and it shows. Every feature is built around one question: did the prospect watch your video?

You get real-time notifications when someone opens your video, heatmaps showing exactly which parts they watched, and native integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and Outreach. The CRM sync alone saves sales reps from manually logging video touchpoints.

The free plan mirrors Loom's 25-video limit, but Pro at $59/month includes unlimited videos, custom branding, and the full analytics suite. If you send more than a handful of prospecting videos per week, the ROI math works out quickly.

**Best for:** Sales reps and account executives who need engagement tracking.
**Skip if:** You just want quick internal updates — Vidyard's sales focus means features you won't use.

## 2. Riverside — Best for High-Quality Recording

**Price:** Free (2 hours) / Standard $15/month / Pro $24/month / Business $35/month

[Riverside](/tools/riverside/) records each participant locally and syncs the tracks afterward. That means studio-quality audio and up to 4K video, even on shaky internet connections. It's the same approach podcast studios use — and it makes a noticeable difference.

The Standard plan at $15/month gives you unlimited recordings, separate audio/video tracks, and AI transcriptions. The Pro tier adds custom branding and live streaming.

Riverside isn't trying to replace Loom for quick Slack-style updates. It's built for content that needs to look and sound polished — podcast episodes, client interviews, tutorial series, and product demos you'll reuse.

**Best for:** Podcasters, content creators, and teams producing external-facing video.
**Skip if:** You record 2-minute status updates — Riverside is overkill for internal comms.

---

## 3. Descript — Best for Recording + Editing in One Tool

**Price:** Free / Hobbyist $24/month / Business $33/month

[Descript](/tools/descript/) treats video like a Google Doc. Record your screen, and Descript generates a transcript. Edit the transcript — delete a sentence, rearrange paragraphs — and the video changes to match. It sounds gimmicky until you try it, then you can't go back.

The free plan includes one watermark-free export per month and basic screen recording. Hobbyist at $24/month removes watermarks and adds AI features like filler word removal and Studio Sound (which cleans up audio recorded on laptop mics). Business at $33/month unlocks team features.

If you regularly find yourself recording in Loom, downloading the file, and then editing in a separate tool, Descript eliminates that entire workflow. Check our [Descript pricing](/blog/descript-pricing-2026) guide for the full tier comparison.

**Best for:** Anyone who edits their recordings before sharing.
**Skip if:** You prefer raw, unedited videos and want the fastest possible share-a-link workflow.

## 4. Tango — Best for Step-by-Step Documentation

**Price:** Free (25 guides) / Pro $16/month

Tango takes a different approach entirely. Instead of recording video, it captures your clicks and keystrokes as you walk through a process, then auto-generates a step-by-step guide with annotated screenshots.

The output looks like a polished how-to document — numbered steps, highlighted UI elements, and optional text descriptions. It's purpose-built for SOPs, onboarding docs, and process documentation.

The free plan includes 25 guides. Pro at $16/month gives you unlimited guides, custom branding, and the ability to blur sensitive data automatically.

**Best for:** Operations teams writing SOPs, trainers building onboarding materials.
**Skip if:** You actually need video — Tango produces documents, not recordings.

---

## 5. Screen Studio — Best for Beautiful Demo Videos (Mac Only)

**Price:** $89 one-time purchase

Screen Studio is a Mac-only screen recorder that automatically adds smooth zoom effects, cursor highlighting, and motion blur. The result looks like those polished product demo videos from major SaaS companies — except you didn't spend hours in After Effects.

There's no subscription. You pay $89 once and own it. No cloud hosting, no sharing links — you export an MP4 and host it wherever you want.

The trade-off is clear: no built-in sharing workflow, no viewer analytics, no team features. But if your goal is producing good-looking product demos or tutorial content, Screen Studio delivers output quality that Loom simply can't match.

**Best for:** Indie developers, product marketers, and anyone making demo videos for websites.
**Skip if:** You need instant share links or work on Windows/Linux.

---

## 6. OBS Studio — Best Free Option (No Limits)

**Price:** Free and open-source

OBS Studio is the nuclear option. It records and streams with zero restrictions — no time limits, no watermarks, no video caps, no account required. Professional streamers and YouTubers have relied on it for years.

The catch is complexity. OBS wasn't designed for quick screen recordings. You configure scenes, sources, and encoding settings. The learning curve is real, and there's no built-in sharing or editing.

But if you need a completely free recorder with full control over quality settings, resolution, frame rate, and audio sources, nothing else comes close. Pair it with a free hosting service and you've replaced Loom at zero cost.

**Best for:** Technical users who want full control and don't mind configuration.
**Skip if:** You want a record-and-share workflow that takes under 30 seconds.

---

## 7. Cap — Best New Open-Source Alternative

**Price:** Free and open-source

Cap is the newest entry on this list — an open-source screen recorder built specifically as a Loom alternative. The interface is minimal: hit record, stop, get a shareable link. That's it.

Because it's open-source, there are no artificial limits on recordings or library size. The project is actively developed and adding features regularly, though it's still less mature than the other options here.

If you like Loom's simplicity but object to the pricing model, Cap is worth watching. It won't match Loom's polish today, but the gap is closing.

**Best for:** Developers and teams who prefer open-source tools.
**Skip if:** You need enterprise features, SSO, or guaranteed uptime.

## Which One Should You Pick?

The right choice depends on what you actually do with your recordings:

- **Replacing Loom for async team updates:** Vidyard or Cap
- **Sales prospecting with tracking:** Vidyard
- **Podcast or interview recording:** Riverside
- **Recording + editing in one tool:** Descript
- **Writing process documentation:** Tango
- **Polished product demos:** Screen Studio
- **Maximum flexibility, zero cost:** OBS Studio

If you're still on [Loom's free plan](/blog/loom-free-vs-paid-2026) and hitting the limits, start with Cap or OBS for a zero-cost switch. If you're paying for Loom Business and want more editing power, Descript at $24/month is the strongest upgrade.
