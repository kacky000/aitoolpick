# Folk CRM Review 2026: A Relationship-First CRM for Modern Teams

There's a category of CRM that doesn't try to be Salesforce. Folk — built by a team in Paris — falls squarely in that camp. It's designed around a simple idea: your relationships are your pipeline, and managing them shouldn't feel like operating an ERP system. After testing it across prospecting, deal tracking, and outreach workflows, here's what actually holds up.

## What Is Folk?

Folk is a lightweight CRM built for teams that live in their inbox and on LinkedIn. The core pitch is speed: import contacts from anywhere, organize them with tags and custom fields, and run email campaigns — all without the onboarding overhead of traditional CRMs.

The killer feature is **folkX**, a Chrome extension that lets you pull contact data directly from LinkedIn profiles, company pages, and even LinkedIn Sales Navigator results into your CRM with a single click. If your sales process starts with LinkedIn prospecting, this alone might justify the subscription.

Folk also includes **Magic Fields**, an AI feature that auto-fills contact details like company size, industry, or location based on available data. It's useful, but limited by the 500 enrichment credits per workspace per month — a constraint that bites fast for active prospecting teams.

## Pricing

No free plan. Folk offers a 14-day trial, then you pick a tier.

| Plan | Price (annual) | Key Limits |
|------|---------------|------------|
| Standard | $24/user/mo | 2,000 emails/mo, folkX, pipeline management |
| Premium | $48/user/mo | Email sequences, deal tracking, dashboards, API |
| Custom | $80+/user/mo | Dedicated support, custom onboarding, SLAs |

The Standard plan covers the basics — pipeline views, email sending, calendar sync, and the folkX extension. But if you want email sequences (multi-step drip campaigns) or the API for custom integrations, you'll need Premium.

Compared to [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) (free CRM tier available) or [Attio](/tools/attio/) (free for up to 3 users), Folk's lack of a free plan is a notable gap. You're paying from day one. The 14-day trial gives you enough time to evaluate, but not enough to run a full sales cycle through it.

## Pros

- **LinkedIn integration is best-in-class.** folkX makes importing contacts from LinkedIn effortless. One click on a profile, and the contact (with company, title, and email) lands in your CRM. No copy-pasting, no CSV exports.
- **Clean, fast UI.** Folk feels like a well-designed productivity app, not enterprise software. Everything loads quickly, the layout makes sense immediately, and you won't need a training session to get started.
- **5,000+ integrations via Zapier and Make.** While native integrations are limited, the Zapier/Make ecosystem fills most gaps — Slack notifications, Google Sheets sync, webhook triggers, etc.
- **Email campaigns built in.** You can send personalized email campaigns directly from Folk without needing Mailchimp or a separate outreach tool. Mail merge with custom variables works well for sequences under 500 recipients.
- **Group contacts by anything.** Tags, lists, and custom views let you slice your contact database flexibly — by deal stage, event, campaign, or whatever taxonomy your team uses.

## Cons

- **No free plan.** Every competitor in this space — HubSpot, Attio, even Streak — offers some kind of free tier. Folk doesn't, which raises the bar for evaluation.
- **Enrichment credits are tight.** 500 credits per month across your entire workspace. A team of 5 prospecting actively will burn through that in the first week. Additional credits cost extra.
- **Feature set still limited.** No built-in reporting beyond basic dashboards (Premium only). No phone/call logging. No advanced automation builder. If you need workflow complexity, you'll outgrow Folk.
- **Email sending limits.** 2,000 emails per month on Standard sounds reasonable until you run a campaign to 800 contacts with a 3-email sequence. Premium raises the cap, but it's still modest compared to dedicated outreach tools.
- **Sequence capabilities are basic.** Email sequences exist on Premium, but lack the conditional branching and A/B testing you'd find in tools like Instantly or Lemlist.

## Rating

**4.0 / 5**

Folk does the relationship management part well, but feature limitations and the absence of a free plan hold it back from a higher score.

## Who Is Folk For?

Folk works best for:

- **Agencies and consultancies** that manage client relationships across multiple projects and need fast contact import from LinkedIn.
- **Small sales teams (2-15 people)** running outbound prospecting where LinkedIn is the primary channel.
- **Recruiters** who live on LinkedIn and need a CRM that mirrors their actual workflow.
- **Founders doing their own sales** who want something lighter than [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) but more structured than a spreadsheet.

It's probably not right for teams that need heavy automation, detailed revenue reporting, complex deal stages, or built-in calling. If your workflow is more data-driven than relationship-driven, consider [Attio](/tools/attio/) or HubSpot instead.

## Verdict

Folk carved out a smart niche: a CRM for people who build relationships on LinkedIn and over email, not through inbound funnels or cold calling. The folkX extension is genuinely the best LinkedIn-to-CRM bridge available, and the overall product feels polished and fast.

The downsides are real, though. No free plan means you're committing $24/user/mo before you've fully tested it. Enrichment limits are stingy. And if your needs grow beyond basic pipeline management and email outreach, you'll hit Folk's ceiling relatively quickly.

For small teams whose sales motion starts with LinkedIn, Folk is a strong pick. For everyone else, [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) (broader features, free tier) or [Attio](/tools/attio/) (more flexible data model, AI-native) will likely serve you better long-term.
