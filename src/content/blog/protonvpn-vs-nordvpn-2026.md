| Plan | Proton VPN Plus | NordVPN Basic |

| Monthly | $9.99 | $12.99 |
| 1-Year | $3.99/mo | $4.59/mo |

Proton VPN is consistently cheaper at every billing term — $0.50/month less on the 2-year plan. And Proton VPN's free tier gives it a massive advantage for users who want to test before paying.

However, NordVPN's Plus plan ($4.49/mo for 2yr) bundles a password manager and dark web monitoring. Proton's equivalent bundle (Proton Unlimited at $7.99/mo) includes email, calendar, and cloud storage — more comprehensive but pricier.

## Privacy & Security

### Proton VPN — The Privacy Champion
- **Swiss jurisdiction** — outside all surveillance alliances
- **Open-source** all apps — anyone can audit the code
- **Secure Core** — traffic routes through privacy-friendly countries before exiting
- **No-logs** — audited by Securitum
- **Tor over VPN** — built-in access to .onion sites

### NordVPN — Strong but Less Transparent
- **Panama jurisdiction** — no data retention laws
- **Closed-source** apps — you can't inspect the code
- **Double VPN** — routes through 2 servers (similar concept to Secure Core)
- **No-logs** — audited by Deloitte (3 separate audits)
- **Threat Protection Pro** — blocks malware, trackers, and phishing sites

**Winner: Proton VPN.** Swiss jurisdiction is objectively stronger than Panama for privacy, and open-source transparency means you don't have to *trust* their claims — you can verify them. NordVPN's triple audit is impressive, but Proton's structural advantage is hard to match.

## Speed

In real-world testing:

| Test | Proton VPN Plus | NordVPN Basic |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| Nearby server | 450-600 Mbps | 550-700 Mbps |
| US (from Asia) | 150-250 Mbps | 200-350 Mbps |
| Latency impact | +15-25ms | +10-15ms |

**Winner: NordVPN.** It's consistently 10-20% faster, with lower latency — especially on long-distance connections. NordVPN's proprietary NordLynx protocol (built on WireGuard) is optimized for speed in ways Proton VPN's standard WireGuard implementation doesn't match.

For most users, both are fast enough for 4K streaming and gaming. The speed difference only matters if you're on a slower connection or need maximum throughput.

## Features Comparison

### Proton VPN Exclusive Features
- **Free plan** with no data limits
- **Secure Core** multi-hop through privacy countries
- **Open-source apps**
- **Tor over VPN**
- **Port forwarding** for P2P

### NordVPN Exclusive Features
- **Threat Protection Pro** (works even with VPN off)
- **Dark Web Monitor**
- **Meshnet** — create private networks between devices
- **Dedicated IP** option (add-on)
- **Smart DNS** for streaming on unsupported devices

NordVPN has more *convenience* features. Proton VPN has more *privacy* features. The choice reflects your priorities.

NordVPN's Smart DNS feature gives it an edge for streaming on devices that can't run VPN apps (gaming consoles, smart TVs without app support). If streaming is a primary use case, NordVPN is more convenient.

## Our Recommendation

**Choose Proton VPN if:**
- Privacy is your top priority, not just a checkbox
- You want to verify claims through open-source code
- You need a free VPN with no data limits
- You're already using Proton Mail, Drive, or Calendar
- You're a journalist, activist, or in a high-risk situation

**Choose NordVPN if:**
- You want the fastest possible speeds
- You need Smart DNS for streaming on all devices
- You want bundled threat protection that works without VPN
- You need a dedicated IP address
- You prefer more server locations and convenience features

Both are top-tier VPNs. You won't go wrong with either. The decision comes down to whether you prioritize *privacy transparency* (Proton) or *speed and features* (NordVPN).
