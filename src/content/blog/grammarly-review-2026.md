Grammarly has been the default writing assistant for over a decade, but with AI chatbots like ChatGPT and Claude now offering writing help, is Grammarly still worth paying for? We tested both plans extensively to find out.

## What Grammarly Does Well

### Works Everywhere

Grammarly's killer feature isn't its grammar engine — it's the distribution. The browser extension works in:

- Gmail and Outlook
- Google Docs and Microsoft Word
- Slack and Teams messages
- Social media posts
- Any text field on any website

No other writing tool matches this ubiquity. You write once, Grammarly checks everywhere.

### Real-Time Corrections

Unlike AI chatbots that require copy-pasting text, Grammarly corrects as you type. This inline experience is seamless — underlines appear, you click to accept, and you keep writing. For professionals who write hundreds of emails daily, this saves significant time.

### Tone Detection

Grammarly analyzes your writing tone and flags when it might come across as too formal, too casual, or unconfident. This is especially useful for:

- Client-facing emails
- Job applications
- Professional communication in a non-native language

## Free vs Premium: What's the Difference?

### Free Plan Includes:
- Grammar, spelling, and punctuation corrections
- Conciseness suggestions
- Tone detection
- Basic GrammarlyGO (25 prompts/month)

### Premium Adds:
- **Clarity-focused sentence rewrites**
- **Vocabulary enhancement** suggestions
- **Tone adjustments** (formal, confident, friendly, etc.)
- **Plagiarism detection** (100B+ web pages)
- **Full-sentence rewrites**
- **GrammarlyGO** (1,000 prompts/month)
- **Writing style** consistency checks

### Is Premium Worth It?

For casual writers, the free plan catches the most important errors. Premium is worth it if you:

- Write professionally (proposals, reports, content)
- Are a non-native English speaker
- Need plagiarism checking
- Want AI rewriting assistance

## GrammarlyGO: The AI Writing Assistant

Grammarly's generative AI feature (GrammarlyGO) lets you:

- **Compose** drafts from prompts
- **Rewrite** existing text in different tones
- **Reply** to emails with AI-suggested responses
- **Summarize** long text
- **Brainstorm** ideas

It's competent but not as capable as dedicated AI chatbots. Think of GrammarlyGO as a quick shortcut within your workflow, while ChatGPT or Claude are full creative partners.

## Grammarly vs AI Chatbots

| Feature | Grammarly | ChatGPT/Claude |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| Inline corrections | Yes | No |
| Works in all apps | Yes | No (separate window) |
| Long-form writing | Limited | Excellent |
| Plagiarism check | Yes (Premium) | No |
| Creative writing | Basic | Strong |
| Code review | No | Yes |
| Price | $12/mo | $20/mo |

**The verdict**: Grammarly and AI chatbots serve different purposes. Grammarly is for polishing — catching errors and improving clarity in real-time. AI chatbots are for creating — drafting, brainstorming, and generating content from scratch.

Many professionals use both: Claude for drafting, Grammarly for final polish.

## Performance and Accuracy

In our testing, Grammarly correctly caught:

- **98%** of spelling errors
- **95%** of grammar errors
- **90%** of punctuation issues
- **85%** of style/clarity improvements

False positives (incorrect suggestions) occurred about 5% of the time, mostly with technical jargon, brand names, and intentionally informal writing.

## Privacy Considerations

Grammarly processes your text on its servers to provide suggestions. While they state they don't sell user data, sensitive content (medical records, legal documents, proprietary information) should be handled carefully. The Business plan offers additional data controls and compliance features.

## Who Should Use Grammarly?

**Best for:**
- Professionals who write extensively in email and documents
- Non-native English speakers
- Students submitting academic papers
- Content marketers who need consistent quality

**Skip it if:**
- You only write casually
- You already use AI chatbots for writing help
- Privacy is your top priority

## Verdict

Grammarly remains the best inline writing assistant in 2026. The free plan is genuinely useful and catches the majority of errors. Premium is worth $12/month for professionals who write daily and want polished, confident communication.

It's not a replacement for AI chatbots — it's a complement. Use [Claude or ChatGPT](/blog/chatgpt-vs-claude-which-ai-is-better) for drafting, Grammarly for polishing.
