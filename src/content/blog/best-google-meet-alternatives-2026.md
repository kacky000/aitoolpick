Google Meet is a solid video conferencing tool, especially if your team already lives inside Google Workspace. But it is not the right fit for everyone. Maybe you need longer meetings on a free plan, better breakout rooms, or tighter integration with a non-Google stack. Whatever the reason, these seven Google Meet alternatives deserve a serious look in 2026.

For a deeper dive into what Google Meet itself offers, check our [Google Meet review](/blog/google-meet-review-2026/).

## What Makes Google Meet Worth Replacing?

Google Meet works well for quick calls and integrates seamlessly with Gmail and Google Calendar. But it has real limitations:

- **Free plan caps at 60 minutes** — fine for a standup, frustrating for a workshop
- **Limited breakout room controls** — basic compared to [Zoom](/tools/zoom/) or Webex
- **No persistent chat or channels** — the meeting ends and context disappears
- **Ecosystem lock-in** — if you don't use Google Workspace, Meet adds friction instead of reducing it
- **Recording only on paid plans** — free users cannot record meetings at all

If any of these matter to your team, the alternatives below address them directly.

## 1. Zoom — Best Overall Alternative

**Pricing:** Free (40 min, 100 participants) | Pro $13.33/user/mo | Business $18.33/user/mo

Zoom remains the default video conferencing tool for a reason. It handles everything from one-on-one calls to 1,000-person webinars without breaking a sweat. Audio and video quality are consistently better than Meet, especially on unstable connections.

**Why pick Zoom over Google Meet:**
- **AI Companion (free)** — automatic meeting summaries, action items, and smart chapters for recordings
- **Superior breakout rooms** — pre-assign, set timers, broadcast messages, and let participants self-select
- **Zoom Apps marketplace** — run polls, whiteboards, and third-party apps inside meetings
- **Zoom Phone** — add VoIP calling without a separate provider
- **Cross-platform reliability** — desktop apps for Windows, Mac, and Linux are polished and fast

**Where Meet still wins:** If your company runs on Google Workspace, Meet is already in your Gmail sidebar — zero friction. Zoom requires a separate app and account.

**Best for:** Teams that need reliable, feature-rich video conferencing regardless of their productivity suite.

For a head-to-head comparison with Microsoft's offering, see [Microsoft Teams vs Zoom 2026](/blog/microsoft-teams-vs-zoom-2026/).

## 2. Microsoft Teams — Best for Microsoft 365 Organizations

**Pricing:** Free (60 min, 100 participants) | Essentials $4/user/mo | Included with Microsoft 365

Teams is not just a video calling app. It combines persistent chat, file sharing, collaborative documents, and video meetings into one workspace. If your company already pays for Microsoft 365, Teams is included at no extra cost — making it the most cost-effective Meet alternative for many organizations.

**Why pick Teams over Google Meet:**
- **Persistent channels** — conversations continue before and after meetings, with threaded replies and file sharing
- **Copilot AI** — meeting recaps, suggested action items, and intelligent search across chat history
- **Deep Office integration** — co-edit Word, Excel, and PowerPoint files without leaving Teams
- **Together Mode** — places participants in a shared virtual space, reducing "gallery view" fatigue
- **Larger free plan** — 60-minute meetings with up to 100 people, plus 5GB cloud storage

**Where Meet still wins:** Simpler interface. Teams can feel overwhelming with its channels, tabs, and nested menus.

**Best for:** Organizations on Microsoft 365 that want meetings, chat, and file collaboration in one place.

## 3. Slack Huddles — Best for Quick, Informal Calls

**Pricing:** Free (1:1 huddles) | Pro $8.75/user/mo | Business+ $12.50/user/mo

Slack Huddles are the opposite of a scheduled meeting. Click a button in any Slack channel and start talking. No calendar invite, no meeting link, no waiting room. It is the digital equivalent of tapping someone on the shoulder.

**Why pick Slack Huddles over Google Meet:**
- **Zero scheduling friction** — start a huddle from any channel or DM [instantly](/tools/instantly/)
- **Screen sharing with drawing** — annotate shared screens in real time
- **Threaded context** — the huddle happens inside a Slack channel where all your project context already lives
- **Multi-person huddles** — up to 50 participants on paid plans
- **Clips** — record short audio or video messages for async follow-up

**Where Meet still wins:** Slack Huddles max out at 50 participants and lack features like breakout rooms, recording, and live captions. For formal meetings, you still need a dedicated tool.

See how Slack stacks up against Microsoft's platform in our [Slack vs Microsoft Teams comparison](/compare/slack-vs-microsoft-teams/).

**Best for:** Teams already on Slack that want spontaneous voice and video conversations without the overhead of scheduling.

## 4. Webex by Cisco — Best for Enterprise Security

**Pricing:** Free (40 min, 100 participants) | Meet $14.50/user/mo | Suite $25/user/mo

Webex targets enterprises that need compliance certifications, end-to-end encryption, and hardware integration with Cisco conference room devices. Its AI Assistant has quietly become one of the best in the category.

**Why pick Webex over Google Meet:**
- **Industry-leading noise cancellation** — Cisco's audio intelligence removes background noise better than any competitor
- **End-to-end encryption** — available for all meetings, not just selected ones
- **Cisco Room devices** — seamless integration with physical conference room hardware
- **AI Assistant** — real-time translation in 100+ languages, meeting summaries, and proactive suggestions
- **FedRAMP and HIPAA compliance** — meets strict government and healthcare requirements

**Where Meet still wins:** Easier setup for small teams. Webex's admin console and enterprise features add complexity that smaller organizations don't need.

**Best for:** Enterprises with compliance requirements, regulated industries, and organizations with Cisco hardware.

## 5. Discord — Best Free Option

**Pricing:** Free | Nitro $9.99/mo (HD video, larger uploads)

Discord started as a gaming platform, but remote teams have adopted it for its always-on voice channels and zero-cost video calls. There are no time limits on the free plan, and voice channels stay open so people can drop in and out throughout the day.

**Why pick Discord over Google Meet:**
- **No time limits** — free video calls with no 60-minute cutoff
- **Always-on voice channels** — join when you want, leave when you want, no scheduling needed
- **Screen sharing** — share your screen at up to 4K/60fps on Nitro
- **Community features** — roles, permissions, bots, and forums for team organization
- **Completely free** — core features cost nothing

**Where Meet still wins:** Professional appearance, calendar integration, and business features like recording, transcription, and admin controls. Discord looks and feels informal.

**Best for:** Small teams, startups, and communities that want free, always-available voice and video.

## 6. Around — Best for Async-First Teams

**Pricing:** Free | Pro $8.50/user/mo

Around is built for teams that treat meetings as the exception, not the rule. Its floating video bubbles sit in the corner of your screen, keeping your face visible without dominating your workspace. Meetings start [instantly](/tools/instantly/) — no links, no scheduling.

**Why pick Around over Google Meet:**
- **Non-intrusive video** — small floating bubbles instead of full-screen gallery view
- **Instant meetings** — click and talk, no calendar invites required
- **Auto-noise cancellation** — handles background noise and echo without manual settings
- **Minimal interface** — no clutter, no tabs, no feature creep
- **Designed for focus** — video stays out of the way so you can keep working during calls

**Where Meet still wins:** Participant limits, recording, and integration with Google's productivity tools. Around is intentionally minimal, which means fewer features.

**Best for:** Remote teams that value async work and want meetings to be quick and unobtrusive.

## 7. Whereby — Best for External Meetings

**Pricing:** Free (1 room, 100 participants, 45 min) | Pro $8.99/mo | Business $11.99/mo

Whereby lets you create permanent meeting rooms with custom URLs. Send someone `whereby.me/your-company` and they join in their browser — no download, no account, no sign-up. This makes it ideal for client calls, interviews, and customer support.

**Why pick Whereby over Google Meet:**
- **Branded meeting rooms** — custom URLs and room backgrounds with your logo
- **No downloads required** — guests join in any browser, instantly
- **Embeddable** — add video calls directly into your website or app with Whereby's API
- **Persistent rooms** — the same URL works every time, no new links to share
- **Simple pricing** — straightforward plans without per-user enterprise pricing

**Where Meet still wins:** Calendar integration, larger meeting capacity on free plans, and the broader Google ecosystem. Whereby is focused specifically on the meeting experience.

**Best for:** Agencies, consultants, and support teams that host calls with external participants.
