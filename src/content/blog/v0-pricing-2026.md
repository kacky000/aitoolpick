**[v0 by Vercel](/tools/v0/)** prices on a monthly subscription plus a credit system, where credits are consumed by AI generations. The headline plan price is easy; the part that determines your real cost is how fast you burn credits. Here's the full breakdown and how it stacks up against rival AI app builders.

## v0 Plans and Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Price | Credits | Right for |
|------|-------|---------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Limited monthly credits | Trying it out |
| Premium | ~$20/month | ~$20 in credits + higher limits | Solo builders |
| Team | ~$30/user/month | Pooled credits, collaboration | Teams |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom credits, SSO | Larger orgs |

Credits are consumed per generation, and more complex prompts (larger context, more output) cost more credits. The subscription includes a monthly credit allotment; heavy users buy more on top.

## Free Plan: Genuinely Useful for Prototyping

v0's free tier gives you real generations each month:

- A monthly credit allowance
- Generate React/Next.js + Tailwind UI from prompts
- Shadcn-style components, production-quality code output
- Direct deploy to Vercel

It's enough to prototype a few screens or test whether v0's output quality fits your workflow. Serious building exhausts the free credits quickly.

## Premium Plan (~$20/month)

Premium is the plan most individual developers and designers use:

- A larger monthly credit allotment (roughly matching the subscription value)
- Higher rate limits and longer context
- Access to the strongest generation models
- Faster generations

The key advantage of v0 over generic AI app builders is **code quality and the Vercel pipeline**: it outputs clean Next.js + Tailwind + shadcn/ui code you actually own, and deploys it to Vercel in one step. For React-stack developers, that's a meaningful edge.

## Team Plan (~$30/user/month)

Team adds collaboration and pooled credits:

- Shared credit pool across the team
- Collaborative projects and shared chats
- Centralized billing and admin
- Higher overall limits

For a product team iterating on UI together inside the Vercel ecosystem, the Team plan keeps everyone on shared credits and projects.

## The Hidden Cost: Credits Burn Faster Than You Expect

The credit model is the thing to watch. Iterating heavily — regenerating a component ten times to get it right — consumes credits each time, and complex prompts cost more per generation. Builders who treat v0 like an unlimited tool blow through a month's credits in days. The discipline that keeps costs down: write tighter prompts, iterate in fewer, larger steps, and refine the generated code locally rather than re-prompting for every tweak.

## Is v0 Worth the Price?

**Free → Premium is worth it** if you build React/Next.js UI regularly and value owning clean, deployable code over a hosted black box. The Vercel integration makes prototype-to-production unusually smooth.

**Team is worth it** for product teams collaborating on UI inside the Vercel ecosystem who want pooled credits.

**Consider alternatives if:** you want full-stack app generation with a backend ([Lovable](/tools/lovable/), [Bolt](/tools/bolt/)) or you're a developer who'd rather work in a full AI editor ([Cursor](/tools/cursor/)). v0 is best when high-quality front-end code and Vercel deployment are exactly what you need.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is v0 by Vercel free?

Yes. v0 has a free plan with a monthly credit allowance, enough to prototype a few screens. Premium (~$20/month) adds more credits, higher limits, and the strongest models.

### How do v0 credits work?

Credits are consumed per AI generation, with more complex prompts costing more. Each plan includes a monthly allotment, and heavy users can buy additional credits. Tight prompting and fewer regenerations keep costs down.

### Is v0 better than Lovable or Bolt?

v0 excels at high-quality front-end (Next.js + Tailwind) code you own, with seamless Vercel deployment. Lovable and Bolt lean toward full-stack app generation. The best choice depends on whether you need front-end UI or a complete app.
