Zoom's free plan is famous for one thing: the 40-minute cutoff on group calls. But beyond that, most people aren't sure what they're actually missing by staying on the free tier — or whether the $13.32/month Pro upgrade is worth it.

Here is the real comparison.

## Feature Comparison

| Feature | Free | Pro | Business |
|---------|------|-----|---------|
| 1:1 meetings | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Group meeting limit | 40 minutes | 30 hours | 30 hours |
| Participants | 100 | 100 | 300 |
| Cloud recording | No | 5 GB | 5 GB |
| Local recording | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Zoom AI Companion | No | Yes | Yes |
| SSO | No | No | Yes |
| Managed domains | No | No | Yes |
| Price | $0 | $13.32/user/month | $18.32/user/month |

## The 40-Minute Limit: How Bad Is It Really?

For many use cases, the 40-minute limit is a genuine friction point:

**Hurts you if:**
- You run team standups that occasionally run long
- You do client demos or sales calls (very awkward to restart mid-call)
- You run training sessions or workshops
- You use Zoom as your team's default communication tool

**Doesn't hurt you if:**
- Most of your meetings are 1:1 (no time limit)
- You primarily join calls hosted by others (someone else's paid account)
- Your team primarily uses another tool (Teams, Meet) and Zoom is occasional

The workaround — rejoining after the 40-minute cutoff — works technically but signals to clients and colleagues that you're on a free tier. For professional contexts, that matters.

## What Paid Plans Actually Add

### Zoom AI Companion (Pro and Above)
This is the most significant upgrade in 2026. AI Companion provides:
- **Meeting summaries** delivered after every call
- **Action items** automatically extracted
- **"What did I miss?"** — ask mid-meeting and get caught up instantly
- **Whiteboard AI** for generating diagrams

Microsoft Teams charges $30/user/month for Copilot to do equivalent things. Zoom includes it in Pro at $13.32. If your team has any use for AI meeting summaries, this alone justifies the upgrade.

### Cloud Recording
Free plans record locally only. If you need to share recordings via link (sales calls, training, async team updates), Pro's 5 GB cloud recording is necessary. Storage can be expanded at $40/year per 1 GB if 5 GB isn't enough.

### Longer Meetings Without Interruption
Pro removes the 40-minute cap entirely — up to 30-hour meetings. Practically, this means never being cut off mid-presentation or mid-whiteboard session.

## Free Alternatives That Don't Have Time Limits

Before upgrading, consider whether Zoom is the right tool:

| Tool | Free Limit | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Google Meet | 60 minutes (with Google account) | Included in free Google accounts |
| Microsoft Teams | Unlimited (basic) | Included in free Microsoft accounts |
| Discord | Unlimited | Better for casual/community calls |
| Jitsi Meet | Unlimited | Open source, no account required |

If your team already uses Google Workspace or Microsoft 365, you may have a paid video conferencing solution you're not fully using.

## When to Upgrade to Pro

Upgrade to Zoom Pro when:
- Client calls or sales demos run more than 40 minutes
- You need cloud recording to share meetings
- You want AI meeting summaries (legitimate time-saver)
- You're using Zoom as your primary team communication platform

At $13.32/user/month, Pro is roughly equivalent to 1 coffee per week. For anyone doing client calls, the productivity return easily exceeds the cost.

## When to Upgrade to Business

Upgrade to Business when:
- You need SSO (often a security/compliance requirement above ~50 employees)
- You need managed domains to enforce company-wide Zoom settings
- Your meetings regularly have 100+ attendees

Business requires a minimum of 10 users, so it's not the right choice for small teams even if you want SSO.
