Looking for the best tools for agencies? We tested and compared the top options available in 2026, evaluating features, pricing, ease of use, and real-world performance.

Agencies run multiple clients, juggle deadlines, and need tools that scale without breaking the budget. Here's what actually works for agency workflows in 2026.

## Project Management

### ClickUp — Best All-in-One for Agencies
- **Price:** Free / $7/user/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Multiple views per project, client-facing dashboards, time tracking built-in, docs
- **Best for:** Full-service agencies managing 10+ clients

ClickUp's flexibility lets you set up different workflows per client without buying separate tools for docs, time tracking, and tasks.

[See ClickUp pricing breakdown →](/blog/clickup-pricing-2026/)

### Monday.com — Best for Client-Facing Work
- **Price:** Free / $9/seat/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Beautiful dashboards to share with clients, CRM built-in, automations
- **Best for:** Agencies that need to impress clients with visual project updates

[Compare ClickUp vs Monday →](/compare/clickup-vs-monday/)

### Asana — Best for Structured Workflows
- **Price:** Free / $10.99/user/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Portfolios for multi-project oversight, templates for repeatable processes
- **Best for:** Agencies with standardized delivery processes

[Compare Asana vs Monday →](/compare/asana-vs-monday/)

## Automation

### Zapier — Best for Connecting Everything
- **Price:** Free / $29.99/month
- **Why agencies love it:** 6,000+ app integrations, client onboarding automations, report scheduling
- **Best for:** Automating repetitive client processes (intake forms, status updates, invoice triggers)

### Make — Best Value for Complex Workflows
- **Price:** Free / $10.59/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Visual workflow builder, cheaper than Zapier for high-volume automations
- **Best for:** Agencies with technical team members who can build sophisticated automations

[Compare Zapier vs Make →](/compare/make-vs-zapier/)

## Design

### Canva — Best for Rapid Content Creation
- **Price:** Free / $15/user/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Brand Kit per client, Magic Resize for multi-platform content, team libraries
- **Best for:** Social media agencies, content agencies producing high-volume visual assets

### Figma — Best for Product Design Agencies
- **Price:** Free / $15/editor/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Client review via prototypes, design system management, Dev Mode for handoff
- **Best for:** Digital agencies building websites, apps, and product interfaces

[Compare Canva vs Figma →](/compare/canva-vs-figma/)

## Communication

### Slack — Best for Internal + Client Communication
- **Price:** Free / $7.25/user/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Channels per client, Huddles for quick calls, integrations with project tools
- **Best for:** Agencies that use Slack Connect to communicate directly with client teams

[Compare Slack vs Teams →](/compare/microsoft-teams-vs-slack/)

## AI Assistants

### ChatGPT — Best for Content Generation
- **Price:** Free / $20/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Draft client proposals, generate content calendars, brainstorm campaign ideas
- **Best for:** Marketing and content agencies scaling output

### Claude — Best for Strategy and Research
- **Price:** Free / $20/month
- **Why agencies love it:** Long documents analysis, strategy documents, client research summaries
- **Best for:** Consulting and strategy agencies processing large amounts of client data
