Perplexity is the AI answer engine that researchers actually trust because it cites sources by default. In 2026, with Pro Search, Spaces, and the Comet browser added, it has quietly become a serious research workstation — not just "AI Google." This is how to use it like a power researcher, not a casual chatter.

## The Four Surfaces of Perplexity in 2026

| Surface | What it does | Plan |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Quick Search** | One-shot answer with citations | Free |
| **Pro Search** | Multi-step search with deeper reasoning | Pro / Max |
| **Spaces** | Saved research projects, custom instructions | Pro / Max |
| **Comet (browser)** | AI-native browser with sidebar Perplexity | Max |

Casual users live in Quick Search. Real research happens in **Pro Search inside a Space**. Spaces are the unlock that most users miss.

## Setting Up a Research Space

1. Click **Spaces** → New Space
2. Add **Instructions**: "You are researching the impact of GLP-1 drugs on the food industry. Prefer peer-reviewed sources from 2023–2026. Always provide author and date."
3. Set **Default model** (GPT-5, Claude, Sonar Pro, or Grok)
4. Upload **reference files** (PDFs, articles you've already collected)
5. Start asking questions — every search in the Space inherits the instructions + files

Spaces solve the biggest research problem: keeping context across days of searches.

## Pro Search vs Quick Search

- **Quick Search** = one search query, one answer
- **Pro Search** = decomposes your question into 3–7 sub-searches, then synthesizes

Use Pro Search whenever the question has multiple facets ("Compare X and Y in 3 dimensions"). Quick Search for factual lookups.

## Citation-First Workflow

The reason researchers prefer Perplexity over ChatGPT: every claim links to a numbered source. The workflow:

1. Ask a research question in Pro Search
2. Scan the answer; click each citation that supports a claim you'd quote
3. Open the source in a new tab to verify the actual passage
4. Drag the source URL into your notes manager (Notion, Obsidian)

Treat Perplexity as a **starting point** that maps the landscape, not a final source. The citations are the value.

## Power Moves

### 1. Focus Modes

Below the search box, pick **Academic** to bias toward peer-reviewed sources, or **Reddit** for community sentiment. Each focus filters the source pool.

### 2. Threads as Research Logs

Every Pro Search session becomes a thread. Pin important threads inside the Space. They serve as a searchable research diary.

### 3. File Upload + Cross-Reference

Upload a PDF in a Space → "What does this paper say about cohort sizes? Cross-reference with current 2026 standards." Combines document QA with live web search.

### 4. Comet Browser for In-Context Research

Comet (Perplexity's browser, Max plan) opens a Perplexity sidebar on any page: "Summarize this article and compare to the consensus view." Native research-while-reading.

## Where Perplexity Beats Other AI Tools

- **Citations everywhere** — see [Perplexity Max review](/blog/perplexity-max-review-2026/) for benchmark details

## FAQ

**Is Perplexity free worth using?** Yes — 5 Pro Searches/day handle casual research. Upgrade when you hit that limit daily.

**Perplexity vs ChatGPT Search?** Perplexity's citations are denser and the focus modes are stronger. ChatGPT is better when you also need code or image generation in the same session.

**Can Perplexity replace Google Scholar?** Not yet — but with Academic focus + Spaces, it surfaces a strong reading list fast. Use Scholar for citation chasing.

**Does Comet replace Chrome?** Comet is Perplexity's browser, Max-only. Most users add the Perplexity extension to Chrome instead.
