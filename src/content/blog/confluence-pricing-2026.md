**[Confluence](/tools/confluence/)** is Atlassian's team workspace and wiki, priced per user across four tiers. The headline per-seat numbers look cheap, but the real cost depends on team size and whether you also pay for [Jira](/blog/jira-pricing-2026). Here's the full 2026 breakdown.

## Confluence Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Per user/mo | Free for | Right for |
|------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Free** | $0 | Up to 10 users | Small teams getting started |
| **Standard** | $6.05 | — | Growing teams |
| **Premium** | $11.55 | — | Larger orgs needing analytics & controls |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | — | Multi-instance, compliance-heavy orgs |

> Per-user prices are approximate and decrease at higher user counts on Atlassian's sliding scale.

### Free — Up to 10 users

Confluence's free tier is genuinely usable for a small team:

- Unlimited pages and spaces
- Up to 10 users
- 2 GB file storage
- Basic page versioning and macros

The 10-user cap is the wall most teams hit. Past that, you move to Standard.

### Standard — ~$6.05/user/month

Everything in Free, plus:

- Up to 50,000 users
- 250 GB storage
- Page permissions and audit logging (basic)
- Business-hours support

Standard is where most mid-size teams live. The per-user price drops as you add more seats.

### Premium — ~$11.55/user/month

Everything in Standard, plus:

- Unlimited storage
- Advanced analytics and page insights
- Bulk admin controls and team calendars
- IP allowlisting and 24/7 support

Premium suits larger organizations that need governance, analytics, and tighter security.

### Enterprise — Custom

Everything in Premium, plus:

- Multiple Confluence instances
- SAML SSO and advanced compliance (via Atlassian Guard)
- Centralized billing across Atlassian products
- 99.95% uptime SLA

Enterprise is negotiated and billed annually, typically for organizations standardizing on the full Atlassian stack.

| Tool | Free plan | Paid starts | Best at |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Confluence | ✅ (10 users) | ~$6.05/user | Structured docs + Jira integration |
| [Notion](/blog/notion-pricing-2026) | ✅ | ~$10/user | Flexible all-in-one workspace |

| [Jira](/blog/jira-pricing-2026) | ✅ (10 users) | ~$7.75/user | Issue and project tracking |

Confluence's strength is its tight integration with [Jira](/blog/jira-pricing-2026) — if your engineering org already runs Jira, Confluence is the natural docs layer. For teams that want a single flexible workspace instead of a dedicated wiki, [Notion](/blog/notion-pricing-2026) is the more modern, all-in-one alternative.

## Is Confluence Worth the Cost?

### It's worth it if:

- You already use **[Jira](/blog/jira-pricing-2026)** and want docs in the same ecosystem
- You need **structured, permissioned** documentation at scale
- Your org values Atlassian's governance and compliance tooling

### Look elsewhere if:

- You want a **flexible, modern workspace** — [Notion](/blog/notion-pricing-2026) is friendlier
- You're a small team that won't outgrow the free tier soon
- You don't use Jira — Confluence's biggest advantage disappears

## Tips to Save on Confluence

1. **Stay on Free under 10 users** — it's fully functional for small teams
2. **Bundle with Jira** — Atlassian discounts when you run multiple products
3. **Right-size your plan** — don't pay Premium for analytics you won't use
4. **Review inactive seats** — Confluence bills per user, so deactivate departed members

## The Bottom Line

Confluence is the obvious documentation choice for any team already living in [Jira](/blog/jira-pricing-2026) — the integration and shared admin make it cohesive and cost-effective. Standalone, it's a capable but more rigid wiki, and teams wanting flexibility often prefer [Notion](/blog/notion-pricing-2026). Under 10 users, the free tier is all you need.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Confluence free?

Yes, for up to 10 users with unlimited pages and 2 GB storage. Beyond 10 users you need the Standard plan, which starts around $6.05 per user per month.

### Does Confluence per-user pricing decrease with team size?

Yes. Atlassian uses a sliding scale, so the effective per-user price drops as you add seats, especially on Standard and Premium.

### Is Confluence or Notion better value?

If you use [Jira](/blog/jira-pricing-2026), Confluence integrates seamlessly and is great value. If you want one flexible workspace for docs, tasks, and databases, [Notion](/blog/notion-pricing-2026) is usually the better fit.
