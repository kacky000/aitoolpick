# ClickUp vs Notion 2026: Which All-in-One Tool Wins?

ClickUp and Notion both promise to replace a stack of apps, but they pull from opposite directions. ClickUp is a project management powerhouse that added docs; Notion is a flexible docs-and-database canvas that added tasks.

## Quick Answer

Choose **ClickUp** if your core need is managing tasks, sprints, and team projects. Choose **Notion** if your core need is documentation, knowledge, and flexible databases.

## Pricing Compared

| Plan | ClickUp | Notion |
|------|---------|--------|
| Free | Generous | Generous (personal) |
| Entry paid | $7/user/mo (Unlimited) | $10/user/mo (Plus) |
| Business | $12/user/mo | $20/user/mo (AI included) |

See the [ClickUp pricing breakdown](/blog/clickup-pricing-2026) and [Notion pricing breakdown](/blog/notion-pricing-2026).

## Where ClickUp Wins

- **Project depth**: Sprints, dependencies, time tracking, and workload views built in.
- **Value**: $7 Unlimited undercuts Notion's paid entry.
- **Task views**: More native ways to slice work—Gantt, board, calendar, workload.

## Where Notion Wins

- **Docs and knowledge**: The best writing and wiki experience of the two.
- **Flexible databases**: Relational databases that bend to any structure.
- **AI**: Notion AI is tightly woven into notes and databases—see [how to use Notion AI](/blog/how-to-use-notion-ai-2026).

## Head-to-Head

| Factor | ClickUp | Notion |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Task management | Excellent | Good |
| Docs/wiki | Good | Excellent |
| Price (entry) | $7 | $10 |
| Best for | Project teams | Knowledge + light tasks |

## Who Should Choose What?

**Choose ClickUp if you:**
- Run sprints and need deep project features
- Want the lowest paid entry price
- Need workload and time tracking

**Choose Notion if you:**
- Live in docs, notes, and wikis
- Want flexible relational databases
- Value built-in AI for writing

## The Bottom Line

**ClickUp** wins on project management depth and price; **Notion** wins on docs, databases, and knowledge. Pick ClickUp to run the work, Notion to document and organize it.
