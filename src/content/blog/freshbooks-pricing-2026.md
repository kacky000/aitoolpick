**[FreshBooks](/tools/freshbooks/)** is the accounting tool built for people who hate accounting — freelancers and service businesses who mostly need to send invoices and get paid. Here's exactly what FreshBooks costs in 2026, including the billable-client limits and per-user fees that quietly raise your bill.

## FreshBooks Pricing in 2026

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (10% off) | Billable clients |
|------|---------|------------------|------------------|
| Lite | **$19/mo** | ~$17/mo | Up to 5 |
| Plus | **$38/mo** | ~$30/mo | Up to 50 |
| Premium | **$65/mo** | ~$54/mo | Unlimited |
| Select | Custom | Custom | Unlimited + dedicated support |

The plan tiers are gated by **billable clients**, not features or transactions — an unusual model that catches people off guard as their client list grows.

## What Each Plan Actually Gets You

- **Lite ($19)** — Unlimited invoices and expenses, but only **5 billable clients**. Fine for a freelancer with a few steady accounts. Includes time tracking, estimates, and basic reports.
- **Plus ($38)** — Raises the cap to **50 clients** and adds the features most users actually want: recurring invoices, client retainers, double-entry accounting reports, and accountant access. This is the sweet spot for most freelancers.
- **Premium ($65)** — **Unlimited clients** plus advanced reporting, project profitability, and customizable email templates. For agencies and busy consultants.
- **Select (custom)** — For larger firms: dedicated account manager, lower payment fees, and a custom onboarding. Typically negotiated in the $300–500/mo range.

## The Hidden Costs

FreshBooks' base price is just the start:

- **Team members** — Each additional user is **+$11/month**. A two-person shop on Plus is really paying $49.
- **Advanced Payments** — Recurring billing, card-on-file, and in-person payments cost **+$20/month**.
- **Payment processing** — Standard card fees apply on top (~2.9% + $0.30).
- **Client cap upgrades** — Cross your billable-client limit and you're forced up a tier, even if you don't need the other features.

## Is FreshBooks Worth It?

### It's worth it if:

- You're a **freelancer or service business** whose main job is invoicing clients
- You want the **friendliest UI** in accounting — no jargon, fast setup
- You bill **time and projects** and want one-click client payments
- You value great mobile apps for on-the-go invoicing

### Look elsewhere if:

- You're on a **tight budget** — [Wave](/blog/wave-pricing-2026) handles invoicing and accounting free
- You have a **team** — the $11/user fee adds up fast; [Xero](/blog/xero-pricing-2026) includes unlimited users
- You need **inventory** or deep accounting — [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026) is built for it

## Tips to Lower Your FreshBooks Cost

1. **Pay annually** for a 10% discount across all tiers.
2. **Watch the client cap** — it's the real upgrade trigger; archive inactive clients to stay under it.
3. **Skip Advanced Payments** unless you truly need recurring billing — it's a silent $20/mo.
4. **Avoid extra seats** where possible — at $11/user, a 3-person team nearly doubles your base cost.

## The Bottom Line

FreshBooks in 2026 is the best invoicing-first tool for freelancers, with most users landing on **Plus ($38)** for its 50-client cap and recurring billing. Just budget for the add-ons: team seats and Advanced Payments can push your real cost well past the sticker. If invoicing is your whole job, FreshBooks earns its price. If you need free books or full accounting, [Wave](/blog/wave-pricing-2026) and [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026) are the alternatives to weigh.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does FreshBooks cost in 2026?

FreshBooks plans are Lite $19/mo, Plus $38/mo, and Premium $65/mo, with a custom-priced Select tier. Paying annually saves about 10%.

### What's the catch with FreshBooks pricing?

Plans are limited by billable clients (5 on Lite, 50 on Plus, unlimited on Premium), and each extra team member costs $11/month. Advanced Payments adds another $20/month.

### Is FreshBooks better than QuickBooks for freelancers?

For invoicing-focused freelancers, yes — FreshBooks is simpler and cheaper to start. [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026) is the better choice if you need inventory, deep reporting, or your accountant requires it.
