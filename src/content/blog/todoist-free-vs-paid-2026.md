**Todoist Free** and **Paid** are both popular tools in their category, but they serve different needs and audiences. This guide compares their features, pricing, and best use cases to help you choose the right one.

Todoist's free plan is one of the most generous in the task management space. But at $4/month, the Pro plan is also one of the cheapest upgrades. Here's exactly what you get — and don't get — at each tier.

## Free Plan: What's Included

Todoist's free plan gives you:

- **5 active projects** (was 80 tasks per project, now unlimited tasks)
- **5 collaborators** per project
- **3 filters** for custom views
- **1 week** of activity history
- Natural language date recognition
- Priority levels (P1–P4)
- Quick Add from any screen
- Cross-platform sync (all devices)
- Basic integrations (Google Calendar, etc.)

That's genuinely useful for personal task management. The 5-project limit is the main constraint.

## Pro Plan ($4/month): What You Unlock

Upgrading to Pro adds:

- **300 active projects** (up from 5)
- **25 collaborators** per project
- **150 filters** (up from 3)
- **Reminders** (location and time-based)
- **Unlimited activity history**
- **Calendar layout** view
- **Task duration** estimates
- **AI-powered features** (task suggestions, Smart Schedule)
- Priority support
- Auto-backups

## Business Plan ($6/user/month): Team Features

The Business plan adds on top of Pro:

- **500 active projects**
- **50 collaborators** per project
- Team inbox and shared projects
- Admin and member roles
- Team activity log
- Priority business support
- Centralized billing

## Is Pro Worth It?

**Yes, if any of these apply:**

1. **You need more than 5 projects.** This is the most common reason to upgrade. If you separate work, personal, and side projects, you'll hit the limit fast.

2. **Reminders are essential.** The free plan has no reminders — a surprising limitation. If you rely on time-based or location-based reminders, Pro is mandatory.

3. **You use filters extensively.** Three filters is restrictive if you build custom views for different contexts (work, errands, urgent, etc.).

4. **AI features appeal to you.** Smart Schedule and AI suggestions can genuinely save time for heavy Todoist users.

**No, if:**

- You're a light user with 5 or fewer projects
- You don't need reminders (you check Todoist regularly anyway)
- You mainly use it for simple daily to-do lists

## Free Plan Limitations That Actually Matter

| Limitation | Impact | Workaround |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| 5 projects | High — runs out fast | Use sections within projects |
| No reminders | Medium | Use phone calendar reminders |
| 3 filters | Medium | Prioritize your most-used views |
| 1 week history | Low | Most people don't check history |

## How It Compares

At $4/month, Todoist Pro is cheaper than almost every competitor:

- **ClickUp**: Free plan is more generous, paid starts at $7/user/month
- **Asana**: Starter plan is $10.99/user/month
- **Notion**: Plus plan is $10/user/month

Todoist Pro is arguably the best value in task management if you want a focused, fast tool without the complexity of a full project management suite.

## The Verdict

Todoist's free plan works for casual users. But $4/month is low enough that most serious users should just upgrade — the reminders feature alone justifies the cost. If you're managing multiple areas of your life in Todoist, Pro removes the friction that the free plan creates.
