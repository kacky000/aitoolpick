**[Typeform](/tools/typeform/)** has reasonable headline prices and brutal response limits. The free plan caps you at 10 responses per month — yes, ten — which makes it useless for almost any real use case. The paid plans get expensive quickly compared to alternatives like [Tally](/tools/tally/) that offer the same one-question-at-a-time experience for free or near-free.

Here's exactly what every Typeform plan costs in 2026, what you actually get, and when it's worth paying for.

## Typeform Plans Overview

| Plan | Price | Responses/month | Best For |
|------|-------|----------------|----------|
| Free | $0 | 10 | Evaluation only |
| Basic | $25/month | 100 | Solo, low volume |
| Plus | $50/month | 1,000 | Small teams |
| Business | $83/month | 10,000 | Mid-market |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Larger orgs |

All prices are billed annually — monthly billing is roughly 25% more. The response caps are hard limits, not soft warnings.

## Free Plan: 10 Responses Is Not a Typo

Typeform's free plan exists mainly as a demo. You get:

- Unlimited forms
- 10 responses/month across all forms
- 10 fields per form
- Typeform branding in footer
- Basic templates

That 10-response cap resets monthly, but you cannot upgrade temporarily — to get more responses, you must commit to a paid plan. For any real use, Free is a non-starter.

## Basic Plan ($25/month)

Basic removes the 10-response cap but caps you at 100 responses/month and one user.

**Basic includes:**
- 100 responses/month
- Unlimited forms
- Remove Typeform branding
- Custom domain (Typeform subdomain)
- Logic jumps
- Custom thank-you screens
- Basic integrations (Slack, [Mailchimp](/tools/mailchimp/), [Notion](/tools/notion/))

**The catch:** 100 responses is still very low. A single popular form can blow through this in a day. Most users on Basic are paying for one form with light steady traffic.

## Plus Plan ($50/month)

Plus is the entry point for teams. The jump in response count is substantial.

**Plus adds:**
- 1,000 responses/month
- 3 users
- Custom subdomain
- File uploads in form responses
- Hidden fields for prefilling
- Custom email notifications

For a small business running 2-3 active forms, Plus is the realistic floor. $50/month for 1,000 responses works out to $0.05 per response.

## Business Plan ($83/month)

Business is for marketing teams running lead gen at volume.

**Business adds:**
- 10,000 responses/month
- 5 users
- Conversion tracking pixels
- Priority support
- Drop-off rates analytics
- Custom branding (full)
- Hubspot, Salesforce integrations
- Folders for organization

At 10,000 responses, the per-response cost drops to $0.008 — that's where Typeform makes sense vs alternatives if you're using it for lead gen.

## Enterprise (Custom)

Enterprise starts in the $12K+/year range and adds SSO, custom data retention, dedicated support, and contractual SLAs. Talk to sales if you're at Business and consistently hitting overages.

## Overage Behavior: Hard Stops, Not Charges

Unlike most SaaS tools, Typeform does not bill overages — once you hit your response limit, your forms stop accepting submissions until you upgrade or the month resets. This is dangerous for lead-gen forms where you'd rather pay overage than miss submissions.

**Mitigation:** Set up notifications when you're at 80% of your response cap. Keep one tier above your typical month if forms run lead gen.

## Typeform vs Alternatives

| Feature | Typeform Plus | [Tally](/tools/tally/) Pro | Google Forms | [Jotform](/tools/jotform/) Bronze |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|--------------|------------------|
| Monthly cost | $50 | $29 | $0 | $39 |
| Response cap | 1,000 | Unlimited | Unlimited | 1,000 |
| One-at-a-time UX | Yes | Yes | No | Optional |
| Logic | Yes | Yes | Basic | Yes |
| Custom branding | Yes | Yes | No | Limited |
| Integrations | Many | Growing | Workspace only | Many |

[Tally](/tools/tally/) is the strongest direct alternative — same UX, unlimited responses, half the price. The Typeform advantage is polish (animations, conditional logic UX, brand recognition) and a deeper integration catalog.

See [Typeform vs Tally](/compare/typeform-vs-tally/) for the full comparison.

## Hidden Costs

**Stripe payments:** Typeform takes payments in forms, but processes via Stripe — Stripe's own fees apply (2.9% + $0.30).

**Video capture:** Higher-tier plans include video question types, but the underlying storage isn't unlimited — long-form video responses count toward limits in unclear ways.

**Multi-language:** Translations are manual; Typeform doesn't auto-translate. Each language is essentially a new form to maintain.

## Which Plan Should You Pick?

- **Personal projects / testing:** Free for one-off, [Tally](/tools/tally/) for ongoing use
- **Solo creator / freelancer:** Basic ($25) or switch to Tally Pro ($29)
- **Small business with 2-3 forms:** Plus ($50)
- **Marketing team running lead gen:** Business ($83)
- **Large org needing SSO + compliance:** Enterprise

The biggest Typeform mistake is paying Plus for a use case that fits free Google Forms or Tally. Use Typeform when the polished one-at-a-time UX measurably improves your conversion rate — otherwise alternatives are cheaper for equivalent function.

## When Typeform Is Worth It

- High-stakes lead-gen forms where conversion matters (the UX measurably outperforms standard forms)
- Customer feedback where you want to feel "designed for the brand"
- Workflows tightly integrated with [HubSpot](/tools/hubspot/) or [Intercom](/tools/intercom/)
- Forms where you've A/B tested and Typeform converts better

## When to Switch

- Low-stakes internal forms → Google Forms
- Volume lead capture → [Tally](/tools/tally/) Pro
- Complex multi-step processes → [Jotform](/tools/jotform/)
- Embedded product feedback → in-product tools like Sprig
