# Bolt.new vs Lovable Pricing 2026: $25 Tokens vs $25 Credits

Bolt.new and Lovable both let you describe an app in plain English and watch the AI build a working, deployable product in the browser. Their entry paid plans even cost exactly the same — $25/month each. But the headline match hides a deep difference: Bolt meters your usage in **tokens** (10 million per month), while Lovable meters in **credits** (100 per month). That metering gap, not the price, decides which one runs out first for the way you actually build.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Bolt.new | Lovable |
|---|----------|---------|
| **Free** | 1M tokens/mo, 300K daily cap, Bolt branding | 5 credits/day (~30/mo max) |
| **Entry paid** | Pro — $25/mo, 10M tokens | Starter — $25/mo, 100 credits |
| **Next tier** | Teams — $30/user/mo, 10M tokens/user | Pro — $50/mo, 400 credits |
| **Metering** | Tokens (every action consumes) | Credits (one per AI message/edit) |
| **Rollover** | Token rollover on paid plans | Credits reset monthly |

## The Free Tiers

Both free plans exist to let you evaluate, not ship.

**Bolt Free** gives you 1 million tokens per month with a 300,000 daily ceiling. That covers a few small projects, but every hosted site carries Bolt branding, uploads cap at 10 MB, and there are no custom domains. The 300K daily token cap is the real bottleneck — a single complex iteration session can exhaust it before you finish.

**Lovable Free** hands you 5 credits per day, maxing out around 30 per month. Each credit is roughly one AI message or edit, so 5/day is enough to prototype a screen or two and judge whether the output matches your taste. It throttles daily, just like Bolt, but the credit model is easier to reason about — you always know exactly how many "moves" you have left.

Verdict on free: Bolt's token pool feels more generous for short bursts, but Lovable's per-credit clarity makes it easier to predict when you'll hit the wall. Neither is a production tool.

## Entry Paid: Pro ($25) vs Starter ($25)

This is the comparison that matters for most solo builders, and the price is identical.

**Bolt Pro — $25/month.** Pro lifts the daily token cap entirely: you get 10 million tokens monthly to burn however you like, plus token rollover so unused balance carries forward. Upload limits rise to 100 MB, and you unlock custom domains, SEO controls, private sharing, and removal of Bolt branding. The catch: tokens are consumed by *every* action — generation, edits, even failed attempts — so a debugging-heavy session drains them faster than you'd expect.

**Lovable Starter — $25/month.** Starter gives you 100 credits per month. Because one credit roughly equals one AI message or edit, 100 credits is enough for a first real app or MVP — but heavy iterators feel the ceiling quickly. Credits don't roll over; they reset each cycle. Lovable's strength is that its full-stack output (Supabase-backed apps, auth, databases) tends to be production-shaped out of the gate.

The real difference: Bolt's 10M tokens with rollover gives more forgiving headroom for intense generation, but token burn is unpredictable. Lovable's 100 credits are easier to budget but run dry faster if you iterate a lot. If you build in long bursts, Bolt's rollover wins; if you value predictable, full-stack output and can work within ~100 moves, Starter holds its own.

## Going Heavier: Bolt Teams vs Lovable Pro

Here the two diverge.

**Bolt Teams — $30/user/month** adds just $5 over Pro and gives each member their own 10M-token allocation plus collaboration features. Five users cost $150/month, scaling linearly.

**Lovable Pro — $50/month** isn't a team tier — it's the power-user single seat, quadrupling Starter's credits to 400/month and adding collaboration. For a solo builder managing multiple projects, $50 for 4x the credits is the natural upgrade before you ever think about seats.

So the paths split: Bolt's next step up is *more people* at $30/seat; Lovable's next step is *more capacity* for one person at $50. Match the path to whether you're scaling a team or scaling your own output.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Solo builder, heavy iteration, want rollover headroom?** Bolt Pro at $25/mo.
- **Solo builder, want predictable "moves" and full-stack output?** Lovable Starter at $25/mo.
- **Power user juggling multiple projects alone?** Lovable Pro at $50/mo for 4x credits.
- **A team of 3+ builders?** Bolt Teams at $30/user/mo with per-seat token pools.
- **Just testing?** Both free tiers throttle daily — try Lovable for credit clarity, Bolt for token volume.

## The Bottom Line

At $25/month each, Bolt and Lovable look like a coin flip — but the metering model breaks the tie. Bolt's 10M tokens with rollover reward long, bursty build sessions and tolerate failed attempts; Lovable's 100 credits reward disciplined, deliberate moves toward production-shaped, full-stack apps. Pick the meter that matches your build rhythm, then decide whether your next step up is people (Bolt Teams) or capacity (Lovable Pro).
