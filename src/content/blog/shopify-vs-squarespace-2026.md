Shopify is built for ecommerce from the ground up. Squarespace is a website builder that added ecommerce. That fundamental difference shapes every feature, pricing decision, and trade-off between the two platforms in 2026.

If you're launching a store with hundreds of SKUs, complex inventory, and multi-channel selling, Shopify is the stronger choice. If you're building a content-rich brand site that also sells a handful of products, Squarespace gives you better design tools at a lower price. Here's the full breakdown.

## Pricing

Pricing looks similar at first glance, but the details matter. For a full breakdown of Shopify's tiers, see our [Shopify pricing guide](/blog/shopify-pricing-2026/).

### Shopify

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| Basic | $39 | $29 |
| Shopify (Grow) | $105 | $79 |
| Advanced | $399 | $299 |
| Plus | From $2,300 | Custom |

All plans include Shopify Payments with no additional transaction fees. Using a third-party payment gateway adds a 0.6%–2% surcharge depending on your plan.

### Squarespace

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per month) |
|------|---------|-------------------|
| Personal | $25 | $16 |
| Business | $33 | $23 |
| Commerce Basic | $36 | $27 |
| Commerce Advanced | $65 | $49 |

The Personal plan doesn't support ecommerce. The Business plan adds selling capability but charges a 3% transaction fee on every sale. Commerce Basic and above eliminate that fee.

**Winner:** Squarespace is cheaper if you sell a small number of products and want a strong website alongside your store. Shopify offers more value for dedicated ecommerce businesses — its built-in POS, multi-channel integrations, and zero transaction fees on all plans (with Shopify Payments) make the higher price worthwhile.

## Templates and Design

Squarespace is the clear design leader. Its 100+ templates are polished, responsive, and cover nearly every industry vertical. The drag-and-drop editor allows granular control over layout, typography, and spacing without touching code. For creative brands, photographers, restaurants, and portfolio sites, Squarespace templates are hard to beat.

Shopify's free theme selection is smaller — roughly a dozen free options — though the Shopify Theme Store includes hundreds of premium themes ($150–$400 one-time). Shopify's editor is functional but less visually refined than Squarespace's. Design customization often requires editing Liquid template code.

**Winner:** Squarespace for design polish and template variety. Shopify for merchants willing to invest in premium themes or custom development.

## Ecommerce Features

This is where Shopify pulls ahead decisively.

### Inventory Management

Shopify supports unlimited products on all plans, inventory tracking across multiple locations, variant management (size, color, material), and automatic stock level updates across channels. Advanced plans include demand forecasting and purchase order tools.

Squarespace handles basic inventory for physical and digital products. It tracks stock levels and sends low-stock alerts, but lacks multi-location inventory, advanced variant combinations, and sophisticated fulfillment workflows.

### Payment Processing

Shopify Payments (powered by Stripe) supports 100+ currencies, local payment methods, and installment options like Shop Pay. Credit card rates start at 2.9% + $0.30 on Basic. Shopify also integrates with 100+ third-party payment gateways.

Squarespace processes payments through Stripe and PayPal. It supports fewer currencies and payment methods. There's no built-in installment payment option, though you can add Afterpay or Klarna through Squarespace Extensions.

### Multi-Channel Selling

Shopify connects natively to Amazon, eBay, Walmart, TikTok Shop, Facebook/Instagram Shop, Google Shopping, and Pinterest. Inventory syncs automatically across all channels.

Squarespace supports Facebook and Instagram Shopping, plus basic Google product feeds. There's no direct integration with Amazon, eBay, or TikTok.

**Winner:** Shopify for ecommerce features — it's not close.

## Apps and Extensions

Shopify's App Store has over 8,000 apps covering every conceivable ecommerce need: email marketing, loyalty programs, subscription billing, dropshipping, reviews, shipping rate calculators, and more. The ecosystem is a core strength — if a feature doesn't exist natively, there's almost certainly an app for it.

Squarespace's extension library is much smaller, around 40 curated integrations. These cover essentials like email marketing (Mailchimp), shipping (ShipStation), and accounting (QuickBooks), but the selection is limited compared to Shopify.

**Winner:** Shopify by a wide margin.

## SEO and Marketing

Both platforms cover SEO basics: custom URLs, meta titles and descriptions, alt text, sitemaps, and SSL. Squarespace generates clean HTML and fast-loading pages out of the box.

Shopify's SEO is solid but occasionally requires apps for features that Squarespace includes natively, like automatic image compression and built-in analytics. Shopify's marketing tools include email campaigns (Shopify Email), discount codes, gift cards, and abandoned cart recovery on all plans.

Squarespace includes a robust blogging engine, built-in email campaigns, social scheduling tools, and detailed site analytics. Its content marketing tools are stronger than Shopify's for businesses that rely heavily on blog content and social media.

For a deeper look at Shopify's capabilities, read our [Shopify review for 2026](/blog/shopify-review-2026/).

**Winner:** Squarespace for content marketing and blogging. Shopify for ecommerce-specific marketing (abandoned carts, email campaigns, discount automation).

## Ease of Use

Squarespace's editor is more intuitive for building beautiful pages. Drag blocks, adjust spacing, preview on mobile — it feels natural even for non-technical users.

Shopify's admin is optimized for store management: adding products, processing orders, managing inventory. The learning curve is slightly steeper for site design but flatter for day-to-day commerce operations.

**Winner:** Squarespace for website building. Shopify for store management.

## Customer Support

Shopify offers 24/7 live chat, email, and phone support on all plans. Response times are generally fast, and the Shopify Community forum is active.

Squarespace provides 24/7 email and live chat support. Phone support is not available. Response quality is good, but wait times can be longer during peak periods.

**Winner:** Shopify for support availability.

## Who Should Choose What

### Choose Shopify If You:
- Run a dedicated ecommerce business with 50+ products
- Need multi-channel selling (Amazon, eBay, TikTok)
- Want a built-in POS for physical retail
- Plan to scale internationally with multi-currency support
- Need advanced inventory management across locations

### Choose Squarespace If You:
- Want a stunning website that also sells products
- Sell fewer than 50 products alongside content or services
- Prioritize design quality over ecommerce depth
- Need strong blogging and content marketing tools
- Prefer an all-in-one platform without app dependencies

For more on Squarespace's strengths, see our [Squarespace review for 2026](/blog/squarespace-review-2026/).

## The Bottom Line

Shopify and Squarespace serve different primary needs. Shopify is an ecommerce platform that can host a website. Squarespace is a website builder that can run a store. Choose based on which side of that equation matters more to your business.

For most serious ecommerce operations, Shopify's deeper feature set, larger app ecosystem, and multi-channel capabilities justify the higher cost. For brand-first businesses where the website experience is as important as the shopping cart, Squarespace delivers better design tools at a lower price.
