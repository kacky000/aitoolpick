AI-powered automation has moved far beyond simple "if this, then that" triggers. In 2026, the leading platforms can build multi-step workflows, draft emails, classify incoming data, and even decide what to do next — all without you writing a line of code.

We tested eight automation platforms to see how their AI features actually perform in practice. Here's what stood out.

## The Big Shift: Agentic Workflows

The most significant trend in automation this year is the rise of agentic workflows. Instead of rigid, predefined paths, AI agents can now observe incoming data, reason about what needs to happen, and execute a chain of actions autonomously.

Zapier and Make both launched agentic features in late 2025, and several competitors have followed. The practical impact: workflows that used to require dozens of conditional branches can now be handled by a single AI step that interprets context and picks the right path. That said, agentic automation is still early — keep a human in the loop for anything high-stakes.

## 1. Zapier — Best All-Around AI Automation

**What it does:** Connects 7,000+ apps with a visual builder. AI features include natural-language workflow creation, AI-powered data formatting, and autonomous multi-step agents.

**Pricing:** Free (100 tasks/month) → Starter $19.99/month → Professional $49/month → Team $69/month → Enterprise custom

**Key AI features:**
- Natural-language Zap builder — describe what you want and Zapier generates the workflow
- AI actions that summarize, classify, extract, and transform data mid-workflow
- Canvas-based agentic mode (launched Q1 2026) for multi-decision workflows

**Best for:** Non-technical teams that want the largest app library and a low learning curve.

Zapier remains the default choice for most teams. The main drawback: costs scale fast once you exceed the free tier. For a detailed comparison with Make, see our [Make vs Zapier breakdown](/blog/make-vs-zapier-2026).

## 2. Make (formerly Integromat) — Best Visual AI Workflow Builder

**What it does:** Drag-and-drop scenario builder with powerful data routing. AI modules let you embed LLM calls directly into workflows.

**Pricing:** Free (1,000 ops/month) → Core $10.59/month → Pro $18.82/month → Teams $34.12/month → Enterprise custom

**Key AI features:**
- Built-in OpenAI and Anthropic modules for text generation, classification, and extraction
- Visual branching with AI-powered routers that classify inputs and direct them down the right path
- Scenario templates with pre-configured AI steps

**Best for:** Teams that need complex, multi-branch automations with fine-grained control over AI behavior.

[Make](/tools/make/) gives you more visual control over data flow than any other tool here. Operations-based pricing is more forgiving for high-volume workflows, though the app library (1,800+ integrations) is smaller than Zapier's.

## 3. n8n — Best Self-Hosted AI Automation

**What it does:** Open-source workflow automation that runs on your own server. Full access to AI/LLM nodes with no vendor lock-in.

**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted, unlimited) → Cloud Starter $24/month → Cloud Pro $60/month → Enterprise custom

**Key AI features:**
- Native LLM nodes for OpenAI, Anthropic, Ollama, and local models
- AI agent node with tool-calling, memory, and multi-step reasoning
- Vector store integration for RAG (retrieval-augmented generation) workflows
- Complete control over prompts, model selection, and data handling

**Best for:** Developers and technical teams that want full ownership of their automation infrastructure and AI stack.

n8n is the standout option if you care about data privacy or want to run local models. The self-hosted version is genuinely free with no execution limits — you just handle hosting and maintenance yourself. For alternatives, check our [best n8n alternatives guide](/blog/best-n8n-alternatives-2026).

## 4. Bardeen — Best for Browser-Based AI Automation

**What it does:** Chrome extension that automates repetitive browser tasks. AI scrapes, fills forms, and moves data between web apps without APIs.

**Pricing:** Free (basic automations) → Professional $10/month → Business $15/month/user

**Key AI features:**
- AI-powered web scraping that understands page structure without CSS selectors
- Smart data extraction from emails, PDFs, and web pages
- Suggested automations based on your browsing patterns

**Best for:** Sales teams and recruiters who spend hours copying data between browser tabs.

Bardeen lives in your browser and automates what you see on screen, making it ideal for apps without API integrations in [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) or Make. The limitation: Chrome-only, and it can break when websites redesign.

## 5. Activepieces — Best Open-Source Zapier Alternative

**What it does:** Open-source automation platform with a Zapier-like interface. Self-host for free or use the cloud version.

**Pricing:** Free (self-hosted, unlimited) → Cloud starts at $5/month

**Key AI features:**
- AI code generation for custom workflow steps
- LLM integration pieces for OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google
- Community-built AI templates

**Best for:** Teams that want a Zapier-like experience without the Zapier price tag.

Activepieces has grown rapidly since launch, with 200+ integrations and a polished interface. Not as mature as n8n for AI-heavy workflows, but far easier to set up.

## 6. Relay.app — Best for Human-in-the-Loop AI Workflows

**What it does:** Automation platform designed around human approvals. AI handles the bulk work, and humans step in for final decisions.

**Pricing:** Free (100 runs/month) → Team $32/month → Business custom

**Key AI features:**
- AI steps for summarization, drafting, and classification
- Built-in approval steps where a team member reviews AI output before proceeding
- Multiplayer workflows with role-based assignments

**Best for:** Teams where AI should assist but not make final decisions — legal, compliance, customer support.

Relay.app makes human approval a first-class feature — not an afterthought like in most platforms. This matters when you're letting AI draft customer-facing content or process sensitive requests.

## 7. Workato — Best for Enterprise AI Automation

**What it does:** Enterprise-grade integration and automation platform. Handles complex business logic across ERP, CRM, HRIS, and finance systems.

**Pricing:** Custom (starts around $10,000/year)

**Key AI features:**
- Workato Copilot for natural-language recipe building
- AI-powered data mapping that learns from past configurations
- Pre-built enterprise connectors with AI enrichment

**Best for:** Large organizations with complex IT environments and compliance requirements.

Workato competes with MuleSoft and Boomi, not Zapier. If your company runs SAP, Workday, NetSuite, and Salesforce, Workato stitches them together with AI-powered logic that adapts as your data schemas change.

## 8. Tray.io — Best for API-Heavy AI Workflows

**What it does:** Low-code automation for technical teams. Strong API handling with visual workflow design.

**Pricing:** Custom (typically $600+/month for teams)

**Key AI features:**
- Merlin AI for intelligent data mapping and transformation
- Universal connector for any REST or GraphQL API
- AI-assisted error handling and retry logic

**Best for:** Revenue ops and data teams that need to build sophisticated integrations between SaaS tools.

Tray.io sits between Make and writing custom code. Powerful for connecting billing systems to CRMs to data warehouses, but the pricing puts it out of reach for small teams.

## How to Choose

**Budget under $50/month?** Start with [Zapier](/tools/zapier/) or Make. Both have free tiers that let you test before committing.

**Need full control over AI models?** Go with n8n. Self-hosting gives you access to local models, custom prompts, and zero data sharing with third parties.

**Enterprise requirements?** Workato and Tray.io handle the compliance, audit trails, and SSO that smaller tools don't.

**Lots of browser-based work?** Bardeen automates what other tools can't — anything that runs in a Chrome tab.
