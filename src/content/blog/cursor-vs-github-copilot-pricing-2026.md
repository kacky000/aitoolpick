# Cursor vs GitHub Copilot Pricing 2026: Real Monthly Cost Compared

Cursor and GitHub Copilot are the two heavyweights of AI-assisted coding, but in 2026 their pricing diverged in a big way. Copilot moved to a usage-based AI-credit system on June 1, 2026, while Cursor runs on a monthly credit pool. On paper Copilot looks cheaper — but the real cost depends entirely on how heavily you lean on premium models.

## Plans at a Glance

| Tier | Cursor | GitHub Copilot |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Free | Hobby (limited) | Free — 2,000 completions + 50 chat/mo |
| Individual | Pro — **$20/mo** | Pro — **$10/mo** |
| Power user | Pro+ — $60/mo | Pro+ — $39/mo |
| Team | Teams — $40/user/mo | Business — $19/user/mo |
| Enterprise | Enterprise (custom) | Enterprise — $39/user/mo |

At sticker price, Copilot is the clear budget option: **$10/mo vs $20/mo** for individuals, and **$19 vs $40 per user** for teams. But both tools now meter premium AI usage, so the entry price is only the floor.

## How Each One Meters Usage

**Cursor** gives you a credit pool equal to your plan price — $20 of premium-model credits on Pro. Tab completions are effectively unlimited; the pool is for the heavier agent and chat requests. Run out, and you move to usage-based overage or wait for the monthly reset.

**GitHub Copilot** replaced "premium requests" with **AI Credits** in June 2026, where 1 credit = $0.01. Each plan includes monthly credits equal to its price — Pro gets $10 in credits, Business gets $19/user. Crucially, **code completions stay unlimited on all paid plans** and don't touch your credits. Credits are only consumed by premium chat and agent actions, scaled by model and tokens.

So both tools now work the same way underneath: a baseline subscription that includes a credit allowance, with completions free and premium agent work metered.

## Where the Real Cost Lands

Because completions are free on both, your bill is driven by **how much premium agent/chat work you do**:

- **Light users** (mostly autocomplete, occasional chat) → Copilot Pro at $10/mo is the cheapest path, and you may never exhaust the $10 credit allowance.
- **Moderate users** → It's close. Cursor's $20 pool gives more premium headroom out of the box; Copilot Pro's $10 allowance may need top-ups, narrowing the gap.
- **Heavy agent users** → Cursor's larger built-in pool (and Pro+/Ultra tiers) tends to be more cost-effective than stacking Copilot AI-credit overages, though it depends on which models you run.

## Beyond Price: What You're Paying For

Cursor's premium buys a purpose-built, AI-first editor with deep multi-model support (Claude, GPT, Gemini) and an aggressive agent mode. Copilot's lower price buys tight integration across the entire GitHub and VS Code ecosystem, plus enterprise features like IP indemnity on the Enterprise tier. If you live inside GitHub's workflow, Copilot's ecosystem value can outweigh Cursor's editor polish — and vice versa.

## Team Math

For a 20-person team, Copilot Business at $19/user/mo runs $4,560/year versus Cursor Teams at $40/user/mo for $9,600/year — more than double. Unless your team are heavy agent users who'd exhaust Copilot's per-seat credits and rack up overages, Copilot is the cheaper team buy by a wide margin.

## The Bottom Line

**GitHub Copilot wins on raw price** — $10/mo individual and $19/user team, with unlimited free completions. **Cursor justifies its premium** for developers who lean hard on multi-model agent work and want a bigger premium pool without managing overages. Try Copilot's free tier first; if you find yourself wanting a more aggressive AI-native editor, Cursor's extra $10/mo is an easy upgrade.
