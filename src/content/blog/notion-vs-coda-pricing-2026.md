# Notion vs Coda Pricing 2026: $10 Workspace vs $10 Doc Platform

Notion and Coda are the two heavyweights of the "docs + databases + automation in one app" category, and their entry paid plans match to the dollar — both $10/user/month. The headline price is a tie, so the real question is what you get for that $10, how the free tiers compare, and what happens to the bill as your team grows. Coda meters by "objects"; Notion meters by features and AI. That difference shapes which one is actually cheaper for you.

## The Plans Side by Side

| | Notion | Coda |
|---|--------|------|
| **Free** | $0, generous for individuals | $0, unlimited docs (object-limited) |
| **Entry paid** | Plus — $10/user/mo | Pro — $10/user/mo |
| **Team tier** | Business — $18/user/mo | Team — $30/user/mo |
| **Top tier** | Enterprise — custom | Enterprise — custom |
| **Metering** | Features + AI + file uploads | Object count + Packs/AI |

## The Free Tiers

**Notion Free ($0)** is genuinely strong for individuals — unlimited pages and blocks for personal use, with limits mainly on file upload size and team collaboration. For a solo user, it's often all you need.

**Coda Free ($0)** gives unlimited docs but caps the **number of objects** (rows, etc.) per doc — you can make as many documents as you like, but each one fills up. The constraint is structural rather than feature-based.

Verdict on free: Notion for solo note-taking and personal wikis; Coda if you want to build many small doc-apps and don't mind per-doc object ceilings. Notion even gives the **Plus plan free to students and educators** with a .edu email — a standout deal if you qualify.

## Entry Paid: Plus ($10) vs Pro ($10)

**Notion Plus — $10/user/month.** Removes file-upload limits, unlocks more collaboration, and is the tier where Notion becomes a real shared workspace. AI is available as part of the paid experience. At $10, it's the natural upgrade once Notion is your primary workspace rather than a personal notebook. (See the [full Notion pricing breakdown](/blog/notion-pricing-2026/).)

**Coda Pro — $10/user/month.** Removes the object limit that constrains the free tier — the single biggest reason serious Coda users upgrade. If you're building doc-apps that outgrow the free object cap, Pro fixes that cleanly. (See [Coda's full pricing](/blog/coda-pricing-2026/).)

The real difference at $10: Notion Plus buys *room and collaboration* for a flexible workspace; Coda Pro buys *scale* for object-heavy docs. Building a team wiki and project hub? Notion's $10. Building interactive doc-apps with lots of data and Packs? Coda's $10.

## Where They Scale — The $12 Gap

This is where the tie breaks. **Notion Business is $18/user/month**; **Coda Team is $30/user/month**. At the team tier, Notion is meaningfully cheaper — $12/user less, which compounds fast across a team. A 10-person team pays $180/mo on Notion Business vs $300/mo on Coda Team. Coda's higher tier earns its price only if you lean hard on live **Pack integrations** and Coda AI; if you don't, that's a lot of money for features you won't touch. Notion's Business tier (SSO, private spaces, advanced permissions) covers what most growing teams actually need.

So while the two are tied at $10 for individuals, **Notion pulls clearly ahead on team economics**. Coda's pitch at scale rests entirely on whether its Packs and doc-first model are central to how you work.

## Which Should You Pick?

- **Solo user or personal wiki** → Notion Free, upgrade to Plus ($10) when you hit file limits.
- **You build object-heavy interactive doc-apps** → Coda Pro ($10) for the object-limit removal.
- **Growing team on a budget** → Notion Business ($18/user) beats Coda Team ($30/user) by $12/user.
- **You live in Coda Packs and Coda AI** → Coda Team ($30) is justified; otherwise it's overpriced.

At the entry level it's a genuine $10 tie decided by use case — flexible workspace (Notion) vs object-scale doc-apps (Coda). But the moment you add a team, Notion's $18 Business tier undercuts Coda's $30 Team tier decisively, making Notion the safer value bet for most growing organizations.
