Jane App has become one of the most popular practice management platforms for allied health and wellness clinics — physiotherapists, chiropractors, massage therapists, dietitians, and multidisciplinary teams. But its plan structure is built around insurance and billing needs, which makes the **Jane App pricing** math different from therapy-only tools. This guide breaks down exactly what you'll pay in 2026 and which plan fits your practice.

## Jane App pricing at a glance (2026)

| Plan | Price (per month) | Best for |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| **Base** | $54 | Solo or small clinics without insurance billing |
| **Insurance** | $79 | Practices submitting insurance claims |
| **Corporate** | $99 | Multi-location and enterprise clinics |

Jane prices **per practice location**, not per practitioner, on the lower tiers — which is a meaningful difference from per-clinician tools like [TherapyNotes](/pricing/therapynotes/) (Solo at $49/clinician). For a growing team, that flat-location pricing can make Jane dramatically cheaper per head.

## What each plan includes

### Base — $54/month
The entry plan covers the core that most clinics need day one:

- Online booking and a customizable booking site
- Scheduling with multiple practitioners and rooms
- Charting and customizable templates
- Credit card processing and invoicing
- Automated appointment reminders (email; SMS billed separately)

This is the right starting point for a cash-pay clinic — wellness, coaching, bodywork — that doesn't touch insurance.

### Insurance — $79/month
Adds the billing engine that allied-health clinics in the US and Canada rely on:

- Insurance claim submission and tracking
- Claims management workflows
- Insurer-specific billing codes and remittance handling

If even one practitioner bills insurance, you'll need this tier. The $25/month jump over Base is small relative to the admin hours saved on claims.

### Corporate — $99/month
For groups running multiple locations or needing centralized control:

- Multi-location reporting and consolidated dashboards
- Advanced permissions and staff roles
- Priority support

## The add-ons that change your real cost

Jane's headline price isn't the whole story. Budget for:

- **SMS reminders** — billed per text, not bundled. High-volume clinics should model this carefully.
- **Payment processing fees** — standard card rates apply on top of the subscription.
- **Jane Payments / terminal hardware** — optional in-person card readers.

Even with these, Jane's per-location pricing usually lands cheaper than per-seat competitors once you pass three or four practitioners.

## Is Jane App worth it in 2026?

For multidisciplinary and allied-health clinics — especially those billing insurance with several practitioners — Jane's per-location model makes it one of the best-value platforms on the market. Solo cash-pay therapists may find a behavioral-health-focused tool a better fit, but anyone running a team of three or more should put Jane near the top of the list.
