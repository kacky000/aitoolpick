**[Notion](/tools/notion/)** and **[ClickUp](/tools/clickup/)** are both pitched as all-in-one workspaces, but they target different jobs — Notion leans into docs and knowledge, ClickUp into project and task management. Their pricing reflects that, and the cheaper option depends entirely on what you need.

This guide compares every tier, what unlocks each upgrade, and which platform costs less for small teams versus growing companies.

## Pricing Overview

### Notion Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Right For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free | $0 | Individuals, light use |
| Plus | $10/user/mo | Small teams, freelancers |
| Business | $20/user/mo | Growing companies |
| Enterprise | Custom | Large org security |

### ClickUp Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Right For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Free Forever | $0 | Personal use |
| Unlimited | $7/user/mo | Small teams |
| Business | $12/user/mo | Mid-size teams |
| Business Plus | $19/user/mo | Multiple teams |
| Enterprise | Custom | Large org security |

*All paid prices are with annual billing. Monthly billing adds roughly 30–40%.*

## The Key Difference: Entry Price vs Feature Depth

**ClickUp is cheaper at the entry tier.** Its Unlimited plan is **$7/user**, undercutting Notion's Plus at $10. ClickUp also packs more *project management* depth into lower tiers — unlimited dashboards, time tracking, and Gantt views land early.

**Notion's value is in docs and structure.** At $10 Plus you get unlimited team blocks and a cleaner knowledge base, but task-management features (timelines, advanced views) are lighter than ClickUp's.

So the "cheaper" tool depends on the job: ClickUp wins on raw price for project management; Notion wins if your real need is documentation and you'd otherwise pay for a separate wiki.

## Cost Scenario: A 15-Person Team

**Entry paid tier:**

- **ClickUp Unlimited**: 15 × $7 = **$105/month**
- **Notion Plus**: 15 × $10 = **$150/month**

**Mid tier (more automation, admin controls):**

- **ClickUp Business**: 15 × $12 = **$180/month**
- **Notion Business**: 15 × $20 = **$300/month**

ClickUp is **30–40% cheaper** at every comparable tier. The gap widens at the Business level, where Notion's jump to $20 is steep.

## Free Plan Comparison

- **ClickUp Free Forever**: Unlimited members and tasks, but caps "uses" of advanced features (e.g., Gantt, dashboards) and storage at 100MB. Surprisingly capable for task management.
- **Notion Free**: Great for individuals; collaborative team use is limited by block allowances and member features.

ClickUp's free tier is the stronger option for a small team that needs task management today. Notion's free tier is better for a solo knowledge worker.

## Hidden Cost Considerations

- **ClickUp AI** is a **separate add-on** (~$7/user/mo) on top of your plan — factor it in if you want AI.
- **Notion AI** is now bundled into paid plans, which can make Notion's effective AI cost more competitive than the sticker prices suggest.

## Verdict

**Choose ClickUp** if your primary need is project and task management and you want the lowest per-seat price. At $7 Unlimited and $12 Business, it's consistently cheaper and more feature-dense for PM work.

**Choose Notion** if documentation, wikis, and knowledge management are central — and you'd rather consolidate docs + light tasks into one clean tool than pay for a separate wiki.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is ClickUp cheaper than Notion?

Yes, at every comparable tier. ClickUp Unlimited is $7/user vs Notion Plus at $10, and ClickUp Business is $12 vs Notion Business at $20.

### Is Notion AI included or an add-on?

Notion AI is now bundled into paid plans. ClickUp AI, by contrast, is a separate add-on of about $7/user/month.

### Which is better for project management?

ClickUp. It includes more native PM features (Gantt, time tracking, dashboards, automations) at lower tiers than Notion.

### Which is better for documentation?

Notion. Its docs, wikis, and knowledge-base experience are cleaner and more mature than ClickUp's Docs feature.
