**[Xero](/tools/xero/)** and **[FreshBooks](/tools/freshbooks/)** solve the same problem from opposite ends. FreshBooks is built for **freelancers and service businesses** who live in invoices; Xero is built for **growing teams** who need full double-entry accounting with unlimited users. Here's how they compare in 2026.

## Xero vs FreshBooks: Pricing

As of March 1, 2026, US monthly pricing:

| Tier | Xero | FreshBooks |
|------|------|------------|
| Entry | Early **$25** | Lite **$19** |
| Mid | Growing **$55** | Plus **$38** |
| Top | Established **$90** | Premium **$65** |
| Users | **Unlimited** | Billed per extra user (+$11/ea) |
| Client cap | Unlimited | Lite caps billable clients |
| Free plan | No | No |

FreshBooks starts cheaper at **$19**, but its lower tiers cap the number of billable clients, and every extra team member adds **~$11/mo**. Xero has no user fees at all.

## Where FreshBooks Wins

- **Invoicing experience.** FreshBooks has the slickest invoice and proposal flow of any tool here — built for client-facing solos.
- **Time tracking + projects.** Native and well-integrated, ideal for consultants billing hours.
- **Simplicity.** Less accounting jargon; non-accountants get productive fast.

Full tiers in our [FreshBooks pricing guide](/blog/freshbooks-pricing-2026) and [FreshBooks review](/blog/freshbooks-review-2026).

## Where Xero Wins

- **Unlimited users.** No per-seat fees — a structural win for any team of 2+.
- **Real double-entry accounting.** Better for inventory, multi-currency, and handing books to an accountant.
- **Scales without re-platforming.** You won't outgrow it the way solos outgrow FreshBooks' caps.

Details in our [Xero pricing guide](/blog/xero-pricing-2026) and [Xero review](/blog/xero-review-2026).

## The Bottom Line

If your business **is** invoicing — solo, client-facing, hours-based — FreshBooks is purpose-built and starts at $19. The moment you add team members or need true accounting depth, **Xero's unlimited-user model wins on both features and cost**. Trial both for a billing cycle; the right answer is usually obvious by the first invoice.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is FreshBooks cheaper than Xero?

At entry level, yes — FreshBooks Lite is $19 versus Xero Early $25. But FreshBooks charges ~$11/mo per extra user while Xero includes unlimited users, so teams of 2+ often pay less on Xero.

### Is FreshBooks real accounting software?

FreshBooks added double-entry accounting on higher tiers, but it's still invoicing-first. For inventory, multi-currency, or accountant-grade books, Xero or [QuickBooks](/blog/quickbooks-pricing-2026) are stronger.

### Which is better for freelancers?

FreshBooks, in most cases — it's designed around the freelance invoicing workflow. See our [best accounting software for freelancers](/blog/best-accounting-software-for-freelancers-2026) for the full ranking.
