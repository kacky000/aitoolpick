# Obsidian vs Craft 2026: Power vs Polish

Obsidian and Craft attract very different note-takers. Obsidian is the local-first, plugin-powered tool for people who want to build their own system. Craft is the beautifully designed, Apple-native app for people who want elegance out of the box. In 2026, choosing between them comes down to one trade-off: **power vs polish**. Here's the full comparison.

## Pricing

| | Obsidian | Craft |
|---|----------|-------|
| Free tier | **Yes** (full app) | Yes (limited) |
| Paid plan | $4/mo (Sync, optional) | **$5/mo** (Pro) |
| Business | Commercial license | $10/mo |
| Data storage | **Local markdown files** | Craft cloud (+ export) |

Both have free tiers and affordable paid plans — Obsidian's Sync is $4/month, Craft Pro is about $5/month. Cost isn't the deciding factor here; the experience is. See our [Obsidian pricing breakdown](/blog/obsidian-pricing-2026/) and [Craft review](/blog/craft-review-2026/) for the specifics.

## Design and experience: Craft's home turf

Craft is one of the **best-looking note apps ever made**. Typography, blocks, page-as-card design, and buttery animations make writing genuinely pleasant. It's deeply integrated with Apple platforms — Shortcuts, share sheet, widgets, Apple Pencil — and feels native on Mac, iPad, and iPhone.

Obsidian, by contrast, looks like what you make it. The default UI is functional and a bit utilitarian. With themes and CSS you can make it beautiful, but **out of the box Craft wins on polish by a mile**.

## Power and extensibility: Obsidian's home turf

Obsidian's strength is its **plugin ecosystem and local files**. Dataview queries, graph view, canvas, custom workflows — if you can imagine a note-taking system, you can probably build it in Obsidian. Your notes are plain markdown you fully own.

Craft is more **opinionated and closed**. It's gorgeous and capable for documents, but you don't get a sprawling plugin marketplace or local-file ownership in the same way. What you see is mostly what you get.

## Data ownership

- **Obsidian:** local-first markdown. Total ownership, total portability, future-proof.
- **Craft:** cloud-based with export options. Convenient sync, but your notes live in Craft's ecosystem.

For users who prioritize "I will own these notes in 20 years," Obsidian is the clear pick.

## Cross-platform

- **Craft:** exceptional on Apple, available on Windows/web, but Apple is where it sings.
- **Obsidian:** truly cross-platform — Windows, Mac, Linux, iOS, Android — all first-class.

If you're outside the Apple ecosystem, Obsidian is the more natural home.

## Which should you choose?

**Choose Craft if you want:**
- The most beautiful writing and document experience available.
- Deep Apple integration.
- Polish over configurability.

**Choose Obsidian if you want:**
- Local-first ownership of plain markdown files.
- A plugin ecosystem to build any workflow.
- True cross-platform support including Linux and Android.

## The bottom line

This isn't a "better app" question — it's a "which kind of person are you" question. **Craft is for people who want beauty and simplicity handed to them. Obsidian is for people who want power and ownership they control.** If you live on Apple devices and value design, try Craft. If you want a future-proof, infinitely extensible second brain, choose Obsidian.
