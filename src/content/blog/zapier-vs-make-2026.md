# Zapier vs Make 2026: Which Automation Tool Wins?

Zapier and Make (formerly Integromat) are the two leading no-code automation platforms of 2026. Both connect your apps and move data automatically—but they trade off simplicity against power and price.

## Quick Answer

Choose **Zapier** if you want the largest app library and the simplest setup. Choose **Make** if you want visual, branching workflows with more logic per dollar.

## Pricing Compared

| Plan | Zapier | Make |
|------|--------|------|
| Free | Yes (limited tasks) | Yes (limited operations) |
| Starter/Core | ~$19.99/mo | ~$9/mo |
| Pro | ~$49/mo | ~$16/mo |
| Billing unit | Tasks | Operations |

Make is generally cheaper per run because it counts granular "operations" and packs more into lower tiers. Zapier charges per "task" and costs more at scale, but its pricing is simpler to predict.

## Where Zapier Wins

- **App coverage**: The largest integration library—8,000+ apps and counting.
- **Ease of use**: Linear trigger-action setup that anyone can build in minutes.
- **AI features**: Built-in AI steps and natural-language Zap creation.
- **Reliability**: Mature platform with strong documentation and support.

## Where Make Wins

- **Visual builder**: A flexible canvas for complex, branching, multi-step scenarios.
- **Price-to-power**: More operations and logic at lower price points.
- **Data control**: Fine-grained handling of arrays, iterators, and routers.
- **Advanced logic**: Better suited to intricate workflows without code.

## Head-to-Head

| Factor | Zapier | Make |
|--------|--------|------|
| App library | Largest | Large |
| Ease of use | Easiest | Moderate |
| Complex workflows | Good | Excellent |
| Price-to-power | Higher cost | Better value |
| Best for | Quick automations | Complex scenarios |

## Who Should Choose What?

**Choose Zapier if you:**
- Want the widest app support and the fastest setup
- Build simple, linear automations
- Prefer predictable, no-fuss configuration

**Choose Make if you:**
- Build complex, branching workflows
- Want more automation volume per dollar
- Like a visual canvas with deep logic control

If you want an open-source angle, compare both against the contenders in [n8n vs Make](/blog/n8n-vs-make-2026/).

## The Bottom Line

**Zapier** wins on simplicity and app coverage; **Make** wins on price-to-power and complex logic. Start with Zapier for quick wins; move to Make when your workflows outgrow simple trigger-action chains. Both offer free tiers to test first.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Make cheaper than Zapier?

Generally yes—Make's operation-based pricing packs more automation into lower tiers, though Zapier's task-based pricing is simpler to predict.

### Which is easier for beginners?

Zapier. Its linear trigger-action setup is the simplest to learn, while Make's visual canvas has a steeper curve.

### Does Zapier have more integrations?

Yes, Zapier has the largest app library, though Make covers most popular apps too.
