**Monarch Money vs Rocket Money** is a common 2026 decision for ex-Mint users. Both connect your accounts and track spending, but Monarch is a premium all-in-one budgeting hub while Rocket Money is an automated savings tool with a free tier. Here's how to choose.

## Quick Verdict

- **Choose Monarch Money** if you want serious budgeting, net worth tracking, and couples features in one ad-free app.
- **Choose Rocket Money** if you want automatic bill negotiation, subscription cancellation, and a free way to monitor spending.

## Pricing Compared

| | Monarch Money | Rocket Money |
|---|---------------|--------------|
| Free tier | Trial only (7 days) | Yes (limited) |
| Paid plan | $14.99/mo or $99.99/yr | $6–$12/month |
| Bill negotiation | No | Yes (takes a cut) |
| Couples access | Included | Yes |

Monarch is a flat subscription with no upsells. Rocket Money has a free tier and "pay what you want" premium. See the [Monarch pricing breakdown](/blog/monarch-money-pricing-2026) for details.

## Different Goals, Different Apps

**Monarch Money** is a full financial hub. Budgeting, net worth, investments, goals, and couples collaboration all live in one clean, ad-free dashboard. It's the closest thing to a Mint replacement that also does more than Mint did. Read our [Monarch Money review](/blog/monarch-money-review-2026).

**Rocket Money** is a money-saving utility. Its standout features find and cancel forgotten subscriptions and negotiate bills like internet and phone for a cut of the savings. Budgeting exists but is secondary, and the free tier covers basic tracking.

## Who Should Pick Which?

- **Serious budgeting and net worth** → Monarch. It's a complete hub.
- **Cutting wasted spending automatically** → Rocket Money. It saves money for you.
- **Want something free** → Rocket Money's free tier.
- **Couples wanting a shared dashboard** → Monarch.

## Verdict

These two solve different problems. **Monarch Money** is the better choice if you want to actively budget and see your whole financial life in one place. **Rocket Money** is the better choice if your main issue is forgotten subscriptions and overpriced bills — and it's free to start. Some people run Rocket Money's free tier for savings while using Monarch as their real budget.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Rocket Money free and Monarch not?

Yes. Rocket Money has a real free tier; Monarch only offers a 7-day trial before requiring a subscription.

### Does Monarch negotiate bills like Rocket Money?

No. Bill negotiation and subscription cancellation are Rocket Money's signature features. Monarch focuses on budgeting and net worth.

### Which is the better Mint replacement?

Monarch, for most users — it's a fuller all-in-one app. Rocket Money is better if you mainly want automated savings rather than active budgeting.
